何謂事奉（Avodah）？── 從會幕的扛抬學習謙卑 What Is Avodah? Learning Humility from the Carrying of the Tabernacle

取自《拿索》篇（民數記 4 章），Morah Giborah McDonald 帶我們進入事奉的核心：עֲבוֹדָה（Avodah）。藉著利未人扛抬會幕的「מַשָּׂא（masa，重擔）」，她教導我們：把你被分派的工作，帶著喜樂去做；不要讓驕傲進來。連搬運燭台、搬運食物，都是神聖的事奉。 Drawn from Parashat Naso (Numbers 4), Morah Giborah McDonald leads us into the heart of service: Avodah (עֲבוֹדָה). Through the Levites' burden-bearing (masa, מַשָּׂא) of the Tabernacle, she teaches: do the job you are assigned with joy; do not let pride enter. Even carrying the candlesticks and the food is a holy act.

在安息日結束、哈夫達拉的燭光熄滅之後，Morah Giborah McDonald 帶我們進入本週《拿索》篇（נָשֹׂא Naso，民數記 4 章）的核心。這一段經文乍看只是一份枯燥的「分工清單」——誰扛帷幔、誰扛器皿、誰扛柱子——但她讓我們看見，在這份清單背後藏著事奉的全部祕密：עֲבוֹדָה（Avodah，事奉）的真正考驗，不在於你被分派到多大的工作，而在於你能否帶著喜樂與謙卑去做最小的工作。連搬運燭台、搬運食物，都是神聖的行為。驕傲，是事奉唯一的敵人。 After Shabbat has ended and the Havdalah candle is snuffed out, Morah Giborah McDonald leads us into the heart of this week's portion, Parashat Naso (נָשֹׂא, Numbers 4). At first glance the text reads like a dry assignment roster — who carries the curtains, who carries the vessels, who carries the poles — but she shows us the whole secret of service hidden inside that list: the real test of Avodah (עֲבוֹדָה, service) is not the size of the task you are handed, but whether you can do the smallest task with joy and humility. Even carrying the candlesticks and the food is a holy act. Pride is the one enemy of service.

一、事奉的兩種面貌 ── עֲבוֹדָה Avodah 與 מַשָּׂא masaI. Two Faces of Service — Avodah and masa

Morah Giborah 從《妥拉》對利未人（Leviim）工作的描述開始。經文把他們的工作分成兩類，她特別把它們標了顏色，好讓人記住：一類是 עֲבוֹדַת עֲבוֹדָה（Avodat Avodah）——「事奉的事奉」，也就是禮儀本身的服事；另一類是 עֲבוֹדַת מַשָּׂא（Avodat masa）——「扛抬的事奉」，也就是搬運會幕一切聖物的勞力。 Morah Giborah begins with how the Torah describes the work of the Levites (Leviim). Their labour is divided into two categories — she even colour-codes them so we remember: one is Avodat Avodah (עֲבוֹדַת עֲבוֹדָה), “the service of service,” the liturgical ministry itself; the other is Avodat masa (עֲבוֹדַת מַשָּׂא), “the service of the burden” — the physical labour of transporting every holy item of the Tabernacle.

「從三十歲直到五十歲，凡前來任職、在會幕裡辦事和扛抬的，都要計算。」——民數記 4:47（《拿索》篇） “From thirty years old up to fifty years old, everyone who came to do the work of service and the work of bearing burdens in the tent of meeting.”— Numbers 4:47 (Parashat Naso)

這個 masa（מַשָּׂא）的字根，正是「背負、扛起、承擔」之意——同一個字根，也用來指「重擔」與「先知的默示（負擔）」。換句話說，《妥拉》從一開始就把「事奉」與「承擔重擔」綁在一起：真正的事奉，從來不是輕省的；它是一份你要用肩膀扛起來的重量。 The root of masa (מַשָּׂא) means exactly “to lift, to carry, to bear” — the same root used for a “burden” and even for a prophet's “oracle” (a weight laid upon him). In other words, the Torah binds “service” to “bearing a burden” from the very start: real Avodah is never weightless. It is a weight you take up on your shoulders.

二、學徒期 ── 為什麼是二十五歲開始，三十歲才上工？II. The Apprenticeship — Why Start at 25 but Serve at 30?

Morah Giborah 指出一個看似矛盾的地方，並提醒我們：「你看，讀經必須讀得仔細。」民數記 8:24 說利未人「從二十五歲起」就要供職，但民數記 4:47 卻說「從三十歲起」。她引用《塔木德》〈Hulin〉24a 的一段「巴萊塔」(baraita)：不可能說三十歲——因為二十五歲已經寫了；也不可能說二十五歲——因為三十歲也已經寫了。那這兩節經文要如何調和？ Morah Giborah flags an apparent contradiction, and reminds us: “You see, you have to read Scripture carefully.” Numbers 8:24 says the Levites serve “from twenty-five years old,” yet Numbers 4:47 says “from thirty years old.” She cites a baraita from the Talmud (Hulin 24a): it is impossible to say thirty, since twenty-five is already stated; and impossible to say twenty-five, since thirty is already stated. So how are the two verses reconciled?

答案，是猶太傳統裡一個極美的觀念：二十五歲開始的是「學徒期」，三十歲才正式「上工」。頭五年，利未人是在學習——學習如何扛抬聖櫃、如何捲收帷幔、如何搬運柱子。Morah Giborah 強調這一點：「非常重要——你需要一段學徒期，才能學會如何扛抬任何屬於哈謝姆（Hashem）的東西。」 The answer is a beautiful idea in Jewish tradition: twenty-five is when the apprenticeship begins; thirty is when one is ready for full service. For five years the Levite is in training — learning how to carry the Ark, how to fold the curtains, how to bear the poles. Morah Giborah underscores it: “Very important — you need an apprenticeship to learn how to carry anything that belongs to Hashem.”

這裡已經藏著第一道關於謙卑的功課：沒有人是一上來就「資格齊全」的。即使是被神揀選、專門承擔聖工的支派，也要先伏在師傅手下，學五年，才被認為「預備好了」。在一個人人都想要「馬上被看見、馬上被使用」的時代，這份「先學徒、後事奉」的次序，本身就是對驕傲的一記溫柔的攔阻。 Here is already the first lesson in humility: no one arrives fully “qualified.” Even the tribe chosen and set apart for sacred work first had to submit under a master, learn for five years, and only then be deemed “ready.” In an age where everyone wants to be seen and used immediately, this order — apprentice first, serve after — is itself a gentle restraint against pride.

三、他們扛的是什麼 ── 都是聖物III. What They Carried — Every Item Holy

Morah Giborah 帶我們一一數算利未人所扛抬的器物，提醒我們這些都不是普通的家具：銅製的洗濯盆（laver）與獻祭所用的一切器皿、一切的鍋、燭台、那盞 menorah（מְנוֹרָה）、陳設餅的桌子；最後，由亞倫蓋上聖櫃、把扛抬的槓穿好。「Avodat masa」這個類別，指的就是在行進途中搬運整個會幕（Mishkan，מִשְׁכָּן）的責任。 Morah Giborah walks us through the items the Levites bore, reminding us these are no ordinary furniture: the bronze laver and all the vessels of the sacrificial service, all the pots, the candelabra, the menorah (מְנוֹרָה), the table of showbread; and finally Aaron would cover the Ark and set its carrying-poles in place. The category “Avodat masa” refers precisely to the responsibility of transporting the whole Tabernacle (Mishkan, מִשְׁכָּן) during travel.

她引用一位拉比——Rabbeinu Bachya（拉比尼·貝卡耶）——對 Avodat masa 這個詞所作的精彩註解。第一個重點是分工的精確性：利未三大家族——גֵּרְשׁוֹן（Gershon，革順）、מְרָרִי（Merari，米拉利）、קְהָת（Kehat，哥轄）——各有清楚的、由哈謝姆親自吩咐摩西（Moshe）的職責。沒有人可以自選工作，也沒有人可以隨意換崗。 She cites a rabbi — Rabbeinu Bachya — for a remarkable comment on the phrase Avodat masa. The first point is the specificity of the assignment: the three Levitical families — Gershon (גֵּרְשׁוֹן), Merari (מְרָרִי), and Kehat (קְהָת) — each had clear duties given by Hashem directly to Moses (Moshe). No one chose his own job; no one swapped posts at will.

第二個重點是體力與奉獻。利未人是專為這份特定的體力勞動被揀選的，而且是在他們體力的巔峰之年——三十到五十歲。承載聖所，需要兩樣東西：足夠的體能，與對聖物之神聖性的心志奉獻。Morah Giborah 反覆強調：「這些都是聖物。」你扛的不是貨物，是神的同在所棲息之處。 The second point is physical strength and dedication. The Levites were chosen for this specific labour, and during their peak years — thirty to fifty. Bearing the sanctuary required two things: enough physical capacity and a dedication of the heart to the sanctity of the items. Morah Giborah repeats it: “These are holy items.” You are not carrying cargo — you are carrying the dwelling-place of God's presence.

四、扛抬即護衛 ── 圍繞會幕的屏障IV. To Carry Is to Guard — A Barrier Around the Tabernacle

Rabbeinu Bachya 的第三個洞見，是 Morah Giborah 特別看重的：扛抬本身，也是一種保護。當利未人環繞並抬起會幕時，他們其實充當了一道護衛的屏障——擋住那本可能因聖物被輕慢而臨到以色列全會眾的烈怒、瘟疫、忿恨與火。換句話說，扛抬聖物的人，是站在聖潔與會眾之間的人。 Rabbeinu Bachya's third insight is one Morah Giborah dwells on: the carrying itself is also a protection. When the Levites surrounded and lifted the Tabernacle, they served as a protective barrier — holding back the wrath, plague, anger, and fire that might otherwise have struck the whole congregation of Israel. In other words, the one who bears the holy things stands between the holiness and the people.

她也順著拉比的思路，把兩類事奉再分得更細：Avodat Avodah 偏向「禮儀」的一面——拉比認為這指向樂器、伴奏，以及各樣準備性的角色；而 Avodat masa 則是實際扛抬器皿的勞力。一個是聲音與預備，一個是肩膀與汗水——但兩者都是 Avodah，都是事奉，沒有高下之分。 Following the rabbi, she draws the two categories finer: Avodat Avodah leans toward the liturgical side — the rabbi takes it to mean the musical instruments, the accompaniment, and the various preparatory roles; while Avodat masa is the labour of physically carrying the vessels. One is sound and preparation, the other is shoulders and sweat — but both are Avodah, both are service, and neither outranks the other.

五、按哈謝姆的口 ── עַל פִּי ה׳ 誰扛什麼，都是神定的V. By the Mouth of Hashem — God Assigns Who Carries What

Morah Giborah 接著講到一個希伯來片語：עַל פִּי ה׳（al pi Hashem）——「按哈謝姆的口」、「照耶和華所吩咐的」。核心的領受是：這場搬運的每一個細節——誰扛哪一件——都是哈謝姆親自定規的。它不是憑人意分配的差事，而是從神口中發出的命令。 Morah Giborah turns to a Hebrew phrase: al pi Hashem (עַל פִּי ה׳) — “by the mouth of Hashem,” “at the command of the LORD.” The core takeaway is that every detail of the transport — who carried which item — was determined by Hashem Himself. It was not a chore handed out by human preference, but a command issued from the mouth of God.

這帶出一個嚴肅的界線：如果你不是利未人，你就不可以「去幫忙搬」。她說得很具體——如果你出於猶大支派、或以法蓮、或拿弗他利、或但支派，卻擅自上前去抬聖物，你很可能會被瘟疫或哈謝姆的火擊打。這不是因為神吝嗇，而是因為這份體力勞動本身就是屬靈的命令，根本不是普通的「人力」。Morah Giborah 直截了當地說：「它根本就不是體力活。」 This draws a serious line: if you were not a Levite, you could not “go and help carry.” She is concrete about it — if you came from the tribe of Judah, or Ephraim, or Naphtali, or Dan, and presumed to step forward and bear the holy things, you would most likely have been struck by plague or the fire of Hashem. Not because God is stingy, but because this physical labour was itself a spiritual command, never mere manpower. As Morah Giborah puts it bluntly: “It was not manual labour at all.”

她指出，這位拉比把字面的解釋（p'shat）、米大示的解釋（midrash）、眾賢哲的領會、甚至會幕搬運的卡巴拉層面，全都整合在一起，為要說明一件事：利未人肩上的「重擔」，是一場神聖的事奉，而不是搬運工的差事。 She notes that this rabbi integrates the literal sense (p'shat), the midrashic reading (midrash), the understanding of the sages, and even the Kabbalistic dimensions of the Tabernacle's transport — all to make one point: the “burden” on the Levites' shoulders was a sacred act of divine service, not a porter's errand.

六、謙卑的事奉 ── עֲנָוָה anavah 與驕傲的危險VI. Service in Humility — anavah and the Danger of Pride

講到這裡，Morah Giborah 把整段教導推向它的心臟：在事奉裡最要緊的，是謙卑——עֲנָוָה（anavah）。她說：「任何為哈謝姆做工的人，在他的事奉裡都需要謙卑。」 Here Morah Giborah drives the whole teaching to its heart: what matters most in service is humility — anavah (עֲנָוָה). She says: “Anybody who works for Hashem needs to be humble in their service.”

她用利未家族之間的對比來說明這一點。革順人（Gershonites）所分到的，是帷幔、罩棚、簾子——這份工作，看起來可能既卑微又繁瑣；而 哥轄人（Kohathites）則扛抬器皿，看起來「更體面」。但 Morah Giborah 把這正面提出來，作為對我們每一個人的話： She illustrates with a contrast between the Levitical families. The Gershonites were given the curtains, the coverings, the screens — work that could seem menial and burdensome; while the Kohathites carried the vessels, which looks “more dignified.” But Morah Giborah lifts this up as a word for every one of us:

「哈謝姆事奉中的任何一部分，都不可被輕看。打掃廁所的人，和教導的人、帶敬拜的人，同樣重要。你若不持守謙卑，你必跌倒。」——Morah Giborah McDonald “No part of Hashem's service is to be despised. The person cleaning the bathrooms is just as important as the person doing the teaching, as the person leading the worship. If you do not keep yourself humble, you will fall.”— Morah Giborah McDonald

這裡是這篇教導最鋒利、也最溫柔的一刀。驕傲，是事奉唯一的敵人。它不是從「做大事」來的，而是從「我做的事比你做的事更重要」這個念頭來的。革順人若因為自己只扛帷幔而自卑、或哥轄人若因為自己扛聖櫃而自高，兩者都已經偏離了 Avodah 的本意。事奉的尺度，從來不是器物的貴賤，而是心的謙卑。 This is the sharpest — and gentlest — cut in the teaching. Pride is the one enemy of service. It does not come from “doing big things”; it comes from the thought, “what I do is more important than what you do.” If the Gershonite despises himself for carrying only curtains, or the Kohathite exalts himself for carrying the Ark, both have already missed the meaning of Avodah. The measure of service is never the value of the object, but the humility of the heart.

七、連搬運燭台與食物，都是神聖的VII. Even Carrying the Candlesticks and the Food Is Holy

這就是整段教導的金句，也是 Morah Giborah 要我們帶回家的一句話：「連搬運燭台和食物，都是一件神聖的行為。」這句話之所以有力，是因為它把神聖的重心，從器物本身的重要性，轉移到了那份被分派的工作上。 This is the golden line of the whole teaching, the one Morah Giborah wants us to carry home: “Even carrying the candlesticks and the food is a holy act.” Its power lies in shifting the centre of holiness away from the importance of the object and onto the assigned task itself.

把這個原則帶進今天的生活，它會重新定義我們所說的「事奉」。它挑戰一個觀念——好像只有那些顯眼的、戲劇性的行動才算神聖。其實，每一個被分派的角色——無論在教會、在家庭、在職場、在社區——都可能是一場真實的 Avodah。Morah Giborah 在這場哈夫達拉聚會裡，先請弟兄們在安息日的餐桌上為妻子朗讀《箴言》31 章「才德的婦人」（Eshet Chayil），又提到丈夫在週五為妻子買花的習俗——這些看似「家常」的舉動，正是 Avodah 落地的樣子：在最日常、最看不見的地方，帶著喜樂與尊榮去服事。 Brought into life today, this principle redefines what we mean by “ministry.” It challenges the notion that only visible, dramatic acts are holy. In truth, every assigned role — in the church, the home, the workplace, the community — can be a genuine Avodah. In this very Havdalah gathering Morah Giborah first asked the husbands to read over their wives the “woman of valour” (Eshet Chayil, Proverbs 31) at the Shabbat table, and recalled the custom of a husband buying his wife flowers on a Friday — these “ordinary” acts are exactly what Avodah looks like when it touches the ground: to serve, with joy and honour, in the most daily and least visible places.

對華人信徒而言，這份信息格外切身。在一個常常以「身分、頭銜、被看見的程度」來衡量價值的文化裡，Avodah 的教導是一帖解藥：真正的事奉不求被看見，只求那份工作本身被分別為聖。你被分派去擺椅子、去洗碗、去看顧孩子、去默默地代禱——這些都不是「等你有天做大事之前」的過渡；它們本身就是聖工。 For Chinese believers this message is especially close to home. In a culture that often measures worth by status, title, and visibility, the teaching of Avodah is an antidote: true service does not seek to be seen; it only seeks that the task itself be set apart as holy. Being assigned to set out chairs, wash dishes, watch the children, or quietly intercede is not a holding pattern “until one day you do something big.” These things are the sacred work itself.

八、結語 ── 把重擔扛起來，迎接那將臨的VIII. Closing — Take Up the Burden, Toward the One Who Comes

這場教導從哈夫達拉的燭光與〈Lekha Dodi〉的歌聲流出——那是在等候 瑪西亞（Mashiach）、等候最終救贖的歌。Morah Giborah 把利未人的肩膀，接到了我們每一個人的肩膀上：承認神放在你生命中的那份「重擔」，接受那份被分派的職責；然後，無論大小、無論顯隱，都用一顆喜樂、奉獻、深深謙卑的心去做它。因為當你這樣事奉時，你就是在參與神聖的工作，一步一步走向那將臨的救贖。 This teaching flows out of the Havdalah candlelight and the song of Lekha Dodi — the song that waits for Mashiach and the final redemption. Morah Giborah joins the Levites' shoulders to our own: acknowledge the “burden” God has placed on your life, accept the duty assigned to you; then, whether grand or small, whether seen or unseen, do it with a heart of joy, dedication, and deep humility. For when you serve this way, you are taking part in the sacred work of God, drawing step by step toward the redemption that is coming.

「哈謝姆事奉中的任何一部分，都不可被輕看。打掃廁所的人，和教導的人、帶敬拜的人同樣重要。連搬運燭台和食物，都是一件神聖的行為。把你被分派的工作，帶著喜樂去做——不要讓驕傲進來；因為你若不持守謙卑，你必跌倒。」 “No part of Hashem's service is to be despised. The one cleaning the bathrooms is just as important as the one who teaches, the one who leads worship. Even carrying the candlesticks and the food is a holy act. Do the job you are assigned — and do it with joy; do not let pride enter, for if you do not keep yourself humble, you will fall.”
