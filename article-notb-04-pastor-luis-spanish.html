當天庭觸及大地 ── Zman Matan Torateinu When Heaven Touches Earth — Zman Matan Torateinu

這段教導原本以西班牙語傳講。一位來自哥倫比亞的牧師——路易斯——在拉斯維加斯新婦之夜（Milchemet HaChatunah，新婦之戰）的長夜中插入一段安靜的默想：五旬節（Shavuot）的五個名字、西奈山的chuppah（新婚帳幔）、四種Anan HaKavot（榮耀的雲彩），以及那位仍然站在山下、等候祂的新郎再來的kallah（新婦）。 This teaching was given in Spanish. A pastor from Colombia — Luis — stepped into the long night of the Las Vegas Night of the Bride (Milchemet HaChatunah, “the war of the wedding”) and inserted a quiet meditation: the five names of Shavuot, the chuppah at Sinai, the four Anan HaKavot (clouds of glory), and the kallah (bride) still standing at the foot of the mountain, waiting for her Bridegroom's return.

「Quiero darle las gracias al Rabino Dr. Isaac Shapira y a todo el equipo de Ahabat Ami.」——「我要感謝拉比博士以撒·沙皮拉，以及 אַהֲבַת עַמִּי（Ahavat Ammi，我民之愛）的全體團隊。」這是路易斯牧師——從哥倫比亞、用西班牙語——在新婦之夜的長夜中，向講台上的kallah（新婦，כַּלָּה）默想開場的第一句話。在這個來自三大洲、橫跨十二個時區的Milchemet HaChatunah（婚禮之戰）的夜晚，路易斯牧師遞給我們一把很小、很安靜、卻很鋒利的鑰匙：五旬節 Shavuot（שָׁבוּעוֹת）的真正名字，是「Zman Matan Torateinu」（זְמַן מַתַּן תּוֹרָתֵנוּ）——「我們的妥拉被頒賜的時刻」。換言之，五旬節不是一個日曆上的日子；它是天庭親自彎下身、觸碰大地的那一刻。 “Quiero darle las gracias al Rabino Dr. Isaac Shapira y a todo el equipo de Ahabat Ami.” — “I want to thank Rabbi Dr. Itzhak Shapira and the entire אַהֲבַת עַמִּי (Ahavat Ammi, ‘love of My people’) team.” That is the opening sentence Pastor Luis — from Colombia, in Spanish — offers as his quiet meditation to the kallah (bride, כַּלָּה) on the long night of The Night of the Bride. On a night when Milchemet HaChatunah (“the war of the wedding”) stretches across three continents and twelve time zones, Pastor Luis hands us one small, quiet, surgically sharp key: the real name of Shavuot (שָׁבוּעוֹת) is Zman Matan Torateinu (זְמַן מַתַּן תּוֹרָתֵנוּ) — “the time of the giving of our Torah.” Which is to say: Shavuot is not a date on a calendar. It is the moment heaven itself stooped down and touched the earth.

一、感謝與位置 ── 一位來自哥倫比亞的牧師I. Gratitude and Place — A Pastor from Colombia

教導的西班牙語原文是這樣開始的：「Quiero darle las gracias al Rabino Dr. Isaac Shapira y a todo el equipo de Ahabat Ami. Especialmente a mi querido pastor y amigo Luis Garcia, quien nos está traduciendo esta noche.」——「我要感謝拉比博士以撒·沙皮拉以及 Ahavat Ammi 全體團隊；特別感謝我親愛的牧師與朋友 Luis Garcia，今晚正在為我們翻譯。」這是路易斯牧師——遠在哥倫比亞、卻透過螢幕進入拉斯維加斯會堂的人——向新婦之夜的長夜中，所有用西班牙語聆聽的拉丁美洲信徒所遞出的第一句話。 The teaching opens, in the original Spanish: “Quiero darle las gracias al Rabino Dr. Isaac Shapira y a todo el equipo de Ahabat Ami. Especialmente a mi querido pastor y amigo Luis Garcia, quien nos está traduciendo esta noche.” — “I want to thank Rabbi Dr. Itzhak Shapira and the entire Ahavat Ammi team; especially my dear pastor and friend Luis Garcia, who is translating for us tonight.” This is Pastor Luis — joining from Colombia, but stepping through the screen into the Las Vegas sanctuary — offering his first sentence into the long Night of the Bride to every Latin American believer listening in Spanish.

他的開場語有兩個值得注意的小細節。第一，他用了「Rabino」（拉比）這個尊稱稱呼沙皮拉博士——拉丁美洲的福音派牧師對「猶太拉比」這個身分發出敬意，本身就是一場神學立場的宣告。第二，他特別指名感謝 Luis Garcia——這提醒我們：今晚的這個「西班牙語段落」其實是雙語的。一個聲音在哥倫比亞用西班牙語講；另一個聲音在拉斯維加斯用英語翻譯。兩種語言、一個新婦。 Two small details in that opening are worth noticing. First, he addresses Dr. Shapira as “Rabino” — a Latin American evangelical pastor honouring a Jewish rabbi with that very title is itself a theological declaration. Second, he names Luis Garcia by name — a reminder that tonight's “Spanish-language segment” is in fact bilingual: one voice in Colombia speaking Spanish, another voice in Las Vegas translating to English. Two languages, one bride.

「Bueno, debido al corto tiempo que tenemos, vamos a compartir un pequeño mensaje relacionado con la fiesta de Shavuot.」——「好，由於我們時間有限，我們將分享一段與五旬節（Shavuot）相關的小訊息。」「小訊息」。請注意他的謙卑。這位牧師並沒有把自己定位為今夜的中心；他知道自己是在拉比之間、在通宵研讀的長河中、在「新婦」這位真正的主角面前——遞上的一個小杯水。我們在Night of the Bride 看了一個又一個來自世界各地的事工——這一段，是哥倫比亞的那一杯水。 “Bueno, debido al corto tiempo que tenemos, vamos a compartir un pequeño mensaje relacionado con la fiesta de Shavuot.” — “Well, because we have a short time, we are going to share a small message relating to the festival of Shavuot.” “A small message.” Notice the humility. This pastor does not position himself as the centre of the night; he knows he stands among rabbis, inside the river of all-night Torah study, in the presence of the true protagonist — the bride — and what he offers is one small cup of water. On The Night of the Bride we watch ministry after ministry step in from across the world; this segment is the Colombian cup.

二、標題 ── 「Zman Matan Torateinu，當天庭觸及大地」II. The Title — “Zman Matan Torateinu, When Heaven Touches Earth”

「El tema, el nombre que he escogido se llama Zman Matan Torateinu. Que significa cuando los cielos tocan la tierra.」——「我選擇的主題的名字叫做 Zman Matan Torateinu（זְמַן מַתַּן תּוֹרָתֵנוּ）。意思就是『當天庭觸及大地』。」這是整段教導的核心圖像。 “El tema, el nombre que he escogido se llama Zman Matan Torateinu. Que significa cuando los cielos tocan la tierra.” — “The topic, the name I have chosen, is Zman Matan Torateinu (זְמַן מַתַּן תּוֹרָתֵנוּ). It means ‘when heaven touches the earth.’” This is the central image of the entire teaching.

這個希伯來短語拆開來看： Unpack the Hebrew phrase:

זְמַן (zman) ── 「時間、時刻」
מַתַּן (matan) ── 「賜下、頒布、禮物」（與「matanah」禮物同根）
תּוֹרָתֵנוּ (Torateinu) ── 「我們的妥拉」（注意所有格「我們的」） זְמַן (zman) — “the time, the moment”
מַתַּן (matan) — “the giving, the gift” (same root as matanah, “a gift”)
תּוֹרָתֵנוּ (Torateinu) — “our Torah” (note the possessive “our”)

合起來：「我們的妥拉被賜下的那一刻」。「我們的」這三個字最動人——它把妥拉從一份古老的、屬於別人的法律典籍，變成每一位站在山下的人的禮物。妥拉不是博物館的展品；妥拉是新郎送給新婦的婚書。 Together: “the moment our Torah was given.” The two words that move most are “our.” They translate the Torah from an ancient legal corpus belonging to someone else into a gift given to every person standing at the foot of the mountain. The Torah is not a museum artefact; the Torah is the marriage document the Bridegroom hands to the bride.

「Siempre me ha apasionado muchísimo entender un poco sobre este mundo físico y su contraparte en el cielo.」——「我一直對於了解這個物質世界與它在天上的對應，懷有極深的熱情。」路易斯牧師這句個人坦白告訴我們：他不是在做一場學術演講；他是在分享自己一生最關切的問題——「上面的世界」與「下面的世界」是怎麼相觸的？ “Siempre me ha apasionado muchísimo entender un poco sobre este mundo físico y su contraparte en el cielo.” — “I have always been very passionate about understanding this physical world and its counterpart in heaven.” That personal confession tells us: Pastor Luis is not giving an academic lecture; he is sharing the one question that has gripped his entire life — how does “the world above” actually touch the world below?

他舉出三個具體的「觸碰點」：妥拉的頒布、Mishkan（מִשְׁכָּן，會幕）中的榮耀雲彩、以及羔羊的婚禮（las bodas del Cordero）。這三者並非彼此分離——它們是同一個婚姻的三個場景：訂婚、同住、與最終的婚禮。 He names three specific points of contact: the giving of the Torah, the clouds of glory over the Mishkan (מִשְׁכָּן, the Tabernacle), and the wedding of the Lamb (las bodas del Cordero). These three are not separate; they are one wedding in three scenes — the betrothal, the cohabitation, and the consummating wedding feast.

三、五旬節的五個名字III. The Five Names of Shavuot

「Vamos a citar lo que nos enseñan los sabios de Israel acerca de los cinco nombres de la fiesta.」——「我們要引述以色列的賢哲們所教導我們、關於五旬節的五個名字。」路易斯牧師沒有跳過拉比的傳統；他把這些名字一個一個唸出來，作為今夜的地圖。 “Vamos a citar lo que nos enseñan los sabios de Israel acerca de los cinco nombres de la fiesta.” — “We are going to cite what the sages of Israel teach us about the five names of the festival.” Pastor Luis does not skip past the rabbinic tradition; he reads each name out loud, one by one, as tonight's map.

他的五個名字（按他在台上「先唸西班牙語、再請 Luis 翻成英語」的節奏）是： The five names, in the rhythm he uses on stage (“first I read it in Spanish, then Luis translates to English”), are:

1. Shavuot (שָׁבוּעוֹת) ── 「el festival de la semana」「週數之節」。焦點在時間與計數——從逾越節算起的七週。

2. Zman Matan Torateinu (זְמַן מַתַּן תּוֹרָתֵנוּ) ── 「el tiempo de la entrega de nuestra Torah」「我們的妥拉被頒賜的時刻」。焦點在西奈山。

3. Chag HaKatzir (חַג הַקָּצִיר) ── 「el festival de la cosecha」「收割節」。焦點在麥子。

4. Yom HaBikkurim (יוֹם הַבִּכּוּרִים) ── 「el día de las primicias」「初熟果之日」。焦點在初熟的果子。

5. Atzeret (עֲצֶרֶת) ── 「conclusión」「終結、收束」。焦點是：五旬節是逾越節的最終完成。—— 出於路易斯牧師對拉比智者傳統的引述 1. Shavuot (שָׁבוּעוֹת) — “el festival de la semana,” the Festival of Weeks. Focus: time and the counting — the seven weeks from Passover.

2. Zman Matan Torateinu (זְמַן מַתַּן תּוֹרָתֵנוּ) — “el tiempo de la entrega de nuestra Torah,” the time of the giving of our Torah. Focus: Mount Sinai.

3. Chag HaKatzir (חַג הַקָּצִיר) — “el festival de la cosecha,” the Festival of the Harvest. Focus: wheat.

4. Yom HaBikkurim (יוֹם הַבִּכּוּרִים) — “el día de las primicias,” the Day of the Firstfruits. Focus: the first fruits.

5. Atzeret (עֲצֶרֶת) — “conclusión,” conclusion or closing. Focus: Shavuot is the final completion of Passover.— Pastor Luis, citing the rabbinic sages

五個名字像新婦頭冠上的五顆寶石——每一顆都從一個角度反射同一束光。其中第二個名字是今夜路易斯牧師選擇凝視的那一顆：Zman Matan Torateinu。「Esta noche nos vamos a enfocar solamente en el punto número dos.」——「今夜我們只專注在第二個名字。」 Five names like five stones on the bride's crown — each refracting the same light from a different angle. The second is the one Pastor Luis chooses to gaze at tonight: Zman Matan Torateinu. “Esta noche nos vamos a enfocar solamente en el punto número dos.” — “Tonight we will focus only on the second one.”

為什麼？因為這個名字——也只有這個名字——明確地把「五旬節」與一個具體的歷史事件連結起來：當摩西帶領以色列站在西奈山下，神親自從天上下來，把妥拉交給祂的新婦的那一刻。其他四個名字描述五旬節的面向；這個名字描述五旬節的身分。 Why? Because this name — and only this name — explicitly connects Shavuot to a concrete historical event: the moment Moses brought Israel to the foot of Sinai and God Himself descended to hand the Torah to His bride. The other four names describe Shavuot's aspects; this name describes Shavuot's identity.

四、從石板到心版 ── 摩西、耶書亞、與 Ruach HaKodeshIV. From Stone to Heart — Moses, Yeshua, and the Ruach HaKodesh

路易斯牧師在這個段落把Zman Matan Torateinu 在新約裡的對應點清晰指出： Here Pastor Luis names the New Testament counterpart of Zman Matan Torateinu very plainly:

「En el contexto de Yeshua, representa la transición de la Torah escrita en tablas de piedra a una Torah escrita en nuestros corazones.」
——「在耶書亞（Yeshua，ישוע）的脈絡裡，五旬節代表的是從刻在石板上的妥拉，過渡到刻在我們心版上的妥拉。」—— 路易斯牧師 “En el contexto de Yeshua, representa la transición de la Torah escrita en tablas de piedra a una Torah escrita en nuestros corazones.”
— “In the context of Yeshua (Yeshua, ישוע), Shavuot represents the transition from a Torah written on stone tablets to a Torah written on our hearts.”— Pastor Luis

這句話濃縮了整個救贖歷史的弧線。從石頭到心。從外面到裡面。從規條到關係。從摩西到耶書亞。但不是「廢除」，而是「內化」——這是一個極重要的區分。 That sentence compresses the entire arc of salvation history. From stone to heart. From outside to inside. From code to relationship. From Moses to Yeshua. But not “abolished” — internalised. That distinction is critical.

路易斯牧師接著立刻處理一個常見的誤解。他並沒有說「妥拉廢除了」；他說：「Así de la misma manera como Moshe fue el mediador en el Sinaí, Yeshua es el mediador de un nuevo pacto.」——「正如摩西（Moshe，מֹשֶׁה）在西奈山是中保，耶書亞是新約的中保。」一個中保，一份盟約，從石頭傳到心裡。耶書亞並不是來「換掉」摩西手中的兩塊石板；祂是來把同一份妥拉從石頭上抄寫到祂百姓的心上。 Pastor Luis immediately heads off a common misreading. He does not say the Torah is abolished. He says: “Así de la misma manera como Moshe fue el mediador en el Sinaí, Yeshua es el mediador de un nuevo pacto.” — “Just as Moshe (Moshe, מֹשֶׁה) was the mediator at Sinai, Yeshua is the mediator of a new covenant.” One mediator, one covenant, copied from stone onto the heart. Yeshua does not come to “swap out” the two tablets in Moses' hands; He comes to take the same Torah off the stone and inscribe it on the hearts of His people.

那「銘刻」的工具是誰？ And the tool of that inscription?

「En Shavuot, el Padre nos envía el Ruach HaKodesh, el Espíritu Santo, para inscribir sus instrucciones dentro de nosotros, internamente. Transformando la obediencia externa en una relación personal de amor.」
——「在五旬節，天父賜下 רוּחַ הַקֹּדֶשׁ（Ruach HaKodesh，聖靈），把祂的教導刻在我們裡面。把外在的順服，轉化為一段個人的、有愛的關係。」—— 路易斯牧師 “En Shavuot, el Padre nos envía el Ruach HaKodesh, el Espíritu Santo, para inscribir sus instrucciones dentro de nosotros, internamente. Transformando la obediencia externa en una relación personal de amor.”
— “At Shavuot, the Father sends us the רוּחַ הַקֹּדֶשׁ (Ruach HaKodesh, the Holy Spirit), to inscribe His instructions inside us. Transforming external obedience into a personal relationship of love.”— Pastor Luis

這一句話幾乎獨自承擔了整個信仰的重量。所有的順服，如果不是出於愛，最後都會變成奴役。所有的妥拉，如果只在石頭上，最後都會變成負擔。耶利米書 31 章的應許——「我要將我的律法放在他們裡面，寫在他們心上」——在Zman Matan Torateinu 的新約對應（使徒行傳第二章那同一個五旬節的早上）裡，成就了。 That sentence almost carries the weight of the whole faith by itself. All obedience, if it is not out of love, eventually becomes slavery. All Torah, if it stays only on stone, eventually becomes burden. Jeremiah 31's promise — “I will put my law within them, and write it on their hearts” — was fulfilled in the new-covenant counterpart of Zman Matan Torateinu: the same Shavuot morning we read in Acts 2.

五、Anan HaKavod ── 不是水汽，是 ShekhinahV. Anan HaKavod — Not Water Vapour, But the Shekhinah

教導從這裡轉入它最動人的部分。路易斯牧師說：「Vamos a ver un punto muy interesante acerca de las nubes de gloria que se conocen como Anan HaKavod.」——「我們要來看一個非常有趣的點：那些被稱為Anan HaKavod（עֲנַן הַכָּבוֹד，榮耀的雲彩）的雲。」 The teaching turns here into its most moving part. Pastor Luis says: “Vamos a ver un punto muy interesante acerca de las nubes de gloria que se conocen como Anan HaKavod.” — “We are going to look at a very interesting point: the clouds called Anan HaKavod (עֲנַן הַכָּבוֹד, the Clouds of Glory).”

他立即釐清誤解： He immediately clears up a misunderstanding:

「No estamos hablando de unas nubes como ustedes las conocen, de vapor de agua, las nubes que estamos viendo constantemente en el firmamento, en el cielo. Estamos hablando de las nubes de gloria que se conocen como Anan HaKavod. Es la Shekhinah.」——「我們講的不是大家以為的那種水汽組成的雲，不是我們抬頭看見、在天空中飄移的那種雲。我們講的是榮耀的雲彩——Anan HaKavod。它就是 שְׁכִינָה（Shekhinah，神的同在）。」 “No estamos hablando de unas nubes como ustedes las conocen, de vapor de agua, las nubes que estamos viendo constantemente en el firmamento, en el cielo. Estamos hablando de las nubes de gloria que se conocen como Anan HaKavod. Es la Shekhinah.” — “We are not talking about the kind of cloud you know — water vapour, the clouds we constantly see drifting across the sky. We are talking about the Clouds of Glory — Anan HaKavod. It is the שְׁכִינָה (Shekhinah, the Divine Presence).”

這個區分極其重要。當聖經說「神在雲中下降」、「雲彩遮蓋了山」、「雲柱在以色列前頭行」、「祂駕雲降臨」——這些從來不是氣象描寫。它們講的是神可見的同在，是Shekhinah 在物質世界中可被肉眼看見的那一層覆蓋。 That distinction matters more than it first sounds. Whenever Scripture says “God descended in a cloud,” “the cloud covered the mountain,” “the pillar of cloud went before Israel,” “He comes on the clouds” — these are never weather reports. They describe the visible presence of God, the layer of the Shekhinah that becomes perceptible to physical eyes in the physical world.

然後路易斯牧師遞出他這場教導的核心句——他自己的「金句」： Then Pastor Luis offers the central sentence of his entire teaching — his own “best quote”:

「Es precisamente estas nubes que sirven como punto de contacto físico entre la parte celestial y la terrenal. Entre lo que está arriba y lo que está abajo.」
——「正是這些雲彩，充當了天界與塵世之間、上與下之間，一個實體的接觸點。」—— 路易斯牧師，原文重點 “Es precisamente estas nubes que sirven como punto de contacto físico entre la parte celestial y la terrenal. Entre lo que está arriba y lo que está abajo.”
— “It is precisely these clouds that serve as a physical point of contact between the celestial and the earthly. Between what is above and what is below.”— Pastor Luis, original emphasis

「punto de contacto físico」——「實體的接觸點」。這個短語值得在心裡重複多次。神並不只是「精神性地」與我們相連；祂創造了一個實體——榮耀的雲彩——作為祂與我們相觸的場所。基督教信仰並不是柏拉圖式的（純精神的、與物質脫節的）；它是道成肉身式的——神願意在物質中與我們同在。 “Punto de contacto físico” — “a physical point of contact.” Worth repeating in the heart several times. God is not merely “spiritually” connected to us; He has made a physical thing — the cloud of glory — as the place where He touches us. Christian faith is not Platonic (purely spiritual, disjoined from matter); it is incarnational — God is willing to be present to us in matter.

六、四種雲彩 ── 同一個婚禮的四幕VI. The Four Clouds — Four Acts of One Wedding

路易斯牧師列出聖經中出現Anan HaKavod 的四個關鍵時刻： Pastor Luis lists the four key moments in Scripture where the Anan HaKavod appears:

第一：西奈山的雲——contrato de matrimonio，「婚姻盟約」。
第二：Mishkan（會幕）上的雲——habitación continua，「持續的同住」。
第三：變像山上的雲——la Torá viviente，「活的妥拉」道成肉身。
第四：耶書亞再來時的雲——la consumación final，「最終的完成」。 One: the cloud at Mount Sinai — contrato de matrimonio, the marriage covenant.
Two: the cloud over the Mishkan — habitación continua, the continual dwelling.
Three: the cloud on the Mount of Transfiguration — la Torá viviente, the living Torah incarnate.
Four: the cloud at Yeshua's return — la consumación final, the final consummation.

注意這四個時刻形成的內在敘事弧：訂婚 → 同住 → 道成肉身 → 婚禮宴席。同一個新郎、同一個新婦、同一個盟約——只是不同的場景。 Notice the internal narrative arc the four moments form: betrothal → cohabitation → incarnation → wedding feast. One Bridegroom, one bride, one covenant — only different scenes.

第一幕：西奈山的雲 ── 神親自下降為新郎Act One: The Cloud at Sinai — God Himself Descends as Bridegroom

「到了第三日早晨，在山上有雷轟、閃電和密雲，並且角聲甚大。營中的百姓盡都發顫…… 西奈全山冒煙，因為耶和華在火中降於山上。山的煙氣上騰，如燒窯一般，遍山大大地震動。」—— 出埃及記 19:16-18 “On the third day in the morning there were thunders and lightnings and a thick cloud on the mountain, and a very loud trumpet blast, so that all the people in the camp trembled… Now Mount Sinai was wrapped in smoke because the LORD had descended on it in fire. The smoke of it went up like the smoke of a kiln, and the whole mountain trembled greatly.”— Exodus 19:16-18

路易斯牧師對這段經文的解讀極其優美：「La nube funcionó como una jupá, como una tienda de matrimonio.」——「那雲彩功能上就是一個 חוּפָּה（chuppah，新婚的帳幔）。」 Pastor Luis's reading of this passage is exquisite: “La nube funcionó como una jupá, como una tienda de matrimonio.” — “That cloud functioned as a חוּפָּה (chuppah, the wedding canopy).”

這個圖像值得停下來。Chuppah 是猶太婚禮中新郎新婦並肩站立的那塊布；它象徵新郎為新婦預備的家。西奈山上的雲——那塊覆蓋全山的密雲——就是神為以色列預備的「家的頂蓋」。「Hashem descendió para desposar a Israel a través de sus mandamientos, a través de la Torah.」——「הַשֵּׁם（Hashem，主）親自下降，透過妥拉、透過祂的誡命，迎娶以色列。」 Pause on the image. A chuppah is the cloth under which the bride and groom stand at a Jewish wedding; it symbolises the home the groom has prepared for his bride. The cloud over Sinai — that thick canopy covering the whole mountain — is the canopy God has prepared for Israel's home. “Hashem descendió para desposar a Israel a través de sus mandamientos, a través de la Torah.” — “הַשֵּׁם (Hashem, the LORD) descended to betroth Israel through His commandments, through the Torah.”

在這個讀法下，妥拉不是一份法律條文。妥拉是 ketubah（כְּתֻבָּה，猶太婚書）。它是新郎在新婚那一夜，當著所有見證人的面，對新婦立下的愛的承諾書。每一條誡命都是一行情書。 In this reading, the Torah is not a piece of legislation. The Torah is the ketubah (כְּתֻבָּה, the Jewish marriage contract). It is the love-promise the Bridegroom signs in front of every witness on the wedding night. Every single commandment is a line of love poetry.

與耶書亞的連結？路易斯牧師指向約翰福音 1:14：「道成了肉身，住在我們中間。」「En el Sinaí, la Torá descendió envuelta en nubes. En Yeshua, la Torá viviente descendió en forma de un ser humano. El Sinaí fue el primer ensayo del cielo tocando la tierra.」——「在西奈山，妥拉裹著雲彩下降；在耶書亞，活的妥拉化作一個人下降。西奈山是天庭觸碰大地的第一場彩排。」 The connection to Yeshua? Pastor Luis turns to John 1:14: “The Word became flesh and dwelt among us.” “En el Sinaí, la Torá descendió envuelta en nubes. En Yeshua, la Torá viviente descendió en forma de un ser humano. El Sinaí fue el primer ensayo del cielo tocando la tierra.” — “At Sinai, the Torah descended wrapped in clouds; in Yeshua, the living Torah descended in the form of a human being. Sinai was the first rehearsal of heaven touching the earth.”

第二幕：Mishkan 之上的雲 ── 從訂婚到同住Act Two: The Cloud over the Mishkan — From Betrothal to Cohabitation

「當時，雲彩遮蓋會幕，耶和華的榮光就充滿了帳幕。」—— 出埃及記 40:34 “Then the cloud covered the tent of meeting, and the glory of the LORD filled the tabernacle.”— Exodus 40:34

「Lo que ocurrió en el Sinaí de forma temporal, ahora se volvió permanente en el Mishkan.」——「在西奈山暫時發生的事，現在在 Mishkan 變成永久的了。」 “Lo que ocurrió en el Sinaí de forma temporal, ahora se volvió permanente en el Mishkan.” — “What happened temporarily at Sinai now became permanent in the Mishkan.”

這是婚姻邁向「同住」的階段。訂婚後的新婦不能總是站在山下、抬頭仰望雲彩；新郎為她預備了一個會幕——一個能跟著她在曠野中行走、能在每一個營地中央立起來的移動之家。路易斯牧師說：「Esa nube guiaba al pueblo. Si la nube se movía, Israel se movía.」——「那雲彩引導百姓。雲彩動，以色列就動。」 This is the marriage moving into the “cohabitation” phase. A betrothed bride cannot stand forever at the foot of a mountain looking up at a cloud; the Bridegroom has built her a Mishkan — a mobile home that walks with her through the wilderness and is pitched at the centre of every campsite. Pastor Luis says: “Esa nube guiaba al pueblo. Si la nube se movía, Israel se movía.” — “That cloud guided the people. When the cloud moved, Israel moved.”

這是從外面到裡面的第一步：神不再只在「那座山」上；祂在「我們營中央」。但這還沒到最深的內化。內化要等到第三幕、第四幕。 This is the first step from outside to inside: God is no longer just on “that mountain”; He is in “the centre of our camp.” But this is still not the deepest internalisation. That waits for Acts Three and Four.

與耶書亞的連結？「Yeshua es el Mishkan definitivo.」——「耶書亞就是那位最終的 Mishkan。」約翰福音 1:14 的希臘文「住在我們中間」（eskēnōsen，ἐσκήνωσεν）字根 skēnē 就是「會幕」的意思。道字面上「支搭祂的會幕在我們中間」。當耶書亞行走在加利利時，Anan HaKavod 沒有消失——它進入了一個人。 The connection to Yeshua? “Yeshua es el Mishkan definitivo.” — “Yeshua is the ultimate Mishkan.” The Greek of John 1:14 — “dwelt among us” — is eskēnōsen (ἐσκήνωσεν); the root skēnē means “tent, tabernacle.” The Word literally “pitched His tabernacle among us.” When Yeshua walked the hills of Galilee, the Anan HaKavod had not disappeared — it had entered into a person.

第三幕：變像山上的雲 ── 同一塊雲，回到了山上Act Three: The Cloud on the Mount of Transfiguration — The Same Cloud, Back on a Mountain

「說話之間，忽然有一朵光明的雲彩遮蓋他們，且有聲音從雲彩裡出來，說：『這是我的愛子，我所喜悅的，你們要聽他！』」—— 馬太福音 17:5 “He was still speaking when, behold, a bright cloud overshadowed them, and a voice from the cloud said, ‘This is my beloved Son, with whom I am well pleased; listen to him.’”— Matthew 17:5

路易斯牧師指出這一刻的身分對應：「Esa era la misma nube del Mishkan.」——「那就是 Mishkan 上的同一朵雲。」 Pastor Luis points to the identity-overlap of this moment: “Esa era la misma nube del Mishkan.” — “That was the same cloud that hovered over the Mishkan.”

同一塊雲。從西奈，到會幕，到變像山。同一個神，同一個同在，同一份婚約——只是越來越靠近祂的新婦。在西奈，雲在山上；在會幕，雲在營中；在變像山，雲包圍著一個人，而那個人是耶書亞。「El diseño de Dios ya no estaba en un lugar físico, sino en la persona de su Hijo, Yeshua.」——「神的設計不再放在某個地方裡了；祂放在祂兒子的身位裡了。」 The same cloud. From Sinai, to the Mishkan, to the Mount of Transfiguration. One God, one Presence, one ketubah — only drawing nearer and nearer to His bride. At Sinai the cloud is on a mountain; in the Mishkan the cloud is in a camp; on the Mount of Transfiguration the cloud is wrapped around a person, and that Person is Yeshua. “El diseño de Dios ya no estaba en un lugar físico, sino en la persona de su Hijo, Yeshua.” — “God's design no longer rested in a physical place; it rested in the person of His Son.”

第四幕：再來時的雲 ── 新郎來接祂的新婦Act Four: The Cloud of His Return — The Bridegroom Comes to Fetch His Bride

「我在夜間的異象中觀看，見有一位像人子的，駕著天雲而來。」（但以理書 7:13）
「看哪，他駕雲降臨，眾目要看見他，連刺他的人也要看見他。」（啟示錄 1:7）—— 但 7:13；啟 1:7 “I saw in the night visions, and behold, with the clouds of heaven there came one like a son of man.” (Daniel 7:13)
“Behold, he is coming with the clouds, and every eye will see him, even those who pierced him.” (Revelation 1:7)— Dan 7:13; Rev 1:7

路易斯牧師把整個敘事拉到頂點：「Su retorno no es algo relacionado al clima porque vemos nubes en el cielo. Él viene escoltado por la misma Shekhinah que estuvo en el Sinaí y en el Mishkan.」——「祂的再來，並不是因為我們在天空中看見了一些雲彩——這跟氣象無關。祂回來的時候，是被同一位 Shekhinah——那位曾在西奈、在會幕的——所護送、所包圍的。」 Pastor Luis pulls the whole narrative to its peak: “Su retorno no es algo relacionado al clima porque vemos nubes en el cielo. Él viene escoltado por la misma Shekhinah que estuvo en el Sinaí y en el Mishkan.” — “His return is not a weather event because we see clouds in the sky. When He comes, He is escorted, surrounded, by the same Shekhinah that was at Sinai and in the Mishkan.”

然後是這場教導裡，我認為最美的一個句子： And then the sentence I think is the most beautiful in this teaching:

「Las nubes de su regreso significan que el novio viene a buscar a su novia bajo la jupá. Completando así el pacto, el contrato de matrimonio que empezó en el monte Sinaí con la entrega de la Torah.」
——「祂回來時的雲彩，意思是：新郎來接祂的新婦進入新婚的帳幔之下。圓滿了那份盟約——那份在西奈山、隨著妥拉的頒賜而開始的婚姻盟約。」—— 路易斯牧師 “Las nubes de su regreso significan que el novio viene a buscar a su novia bajo la jupá. Completando así el pacto, el contrato de matrimonio que empezó en el monte Sinaí con la entrega de la Torah.”
— “The clouds of His return mean: the Bridegroom is coming to fetch His bride under the chuppah. Completing the covenant — the marriage contract that began at Sinai with the giving of the Torah.”— Pastor Luis

這是整本聖經的弧線，在哥倫比亞牧師的西班牙語裡，用最簡單的句子說出來。從西奈的訂婚，到變像山的彩排，到髑髏地撕裂的幔子，到復活清晨空墳墓裡留下的細麻布——一切都是為了那同一場婚禮，那場新郎終於把新婦領入chuppah之下的婚禮。 That is the arc of the whole Bible, said in the simplest Spanish by a pastor from Colombia. From the betrothal at Sinai, through the rehearsal on the Mount of Transfiguration, through the curtain torn at Calvary, through the linen left folded in the empty tomb on resurrection morning — all of it is for that one wedding: the wedding where the Bridegroom finally leads His bride beneath the chuppah.

七、撕裂的幔子 ── 物質與靈性的接縫被縫合VII. The Torn Veil — The Seam Between Matter and Spirit Is Sewn Shut

在轉入結語之前，路易斯牧師遞出一個小小的、但極具份量的神學觀察：「Por eso se dice que Yeshua rompió el velo del templo, porque quitó toda separación entre lo físico y lo espiritual.」——「這就是為什麼說耶書亞撕裂了聖殿的幔子——因為祂去除了一切物質與靈性之間的隔絕。」 Before stepping toward his closing, Pastor Luis offers one small but weighty theological observation: “Por eso se dice que Yeshua rompió el velo del templo, porque quitó toda separación entre lo físico y lo espiritual.” — “This is why it is said that Yeshua tore the temple veil — because He removed every separation between the physical and the spiritual.”

這個句子整合了整場教導。如果Anan HaKavod 是「punto de contacto físico」——「實體的接觸點」——那撕裂的幔子就是那個接觸面被永久打開了。再也沒有一個遮住至聖所的厚布；再也沒有一個只能由大祭司一年一次穿越的隔斷。整個宇宙都成了至聖所。神不再「住在山上」、「住在會幕中央」、「住在某個身位裡」——祂住在每一個被聖靈內住的心裡，並等待著祂的全宇宙婚禮宴席。 That sentence integrates the whole teaching. If the Anan HaKavod is the “punto de contacto físico” — the physical point of contact — then the torn veil means that contact surface has been opened permanently. There is no longer a thick fabric blocking the Holy of Holies; no longer a partition only the High Priest can pass through once a year. The entire universe has become the Holy of Holies. God no longer “lives on the mountain,” “lives in the centre of the camp,” or “lives in one person” — He lives in every heart the Spirit indwells, awaiting His cosmic wedding feast.

這也回應了路易斯牧師一開始那個個人問題：「我一直對於了解這個物質世界與它在天上的對應，懷有極深的熱情。」答案是：物質與天庭的接縫，已經在十字架上被縫合了。我們今天活在一個「兩個世界已經接通」的時代——只等新郎正式把新婦領進帳幔之下。 It also answers Pastor Luis's opening personal question: “I have always been passionate about understanding this physical world and its counterpart in heaven.” The answer: the seam between matter and heaven has already been sewn shut at the cross. We live today in an age where “the two worlds have already been connected” — only awaiting the moment the Bridegroom formally leads the bride beneath the canopy.

八、對華人 / 西語信徒的意義VIII. What This Means for Chinese and Spanish-Speaking Believers

第一，整場教導是用西班牙語講的——這本身就是一個神學陳述。神並不只用希伯來語、希臘語、英語說話；祂也用拉丁美洲牧者帶著哥倫比亞口音的西班牙語說話。新婦在每一種語言裡都有她的第一句話。對華人信徒來說，這應當是一個提醒：我們也有我們的「中文 Zman Matan Torateinu」要說。福音不是英美的轉述——它是新郎用我們自己的話對我們自己的心所說的婚書。 First, the entire teaching is given in Spanish — and that itself is a theological statement. God does not speak only in Hebrew, Greek, and English; He speaks in the Spanish of a Latin American pastor with a Colombian accent. The bride has her first sentence in every language. For Chinese believers, this is a reminder: we too have our own “Chinese Zman Matan Torateinu” to speak. The Gospel is not an Anglo-American re-transmission — it is a marriage covenant the Bridegroom signs in our own language, to our own hearts.

第二，從石板到心版 的圖像——「de las tablas de piedra al corazón」——對中華文化裡「心」(xin，心) 這個字承載的道德主體的意義，有極深的共鳴。儒家說「誠於中，形於外」；五旬節給這個直覺一個神學的依據：真正的順服必然從心裡長出來，靠的是被神的靈所重寫的內裡，而不是靠外面的條文。 Second, the image of moving from stone to heart — “de las tablas de piedra al corazón” — resonates deeply with the Chinese cultural sense of xin (心, “heart-mind”) as the seat of moral agency. Confucius said, “What is sincere within takes form without.” Shavuot gives that intuition a theological foundation: real obedience grows from the inside, from an inner life rewritten by the Spirit of God — not from external rules.

第三，四種雲彩 給我們一個聖經的時間觀：歷史不是一條無意義的直線；它是一場婚禮的四幕，從訂婚走向婚宴。對華人基督徒，這應該是一個對「等待」的重新發現——我們不是在等一個抽象的「世界末日」；我們是在等一位具體的新郎，騎著同一塊雲彩，回來牽祂的新婦的手。 Third, the four clouds give us a biblical sense of time: history is not a meaningless line; it is the four acts of one wedding, moving from betrothal to wedding feast. For Chinese Christians, this should be a fresh discovery of the meaning of waiting — we are not waiting for an abstract “end of the world”; we are waiting for a concrete Bridegroom, riding on the same cloud, returning to take His bride by the hand.

第四，Tikkun Leil Shavuot——五旬節通宵守夜研讀妥拉的傳統——是路易斯牧師結尾時的祝福。他說：「Que puedan hacer ese Tikkun Leil Shavuot, que puedan mantenerse despiertos toda esta noche.」——「願你們能完成 Tikkun Leil Shavuot，能整夜保持清醒。」對華人信徒，這是一個實踐的邀請——不只是讀文章，不只是聽教導，而是真的撥出一夜、真的徹夜不眠地與神的話相處，讓新郎在你心裡刻祂的婚書。 Fourth, Tikkun Leil Shavuot — the tradition of staying awake all night on Shavuot studying Torah — is Pastor Luis's closing blessing. He says: “Que puedan hacer ese Tikkun Leil Shavuot, que puedan mantenerse despiertos toda esta noche.” — “May you be able to keep this Tikkun Leil Shavuot, may you stay awake all this night.” For Chinese believers, this is an invitation to practice — not just to read articles, not just to listen to teachings, but to actually set aside a night, to actually stay awake with the word of God, to let the Bridegroom inscribe His ketubah on your heart.

九、結語 ── 從哥倫比亞發出的呼召IX. Closing — A Call from Colombia

路易斯牧師的結語極其樸實。他沒有刻意拔高聲量，他只是把他手中的小杯水放下，然後從遙遠的安第斯山脈方向遞出一個祝福： Pastor Luis's closing is plain and unhurried. He does not strain to raise the volume; he simply sets down the small cup of water in his hand and, from somewhere in the direction of the Andes, hands across a blessing:

「Desde acá de Colombia les envío un saludo muy especial. Y jagsa-meaj para todos. Muchas bendiciones. Muchas gracias por su tiempo.」——「我從哥倫比亞發出一個很特別的問候。願 חַג שָׂמֵחַ（Chag Sameach，節期喜樂）臨到你們眾人。許多祝福。感謝你們的時間。」 “Desde acá de Colombia les envío un saludo muy especial. Y jagsa-meaj para todos. Muchas bendiciones. Muchas gracias por su tiempo.” — “From here in Colombia, I send a very special greeting. חַג שָׂמֵחַ (Chag Sameach, ‘Joyful festival’) to you all. Many blessings. Thank you for your time.”

沙皮拉拉比博士在他結束後立刻說：「Muchas gracias, Pastor Luis, a ti. Y que tengas una bendecida fiesta. Shalom.」——「謝謝你，路易斯牧師。願你有一個蒙福的節期。Shalom。」兩個牧者、兩個語言、一個新婦——在「Shalom」這個希伯來字裡相互鞠躬。 Rabbi Dr. Shapira responds immediately: “Muchas gracias, Pastor Luis, a ti. Y que tengas una bendecida fiesta. Shalom.” — “Thank you, Pastor Luis. May you have a blessed festival. Shalom.” Two pastors, two languages, one bride — bowing to one another in the Hebrew word Shalom.

這就是Night of the Bride 的本質。不是一場大型晚會；是一個由許多小杯水構成的長河。從拉斯維加斯、從哥倫比亞、從韓國、從非洲、從中國的小群房間裡——每一個都是新婦的一個聲音。每一個都不可被替換。每一個都不可被略過。新郎正在收集這些聲音，準備在祂回來那日，把祂的新婦從每一族每一方各國各語言中聚攏到 chuppah之下。 That is what The Night of the Bride actually is. Not a large gala — a long river made of many small cups of water. From Las Vegas, from Colombia, from Korea, from Africa, from small living rooms in China — each one is a voice of the bride. Each one cannot be replaced. Each one cannot be skipped over. The Bridegroom is gathering these voices, preparing for the day He returns, to gather His bride from every tribe, tongue, people, and nation under the chuppah.

路易斯牧師那一杯，是西班牙語的、來自哥倫比亞的、安靜的、帶著對 Anan HaKavod 一生熱情的 ── 那一杯。 Pastor Luis's cup is the Spanish-speaking one — from Colombia, quiet, carrying a lifelong passion for the Anan HaKavod. That one.
