וַיֵּלֶךְ 利来篇：摩西的最後一日與「剛強壯膽」 Vayelech — Moses' Final Day and the Call to Be Strong and Courageous

妥拉週的最後幾篇進入了摩西生命的盡頭。在利来篇（申命記 31 章）裡，這位一百二十歲的先知「走出去」向全以色列告別——他不能進入應許之地，卻把棒子交給約書亞，把妥拉寫下來交給祭司，並留下那句橫貫整本聖經的命令：「你當剛強壯膽。」 As the yearly cycle nears its close, we arrive at the end of Moses' life. In Vayelech (Deuteronomy 31) the 120-year-old prophet “goes out” to take leave of all Israel — barred from the Land himself, he hands the staff to Joshua, writes the Torah and entrusts it to the priests, and leaves behind the command that echoes through all of Scripture: “Be strong and courageous.”

沙皮拉拉比博士 · Ahavat Ammi 事工 Rabbi Dr. Itzhak Shapira · Ahavat Ammi

妥拉的最後幾週讀經，是一個老人緩緩走向他生命的盡頭。利来篇（פָּרָשַׁת וַיֵּלֶךְ，Parashat Vayelech）只有短短三十節——是整本妥拉中最短的一段——卻承載了極重的份量：摩西的最後一日。這一段以一個動詞開場：וַיֵּלֶךְ（Vayelech，「他就去」、「他走了出去」）。一百二十歲的摩西，眼目沒有昏花、精神沒有衰敗，卻知道自己不能過約旦河。於是他「走出去」——走向百姓、走向交棒、走向告別——把整個民族交在神與約書亞的手中，並在那一日把一句話刻進以色列的記憶裡：「你當剛強壯膽（חֲזַק וֶאֱמַץ，chazak ve'ematz）。」 The final weeks of the Torah cycle are an old man walking slowly toward the end of his life. Parashat Vayelech (פָּרָשַׁת וַיֵּלֶךְ) is only thirty verses — the shortest portion in the whole Torah — yet it carries enormous weight: the last day of Moses. It opens on a single verb: וַיֵּלֶךְ (Vayelech, “and he went,” “and he went out”). At a hundred and twenty, his eyes undimmed and his vigour unabated, Moses knows he will not cross the Jordan. So he “goes out” — out to the people, out toward the handover, out toward farewell — placing the whole nation into the hands of God and of Joshua, and engraving into Israel's memory a single command for that day: “Be strong and courageous” (חֲזַק וֶאֱמַץ, chazak ve'ematz).

一、「他就去」── 一個動詞裡的整篇妥拉I. “And He Went” — A Whole Portion in One Verb

在希伯來傳統中，每一篇妥拉的名字都取自它開頭的第一個重要字。利来篇取自申命記 31:1 的開頭：וַיֵּלֶךְ מֹשֶׁה（Vayelech Moshe，「摩西就去」）。一個動詞——「他去了」——成了整篇的標題。問題隨之而來：摩西「去」哪裡？經文沒有說目的地，因為重點不是他去到何處，而是他主動地走了出去。 In Hebrew tradition every Torah portion takes its name from the first significant word of its opening line. Vayelech is drawn from the opening of Deuteronomy 31:1: וַיֵּלֶךְ מֹשֶׁה (Vayelech Moshe, “And Moses went”). A single verb — “he went” — becomes the title of the whole portion. And immediately the question arises: where did Moses “go”? The text names no destination, because the point is not where he arrived, but that he went out of his own accord.

拉比們長久以來都在這個字上停留。在前一篇（站立篇，Nitzavim）中，全以色列「站立」在神面前；現在，摩西「走出去」到他們中間。一個靜，一個動。年邁的領袖沒有等百姓來到他帳棚門口聽他最後的話，他自己起身，走遍營中，挨家挨戶地去向他所牧養了四十年的這一代人告別。領袖的最後一個動作，不是接受服事，而是去服事。 The sages have long lingered on this one word. In the previous portion (Nitzavim, “you are standing”) all Israel stands before God; now Moses goes out among them. One is stillness, the other motion. The aged leader does not wait for the people to come to his tent for his final words — he rises and walks through the camp, going from tribe to tribe to take leave of the generation he has shepherded for forty years. The last act of the leader is not to be served, but to go and serve.

這就是妥拉珍珠的精神：在一個最容易被讀者跳過的小字裡，藏著整段經文的心臟。摩西「去了」——他用腳走出了一生最後的順服。 This is the spirit of Pearls of the Torah: in the smallest word a reader is most tempted to skip past lies the heart of the whole passage. Moses “went” — with his own feet he walked out the last obedience of his life.

二、「我不能再出入」── 一百二十歲的交棒II. “I Can No Longer Come and Go” — The Handover at 120

摩西開口的第一句話，就是面對自己生命的盡頭，毫不迴避： Moses' opening words face the end of his own life without flinching:

「我現在一百二十歲了，不能照常出入；耶和華也曾對我說：『你必不得過這約旦河。』」——申命記 31:2 “I am a hundred and twenty years old today. I am no longer able to go out and come in. The LORD has said to me, ‘You shall not cross over this Jordan.’”— Deuteronomy 31:2

這句話裡有一層深刻的張力。申命記 34:7 明明告訴我們：「摩西死的時候年一百二十歲，眼目沒有昏花，精神沒有衰敗。」他在體力上並沒有「不能」。所以「不能出入」指的不是衰老，而是神的命定——他的職分到此為止，他的時代結束了。「出入」(לָצֵאת וְלָבוֹא，latzet velavo) 在希伯來文裡正是「領導、帶兵、治理」的慣用語。摩西是在說：帶領這民進地的，不再是我了。 There is a deep tension in this line. Deuteronomy 34:7 tells us plainly: “Moses was a hundred and twenty years old when he died. His eye was not dim, nor his vigour gone.” Physically he was not “unable.” So “no longer able to go out and come in” is not about decline but about divine appointment — his office ends here, his era is over. The phrase “go out and come in” (לָצֵאת וְלָבוֹא, latzet velavo) is the Hebrew idiom for leading, marching out troops, governing. Moses is saying: it is no longer I who will bring this people into the Land.

這是聖經中最艱難的功課之一：學習在自己沒有完成的事上鬆手。摩西帶領以色列四十年，從紅海到約旦河邊，卻在最後一里路被攔下。他大可以怨恨、可以爭辯、可以緊抓不放——但他選擇「走出去」，去把民交給下一位。一個領袖最大的成熟，往往不在於他做成了什麼，而在於他能不能在恰當的時刻，把棒子交出去。 This is one of Scripture's hardest lessons: learning to let go of the very thing you did not get to finish. Moses led Israel for forty years, from the Red Sea to the bank of the Jordan, only to be stopped on the last mile. He could have resented it, argued it, clung to it — instead he chose to “go out” and entrust the people to the next man. A leader's deepest maturity often lies not in what he accomplished, but in whether he can, at the right moment, hand off the staff.

三、剛強壯膽 ── חֲזַק וֶאֱמַץ 的雙重呼召III. Be Strong and Courageous — The Twofold Chazak Ve'ematz

在這一篇短短的經文裡，「剛強壯膽」(חֲזַק וֶאֱמַץ，chazak ve'ematz) 出現了不只一次——先是摩西對全以色列說（申 31:6），接著是摩西當著眾人的面對約書亞說（申 31:7），最後是神親自對約書亞說（申 31:23）。同一句命令，從先知的口、再到神自己的口，層層加重。 In this short portion the command “be strong and courageous” (חֲזַק וֶאֱמַץ, chazak ve'ematz) appears more than once — first Moses speaks it to all Israel (Deut 31:6), then Moses speaks it to Joshua before the eyes of the people (Deut 31:7), and finally God Himself speaks it to Joshua (Deut 31:23). The same command, from the prophet's mouth and then from God's own, deepening layer by layer.

「你們當剛強壯膽，不要害怕，也不要畏懼他們，因為耶和華你的神和你同去；祂必不撇下你，也不丟棄你。」——申命記 31:6 “Be strong and courageous. Do not fear or be in dread of them, for it is the LORD your God who goes with you. He will not leave you or forsake you.”— Deuteronomy 31:6

希伯來文這兩個動詞各有重量。Chazak（חֲזַק）是「強壯、抓緊、堅固」——是外在的剛強，是面對敵人、面對爭戰時的力量。Ematz（אֱמַץ）則更偏向「鼓起勇氣、堅定心志、咬緊牙關」——是內在的膽量。摩西沒有只說「要勇敢」；他把外在的力量與內在的勇氣並排放在一起，因為進入應許之地既需要打仗的膀臂，也需要不退縮的心。 Each of these Hebrew verbs carries its own weight. Chazak (חֲזַק) means “be strong, take firm hold, be sturdy” — outward strength, the power for facing enemies and battle. Ematz (אֱמַץ) leans more toward “take courage, steel the heart, resolve” — inward boldness. Moses does not merely say “be brave”; he sets outward strength and inward courage side by side, because entering the Land takes both an arm that can fight and a heart that will not shrink back.

而這命令的根基，不在以色列自己的能力，而在那句應許：「祂必不撇下你，也不丟棄你」(לֹא יַרְפְּךָ וְלֹא יַעַזְבֶךָּ)。剛強壯膽從來不是一種「靠自己硬撐」的口號；它是因為神同去而生出的底氣。希伯來書 13:5 正是直接引用這句話來安慰新約的信徒——這命令從約旦河邊一路傳到了我們今天。 And the ground of the command is not Israel's own ability but the promise attached to it: “He will not leave you or forsake you” (לֹא יַרְפְּךָ וְלֹא יַעַזְבֶךָּ). “Be strong and courageous” was never a slogan about gritting your teeth alone; it is the confidence that grows out of the fact that God goes with you. Hebrews 13:5 quotes this very line to comfort believers under the New Covenant — the command travels all the way from the bank of the Jordan down to us today.

四、約書亞的受命 ── 棒子如何交出去IV. The Commissioning of Joshua — How the Staff Is Handed Over

利来篇的核心，是領導權的交接。摩西召了約書亞來，「在以色列眾人眼前」對他說話（申 31:7）——這一點極其重要。交棒不是私下的密談，而是公開的授權：百姓親眼看見摩西把祝福、責任與權柄交在約書亞身上，這樣將來他們才會跟隨他。 At the heart of Vayelech is the transfer of leadership. Moses summons Joshua and speaks to him “in the sight of all Israel” (Deut 31:7) — and this detail matters greatly. The handover is not a private conversation but a public commissioning: the people see with their own eyes Moses place blessing, responsibility, and authority upon Joshua, so that afterward they will follow him.

然後在 31:23，神親自向約書亞顯現、親自下命令——彷彿要讓約書亞知道：你所領受的呼召，最終不是來自摩西，而是來自那位差遣摩西的神。摩西是橋樑，不是源頭。真正的接班，是當新領袖的眼目從前任身上，轉向那位永不更替的神。 Then in 31:23 God Himself appears to Joshua and gives the command directly — as if to let Joshua know that the call he has received comes finally not from Moses but from the God who sent Moses. Moses is the bridge, not the source. True succession happens when the new leader's eyes lift from his predecessor and turn toward the God who is never replaced.

這裡也藏著一個希伯來名字的珍珠：「約書亞」(יְהוֹשֻׁעַ，Yehoshua) 的意思正是「耶和華是拯救」——與「耶書亞」(Yeshua) 同一個字根。摩西（律法）不能把百姓帶進應許之地；要把他們領進去的，是名叫「耶和華拯救」的那一位。對於讀福音書希伯來根源的人，這個交棒本身就是一幅圖畫。 Here too is hidden a pearl in a Hebrew name: “Joshua” (יְהוֹשֻׁעַ, Yehoshua) means precisely “the LORD is salvation” — the same root as “Yeshua.” Moses (the Law) cannot bring the people into the Promised Land; the one who leads them in bears the name “the LORD saves.” For anyone reading the Hebrew roots of the Gospel, the handover itself is a picture.

五、寫下妥拉與「哈阿茲努」之歌V. Writing the Torah and the Song of Ha'azinu

在他生命的最後一日，摩西做了兩件留存到永遠的事。第一，他「將這律法寫出來」(申 31:9)，交給抬約櫃的利未祭司，並吩咐每七年的住棚節要當著全以色列——男人、女人、孩子、寄居的——誦讀這律法（申 31:10-12）。妥拉不能只活在摩西一人的心裡；它必須被寫下來、被傳遞、被一代又一代地宣讀。 On the last day of his life Moses does two things that will outlast him forever. First, he “wrote this Torah” (Deut 31:9) and gave it to the Levitical priests who carried the ark, commanding that every seventh year at the Feast of Booths it be read aloud before all Israel — men, women, children, and the sojourner (Deut 31:10–12). The Torah cannot live only in Moses' own heart; it must be written down, handed on, and read aloud generation after generation.

第二，神吩咐摩西寫下一首歌——就是下一篇哈阿茲努（הַאֲזִינוּ，Ha'azinu，「側耳聽」）之歌（申 31:19-22）。為什麼是歌，而不是又一篇講章？因為神知道人會忘記律法，卻不容易忘記旋律。「這歌必在他們後裔的口中作見證」（申 31:21）——當以色列在日後悖逆、災禍臨到時，這首歌會像一個藏在記憶裡的見證人，重新喚醒他們，把他們帶回תְּשׁוּבָה（teshuvah，悔改、回轉）。 Second, God commands Moses to write a song — the song of the next portion, Ha'azinu (הַאֲזִינוּ, “Give ear”) (Deut 31:19–22). Why a song and not another sermon? Because God knows people forget laws but do not easily forget a melody. “This song shall confront them as a witness, for it will not be forgotten from the mouths of their offspring” (Deut 31:21) — when Israel later rebels and disaster comes, the song will rise like a witness hidden in memory, awakening them and drawing them back to תְּשׁוּבָה (teshuvah, repentance, turning back).

這是一個沉重卻滿有恩典的安排：神早已知道祂的百姓會跌倒，卻在他們跌倒之前，就為他們預備了回家的路。寫下的妥拉是審判的見證，也是悔改的呼喚。神不是等到人徹底失喪才出手；祂在告別的那一刻，已經把回轉的種子埋進了一首歌裡。 It is a heavy yet grace-filled arrangement: God already knows His people will fall, yet before they fall He has prepared the road home for them. The written Torah is both a witness for judgment and a summons to repentance. God does not wait until His people are utterly lost before acting; even in the hour of farewell He has buried the seed of return inside a song.

六、結語 ── 把你的「利来」走出去VI. Closing — Go Out and Walk Your Own Vayelech

利来篇是一篇關於「結束」的妥拉——但它教我們的，是如何讓一個結束變成另一段生命的起點。摩西不能進地，卻寫下了妥拉、寫下了歌、立了約書亞、向全民說了「剛強壯膽」。他把自己無法完成的，忠心地交了出去。從出埃及到曠野、從西奈山到約旦河——這條漫長的路上，沒有一個階段可以跳過，也沒有一個交棒是浪費的。 Vayelech is a portion about endings — but what it teaches is how to let an ending become the beginning of another life. Moses cannot enter the Land, yet he writes the Torah, writes the song, commissions Joshua, and speaks “be strong and courageous” to the whole nation. What he cannot finish himself, he faithfully hands on. From Egypt to the wilderness, from Sinai to the Jordan — on this long road no stage can be skipped, and no handover is ever wasted.

對今天的我們，這篇短短的妥拉留下三個珍珠：第一，像摩西一樣主動「走出去」——在你被呼召的崗位上服事到最後一刻。第二，在那不能再「出入」的時候，學習把棒子交出去，把眼目從自己轉向那位永不更替的神。第三，把妥拉寫下來、唱出來、傳下去，好讓回轉的路，永遠為下一代敞開。 For us today this short portion leaves three pearls: First, like Moses, actively “go out” — serve to the very last moment in the place to which you are called. Second, when you can no longer “come and go,” learn to hand off the staff, lifting your eyes from yourself to the God who is never replaced. Third, write, sing, and pass on the Torah, so that the road of return stays open forever for the next generation.

「你當剛強壯膽（חֲזַק וֶאֱמַץ，chazak ve'ematz）！不要懼怕，也不要驚惶，因為你無論往哪裡去，耶和華你的神必與你同在。走出去——把妥拉寫下來、唱出來、交下去。Am Yisrael Chai。」 “Be strong and courageous (חֲזַק וֶאֱמַץ, chazak ve'ematz)! Do not be frightened or dismayed, for the LORD your God is with you wherever you go. Go out — write the Torah down, sing it, hand it on. Am Yisrael Chai.”
