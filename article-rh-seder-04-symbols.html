Seder 的象徵與閉幕的祝福 The Seder's Symbols and the Closing Blessing

羅什哈沙拿之夜的逐席來到象徵的餐桌：為著一樣樣「象徵之物」(simanim) ──蘸蜜的蘋果、石榴、年首之頭、滿溢的豆莢──祝福、宣告、並且切斷。當「少了一個」時，我們齊聲呼喊：「再給我一個！」最後，羊角號吹響，閉幕的祝福把全世界的韓語會眾差派進入新的一年。 The Rosh HaShanah Seder arrives at its symbolic table: blessing, declaring, and cutting over each of the simanim — the apple dipped in honey, the pomegranate, the head of the year, the overflowing beans. When “one is missing,” the room cries together: “Give me one!” At last the shofar sounds, and the closing blessing sends the worldwide Korean congregation into the new year.

羅什哈沙拿之夜的最後一段，不再是講道，而是一張餐桌。拉比帶領全世界的韓語與華語會眾，一樣一樣拿起桌上的「象徵之物」(simanim)──韭蔥、蘸蜜的蘋果、紅蘿蔔、棗子、石榴、豆莢、年首之頭──為每一樣祝福、宣告、咬下。每一種蔬菜在希伯來文裡都藏著一個動詞：切斷、沉默、撕毀、充滿、成為頭。這不是迷信，而是把悔改演成可以握在手裡、可以吃進口裡的禮儀。當豆莢「少了一個」時，全場齊聲呼喊：「再給我一個！」──因為新的一年，誰也不該有任何缺欠。最後，羊角號吹響十次，閉幕的祝福把眾人差遣進入 5786 年。 The final stretch of the Rosh HaShanah Seder is no longer a sermon — it is a table. The rabbi leads the worldwide Korean and Chinese congregations to lift, one by one, the simanim (symbolic foods) before them — leek, apple in honey, carrot, date, pomegranate, beans, the head of the year — and to bless, declare, and bite into each. Every vegetable hides a Hebrew verb: to cut, to silence, to tear, to fill, to become the head. This is not superstition; it is repentance turned into a liturgy you can hold in your hand and swallow with your mouth. When a plate of beans comes up “one missing,” the whole room cries out: “Give me one!” — because in the new year no one should lack a thing. At last the shofar sounds ten times, and the closing blessing sends everyone out into 5786.

一、韭蔥與切斷 ── 把口中的罪割掉I. The Leek and the Cut — Severing the Sins of the Mouth

第一樣象徵之物 (siman) 是韭蔥。它的希伯來名字是 כְּרֵתִי kreti，字根 כָּרַת karat 的意思就是「切斷」。拉比說：這道蔬菜的名字本身就是「切斷」──所以今晚我們要把它咬斷，象徵把「我們口中的罪、我們會中的罪、我們一切的罪」全部切除。會眾在吃之前一同誦讀那句傳統的禱詞開頭：Yehi ratzon milefanecha（「願這在你面前蒙悅納」），並奉耶書亞的名禱告。 The first siman is the leek. Its Hebrew name is כְּרֵתִי kreti, from the root כָּרַת karat, “to cut off.” The rabbi says: the very name of this vegetable is “cut” — so tonight we bite through it as a sign of severing “the sins of our mouth, the sins of our assembly, all of our sins.” Before eating, the congregation reads together the opening of the traditional petition, Yehi ratzon milefanecha (“May it be Your will”), and prays in the name of Yeshua.

這一刻是充滿能力的。拉比向那些只在線上參與、手邊沒有韭蔥的人說：「拿一根紅蘿蔔代替也可以──只要你相信。」他宣告：撒但在你生命中被切斷了，不是被搖動，而是被完全切除。「你正站在神的家中。」線上、線下的人一同站起來，把這象徵之物舉起，當作一把刀。 The moment is charged. To those watching only online, with no leek at hand, the rabbi says: “Take a carrot instead — only believe.” He declares: Satan has been cut off in your life — not merely shaken, but severed entirely. “You are standing in the house of God.” Online and in the room, people rise and lift the symbol like a blade.

摩西「拿那牛犢用火焚燒，磨得粉碎，撒在水面上，叫以色列人喝。」——出埃及記 32:20 Moses “took the calf… burned it with fire, ground it to powder, scattered it on the water and made the people of Israel drink it.”— Exodus 32:20

拉比把這個畫面接到桌上的禮儀：正如摩西把金牛犢磨碎、叫百姓喝下、把他們的罪「吃掉」，今晚我們也把切斷的罪吃進去、嚥下去，使它在我們裡面再無立足之地。「阿們，哈利路亞，向主獻上榮耀的掌聲。」 The rabbi ties this image to the table: just as Moses ground the golden calf, made the people drink it, and had them “eat” their sin, tonight we eat the severed sin, swallow it, so it has no foothold left in us. “Amen, hallelujah — give the Lord a thunder of glory.”

二、紅蘿蔔與赦免 ── 趕走以掃，先饒恕II. The Carrot and Forgiveness — Driving Out Esau by First Forgiving

桌上紅色的蔬菜（紅蘿蔔）引出另一個希伯來文的雙關。拉比問：「你在聖經裡讀過『紅色的人』嗎？」──那是以掃，「以東」(אֱדוֹם Edom，意思是「紅」)、那位「身體發紅，渾身有毛」的長子。在彌賽亞信仰的傳統裡，紅蘿蔔對應的動詞是 כָּרַע kara（「擊打、使屈服」），讀作「karah / kah」。今晚我們對以掃、對我們的仇敵說：「Kah!」 The red vegetable on the table (the carrot) opens another Hebrew wordplay. The rabbi asks: “Have you ever read of a ‘red man’ in Scripture?” — that is Esau, the firstborn who came out “red and hairy,” whose nation is Edom (אֱדוֹם, “red”). In the Messianic tradition the verb attached to this siman is כָּרַע kara (“to strike down, to make bow”), voiced “kah.” Tonight we say to Esau, to our enemies: “Kah!”

但拉比立刻把這把「擊打」的刀，調轉成一面饒恕的鏡子。他提起查理（Charlie）的遺孀──在對殺害丈夫的兇手說任何話之前，她先說：「我饒恕你。」拉比說：你若想真的把以掃趕出去，你必須先饒恕那傷害你的人。今晚撒但是藉著「不饒恕的心」抓住你的；以掃之所以不肯離開，正因為你心裡仍有不肯放下的人。 But the rabbi immediately turns the striking blade into a mirror of forgiveness. He recalls the widow of Charlie — before she said anything to the man who murdered her husband, she said: “I forgive you.” If you truly want to drive Esau out, the rabbi says, you must first forgive the one who wounded you. Tonight Satan has held you by an unforgiving heart; Esau will not leave precisely because there is still someone you refuse to release.

耶書亞說：「父啊，赦免他們！因為他們所做的，他們不曉得。」——路加福音 23:34 Yeshua said: “Father, forgive them, for they know not what they do.”— Luke 23:34

會眾跟著拉比一句一句宣告：「我釋放他們。我饒恕他們。我帶著心中的饒恕進入羅什哈沙拿。我們為他們禱告，卻在我心裡放他們走。以掃的靈現在離開我了；你在我身上再沒有合法的權柄。」拉比提醒：我們並不是與屬血氣的人爭戰，而是與邪惡的實體爭戰── Line by line the congregation declares with the rabbi: “I release them. I forgive them. I enter Rosh HaShanah with forgiveness in my heart. We pray for them, yet in my heart I let them go. The spirit of Esau leaves me now; you have no legal authority over me any longer.” The rabbi reminds them: we do not wrestle against flesh and blood, but against evil entities —

「我們並不是與屬血氣的爭戰，乃是與那些執政的、掌權的、管轄這幽暗世界的，以及天空屬靈氣的惡魔爭戰。」——以弗所書 6:12 “For we wrestle not against flesh and blood, but against principalities, against powers, against the rulers of the darkness of this world, against spiritual wickedness in high places.”— Ephesians 6:12

三、棗子與沉默 ── 叫控告者閉口III. The Date and Silence — Making the Accuser Mute

接著是棗子 (תָּמָר tamar)。拉比先問一個看似平常的問題：「棗子長在樹上的時候，總是甜的嗎？」答案是：對那些有信心、有信仰文化的人來說，裡面有甜。「不要為明天憂慮──明天是屬耶書亞的世界。」這象徵之物更新我們的心思，提醒我們：即使在艱難的日子，也要有 shalom（平安）。 Next comes the date (תָּמָר tamar). The rabbi opens with a simple question: “When a date grows on the tree, is it always sweet?” The answer: for those with faith and a culture of faith, there is sweetness within. “Do not worry about tomorrow — tomorrow belongs to Yeshua's world.” This siman renews the mind and insists that even in hard days there must be shalom.

「耶和華說：我知道我向你們所懷的意念，是賜平安的意念，不是降災禍的意念，要叫你們末後有指望。」——耶利米書 29:11 “For I know the thoughts that I think toward you, says the LORD, thoughts of peace and not of evil, to give you a future and a hope.”— Jeremiah 29:11

拉比拆開 tamar 這個字，指出它與 יָתוֹם yatom（「孤兒、被迫沉默的人」）字母相通。Yatom 是「被安靜下來的人」。今晚我們要把這沉默掉轉過來：不再是我們被迫沉默，而是叫撒但閉口。拿起棗子，與拉比一同誦讀耶書亞在約翰福音 16:33 的話：「我將這些事告訴你們，是要叫你們在我裡面有平安……你們可以放心，我已經勝了世界。」 The rabbi unfolds the word tamar, noting its kinship with יָתוֹם yatom (“orphan,” “one made silent”). Yatom is the one who has been quieted. Tonight we reverse the silence: it is no longer we who are silenced — we make Satan mute. Lifting the date, the congregation reads with the rabbi Yeshua's words in John 16:33: “These things I have spoken to you, that in Me you may have peace… be of good cheer, I have overcome the world.”

「這就是得勝。安靜下來的，是這個──撒但啊，閉口！這控告今天要永遠安靜！哈利路亞，閉口！」拉比宣告：我們從撒但手中奪回主權，以掃離開，使我們裡面的內在的雅各更剛強，於是我們敢於相信美好的將來──不論是在韓國的土地、中國的土地，凡信靠 HaShem 的人，都有美好的未來。 “This is victory. The thing made silent is this — Satan, be quiet! This accusation will be silent forever today! Hallelujah — be still!” The rabbi declares: we take back sovereignty from Satan, Esau departs, the inner Jacob within us grows stronger, and so we dare to believe in a good future — whether in the land of Korea or the land of China, for all who trust in HaShem, a good future is coming.

四、石榴與滿溢 ── 剝去外殼，迎接充滿IV. The Pomegranate and Fullness — Peeling the Shell to Receive Abundance

有東西離開，就必須有東西進來。現在輪到桌上美麗的石榴 (רִמּוֹן rimon)。傳統說石榴有 613 顆籽，正如妥拉的 613 條誡命；石榴也象徵「外邦人的豐滿」(羅馬書 11:25)。拉比說：既然不潔的已經離開，現在要被充滿。「想要這充滿的人有多少？把幾顆石榴籽放在手裡，哈利路亞！」 When something leaves, something must come in. Now comes the beautiful pomegranate (רִמּוֹן rimon). Tradition says it holds 613 seeds, as the Torah has 613 commandments; it also signifies “the fullness of the Gentiles” (Romans 11:25). The rabbi says: now that the unclean has left, we are to be filled. “How many want this fullness? Put a few pomegranate seeds in your hand — hallelujah!”

石榴的外皮在希伯來文叫 קְלִיפָּה klipah（複數 klipot，「殼、外皮」，在猶太神祕傳統中指包裹聖潔之光的「殼」、罪的苦澀）。拉比說：你不會吃石榴的殼。要得豐滿，就必須把 klipah 完全剝掉。「今天我禱告：願一切的 klipot 都離開神的百姓。罪的苦澀──那很苦──今天離開我們。我們一感恩，就被充滿。」 The pomegranate's rind is called קְלִיפָּה klipah (plural klipot, “shell, husk” — in Jewish mystical tradition, the shell that traps the holy light, the bitterness of sin). The rabbi says: you do not eat the rind. To be full, you must peel the klipah away entirely. “Today I pray every klipah leaves God's people. The bitterness of sin — it is bitter — leaves us today. The moment we give thanks, we are filled.”

「跟著我說：我相信你的豐滿。藉著你的石榴，願全以色列得救。奉耶書亞的名禱告。」會眾嚐著甜，齊聲：「甜哪！我愛你們！哈利路亞，充滿！」 “Say after me: I believe in your fullness. Through your pomegranate, may all Israel be saved. In the name of Yeshua we pray.” Tasting the sweetness, the room calls out together: “How sweet! I love you! Hallelujah — fullness!”

五、豆莢與「少了一個」 ── 求兩份的充滿V. The Beans and “One Is Missing” — Asking for a Double Portion

桌上還有豆莢類（青豆、葫蘆瓜之屬）。其中一種傳統上叫 רוּבְּיָא rubia，代表「我們的功德」(merit) 的增多。拉比叫每人拿兩顆：一顆代表屬靈的充滿，一顆代表屬肉身（物質）的充滿，兩樣都帶到禱告裡。「需要屬靈供應的人？需要工作的人？需要加薪的人？需要錢的人？快、快，兩顆都拿起來！」 There are also legumes on the table (green beans, gourd, and the like). One, traditionally called רוּבְּיָא rubia, represents the increase of “our merit.” The rabbi has each person take two pieces: one for spiritual fullness, one for physical (material) fullness, bringing both into prayer. “Who needs spiritual supply? Who needs a job? Who needs a raise? Who needs money? Quick, quick — hold both!”

就在這時，一個動人的小插曲發生：有人手裡少了一顆。拉比沒有讓它過去──他高聲說：「一顆不見了！再給我一個！手不可以是空的！等一下，等一下……拉比給你！拉比給的，這是拉比給的。」他把自己手中的分出去。這個小動作把整段禮儀的心臟顯露出來：新的一年，誰也不該有缺欠；少了的那一個，必要被補滿。 At this point a moving little episode breaks out: someone's hand comes up one short. The rabbi will not let it pass — he calls out: “One is missing! Give me one! A hand cannot be empty! Wait, wait… the rabbi gives it to you! It is the rabbi who gives it.” He shares from his own hand. The small gesture lays bare the heart of the whole liturgy: in the new year no one should lack; the one that is missing must be made full.

「站起來！大家站起來！Rubia! 哦，我的天，謝謝你們，中國的弟兄姊妹們做得真好。」拉比帶領大家為「屬靈與屬肉身的同得充滿」禱告，連線上參與的人也一同誦讀 Yehi ratzon，求神使我們的功德增多、被高舉。 “Stand up! Everyone stand! Rubia! Oh my goodness — thank you, the Chinese brothers and sisters are doing so well.” The rabbi leads everyone to pray for both spiritual and physical fullness, and even those watching online recite Yehi ratzon together, asking God that our merit be increased and lifted high.

六、年首之頭 ── 要作頭，不作尾VI. The Head of the Year — Be the Head, Not the Tail

現在來到許多人最緊張、卻最有份量的象徵之物：年首之頭（傳統上用魚頭或羊頭）。拉比安慰大家：「別擔心，今天你不必吃魚頭。」這象徵的意義來自申命記的應許：神要你作頭，不作尾。「今晚，願在這桌邊的每一個人，都成為領袖。」 Now comes the siman many find most intimidating yet most weighty: the head of the year (traditionally a fish head or ram's head). The rabbi reassures everyone: “Don't worry — you don't have to eat the fish head today.” Its meaning comes from the promise of Deuteronomy: God means you to be the head and not the tail. “Tonight, may everyone at this table become a leader.”

「耶和華必使你作首不作尾，但居上不居下。」——申命記 28:13 “And the LORD shall make you the head and not the tail; you shall be above only, and not beneath.”— Deuteronomy 28:13

拉比請每一桌的「領袖」站起來、把盤子象徵性地舉起；接著分發鯷魚（小魚），叫大家分著吃。「主啊，今天我禱告，願你們每一個人都作領袖──在家中作領袖、在社會中作領袖，成就一切。你們知道自己是頭！」眾人因「神使你作頭」而站立、歡呼。 The rabbi calls the “leader” at each table to stand and lift the plate symbolically; then anchovies (small fish) are passed and shared. “Lord, today I pray everyone here becomes a leader — a leader at home, a leader in society, accomplishing all things. You know that you are the head!” The room rises and shouts because “God has made you the head.”

「主必快快地、徹底地施行祂的判語在世上。我必照各人的行為報應各人。」——羅馬書 9:28；參啟示錄 22:12 “For the Lord will execute His word upon the earth, finishing it and cutting it short… I will repay every man according to his deeds.”— Romans 9:28; cf. Revelation 22:12

七、解開誓言與羊角號的十個理由VII. Releasing Vows and the Ten Reasons for the Shofar

在吹角之前，拉比加入一個「例外」的環節，因為 siman kruf（這個解誓的時刻）很重要：你想要自由嗎？凡曾經許願、起誓卻沒有守住的人，請站起來。「主啊，我們來到你面前，懇求你釋放我們脫離那些我們許了卻沒有守的承諾。神是終極守約的那一位──願祂使我們也能彼此守約。求你釋放我們，脫離我們的承諾、我們的言語。是就說是，不是就說不是。藉著彌賽亞的功勞。阿們。」 Before sounding the shofar, the rabbi adds an “exception” — because siman kruf, this moment of releasing vows, matters: Do you want freedom? Everyone who has ever made a vow or oath and failed to keep it, stand up. “Lord, we come before You and plead: release us from the promises we made and did not keep. God is the ultimate Keeper of covenant — may He make us keep faith with one another too. Release us from our vows and our words. Let our yes be yes and our no be no. By the merit of the Messiah. Amen.”

然後拉比列出「為什麼今晚要吹羊角號 (שׁוֹפָר shofar)」的十個理由──他用英語說，會眾用韓語應答。其中包括：(1) 這是創世之日的記念；(2) 這是悔改(teshuvah) 的頭十日；(3) 以色列在西奈山說「我們必遵行，也必聽從」(na'aseh v'nishma) 的記念；(4) 先知的警告之聲；(5) 為被毀的聖殿、為耶路撒冷哀哭的記念；(6) 以撒在摩利亞山上甘心捨命（Akedah）的記念──願主因祂的名保守那些為以色列、為祂的名獻上性命的人。 Then the rabbi lists the ten reasons “why we sound the shofar (שׁוֹפָר shofar) tonight” — he speaks in English, the congregation answers in Korean. Among them: (1) it remembers the day of creation; (2) it opens the first ten days of repentance (teshuvah); (3) it recalls Israel at Sinai saying “we will do and we will hear” (na'aseh v'nishma); (4) it is the prophet's voice of warning; (5) it remembers the weeping over the ruined Temple and over Jerusalem; (6) it remembers Isaac who gave his life willingly on Moriah (the Akedah) — may the Lord, for His name's sake, guard those who give their lives for Israel and for His name.

「城中若吹角，百姓豈不驚恐嗎？」——阿摩司書 3:6 “Shall a trumpet be blown in the city, and the people not be afraid?”— Amos 3:6

其餘的理由指向主的日子：神為我們預備了禱告，百姓潔淨自己；那是「無比偉大、極其可畏」的日子，shofar 的聲音宣告主的日子近了，要為主預備道路；末後，羊角號要把全以色列重新聚集。拉比提醒：今晚在這線上崇拜裡吹角，並非循守一切傳統儀規，卻是邁出極重要的一步──「此刻天使都在歡喜。」 The remaining reasons point to the Day of the Lord: God has prepared prayers for us, and the people cleanse themselves; it is the day “great and very terrible,” and the shofar announces that the Day of the Lord is near — prepare the way of the Lord. And at the last, the shofar will regather all Israel. The rabbi notes: to sound the shofar in this online service tonight is not to keep every traditional rubric, but it is a momentous step — “right now the angels are rejoicing.”

八、結語 ── 把舊的倒空，迎接第二次的人生VIII. Closing — Empty Out the Old, Receive a Second Life

禮儀走到尾聲，拉比把整夜的象徵之物收攏成一個呼召：「把裡面舊的東西全部倒出去，把新的東西放進你裡面。」不要活在「舊的」之下；要常常更新，住在「新的」裡面。「今天就接受──接受你的第二次人生，接受你的新開始。」他奉「以色列」這名字的功勞祝福眾人，也求神保守眾人的口、保守眾人的行為與一切的道路。 As the liturgy draws to a close, the rabbi gathers the whole night of simanim into one call: “Pour out everything old inside you, and put the new things into you.” Do not live under “the old”; be always renewed, dwelling in “the new.” “Receive it today — receive your second life, receive your new beginning.” He blesses everyone by the merit of the name “Israel,” and asks God to guard their mouths, their deeds, and all their ways.

最後是節期的問安，他用韓文一遍遍呼喊「彌賽亞！彌賽亞！」，並向全會眾道出新年的願望：「新年蒙福！(Shanah Tovah) 願主與你們同在，願生命與你們同在，願主把一切都賜給你們。」華語、韓語、線上、線下的眾人，一同進入 5786 年。 At the last comes the festival greeting; in Korean he cries again and again “Mashiach! Mashiach!,” and speaks the new-year wish over the whole assembly: “Shanah Tovah — a good year! May the Lord be with you, may life be with you, may the Lord give you everything.” Chinese and Korean, online and in the room, all step together into 5786.

「把舊的全部倒空，把新的領受進來。撒但已被切斷，控告已被止息，咒詛已被撕毀，缺欠已被補滿。今晚你不再作尾，乃要作頭。願主保守你的口、你的行為、你一切的道路。新年蒙福，Shanah Tovah──彌賽亞的君王將要回來。」 “Empty out all that is old; receive all that is new. Satan is cut off, the accusation is silenced, the curse is torn up, and what was missing is made full. Tonight you are no longer the tail but the head. May the Lord guard your mouth, your deeds, and all your ways. Shanah Tovah — a good year — for the King of the Messiah is returning.”
