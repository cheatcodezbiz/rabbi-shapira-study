Slichot ── 悔罪之禱與羊角號的呼召 Slichot — The Penitential Prayers and the Call of the Shofar

在通往新年 (Rosh Hashanah) 的三十個夜晚裡，瑟法迪 (Sephardic) 傳統的悔罪之禱 (Selichot) 每夜響起。這是一份導覽：什麼是 Selichot？什麼是「十三項憐憫的屬性」？羊角號每天呼召我們什麼？而這一切，又如何引向那「為王加冕之日」？ For thirty nights leading to Rosh Hashanah, the Sephardic Selichot services rise in prayer. This is a guide: What is Selichot? What are the Thirteen Attributes of Mercy? What does the shofar call us to each night? And how does it all lead to the “inauguration of the King”?

夜半時分，一群分散在世界各地的人，在螢幕前一同醒來。不是為了娛樂，而是為了悔改。這就是 סְלִיחוֹת Selichot（「悔罪之禱」）——在通往新年 רֹאשׁ הַשָּׁנָה Rosh Hashanah（吹角節）的日子裡，每夜響起的赦罪祈禱。在 Ahavat Ammi 事工的「Kol HaShofar · 100 道門 100 羊角號」系列中，瑟法迪 (Sephardim) 傳統的 Selichot 連續進行三十天，每一夜都把悔罪的禱告與一段「羊角號的律法」(halachot) 教導交織在一起。這篇文章不是某一夜的紀錄，而是整個系列的導覽：帶你認識 Selichot 是什麼、三十天的習俗從何而來、十三項憐憫屬性為何是這場服事的心臟，以及羊角號的呼召如何把我們一路引向「為王加冕之日」。 In the dead of night, people scattered across the world wake at their screens — not for entertainment, but for repentance. This is סְלִיחוֹת Selichot (“penitential prayers, prayers for forgiveness”) — the petitions for pardon that rise night after night on the road to רֹאשׁ הַשָּׁנָה Rosh Hashanah, the Feast of Trumpets. In Ahavat Ammi's “Kol HaShofar · 100 Gates 100 Shofarot” series, the Selichot run for thirty straight nights according to the tradition of the Sephardim, and every single night weaves the liturgy of repentance together with a teaching on the halachot (laws) of the shofar. This article is not the record of one night; it is a guide to the whole series — what Selichot is, where the thirty-day custom comes from, why the Thirteen Attributes of Mercy are the heart of the service, and how the call of the shofar carries us all the way to the “inauguration of the King.”

一、什麼是 Selichot？I. What Is Selichot?

סְלִיחוֹת Selichot（單數 selichah，「赦免、原諒」）是一套猶太人的悔罪祈禱，傳統上在深夜或清晨黎明前誦讀，為的是在審判的日子來臨之前，求神的赦免與憐憫。它不是一篇禱告，而是一整套禮儀——詩篇、認罪文、求赦的詩歌 (piyyutim)，以及貫穿全程的核心：對神十三項憐憫屬性的呼求。 The סְלִיחוֹת Selichot (singular selichah, “pardon, forgiveness”) are a body of Jewish penitential prayers, recited traditionally in the dead of night or before the dawn, to seek God's forgiveness and mercy before the Day of Judgment arrives. It is not a single prayer but an entire liturgy — psalms, confessions, petitional poems (piyyutim), and, running through all of it, the heartbeat of the service: the invocation of God's Thirteen Attributes of Mercy.

在這個系列裡，主領者每一夜都從同樣的節奏開始：先教導一個希伯來字，由這個字引向領唱者 (חַזָּן chazan) 所帶領的 Selichot 服事，再引向當夜的特別教導。開幕之夜的那個字，就是 שׁוֹפָר shofar（羊角號）。 In this series the host opens each night with the same rhythm: first he teaches one Hebrew word; that word leads into the Selichot service led by the cantor (חַזָּן chazan); and that leads into the special teaching for the night. The word on the opening night is the word itself — שׁוֹפָר shofar, the ram's horn.

主領者特別點出一個美麗的字源：shofar（שׁוֹפָר）與動詞 leshaper（לְשַׁפֵּר，「改善、使更好」）同根。米大示 (Midrash) 也把它連於「Shapru et maasechem」——「改善你們的行為」。換句話說，羊角號不只是一個聲音，它是一道命令：在這三十天裡，每個人都要在自己的行為、禱告、信心、誡命 (mitzvot) 與施捨 (tzedakah) 上，經歷一場名為 shippur（「改善」）的過程。 The host points to a beautiful etymology: shofar (שׁוֹפָר) shares a root with the verb leshaper (לְשַׁפֵּר, “to improve, to make better”). The Midrash, too, ties it to “Shapru et maasechem” — “improve your deeds.” In other words, the shofar is not merely a sound; it is a command. Across these thirty days, each person is to undergo a process called shippur (“improvement”) in his conduct, his prayer life, his faith, his mitzvot, and his tzedakah (charity).

「羊角號的聲音，應當叫每一個靈魂渴望變得更好。你能做得更好——神正呼召你今天升得更高，登上更高的諸天。」—— 開幕之夜的教導 “The sound of the shofar should cause each and every soul to want to do better. You can do better — God is calling you to rise higher, to ascend to higher heavens today.”— from the opening night's teaching

值得留意的是：在這個系列裡，Selichot 與「100 道門 100 羊角號」(100 Gates 100 Shofarot) 這個更大的出版計劃是結合在一起的。「100 道門」這個意象本身就極富份量——它讓人想起悔改有「許多道門」，而每一夜的禱告與每一聲號角，都是一道被推開的門。三十個夜晚，就是三十次被邀請走進更深的歸回。 It is worth noting that in this series the Selichot are bound up with a larger publication, “100 Gates 100 Shofarot.” The image of “100 gates” carries its own weight — it evokes the idea that repentance has many doors, and that each night's prayer and each blast of the horn is one more door pushed open. Thirty nights are thirty invitations to step into a deeper return.

二、三十天的瑟法迪習俗II. The Thirty-Day Sephardic Custom

在誦念 Selichot 的時間長短上，猶太傳統並不一致。亞實基拿 (Ashkenazi) 的習俗通常只在 Rosh Hashanah 之前數天才開始；而瑟法迪 (סְפָרַדִּים Sephardim) 的傳統則整整持續一個月——從以祿月 (אֱלוּל Elul，希伯來曆的第六個月) 的第一天起，每天誦念 Selichot，直到新年。這正是本系列所跟隨的傳統：三十天的悔罪之禱，引我們進入吹角節。 Jewish tradition does not agree on how long Selichot are said. The Ashkenazi custom typically begins only a few days before Rosh Hashanah; the Sephardic (סְפָרַדִּים Sephardim) tradition stretches across an entire month — reciting Selichot every day from the first of אֱלוּל Elul (the sixth month of the Hebrew calendar) until the New Year. This is the tradition the series follows: thirty days of penitential prayer leading us into the Feast of Trumpets.

以祿月為什麼如此特別？拉比施內爾·扎勒曼 (Rabbi Schneur Zalman of Liadi，哈巴德哈西迪運動的創始人) 用一個著名的比喻來解釋：在以祿月，「君王在田間」(The King is in the field)。一年之中的其他時候，王坐在祂天上的殿裡，混雜的眾人——像你我這樣的平常人——無法輕易朝見祂。但在以祿月，王親自走出宮殿，到田野間，與每一個普通人尋求一場面對面的、私人的交談。 Why is Elul so special? Rabbi Schneur Zalman of Liadi — founder of Chabad Hasidism — explains it with a famous parable: in the month of Elul, “the King is in the field.” The rest of the year the King sits in His heavenly hall, where the mixed multitude — ordinary people like you and me — cannot easily approach Him. But in Elul the King Himself goes out to the field, seeking a personal, face-to-face conversation with each and every common person.

「平時要朝見王，你必須是聖潔的，活在聖潔中。但在以祿月，哈示姆 (Hashem) 向所有人敞開。所有的分隔 (mechitzot)、所有的屏障都被挪去——而羊角號的聲音，正是挪去這些屏障的聲音，宣告：王在這裡，祂正尋找你們每一個人。」 “Usually, to reach the King you must be holy, living your life in holiness. But in Elul, Hashem is accessible to all. All the mechitzot, all the barriers are removed — and the sound of the shofar is the very sound that removes them, announcing: the King is here, and He is seeking each and every one of you.”

這就是「Siyata Dishmaya」（「天上的幫助」）的日子。在這三十天裡，即使是離王最遠的人，也擁有一條特別的、通往王的捷徑。王走出宮殿的那一刻，每一個人都能來到祂面前。本系列每天的服事——除了安息日之外——都在這份「敞開的恩典」中進行。 These are the days of “Siyata Dishmaya” (“the help of Heaven”). In these thirty days, even those who are very far from the King are granted special access to Him. The moment the King leaves His palace, every person can come to Him. Each daily service of the series — every day except Shabbat — unfolds inside this open grace.

三、服事的結構III. The Structure of the Service

雖然每一夜都有它獨特的教導，Selichot 服事本身遵循一個穩定的結構。明白這個結構，能幫助你不只是「聽見」，更是「進入」這場禱告： Though each night carries its own teaching, the Selichot service itself follows a steady structure. Knowing the shape helps you not merely “hear” the prayer but enter it:

一、開場與詩篇。服事常以《詩篇》的讚美開始——「Ashrei ha'am，這百姓便為有福」——把心思從日常的焦慮提升到對王的敬畏與信靠。詩篇 27 篇（「耶和華是我的亮光，是我的拯救」）尤其常在這段日子裡出現，宣告在審判的季節中，神仍是避難所。 1. Opening and Psalms. The service often begins with psalms of praise — “Ashrei ha'am, happy is the people” — lifting the heart from daily anxiety into awe and trust before the King. Psalm 27 (“The LORD is my light and my salvation”) recurs throughout this season, declaring that even in the season of judgment, God is a refuge.

二、認罪 (Vidui)。會眾低頭認罪，承認自己的虧欠：「我們的父，我們的王，我們在你面前犯了罪。」這正是 Avinu Malkenu（「我們的父，我們的王」）這段催人淚下的禱文的位置——一句一句地求王賜下美好的年日、廢去仇敵的惡謀、赦免一切的過犯，並把我們「記在生命冊上」。 2. Confession (Vidui). The congregation bows and confesses its debt: “Our Father, our King, we have done wrong in Your presence.” This is the place of Avinu Malkenu (“Our Father, our King”) — that tear-drawing litany that pleads, line after line, for a good year, for the nullifying of enemies' plans, for forgiveness of every transgression, and to be “inscribed in the book of life.”

三、十三項憐憫屬性。這是整場服事的核心高峰，我們將在下一節詳細展開。 3. The Thirteen Attributes of Mercy. This is the central climax of the entire service — the subject of the next section.

四、求赦的詩歌 (Piyyutim)。每一夜都有屬於那一夜的悔罪詩歌，例如「第二日的 Selichot」描繪一個在חֲצוֹת chatzot（「半夜」）醒來、為自己的羞愧而流淚、晝夜以眼淚為食的人。這些詩歌把抽象的悔改，化為一個個有血有肉、夜半呼求的靈魂。 4. Petitional poems (Piyyutim). Each night has poems proper to it. The “Selichot for the second day,” for instance, pictures a soul who wakes at חֲצוֹת chatzot (“midnight”), weeping over the shame of his faults, his tears his bread by day and by night. These poems turn abstract repentance into flesh-and-blood souls crying out in the dark.

五、羊角號與每日的律法。在這個特別的版本裡，服事還加入了羊角號的吹奏，以及一段關於羊角號 הֲלָכָה halachah（律法、條例）的教導——每天一條，引導會眾在三十天裡逐步認識這枝號角的全部規矩與意義。 5. The shofar and the daily law. In this special edition the service also includes the blowing of the shofar and a teaching on a single point of shofar הֲלָכָה halachah (law) — one each day, guiding the congregation step by step, over thirty days, into the full body of the horn's rules and meaning.

四、十三項憐憫的屬性 ── 這場服事的心臟IV. The Thirteen Attributes of Mercy — the Heart of the Service

如果說 Selichot 有一個跳動的心臟，那就是 שְׁלֹשׁ עֶשְׂרֵה מִדּוֹת Shelosh-Esreh Middot——「十三項（憐憫的）屬性」。這段禱告的根源在出埃及記 34:6-7：當摩西第二次上西奈山求神赦免以色列的金牛犢之罪時，神親自從雲中降臨，在摩西面前經過，並宣告自己的名： If Selichot has a beating heart, it is the שְׁלֹשׁ עֶשְׂרֵה מִדּוֹת Shelosh-Esreh Middot — the “Thirteen Attributes of Mercy.” Their source is Exodus 34:6-7: when Moses ascended Sinai a second time to plead forgiveness for Israel's sin of the golden calf, God Himself came down in the cloud, passed before Moses, and proclaimed His own name:

「耶和華，耶和華，是有憐憫、有恩典的神，不輕易發怒，並有豐盛的慈愛和誠實，為千萬人存留慈愛，赦免罪孽、過犯，和罪惡。」——出埃及記 34:6-7 “Adonai, Adonai — a merciful and gracious God, slow to anger, abounding in kindness and truth, keeping mercy for thousands of generations, forgiving iniquity and transgression and sin.”— Exodus 34:6-7

猶太傳統視這段經文為一份「立約的應許」：神彷彿向摩西保證，每當以色列在祂面前誦念這十三項屬性、並按它們而行，他們必不空手而回。所以在 Selichot 中，當會眾來到認罪的高峰，他們就一同呼求這位「坐在憐憫寶座上的全能君王」——那位「恩慈地赦免祂百姓的罪、一個一個地使它們消逝」的王。 Jewish tradition treats this passage as a covenantal promise: God, as it were, assures Moses that whenever Israel recites these thirteen attributes before Him and acts upon them, they will not return empty. So in Selichot, when the congregation reaches the climax of confession, they invoke together this “Almighty King who sits upon the throne of mercy” — the King who “graciously pardons the sins of His people, making them pass away one by one.”

這是 Selichot 最深的安慰：悔改的根基，不是我們的善行，而是神的本性。我們無功可誇——「我們沒有可以稱義的行為，求你以公義和慈愛待我們」——但神本身就是憐憫的。十三項屬性提醒我們：審判的季節，同時也是憐憫敞開的季節。 This is the deepest comfort of Selichot: the ground of repentance is not our merit but God's nature. We have nothing to boast in — “we have no deeds to justify us; deal with us in righteousness and love” — yet God Himself is merciful. The Thirteen Attributes remind us that the season of judgment is, at the very same time, the season of mercy thrown open.

五、羊角號的呼召V. The Call of the Shofar

在這三十天裡（安息日除外），每一夜都有羊角號吹響。為什麼是羊角號？因為它的聲音不是用來「聽」的，而是用來「喚醒」的。它穿透耳朵，直達心靈，撼動我們的自滿，逼我們的靈魂醒過來、留心。本系列的主領者一再強調：「不要只用耳朵聽，要用心聽。」 In these thirty days (except Shabbat), the shofar sounds every night. Why the shofar? Because its sound is meant not to be “heard” but to awaken. It pierces past the ears straight to the soul, shatters our complacency, forces the spirit to wake and pay attention. The host of the series says it again and again: “Do not listen only with your ears — listen with your heart.”

回到那個字源：shofar 與 leshaper（「改善」）同根。羊角號的每一聲，都是一道呼召：שַׁפְּרוּ אֶת מַעֲשֵׂיכֶם Shapru et maasechem——「改善你們的行為。」這就是這三十天的本質：它們被稱為「shippur 的日子」，是一段向內看、而不只是向外看的時間。在這些日子裡，我們向哈示姆發問：我可以在哪裡變得更好？ Back to that etymology: shofar shares a root with leshaper (“to improve”). Every blast of the horn is a summons: שַׁפְּרוּ אֶת מַעֲשֵׂיכֶם Shapru et maasechem — “improve your deeds.” That is the essence of these thirty days: they are called “the days of shippur,” a time to look inward and not merely outward. In these days we ask Hashem: where can I become better?

這也正是 תְּשׁוּבָה teshuvah（「悔改、歸回」）的核心。Teshuvah 的字根是「歸回」——不是單單為過去懊悔，而是轉身歸向神，並在行為上真正改變。羊角號宣告：王在田間敞開了大門，שׁוּבוּ אֵלַי וְאָשׁוּבָה אֲלֵיכֶם Shuvu elay ve'ashuvah aleichem——「你們轉向我，我就轉向你們。」唯一需要做的，就是「出來」(yetziah)——無論你今天被什麼所困，從中走出來，緊緊依附於萬王之王。 This is the very core of תְּשׁוּבָה teshuvah (“repentance, return”). The root of teshuvah is “to return” — not merely regret for the past but turning back to God, with genuine change in conduct. The shofar announces that the King has opened the gates in the field: שׁוּבוּ אֵלַי וְאָשׁוּבָה אֲלֵיכֶם Shuvu elay ve'ashuvah aleichem — “Return to Me, and I will return to you.” The one thing required is to “come out” (yetziah) — whatever your boundary today, step out of it and cleave to the King of kings.

這也是 Selichot 之所以在חֲצוֹת chatzot（半夜）誦念的原因之一。半夜是世界最安靜、最少分心的時刻；按猶太傳統，這也是神特別「臨近」的時刻。在這寂靜中醒來，刻意離開睡眠去禱告，本身就是一個悔改的舉動——它向神宣告：我願意為你，把我的安舒擱在一旁。悔罪詩歌中那個「在半夜醒來、為羞愧流淚」的靈魂，正是我們每一個人的寫照。 This is also part of why Selichot are recited at חֲצוֹת chatzot (midnight). Midnight is the quietest, least distracted hour of the world; in Jewish tradition it is also an hour when God is especially “near.” To wake in that silence, to leave sleep deliberately in order to pray, is itself an act of repentance — it declares to God: I am willing to set my comfort aside for You. The soul in the piyyut who “wakes at midnight and weeps over his shame” is a portrait of every one of us.

但王在以祿月只要求一件事：你來見祂的時候，要帶著大喜樂。「無論你今天的屬靈光景如何，都要帶著極大的喜樂出來。」羊角號不是定罪的號角，而是邀請的號角：王在外面等你，不要遲延，奔向祂吧。 But in Elul the King asks one thing: when you come to meet Him, come with great joy. “Whatever your spiritual condition today, come out with great joy.” The shofar is not a horn of condemnation but of invitation: the King is waiting outside for you — do not delay; run to Him.

六、引向「為王加冕之日」VI. Leading to the “Inauguration of the King”

三十夜的 Selichot 並非漫無目的。它們全都指向一個高峰：רֹאשׁ הַשָּׁנָה Rosh Hashanah，「年之首」，也就是吹角節。在猶太傳統中，Rosh Hashanah 被稱為「為王加冕之日」(the day of the inauguration of the King)——在這一天，會眾以號角的聲音，公開地宣告、接受、並為神加冕為全地的君王。 The thirty nights of Selichot are not aimless. They all point to one summit: רֹאשׁ הַשָּׁנָה Rosh Hashanah, “the head of the year,” the Feast of Trumpets. In Jewish tradition, Rosh Hashanah is called “the day of the inauguration of the King” — the day on which the congregation, with the voice of the horn, publicly proclaims, receives, and crowns God as King over all the earth.

這就解釋了為什麼這三十天如此重要。你不會毫無預備地去參加君王的加冕大典。Selichot 正是那場預備：潔淨自己、改善行為、歸回到王面前，好讓你在加冕之日，能以潔淨的心、喜樂的臉，迎見那位走出田間、又重登寶座的王。羊角號既呼召我們悔改，也宣告君王的到來。 This is why the thirty days matter so much. You do not arrive unprepared at a king's coronation. Selichot is that preparation: cleansing oneself, improving one's deeds, returning to the King, so that on the day of inauguration you may meet — with a clean heart and a joyful face — the King who went out into the field and now ascends His throne again. The shofar both calls us to repent and heralds the coming of the King.

而這條路並未在 Rosh Hashanah 就停下。新年之後，還有「敬畏之十日」(Ten Days of Awe)——羊角號的呼召在這十天裡愈發加強，一路引向 יוֹם כִּפּוּר Yom Kippur（贖罪日）這個悔改季節的最高峰。Selichot 是這整段旅程的序幕，是把全地的心思，提前三十天就調向那位君王。 And the road does not stop at Rosh Hashanah. After the New Year come the “Ten Days of Awe” — ten days in which the call of the shofar intensifies, leading all the way to יוֹם כִּפּוּר Yom Kippur, the Day of Atonement, the summit of the whole season of repentance. Selichot is the overture to that entire journey, tuning the heart of the world toward the King a full thirty days in advance.

七、向全世界、用眾語言廣播VII. Broadcast to the World, in Many Tongues

這個系列有一個動人的特點：它不只用一種語言播出。本年度的 Selichot 服事「不只用英文，更用無數其他語言」傳送出去——觀眾若用西班牙文或韓文收看，可以調到對應語言的頻道一同參與。這正是 Ahavat Ammi（希伯來文「愛我的子民」）事工的異象：把以色列悔改與禱告的季節，向列國敞開。 One moving feature of this series: it does not broadcast in a single language. This year's Selichot services are transmitted “not just in English, but in countless other languages” — viewers watching in Spanish or Korean can tune to the appropriate channel and join in their own tongue. This is the vision of Ahavat Ammi (Hebrew, “love of My people”): to open Israel's season of repentance and prayer to the nations.

對華人信徒而言，這也是一份邀請。我們未必跟隨瑟法迪的禮儀，但 Selichot 所教導的——悔改是歸回，預言隨悔改而轉，王在憐憫中敞開大門——這些都是聖經本身的脈動。當我們在自己的禱告生活中，學習夜半醒來、向內省察、求神「改善我們的行為」時，我們也就與這三十天的呼召同行。 For Chinese believers this is also an invitation. We may not follow the Sephardic rite, but what Selichot teaches — that repentance is return, that prophecy turns with repentance, that the King opens His gates in mercy — is the pulse of Scripture itself. When we learn, in our own prayer lives, to wake in the night, to examine ourselves inwardly, and to ask God to “improve our deeds,” we walk in step with the call of these thirty days.

八、如何使用這份導覽VIII. How to Use This Guide

如果你打算跟隨本系列，這裡有三個簡單的建議。第一，每夜抓住「一個字」。主領者的設計就是每晚先教一個希伯來字（如 shofar、teshuvah、chatzot）。把那個字記在心裡一整天，讓它成為你當天的默想。 If you intend to follow the series, here are three simple suggestions. First, catch the “one word” each night. The host's design is to teach a single Hebrew word each evening (such as shofar, teshuvah, chatzot). Carry that word in your heart through the day and let it become your meditation.

第二，聽見羊角號時，回應它。不要只把它當作一個聲音。當號角響起，在心裡說：「Shapru et maasechem——改善我的行為。」讓那一聲成為一次微小的、真實的回轉。 Second, when you hear the shofar, answer it. Do not treat it as mere sound. When the horn blows, say in your heart: “Shapru et maasechem — improve my deeds.” Let that single blast become one small, real turning.

第三，記住屬性，不是功德。當你為自己的虧欠憂傷時，不要停在罪疚裡。回到十三項憐憫的屬性——回到神的本性。悔改的盼望從來不建立在「我夠不夠好」，而建立在「耶和華，耶和華，是有憐憫、有恩典的神」。 Third, remember the attributes, not your merit. When you grieve over your debt, do not stay in guilt. Return to the Thirteen Attributes of Mercy — return to the nature of God. The hope of repentance never rests on “am I good enough,” but on “Adonai, Adonai, a merciful and gracious God.”

九、結語 ── 醒來吧，王在等你IX. Closing — Awake, the King Is Waiting

這正是 Selichot 真正的意義所在：「朋友們，我們所有人都當醒來。這正是這一切的核心——喚醒我們，喚醒我們的靈魂。」三十個夜晚，三十次的呼召，把分散全地的人，調向同一位敞開大門、走入田間的君王。 This is what Selichot is truly about: “Friends, it is important for all of us to wake up. That is what this is really all about — to awaken us, to awaken our soul.” Thirty nights, thirty calls, tuning people scattered across the earth toward the one King who has opened His gates and walked out into the field.

「王在田間，門已敞開，祂正站在宮門外，等候我們每一個人。無論你今天的屬靈光景如何——帶著大喜樂出來。你將要聽見的羊角號，是來提醒你：王在等你。不要遲延，奔向王吧。」 “The King is in the field, the gates are open, and He is standing just outside, waiting for each and every one of us. Whatever your spiritual condition today — come out with great joy. The shofar you are about to hear is here to remind you: the King is waiting for you. Do not delay; run to the King.”
