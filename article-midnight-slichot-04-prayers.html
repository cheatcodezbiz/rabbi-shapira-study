悔罪之禱與閉幕的祝福 The Slichot Prayers and the Closing Blessing

服事走進悔罪之禱（סְלִיחוֹת Selichot）本身，也走向閉幕——這是向憐憫的呼求、十三屬性的吟誦，以及那把全球會眾送入夜色、卻已親近君王的祝福。和好先行，救贖隨之；願這一夜成為加速 Geulah（救贖）的一夜。 The service moves into the Slichot penitential prayers (סְלִיחוֹת Selichot) themselves and into the close — the cry for mercy, the chanting of the Thirteen Attributes, and the blessing that sends the worldwide congregation out into the night having drawn near to the King. Reconciliation first, redemption after; may this night be one that hastens the Geulah.

夜已深。在多明尼加共和國的會場裡，全球各地的會眾透過直播一同站立——服事終於走到了它的心臟：悔罪之禱（סְלִיחוֹת Selichot）本身。先是低聲的認罪——「我們犯了罪，我們行了不義，我們頑梗，我們敗壞……」——然後是向著憐憫（רַחֲמִים rachamim）的呼求，是十三屬性（שְׁלֹשׁ עֶשְׂרֵה מִדּוֹת Shelosh-Esreh Middot）的吟誦。但拉比沙皮拉在這最後一段裡留下一個出人意料的轉折：悔罪服事不只是朝向天的，它必須先發生在我們彼此之間。真正加速 Geulah（救贖）的，是你今夜願不願意走向那個傷害過你的人，重新稱他為弟兄。 It is late in the night. In the hall in the Dominican Republic, a congregation gathered from across the world stands together over a livestream as the service finally reaches its heart: the Slichot penitential prayers (סְלִיחוֹת Selichot) themselves. First the whispered confession — “we have transgressed, we have acted wrongly, we have been obstinate, we have corrupted…” — then the cry toward mercy (רַחֲמִים rachamim), the chanting of the Thirteen Attributes (שְׁלֹשׁ עֶשְׂרֵה מִדּוֹת Shelosh-Esreh Middot). But in this final stretch Rabbi Shapira hands us an unexpected turn: the penitential service is not only directed upward — it must happen first between us. What truly hastens the Geulah is whether, tonight, you are willing to walk toward the one who wounded you and call him a brother again.

一、悔罪之禱 ── 認罪與潔淨的呼求I. The Slichot Prayers — Confession and the Cry to Be Cleansed

悔罪服事的核心，是一段古老的認罪文（Vidui，וִדּוּי）。會眾不是一個一個地說「我」，而是同聲說「我們」：「我們犯了罪，我們悖逆，我們頑梗，我們敗壞，我們行了可憎的事，我們走偏，也使別人走偏。」這份認罪是複數的、是團體的——因為在悔罪之禱裡，沒有人是局外人。 At the centre of the penitential service is an ancient confession (Vidui, וִדּוּי). The congregation does not say “I” one by one; it says “we” in one voice: “We have transgressed, we have rebelled, we have been obstinate, we have corrupted, we have acted abominably, we have gone astray, and we have led others astray.” The confession is plural, communal — because in the Slichot, no one stands outside.

「誰能知道自己的錯失呢？願你赦免我隱而未現的過錯。求你救我們脫離一切故意的罪，潔淨我們脫離一切的污穢。」——取自悔罪禱文（參詩篇 19:12） “Who can discern his own errors? Cleanse me from hidden faults. Save us from all our willful sins, and purify us from all our impurity.”— from the Slichot liturgy (cf. Psalm 19:12)

緊接著的，是以西結書 36:25 的應許被禱告出來——「我必用清水灑在你們身上，你們就潔淨了；我要潔淨你們，使你們脫離一切的污穢，又脫離一切的偶像。」這是悔罪之禱的盼望所在：人的認罪向上呈遞，神的潔淨向下傾倒。會眾以「阿們」（אָמֵן Amen，「神是信實的王」）作結，把整個重擔交在那位信實者的手中。 There follows the promise of Ezekiel 36:25 prayed back to God — “I will sprinkle clean water on you, and you shall be clean; I will cleanse you from all your filthiness and from all your idols.” Here is the hope of the Slichot: human confession rises up, divine cleansing pours down. The congregation seals it with “Amen” (אָמֵן, “the LORD is a faithful King”), placing the whole weight in the hands of the Faithful One.

二、從多明尼加直播全世界 ── 義人也受審判II. Broadcasting to the World from the Dominican Republic — Even the Righteous Are Judged

拉比向各地透過直播加入的人致謝：「謝謝你們今夜與我們同在這悔罪服事中。我相信主的靈正運行在全世界。」他們從多明尼加共和國的島上向外傳送，眼看著外邦信徒在這座島上覺醒、興起——而這與一年前同一座島上的光景，已截然不同。 The rabbi thanks all those joining by livestream from around the globe: “Thank you for being here with us in this Slichot service. I believe the Spirit of the Lord is moving across the whole world.” They are broadcasting outward from the island of the Dominican Republic, watching an awakening rise among the Gentile believers there — something very different from what stood on this same island exactly a year before.

他點出一個容易被忽略的真理：悔罪與審判的信息臨到每一個人。啟示錄教導我們，義人與惡人都要受審判。我們不該以為只有惡人會被審判——義人也要被審判。所以他邀請每一個人，在自己也知道有虧欠的那個領域裡悔改；而這正是 Geulah（救贖）的一味必不可少的材料。 He names a truth easily overlooked: the message of penitence and judgment falls on everyone. The book of Revelation teaches that both the righteous and the wicked are judged. We must not imagine that only the wicked face judgment — the righteous are judged too. So he invites each person to repent in the very area where he knows himself to be guilty; and this, he says, is an indispensable ingredient of the Geulah, the redemption.

三、摩西與葉忒羅 ── 救贖始於和好III. Moses and Jethro — Redemption Begins with Reconciliation

拉比把整段服事的重量，壓在一段令人意外的經文上：出埃及記 18 章，摩西與岳父葉忒羅（Yitro）重逢。他說，這次重逢是末後日子裡猶太人與基督教之間和好的圖畫。在塔尼亞（Tania）的教導中，「人民若不合一，就沒有王可以登基掌權」——我們需要一位王，但也需要百姓預備好，王才能作王。 Rabbi Shapira rests the weight of the whole service on a surprising passage: Exodus 18, where Moses is reunited with his father-in-law Jethro (Yitro). This reunion, he says, is a picture of the reconciliation between the Jew and Christianity in the last days. As the Tanya teaches, “there is no king until the people are unified so that he can reign” — we need a King, but we also need the people made ready, so that the King can be King.

他提出一個尖銳的更正：以為彌賽亞會來、把所有該做的工都替我們做完，是個錯誤。「悔罪服事不是只與天有關的事。悔罪服事必須臨到我們每一個人身上——先在彼此之間，然後才向著天。」彼此之間的悔罪，才是真正加速 Geulah 的力量，不只在今世，也在來世。 He offers a sharp correction: it is an error to think the Messiah will come and do all the work that is not His to do. “It is a mistake to think the Slichot service is only about heaven. The Slichot service has to happen upon each one of us — first between one another, and only then toward heaven.” A penitential service between people is what truly accelerates the Geulah — not only in this world we live in, but in the world to come.

四、隱藏的暗號 ── Tzipporah、Poretz 與 ElulIV. The Hidden Codes — Tzipporah, Poretz, and Elul

拉比帶我們進入文本的更深處。葉忒羅把摩西的妻子西坡拉（צִפֹּרָה Tzipporah）一同帶來——而當你重排西坡拉名字的字母時，會浮現出「Poretz」（פֶּרֶץ，「突破者」）這個名字，正是彌賽亞的名號之一（出於「uparatzta」，「你要向四方突破擴展」）。這像是一個被輕輕遞到我們手中的暗號：與葉忒羅和好，就是與西坡拉和好，也就是與彌賽亞和好。 The rabbi takes us deeper into the text. Jethro brings with him Moses' wife Tzipporah (צִפֹּרָה) — and when you rearrange the letters of her name, the name “Poretz” (פֶּרֶץ, “the one who breaks through”) emerges, one of the names of the Messiah (from “uparatzta,” “you shall break forth and spread out”). It is like a clue pressed quietly into our hands: to be reconciled with Jethro is to be reconciled with Tzipporah, which is to be reconciled with the Messiah.

然後是這段教導的高峰。出埃及記 18:7 說，當摩西出去迎接岳父，「他們彼此問安」（וַיִּשְׁאֲלוּ אִישׁ לְרֵעֵהוּ，Vayishalu ish l'reuehu）——他們像弟兄一樣彼此問候平安。拉比要我們看每個詞的首字母： Then comes the peak of the teaching. Exodus 18:7 says that when Moses went out to meet his father-in-law, “they asked after one another's welfare” (וַיִּשְׁאֲלוּ אִישׁ לְרֵעֵהוּ, Vayishalu ish l'reuehu) — they greeted each other in peace, as brothers. The rabbi asks us to look at the first letter of each word:

וַיִּשְׁאֲלוּ (Vav) · אִישׁ (Aleph) · לְרֵעֵהוּ (Lamed) → א־ל־ו־ל ＝ אֱלוּל（Elul） וַיִּשְׁאֲלוּ (Vav) · אִישׁ (Aleph) · לְרֵעֵהוּ (Lamed) → spelling א־ל־ו־ל ＝ אֱלוּל (Elul)

「彼此問安」這個動作裡，藏著「以祿月」（Elul）的名字——就是悔罪、預備、轉向神的那個月份。拉比們說，摩西與葉忒羅之間的關係，正是以祿月、高聖日與 Geulah 的原型。換句話說：真正的悔罪之月，密碼就寫在「兩個曾經分裂的人重新彼此問安」這一刻裡。 In the very act of “asking after one another's welfare,” the name of the month Elul is hidden — the month of repentance, preparation, and turning back to God. The sages say that the relationship between Moses and Jethro is the prototype of Elul, of the High Holy Days, and of the Geulah. In other words: the true month of penitence has its code written into the moment when two who were once divided greet one another in peace again.

五、給今夜的兩個原則 ── 「他仍是你的弟兄」與 Lech L'ShalomV. Two Principles for Tonight — “He Is Still Your Brother” and Lech L'Shalom

拉比要我們把兩個原則帶進今夜的悔罪之禱裡。第一個原則，藏在「ish l'reuehu」（為弟兄著想）這幾個字裡：那些傷害過你、出賣過你、在背後說你壞話、使你痛苦的人——他們仍然是你的弟兄。你或許會說「我不要這樣的弟兄」。但這正是那把鑰匙：看著那些辜負你的人，改變自己的心思，承認他們仍是你的弟兄。葉忒羅曾保守摩西的妻子西坡拉——這裡面有極深的情感糾結，但他依然是「第一號的弟兄」。要在祭壇前獻上你的禮物、與弟兄和好，先記得：他仍是你的弟兄。 The rabbi asks us to carry two principles into tonight's Slichot. The first is hidden in “ish l'reuehu” (to care for the brother): those who have wounded you, betrayed you, spoken evil of you, caused you pain — they are still your brothers. You may say, “I do not want brothers like that.” But that is the key: to look at the people who have failed you and change your mind, acknowledging that they remain your brothers. Jethro had kept Moses' wife Tzipporah — there is a great tangle of emotion here — yet he is still “brother number one.” Before you leave your gift at the altar and reconcile with your brother, remember first: he is still your brother.

第二個原則，藏在他們對話的措辭裡。經文用得極其刻意：他們是「le-shalom」地交談，而不是「be-shalom」。「Be-shalom」意味著一段政治正確、客客氣氣的對話——禮貌、和氣，卻僅止於此。但「le-shalom」（לְשָׁלוֹם）的意思是「朝向平安、趨向完全」。Shalom 的根是 shalem（שָׁלֵם，「完整、圓滿」）。所以這段對話的方向是什麼？Le-shalom——朝向另一個人的完整。 The second principle is hidden in the wording of their exchange. The text is deliberate: they spoke “le-shalom,” not “be-shalom.” “Be-shalom” would mean a politically correct, cordial conversation — polite, amicable, and no more. But “le-shalom” (לְשָׁלוֹם) means “toward peace, toward wholeness.” The root of shalom is shalem (שָׁלֵם, “complete, whole”). So where is this conversation headed? Le-shalom — toward the wholeness of the other person.

拉比把這層意思說透：你可以與某人進行一場艱難的對話，而你說的每一句話，目的都是為了使對方得益處，而不是為了自己。如果我做這場和好是為了我自己、而不是為了對方，那麼結果連我自己也得不到平安，對方也得不到。這就是今夜給我們的那個詞——Le-shalom。而他們最後彼此說的是什麼？「Lech le-shalom」——「你如今圓滿了，你自由了，去吧，往平安裡去。」 The rabbi makes it plain: you can have a hard conversation with someone in which everything you say is aimed at that person's benefit, not your own. If I do this reconciliation for myself rather than for the other, then there is no shalom — not for me, and not for the other. This is the word for us tonight — Le-shalom. And what did they finally say to one another? “Lech le-shalom” — “You are now full, now whole; you are free. Go — go toward peace.”

六、閉幕的呼召 ── 釋放、和好、迎接彌賽亞VI. The Closing Charge — Release, Reconcile, Welcome the Messiah

拉比把這場全國性、甚至國際性的和好，放在他自己的見證裡：「今年，是我三十年事工中受傷最深的一年。」但他說——Lech le-shalom。對那傷害你的人說：「我釋放你，我赦免你，讓我們一同尋見圓滿。」這很難，卻正是悔罪服事在這一夜要在我們身上成就的事。 The rabbi sets this national — even international — reconciliation inside his own testimony: “This year I have been more wounded than ever before in thirty years of ministry.” And yet, he says — Lech le-shalom. Say to the one who hurt you: “I release you, I set you free; let us find wholeness together.” It is hard. But it is precisely what the Slichot service is meant to work in us this night.

於是悔罪之禱與閉幕的祝福在這裡合而為一。會眾已經認了罪，已經呼求了憐憫（רַחֲמִים rachamim），已經吟誦了十三屬性（שְׁלֹשׁ עֶשְׂרֵה מִדּוֹת）。如今差遣他們進入夜色的祝福（בְּרָכָה berakhah），不是一句空話，而是一份託付：帶著你今夜所領受的圓滿，走向那曾與你為敵的人，重新使他作你的弟兄。因為這——按整個午夜服事的見證——正是迎接耶書亞回來、加速 Geulah 的道路。 And so the Slichot prayers and the closing blessing become one here. The congregation has confessed; it has cried out for mercy (רַחֲמִים rachamim); it has chanted the Thirteen Attributes (שְׁלֹשׁ עֶשְׂרֵה מִדּוֹת). Now the blessing (בְּרָכָה berakhah) that sends them into the night is not an empty word but a commission: take the wholeness you received tonight and walk toward the one who was your enemy, and make him your brother again. For this — by the witness of the whole Midnight service — is the very road by which we welcome Yeshua back and hasten the Geulah.

「去吧，往平安裡去。你今夜已蒙潔淨、已蒙釋放。找到那個人——重新使他作你的弟兄；與他同行，朝向圓滿（Le-shalom）。如此，你就在迎接彌賽亞回來。願今夜開始的和好，加速救贖的來臨。阿們。」 “Lech le-shalom — go toward peace. Tonight you are cleansed; tonight you are set free. Find that person — make him your brother again; walk with him toward wholeness (le-shalom). In doing so, you are welcoming the Messiah back. May the reconciliation begun this night hasten the redemption. Amen.”
