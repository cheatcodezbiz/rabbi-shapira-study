開場 ── 西班牙語世界與那呼召 Opening — The Spanish-Speaking World and the Call

午夜的悔罪服事以西班牙語開場，把整個西語世界迎進這場全球的聚集。שׁוֹפָר 角聲吹響一百次；呼召發出去了——還有盼望，世界正被聚攏，猶太人與外邦人在君王面前合而為一。 The Midnight Slichot service opens in Spanish, welcoming the Spanish-speaking world into a worldwide gathering. The שׁוֹפָר shofar is sounded one hundred times; the call goes out — there is hope, the world is being drawn together, Jews and Gentiles as one before the King.

「各位，平安，晚安。我是沙皮拉拉比，與我同在的是 Giovanni Burgos 摩雷（Moré，教師）——這是一場非常特別的服事，名為 סְלִיחוֹת Selichot（悔罪祈禱）的午夜服事。」就在這句話裡，整個夜晚被定了調。今夜，西語世界與英語世界被刻意併在一起；兩間 yeshivot（經學院）、兩個頻道——Ahavat Ami 英語與 Ahavat Ami 西語——合而為一。因為這一夜太聖了，不容分割。 “Good evening, everyone — peace, and good night. I am Rabbi Shapira, with the Moré (teacher) Giovanni Burgos, for a very special service: the midnight service of סְלִיחוֹת Selichot, the prayers of penitence.” In that one sentence the whole night is set. Tonight the Spanish-speaking world and the English-speaking world are deliberately joined; two yeshivot, two channels — Ahavat Ami English and Ahavat Ami Spanish — are made one. Because this night is too holy to keep apart.

一、把兩個世界合在一起I. Joining the Two Worlds

拉比一開口就說清楚我們身在何處：「你若留意，我們此刻同時在西語世界與英語世界之中。你們許多人是在 Ahavat Ami 英語 頻道上觀看的，但我要你們知道，我們另有一個獨立的頻道，就是 Ahavat Ami 西語。」正因今夜這聖夜的份量重大，他們決定把兩間經學院、兩個頻道，為這一夜聚在一處。 The rabbi opens by telling us exactly where we are: “If you notice, we are right now in both the Spanish-speaking world and the English-speaking world. Many of you are watching us on Ahavat Ami English, but I want you to know that we also have a separate channel, Ahavat Ami Spanish.” Because of the weight of this holy night, they have decided to bring the two yeshivot and the two channels together for this one night.

他特別停下來，向那些不會西語、不會英語、也不會希伯來語的人說話：「即便你三種語言一句都不懂，你依然可以默想這一切。」這正是整場服事的精神——這不是一場只屬於某一語言、某一群人的聚會，而是一個敞開的門。今夜，西語世界被迎進來。 He pauses deliberately to speak to those who know neither Spanish, nor English, nor Hebrew: “Even so, you can still meditate on all of this.” That is the spirit of the whole service — this is not a gathering belonging to one language or one people, but an open door. Tonight, the Spanish-speaking world is welcomed in.

他也指向那份文獻：《一百道門，一百支角》(Cien Puertas, Cien Shofarot)——一部為 רֹאשׁ הַשָּׁנָה Rosh Hashanah（新年）所有禮儀部分、為 seder（次序）、也為一路通往 Yom HaKippurim（贖罪日）的 Selichot 服事而預備的重要出版物，有英文與西班牙文兩種版本，可在 shofar.shubu.tv 上找到數位版或印刷版。 He also points to the text: “One Hundred Gates, One Hundred Shofarot” (Cien Puertas, Cien Shofarot) — an important publication for every liturgical part of רֹאשׁ הַשָּׁנָה Rosh Hashanah, for the seder (order), and for the Selichot service that carries us all the way to Yom HaKippurim (the Day of Atonement). It exists in both English and Spanish, in digital and print form, at shofar.shubu.tv.

二、同一個信息 ── 不分種族II. One Message — No Race, No Color

他直白地說出今年合併兩場服事的理由：「我們的心願是吸引每一個人，來聽同一個信息，說同樣的話，作同樣的悔改。」接著是那句貫穿全夜的核心： He states plainly why the two services were merged this year: “Our desire is to draw everyone, to hear the same message, to say the same words, and to make the same repentance.” Then comes the line that runs through the whole night:

「這不是種族、膚色或族裔的事——這一切都是關乎這一夜所代表的『聖』。我們站在那聖潔可稱頌者面前，說：求祢赦免我們、賜我們贖罪、遮蓋我們一切的罪。」——沙皮拉拉比，開場 “This is not a matter of race, of color, of ethnicity — it is all about the holiness this night represents. We stand before the Holy One, blessed be He, and we say: forgive us, grant us atonement, cover all our sins.”— Rabbi Shapira, opening

他糾正一個常見的誤解：以為 Yom HaKippurim 那天才是我們悔改的時候。不。悔改要更早就開始，趁還有時間，在贖罪日之前。「今天，」他說，「是一個全世界的悔改之日。」這正是 תְּשׁוּבָה teshuvah（回轉、悔改）的精神：它不是日曆上的一個點，而是一條一路走向贖罪日的道路。 He corrects a common misunderstanding: that Yom HaKippurim is the day we repent. No. Repentance begins earlier, while there is still time, before the Day of Atonement. “Today,” he says, “is a worldwide day of repentance.” This is the spirit of תְּשׁוּבָה teshuvah (turning, repentance): not a single point on the calendar, but a road that leads all the way to Yom HaKippurim.

三、午夜 ── 黑暗中仍有盼望III. Midnight — Hope in the Darkness

他不掩飾這是艱難的一週。有人因以色列之神的名而被殺害。他多年來一直在說，遵行誡命的窗口正一次比一次變小，流亡之痛(el dolor del exilio) 正越來越真實。「若你感受不到這流亡之痛，」他說，「你大概就還在這些日子所代表的一切之外。」 He does not hide that it has been a hard week. People have been murdered in the name of the God of Israel. For years, he says, he has watched the window for keeping a commandment grow narrower each time, and the pain of the exile (el dolor del exilio) grow more real. “If you do not feel this pain of exile,” he says, “you are probably still on the outside of everything these days represent.”

為什麼選在午夜？因為以色列的眾子幾乎是在午夜出埃及的；因為我們刻意在最黑暗的時刻舉行這場 Selichot 服事，正如《耶利米哀歌》所說。然而就在這黑暗裡，他的聲音抬高了： Why midnight? Because the children of Israel went out of Egypt practically at midnight; because we deliberately hold this Selichot service in the darkest hours, as the Book of Lamentations describes. And yet, precisely in that darkness, his voice rises:

「請專心聽我說：如果你今天在這裡，你還活著、還在呼吸——就有盼望。一秒都不要浪費，去成就這三件事：悔改、禱告、施捨（sedaca / צְדָקָה）。」——沙皮拉拉比 “Listen to me carefully: if you are here today, and you are alive, and you are breathing — there is hope. Do not lose a single second to fulfil these three things: repentance, prayer, and giving tzedakah (sedaca / צְדָקָה).”— Rabbi Shapira

他引用 Gordon 領唱者（Hazán）在 Rosh Hashanah 所唱的那句古老的話：「Teshuvah, Tzedakah u-Tefilah ma'avirin et ro'a ha-gezerah」——「悔改、施捨、禱告，能挪去那嚴厲判決的兇惡。」拉比說：「我們越過並使這判決變甜。」天上原有一道針對全人類的判決；但今夜，我們可以在以色列功德的天平上，加上一點重量。 He cites the ancient line the Hazán (cantor) Gordon sings at Rosh Hashanah: “Teshuvah, Tzedakah u-Tefilah ma'avirin et ro'a ha-gezerah” — “Repentance, charity, and prayer avert the severity of the decree.” The rabbi says: “We pass over and sweeten the decree.” There was a decree from heaven against all humanity; yet tonight, we can add a little weight onto the scale of merit for all Israel.

四、外邦人也能添上功德IV. The Gentile, Too, Can Add to the Merit

這裡他把一條保羅式的線索引進來：「保羅在《羅馬書》裡的關鍵就是——一個被接到以色列裡的人，可以加添以色列的功德。」當外邦人藉著那名為「豐滿」(la plenitud) 的過程，向以色列顯明自己時，他們就在加添以色列群體的功德。 Here he weaves in a Pauline thread: “Paul's key point in the book of Romans is that one who has been grafted into Israel can add to the merit of Israel.” When the Gentile shows himself toward Israel, through a process called “the fullness” (la plenitud), he is adding to the corporate merit of Israel.

所以這個問題不只是「我必須做什麼？」而是「為什麼？」為什麼我們呼召所有猶太人與外邦人一同來作這 Selichot 服事？「為要挪去那臨到以色列的判決，挪去那壓在以色列身上的重擔。」他見證自己這一週站在基督徒面前，作見證談到 Shevarim 之聲——那破碎、被擊打之聲，是我們行走在通往 Rosh Hashanah 的路上時，當在永恆者面前持守的。 So the question is not only “What must I do?” but “Why?” Why do we call all Jews and Gentiles to make this Selichot service together? “To lift off the decree against Israel, to lift off what weighs upon Israel.” He testifies that this week he stood before Christians, bearing witness to the sound of the Shevarim — the broken, shattered sound we are to carry before the Eternal One as we march on the road toward Rosh Hashanah.

他也把這呼召落到極實際的地步：站在裂口中為以色列代求。就在這個安息日，又有一起恐怖攻擊；這一週以十人遇害開始，週五早晨又是一場兇殺。我們也當說：「主啊，求祢赦免我們的行為——我們的行為可能拖延了彌賽亞的到來。」他懇求眾人有信心，慷慨地施捨（透過 PayPal 或「以色列行動」基金），「為了錫安的好處」。 He brings the call down to the most practical level: stand in the breach for Israel. On this very Shabbat, another terror attack; the week began with ten people killed, and Friday morning brought yet another murder. We too must say: “Lord, forgive us for our conduct — our conduct can delay the coming of the Messiah.” He pleads with everyone to have faith and to give generously (through PayPal or the “Operation Israel” fund), “for the good of Zion.”

五、不是兩個，乃是一個V. Not Two, But One

服事即將正式進入 Selichot 之前，拉比把那合一說成一句祈願。他用他帶口音、半西半英的話語，幾乎像在禱告：「No te zavata mi inglés, zavate mi español... javate a mi español y javata a mi inglés」——願祂悅納我的英語，也悅納我的西語；不是作為彼此分開的個體，而是作為一個整體。「我們站在永恆者面前。」 Just before the service formally enters Selichot, the rabbi turns that unity into a prayer. In his accented, half-Spanish, half-English speech, almost as if praying: “No te zavata mi inglés, zavate mi español… javate a mi español y javata a mi inglés” — may He receive my English and receive my Spanish; not as separate beings, but as one single body. “We stand before the Eternal One.”

於是讚美升起，雙語交織：「Gloria a Dios — Gloria a Deus」（榮耀歸神）。Miriam 牧者被致謝，會眾被問候——「今晚大家都好嗎？這敬拜何等美！這夜何等奇妙！」這場服事不只在傳遞教義；它在把世界聚攏，把每一個語言的人帶到同一位君王面前。 And so praise rises, bilingual and interwoven: “Gloria a Dios — Gloria a Deus” (Glory to God). Pastora Miriam is thanked, the congregation greeted — “How is everyone doing tonight? What beauty of worship! What an incredible night!” This service is not merely delivering doctrine; it is drawing the world together, bringing people of every language before the one King.

這也正是「一百道門，一百支角」這部文獻所要承載的異象：一份信息，以英文與西文同時印出，向不同語言、不同膚色、不同族裔的人發出同一個呼召。今夜的合併——兩間經學院、兩個頻道——本身就是這份異象的活畫面。沒有人被排在門外；連那聽不懂任何一種語言的人，也被邀請「默想這一切」。這場聚集的形狀，本身就是它的信息。 This is exactly the vision the text “One Hundred Gates, One Hundred Shofarot” is meant to carry: one message, printed in English and Spanish at once, sounding a single call to people of different languages, different colors, different ethnicities. Tonight's merging — two yeshivot, two channels — is itself a living picture of that vision. No one is left outside the door; even the one who understands none of the languages is invited to “meditate on all of this.” The very shape of this gathering is its message.

六、那角聲 ── 一百次的呼召VI. The Shofar — The Call Sounded a Hundred Times

神有祂自己的日曆——祂自己的日子、自己的時候。Giovanni 摩雷提醒會眾：以色列的眾子出埃及之後，開始聽見那些「奇異」的吩咐——在這日守逾越節，在那日吹號，在另一日搭棚。我們必須明白這些日子。而就在八天後的那個週一，是聖經日曆上最重要的日子之一——《利未記》23 章所稱的「紀念的日子」：「七月初一，要守為紀念的日子。」 God has His own calendar — His own days, His own appointed times. Moré Giovanni reminds the congregation: after the children of Israel came out of Egypt, they began to hear those “strange” commands — on this day keep the Passover, on that day blow the trumpets, on another day build tabernacles. We must understand these days. And eight days from now, on that Monday, comes one of the most important days on the biblical calendar — what Leviticus 23 calls “the Day of Remembrance”: “In the seventh month, on the first day, it shall be a day of remembrance.”

在紀念什麼？紀念君王要來了。在這節期之前的三十天，我們宣告：「El Rey está en el campo」——「君王在田間。」然後到了那一天，我們吹 שׁוֹפָר shofar 角一百次。有一個日子，撒但會困惑——就是吹角節（Rosh Hashanah）。每一次你聽見角聲，我們就是在向諸天提醒那已成就的代贖——亞伯拉罕上摩利亞山、神預備了那隻公羊作替代（公羊角的 shofar），而那也是另一位兒子的影兒，祂也作為替代來到世界。「我有一個好消息告訴你，」他說，「祂的名字是 Yeshua。」 Remembering what? That the King is coming. For thirty days before the feast we proclaim: “El Rey está en el campo” — “The King is in the field.” Then, on that day, we sound the שׁוֹפָר shofar one hundred times. There is one day on which Satan is confused — the Feast of Trumpets (Rosh Hashanah). Every time you hear the shofar, we are reminding the heavens of the substitution already made: Abraham went up Mount Moriah, and God provided the ram as a substitute (the ram's-horn shofar) — which is itself a figure of another Son who also came into the world as a substitute. “I have news for you,” he says, “His name is Yeshua.”

為什麼是一百次？因為「一百道門正在開啟」——那些不屬於這世界、屬於其他世界的眾門戶。有一個日子，神說：「我正在聽你，我正在留意你——你想對我說什麼？」而有三件事你當說，由 shofar 的三個聲音所托出。第一個聲音是 tequia（תְּקִיעָה Tekiah），一個悠長的聲音：「閉上眼睛，聽那角聲——這聲音在說：來，歸正、列隊，君王要來了。」第二個聲音是 שְׁבָרִים Shevarim，三個斷裂的聲音——「請閉上你們的眼睛，我們來看那靈所要說的。」服事，就在這破碎之聲中，正式開始。 Why a hundred? Because “one hundred gates are opening” — the portals that are outside this world, that belong to other worlds. There is a day on which God says: “I am listening to you, I am paying attention to you — what do you want to tell Me?” And there are three things you should say, carried by the three sounds of the shofar. The first is tequia (תְּקִיעָה Tekiah), one long blast: “Close your eyes and hear the shofar — this sound is saying: come to order, fall into line, the King is coming.” The second is שְׁבָרִים Shevarim, three broken sounds — “Please close your eyes, and let us see what the Spirit says.” And there, on that broken sound, the service formally begins.

「請專心聽我說：如果你今天還活著、還在呼吸——就有盼望。Teshuvah、Tefilah、Tzedakah 能使那判決變甜。我們不是各自分開的個體，乃是合而為一，同站在永恆者面前。君王在田間——El Rey está en el campo——而那角要吹響一百次。」 “Listen carefully: if you are alive today and breathing — there is hope. Teshuvah, Tefilah, and Tzedakah can sweeten the decree. We are not separate beings, but one — standing together before the Eternal One. The King is in the field — El Rey está en el campo — and the shofar shall sound a hundred times.”
