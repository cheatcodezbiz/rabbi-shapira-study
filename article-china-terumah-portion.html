给中国的「举献物」── 会幕、洁净的心、与弥赛亚的妥拉 The Terumah Portion for China — Tabernacle, a Purified Heart, and the Torah of Messiah

在沙皮拉拉比给中国弟兄姊妹的简短安息日寄语中，他把Terumah（举献物）篇的每一项金、银、铜，与一种具体的罪连起来，并指出：神建造会幕，是要从内里洁净人的心，最终成就「弥赛亚的妥拉」——彼此担当重担。 In a brief Shabbat message addressed to brothers and sisters in China, Rabbi Shapira ties each element of Terumah (the offering portion) — gold, silver, bronze — to a specific sin, then unfolds the conclusion: God built the Tabernacle to cleanse the heart from the inside, and the goal of it all is “the Torah of the Messiah” — bearing one another's burdens.

沙皮拉拉比博士 · Ahavat Ammi 事工 Rabbi Dr. Itzhak Shapira · Ahavat Ammi Ministries

这段不到六分钟的安息日寄语开始得很温柔——「Shalom, Shuvu China，亲爱的我们在中国内的弟兄姊妹们」——但很快就钻入Terumah（תרומה，「举献物」）篇的核心：会幕里每一样金、银、铜，都不是装饰，而是用来对付一种具体的罪。拉比把这个观察推向一句必须被慢慢嚼的话：「我们的行为是非常重要。但我们的心要从里面被洁净，行为只是心的结果。」他用《加拉太书》六章二节作结：「你们各人的重担要互相担当，如此就完全了基督的律法。」拉比说：「这就是弥赛亚的妥拉 (Torah of Mashiach)。」 This six-minute Shabbat message opens gently — “Shalom, Shuvu China — dear brothers and sisters inside China” — but quickly drives to the heart of Parashat Terumah (תרומה, “heave-offering”): every gold, silver, and bronze element in the Tabernacle is not décor but a remedy for a specific sin. From there the rabbi gives a sentence that has to be chewed slowly: “Actions are important. But our hearts have to be purified from inside. Action is just the result of the heart.” He closes with Galatians 6:2 — “Bear one another's burdens, and so fulfill the law of Christ” — and tells the Chinese community: “This is the Torah of Mashiach.”

一、Shalom, Shuvu China ── 这个问安本身就是预言I. Shalom, Shuvu China — The Greeting Is Itself a Prophecy

拉比沙皮拉用一句希伯来文开口：「Shalom, Shuvu China.」这不只是一句招呼。Shalom（שלום）不是「没有冲突」——它是「完整、平安、丰盛」。Shuvu（שובו）是「归回」、「悔改」的众数命令式——它是先知何西阿对以色列说的那个词：「以色列啊，你要归回（shuvu）耶和华你的神。」（何西阿书 14:1）。 The rabbi opens in Hebrew: “Shalom, Shuvu China.” This is not a casual greeting. Shalom (שלום) is not “absence of conflict” — it is wholeness, peace, abundance. Shuvu (שובו) is the plural imperative “return!” / “repent!” — the very word the prophet Hosea spoke to Israel: “Return (shuvu), O Israel, to the Lord your God” (Hosea 14:1).

把这两个词放在中国名字前面——「Shalom, Shuvu China」——拉比其实在做一件先知性的事：他把先知何西阿对以色列说过的呼召，同样的话，套在中国头上。归回不是地理动作，是心的动作。中国不需要「成为犹太人」——但中国需要归回那一位与亚伯拉罕立约的神。 By placing these two words before the name “China” — “Shalom, Shuvu China” — the rabbi is doing something prophetic: he is laying onto China the same call the prophet Hosea once placed on Israel. Returning is not a geographical action; it is a movement of the heart. China does not need to “become Jewish” — but China is being called to return to the God who covenanted with Abraham.

拉比顺势引用诗篇 133:1：「Hineh mah tov u'mah na'im shevet achim gam yachad」（הנה מה טוב ומה נעים שבת אחים גם יחד，「看哪，弟兄和睦同居是何等的善，何等的美。」）。他注意到一件事：神工正同时在韩国（济州岛）、在中国大陆运行，「丝绸之路」正在重新被神接通——这一次不是为了丝与瓷，而是为了「合一」(yachad, יחד)。 He naturally moves into Psalm 133:1: “Hineh mah tov u'mah na'im shevet achim gam yachad” (הנה מה טוב ומה נעים שבת אחים גם יחד, “Behold, how good and pleasant it is for brothers to dwell together in unity”). He notes something striking: God is moving simultaneously in Korea (Jeju Island) and in mainland China; the Silk Road is being reconnected — this time not for silk or porcelain, but for “yachad” (יחד, “togetherness”).

二、Terumah ── 每一样元素对付一种罪II. Terumah — Every Element Answers a Specific Sin

本周的妥拉份额是Terumah（תרומה，「举献物」/「奉献」），出埃及记 25–27 章。神命令以色列建造会幕（Mishkan，משכן，「居住之处」）。所列的材料极为具体：金 (zahav, זהב)、银 (kesef, כסף)、铜 (nechoshet, נחושת)、紫色、蓝色、朱红色线、细麻、山羊毛、皂荚木、橄榄油、香料。 This week's portion is Terumah (תרומה, “heave-offering” / “contribution”) — Exodus 25–27. God commands Israel to build the Tabernacle (Mishkan, משכן, “dwelling place”). The materials are specified with striking detail: gold (zahav, זהב), silver (kesef, כסף), bronze (nechoshet, נחושת), purple, blue, scarlet, fine linen, goat hair, acacia wood, olive oil, spices.

「那么在这里面的要素里面的，它是完全跟罪有关系的。慢慢来，慢慢来哈。那么这个是一点一点是跟我们有这个心有关。」——沙皮拉拉比博士 “Each element had to deal with a specific type of sin that they were having. Slowly, slowly. He cleansed their heart.”— Rabbi Dr. Itzhak Shapira

拉比借助一个古老的米德拉什 (midrash, מדרש) 传统：会幕材料对应着以色列在旷野所犯的罪。金对应金牛犊（出埃及记 32 章）——以贪婪和偶像崇拜被试炼过的心，要把同样的金子献回给神。银对应人头税里那「半舍客勒」(machatzit ha-shekel, מחצית השקל) 的救赎银，它对付「自己对神和邻舍漠不关心」的罪。铜对应铜祭坛与铜洗濯盆，对付「言语」和「行为」上的不洁。 The rabbi draws on an old midrash (מדרש) tradition: each Tabernacle material corresponds to a sin Israel committed in the wilderness. Gold answers the golden calf (Exodus 32) — the heart that was tested in greed and idolatry is asked to give that very gold back to God. Silver answers the half-shekel (machatzit ha-shekel, מחצית השקל) of redemption — the remedy for indifference toward God and toward one's neighbor. Bronze, mirroring the bronze altar and laver, addresses uncleanness of speech and deed.

拉比的话很耐心——他重复了两次「慢慢来，慢慢来」(l'at, l'at)：洁净不是一次完成的。神并没有用一句话把以色列的心立刻变干净；他用了一整本《利未记》、一整张材料清单、一整套献祭体系、四十年的旷野——「一点一点」地，把心洁净。 The rabbi's tone is deliberately patient — he repeats twice, “slowly, slowly” (l'at, l'at): purification is not instantaneous. God did not cleanse Israel's heart with a single sentence; He used the whole book of Leviticus, the full materials list, the entire sacrificial system, and forty wilderness years — “little by little” — to cleanse the heart.

三、心是源头，行为是结果III. The Heart Is the Wellspring; Action Is the Result

拉比由此推出整段教导的关键句。请把它在心里读慢一些： From there the rabbi reaches the pivotal sentence of the entire teaching. Read it slowly:

「我们的行为是非常重要。但我们的心是更要倾向于要成为更圣洁和纯洁，因为我们的从内心要决定出我们的行为。」——沙皮拉拉比博士 “Actions are important. But our hearts must be purified from inside — and the action is just the result of the heart.”— Rabbi Dr. Itzhak Shapira

这是一个完整的犹太-弥赛亚教义。先知耶利米早就预言：「日子将到，我要与以色列家另立新约，…我要将我的律法放在他们里面，写在他们心上。」（耶利米书 31:31–33）大卫王在诗篇五十一篇恳求：「神啊，求你为我造清洁的心。」主耶书亚 (Yeshua) 也说：「人心里所充满的，口里就说出来。」（马太福音 12:34） This is a fully Jewish-Messianic doctrine. Jeremiah prophesied: “Behold, the days are coming — I will make a new covenant with the house of Israel… I will put my law within them, and I will write it on their hearts” (Jeremiah 31:31–33). David prays in Psalm 51: “Create in me a clean heart, O God.” And our Lord Yeshua Himself: “Out of the abundance of the heart the mouth speaks” (Matthew 12:34).

拉比此处的修正是温柔但锋利的：在一个对「外在虔诚」过度热情的群体里——「我们有很多人是想要快快学希伯来文、学妥拉、学一切」——他提醒大家：学是好的，但学只是结果；先求心被洁净，再求知识丰满。否则，知识不会嫁接到树上，它会浮在外面。 The rabbi's corrective here is gentle but pointed. In a circle that's enthusiastic about external observance — “many of us are eager, eager to learn Torah, to learn Hebrew, to learn everything” — he reminds them: study is good, but study is only the result; first ask for the heart to be cleansed, then ask for knowledge to be filled. Otherwise, knowledge will not graft into the tree; it will only float on the surface.

四、彼此担当重担 ── 弥赛亚的妥拉IV. Bear One Another's Burdens — The Torah of Mashiach

心被洁净之后，必然结出一种果子。拉比引用使徒保罗在《加拉太书》六章二节的话作为这种果子的标记： After the heart is cleansed, a particular fruit must follow. The rabbi takes the apostle Paul's word in Galatians 6:2 as the mark of that fruit:

「你们各人的重担要互相担当，如此就完全了基督的律法。」——加拉太书 6:2 “Bear one another's burdens, and so fulfill the law of Christ.”— Galatians 6:2

希腊原文 τὸν νόμον τοῦ Χριστοῦ（「基督的律法」）在希伯来-弥赛亚传统中被读作 Torat HaMashiach（תורת המשיח，「弥赛亚的妥拉」）。拉比直接把这句经文与Terumah（举献物）连起来：你献什么给神？最高的「举献物」并不是金子，也不是银子——它是你愿意把别人的重担提到自己肩上的那个心。 The Greek τὸν νόμον τοῦ Χριστοῦ (“the law of Christ”) is read in the Hebrew-Messianic tradition as Torat HaMashiach (תורת המשיח, “the Torah of the Messiah”). The rabbi directly ties this verse to Terumah (the offering): what do you bring to God? The highest terumah is not gold and not silver — it is a heart willing to lift another's burden onto your own shoulder.

「要要要要承担彼此的这个重担。那么这个就是说，弥赛亚的妥拉 (Torah of Mashiach)。」——沙皮拉拉比博士 “Bear each other's burdens. Take each other's burdens. Yes — this is the Torah of Mashiach.”— Rabbi Dr. Itzhak Shapira

请注意拉比的步骤：从Terumah（外在的金属）到洁净的心（内在的状态），再到担当重担（外在的行为）——内外之间，由「心」做总开关。这正是「Mishkan」（מלשכן，居住之处）的真正意义：神不再只住在一个木板与金箔搭起来的帐幕里；他要住在一群心被洁净、彼此担当重担的人中间。 Notice the rabbi's sequence: from Terumah (the outward metals) → to a purified heart (the inward state) → to bearing burdens (the renewed outward action) — with the heart as the master switch between inside and outside. This is the true meaning of “Mishkan” (משכן, “dwelling place”): God no longer needs a tent of acacia boards and gold leaf; He intends to dwell in a community whose hearts are clean and who carry each other's burdens.

五、关于分辨 & 给中国弟兄姊妹的呼召V. A Word on Discernment & The Call to China

简短的分辨：「会幕的每一样元素对应一种具体的罪」这一对应——金对应金牛犊、银对应漠然、铜对应不洁——是经典的drash（דרש，讲道层次的解经），不是字面的p'shat（פשט）。这种解读是拉比传统两千年来的宝贵财富，但它是属灵的应用，不是字面的「这就是经文明说的」。把它当作智慧的应用来接受，不要把它误当作字面意义的字典。 A short word on discernment. The point-for-point correspondence — gold for the golden calf, silver for indifference, bronze for uncleanness — is classic drash (דרש, the homiletical layer), not literal p'shat (פשט). This kind of reading is a precious treasure of two millennia of rabbinic tradition, but it is a spiritual application, not a literal dictionary entry. Receive it as wisdom-shaped application, not as the verse's plain sense.

给中国弟兄姊妹的呼召很清楚，也很温柔——它有三层：

第一，归回 (Shuvu)。把心从偶像 (无论是金牛犊式的财利、还是知识虚荣) 里拿出来，归给那位立约的神。
第二，洁净 (慢慢来)。让神「一点一点」地工作；不要追求一次完成的虔诚，要追求逐日被磨亮的心。
第三，担当 (Torah of Mashiach)。把另一位中国弟兄姊妹的重担提到自己的肩上——为他的家庭祷告，为他的工作奉献，为他的孩子陪读。这就是「弥赛亚的妥拉」在中国本土最朴素的活法。 The call to China is clear and gentle, in three layers:

First, return (Shuvu). Lift the heart out of its idols — whether the calf of profit or the calf of intellectual vanity — and return it to the covenanting God.
Second, be cleansed (slowly, slowly). Let God work “little by little”; pursue not a one-shot piety but a heart that is polished day by day.
Third, carry (the Torah of Mashiach). Lift another Chinese brother or sister's burden onto your own shoulder — pray for his family, give for his work, sit beside his child as she studies. This is the simplest, most local expression of the Torah of Mashiach.

拉比在结束时说了一句让人心疼的话——一句不是教义、却是父亲心肠的话：「我想你们。我希望我要再回到那里地方。」一个犹太拉比，对中国家庭教会的弟兄姊妹，说「我想你们」。这本身就是会幕的图景——神不只是要被敬拜，他要与他的百姓住在一起。 He closes with a line that pierces — not doctrine but a father's heart: “I miss you. I can't wait to come back.” A Jewish rabbi saying to brothers and sisters in China's house churches, “I miss you.” That is itself the Tabernacle's whole point — God does not merely want to be worshipped; He wants to dwell with His people.

「要继续让我们鼓励彼此，承担彼此的重担——这就是弥赛亚的妥拉 (Torah of Mashiach)。我想你们。我希望再回到那里。要帮助彼此。Shabbat Shalom。」——沙皮拉拉比博士 “Continue to encourage one another. Bear each other's burdens — this is the Torah of Mashiach. I miss you. I can't wait to come back. Help each other. Shabbat Shalom.”— Rabbi Dr. Itzhak Shapira
