全球的希望呼召：在彌賽亞時代的合一與救贖 The Global Call for Hope: Unity and Redemption in the Messianic Age

在長達六小時十七分鐘的「新婦之夜 5786」(Milchemet HaChatunah，新婦之戰) 全球直播尾聲，來自巴塞隆納的摩西斯牧師以西班牙語領導全世界進入合一的禱告——為奮力存活的全球餘民，為以色列的合一，為新的節期 נוּסָח nusach（旋律設定），為即將回來的彌賽亞王。 As the six-hour-seventeen-minute global broadcast of “Night of the Bride 5786” (Milchemet HaChatunah, the War of the Wedding) drew to its close, Pastor Moises from Barcelona — teaching in español — led the world into the prayer of unity: for the global remnant clinging to hope, for the unity of Israel, for a new festival נוּסָח nusach (melodic setting), and for the soon-returning King Messiah.

「Hermanos, oremos por la esperanza——弟兄們，讓我們為希望禱告。」當夜的全球直播已經進行了六個小時又十七分鐘，沙皮拉拉比、戈登 Chazan、與來自世界各地的歌者已經輪番上陣。輪到摩西斯牧師時，他帶著巴塞隆納的口音與西班牙語的溫度開口——他不是來「總結」這場 Milchemet HaChatunah（新婦之戰）的，他是來把整個世界收進一場合一的禱告裡：為非洲布圖的妥拉中心、為衣索匹亞的雷姆巴人 (Lemba)、為巴塞隆納那座「地表最反猶城市」中的彌賽亞會堂、為以色列國會 (Knesset) 與軍隊的合一、為節期帶來的新 nusach（旋律設定）——最終，為彌賽亞王大衛之子的回歸。這段七十四分鐘的閉幕，是整場新婦之夜的最後一個 Amen。 “Hermanos, oremos por la esperanza — brothers, let us pray for hope.” The global broadcast was already six hours and seventeen minutes deep. Rabbi Shapira, Chazan Gordon, and singers from across the world had each taken their turn. When it was Pastor Moises' turn, he opened with the warmth of Barcelona Spanish — not to “wrap up” the Milchemet HaChatunah (the War of the Wedding), but to gather the entire world into a single prayer of unity: for the Torah centre in Butu, Africa; for the Lemba people of Ethiopia; for the Messianic congregation in Barcelona, “the most antisemitic city on earth”; for the unity of Israel's Knesset and army; for a new nusach (melodic setting) for the festivals — and finally, for the return of Mashiach ben David, the King Messiah, Son of David. These seventy-four minutes are the last Amen of the entire Night of the Bride.

一、為什麼是西班牙語？為什麼是巴塞隆納？I. Why Spanish? Why Barcelona?

沙皮拉拉比在介紹摩西斯牧師時，幾乎是哽咽的：「請為摩西斯牧師與他的妻子珊德拉指名禱告。他們在世上最壞的地方。他們在巴塞隆納牧養一間彌賽亞會堂——這座城市可以說是地表上最反猶的城市。我們必須為他們禱告——禱告希望。」這場直播的「主流語言」是英文與希伯來文，但摩西斯牧師選擇用西班牙語結束整場節目，這本身就是一個神學的宣告：「彌賽亞的新婦不是一個語言、一個國族、一個地理區的事。她是全球的。」 When Rabbi Shapira introduced Pastor Moises, his voice was nearly catching: “Pray by name for Pastor Moises and his wife Sandra. They are in the worst place of all. They are shepherding a Messianic congregation in Barcelona — arguably the most antisemitic city on the face of the earth. We need to pray for them — pray for hope.” The “lingua franca” of this broadcast had been English and Hebrew, but Pastor Moises chose to close the program in español — and that choice was itself a theological declaration: “The Bride of the Messiah is not a matter of one language, one nation, one geography. She is global.”

巴塞隆納本身就是一個歷史的傷痕。1391 年的反猶大屠殺幾乎滅絕了當地的猶太社群；1492 年的驅逐令把剩下的猶太人逼離西班牙——這就是 Anusim（被迫者）的起源：那些表面上皈依、暗地裡仍守安息日的「隱藏猶太人」。今天，巴塞隆納的街道上仍能聽到反猶塗鴉、看到對以色列國旗的焚燒。摩西斯牧師與珊德拉師母在這樣的環境裡開設一間彌賽亞會堂——這本身就是 chutzpah（聖潔的勇氣），是對歷史的回應。 Barcelona itself is a historic wound. The 1391 anti-Jewish massacres nearly extinguished its Jewish community; the 1492 expulsion drove the remnant out of Spain — this is the origin of the Anusim (“the forced ones”), the “hidden Jews” who outwardly converted but secretly kept Shabbat. Today, antisemitic graffiti and burning Israeli flags are still visible on Barcelona's streets. For Pastor Moises and Sandra to plant a Messianic congregation in that very city is chutzpah — holy boldness, an act of historical reply.

當摩西斯牧師說「oremos por la esperanza」（讓我們為希望禱告）時，他指的不是抽象的「樂觀」，而是先知耶利米所說的、在最黑暗的時候神所應許的「終局的盼望」(耶 29:11)。「希望」這個詞，在巴塞隆納的反猶街道上，是一種武器。 When Pastor Moises said “oremos por la esperanza” (let us pray for hope), he did not mean an abstract “optimism.” He meant the “end-time hope” God promised through the prophet Jeremiah in the darkest hour (Jer 29:11). On the antisemitic streets of Barcelona, the word “hope” is a weapon.

二、全球餘民 ── 非洲、衣索匹亞、加泰隆尼亞II. The Global Remnant — Africa, Ethiopia, Catalonia

摩西斯牧師的禱告從巴塞隆納開始，但不停在那裡。他帶著直播中的全世界，一個區域、一個區域地呼求 Adonai（主）。「請對我說『Amen』，為非洲。」他點名布圖的妥拉中心 (Torah center in Butu)——這是中非的一間彌賽亞會堂，正在為超過五十名孩童（多數十二歲以下）提供每週的安息日教導。然後是衣索匹亞的雷姆巴人 (Lemba)——南非與辛巴威之間的一個非洲族群，DNA 檢測證實他們攜帶古老的猶太祭司支系 (Cohen Modal Haplotype) 的標記，他們自己的口傳歷史宣稱是古代以色列十二支派中「失落的支派」之一的後裔。 Pastor Moises' prayer began in Barcelona but did not stop there. With the entire global broadcast listening, he called upon Adonai (the Lord) region by region. “Say to me ‘Amen' for Africa.” He named the Torah centre in Butu — a Messianic congregation in central Africa now teaching the Shabbat to more than fifty children, the majority under the age of twelve. Then the Lemba people of southern Africa — a tribal community in Zimbabwe and South Africa whose DNA carries the markers of the ancient priestly Cohen Modal Haplotype, and whose oral history claims descent from one of the “lost tribes” of ancient Israel.

這份名單看似零散——加泰隆尼亞、布圖、雷姆巴——但摩西斯牧師正是要打破一種隱藏的偏見：救贖不是只發生在耶路撒冷與紐約之間。它正在每一個被遺忘的角落發生。在非洲農村的塵土中，有孩子在學妥拉；在被迫害的歐洲城市裡，有家庭在守安息日；在 DNA 中沉睡了千年的祭司血統，在今天正一點一點甦醒。「我們相信 $200,000 美金可以做成這件事。主啊，讓我們把它完成。」——這個具體的金額把「全球餘民」從一個概念變成了一個可衡量的、可加入的、可奉獻的事工。 This list seems scattered — Catalonia, Butu, Lemba — but Pastor Moises was deliberately breaking a hidden prejudice: redemption is not happening only between Jerusalem and New York. It is happening in every forgotten corner. In the dust of African villages, children are learning Torah; in persecuted European cities, families are keeping Shabbat; in DNA that has slept for a thousand years, priestly lineages are stirring awake. “We believe $200,000 can get this done. Lord, let's get it done.” — that concrete number transformed “the global remnant” from a concept into a measurable, joinable, fundable ministry.

這對華人信徒尤其有共鳴：我們也曾經是「失落的支派」嗎？我們也是「被遺忘的餘民」嗎？摩西斯牧師的禱告把每一個被神所知道、但被歷史所忽視的群體，放在同一個 Amen 裡。 This resonates especially with Chinese believers: are we also a “lost tribe”? Are we also a “forgotten remnant”? Pastor Moises' prayer places every community known by God yet overlooked by history inside the same Amen.

「我向他們宣告耶利米先知的話：『我給你們一個有指望的將來，在末後的日子。』」——摩西斯牧師，引耶利米書 29:11 “I speak upon them the prophet Jeremiah: ‘I am giving you a hopeful future in the last days.'”— Pastor Moises, citing Jeremiah 29:11

三、孩子們要興起作獅子III. The Children Shall Rise Up as Lions

摩西斯牧師在禱告非洲時，停留得最久的不是大人，而是孩子。「想像這間會堂——但超過百分之五十是十二歲以下的孩子。幾十個、又幾十個的孩子。這是神擺在我們面前的。願他們興起，作獅子。能不能為我聽見一個 Amen？為非洲的青年，今天說一個 Amen！」 When praying for Africa, Pastor Moises lingered longest not on the adults but on the children. “Imagine this synagogue — but more than fifty percent are kids under the age of twelve. Dozens and dozens of children. This is what God has placed before us. May they rise up as lions. Can you say Amen? Say Amen for the youth in Africa today!”

「獅子」這個意象在希伯來聖經中極具份量。創世記 49:9 中，雅各祝福猶大時說：「猶大是個小獅子。」啟示錄 5:5 稱彌賽亞為「猶大支派中的獅子」。當摩西斯牧師為非洲的孩子們禱告「願他們興起作獅子」時，他是在說：這些孩子是彌賽亞家族的繼承人，是猶大支派譜系的延伸，不是「被援助的對象」，而是「將要繼承王國的繼承人」。 The image of the lion carries enormous weight in the Hebrew Bible. In Genesis 49:9, Jacob blesses Judah: “Judah is a lion's whelp.” Revelation 5:5 names the Messiah “the Lion of the tribe of Judah.” When Pastor Moises prayed for the African children to “rise up as lions,” he was saying: these children are heirs of the Messianic family, branches of the Judah lineage — not “objects of charity” but “coming heirs of the Kingdom.”

這是一種對非洲教會的尊嚴神學。第三世界基督徒長期被西方福音派視為「需要被牧養」的對象。摩西斯牧師把這個論述翻轉過來——他們不是受助者，他們是未來的領袖。 This is a theology of dignity for the African church. Third-world Christians have long been treated by Western evangelicalism as “those who need to be pastored.” Pastor Moises flips that narrative — they are not recipients; they are the coming leaders.

對華人教會的應用：當我們想到「華人事工」時，我們是把華人信徒看作「需要被牧養的差傳對象」，還是看作「將要興起作獅子的下一代領袖」？神學決定了我們投資的方向。 Application for the Chinese church: when we think “Chinese ministry,” are we picturing Chinese believers as “mission objects in need of pastoring,” or as “the next generation of lions about to rise”? Our theology determines where we invest.

四、為以色列禱告 ── 合一比任何事都重要IV. Praying for Israel — Unity Above All Else

摩西斯牧師為全球餘民禱告之後，把焦點轉向以色列。他先用希伯來文禱告，再用西班牙文翻譯：「我們明白在天上，神對在這節期裡所有以色列人都將是有恩典的。我們為以色列百姓求祝福。我們為內塔尼亞胡禱告，為國會 (Knesset) 禱告，為所有的組織禱告——國會與軍隊、所有的議員、所有特拉維夫的百姓——願他們合一。父啊，不要分裂這個 Knesset。不要分裂祢此時所設立的，父啊。」 After praying for the global remnant, Pastor Moises turned to Israel. He prayed first in Hebrew, then translated into Spanish: “We understand that in heaven, God will be gracious with all the people of Israel on this holiday. We pray a blessing on all Israel. We pray for Netanyahu, for the Knesset, for all the organizations — the Knesset and the army, all the people of Congress, all the people of Tel Aviv — that they would be together. Father, do not separate this Knesset. Do not separate what You have set in place at this hour.”

這個禱告的時間點是 5786 年的逾越節後不久——以色列國內因為司法改革、徵兵法、加薩戰爭的處理方式正處於建國以來最嚴重的政治撕裂。摩西斯牧師沒有為任何一方表態（不為內塔尼亞胡、不為反對黨、不為左翼、不為右翼），他只為一件事禱告：合一。 The timing of this prayer was just after Pesach 5786 — Israel was in the worst political fracture since its founding, over judicial reform, conscription law, and the conduct of the Gaza war. Pastor Moises did not take sides (not for Netanyahu, not for the opposition, not for the left, not for the right). He prayed for one thing only: unity.

他重複了三次：「主啊，我今天為合一禱告，勝過其他任何事。」這個重複本身就是一個神學陳述：合一不是「眾多重要禱告事項之一」，而是所有其他禱告得以成就的前提。 He repeated it three times: “Lord, I pray for unity today, more than anything else.” The repetition itself is a theological statement: unity is not “one among many important prayer concerns” — it is the precondition for every other prayer being answered.

「主啊，我今天為合一禱告，勝過其他任何事。讓我們一同說 Naseh v'Nishma——當我們預備迎接彌賽亞王時，願我們都能說出 Naseh v'Nishma。」 “Lord, I pray for unity today, more than anything else. Let us all say Naseh v'Nishma together — may we say Naseh v'Nishma as we prepare to receive the King Messiah.”

Naseh v'Nishma（נַעֲשֶׂה וְנִשְׁמָע，「我們將要遵行，我們將要聆聽」）出自出埃及記 24:7。當摩西在西奈山下把妥拉的話讀給以色列百姓聽時，他們的回應是：「Naseh v'Nishma——我們先順服，然後再去明白。」這在拉比傳統中被視為以色列百姓的最高屬靈時刻——他們對神的話的回應，是先順服、後理解。摩西斯牧師引用這個詞，是在把以色列國今天的政治分裂放回西奈山下：你們應該再一次一同說「我們順服」。 “Naseh v'Nishma” (נַעֲשֶׂה וְנִשְׁמָע, “We will do, and we will hear”) is from Exodus 24:7. When Moses read the words of the Torah to Israel at the foot of Sinai, their answer was: “Naseh v'Nishma — we obey first, then we will understand.” In rabbinic tradition this is regarded as Israel's highest spiritual moment — their response to God's word was obedience before comprehension. Pastor Moises invoked these words to place the political fracture of present-day Israel back under Mount Sinai: you must again say, together, “we will obey.”

五、新 nusach ── 給節期的新旋律V. The New Nusach — New Melodies for the Festivals

在禱告之後，摩西斯牧師把全球的觀眾帶入這場閉幕的禮儀核心——一個全新的 nusach（נוּסָח，旋律設定）。他說：「我現在要帶你們進入一些你們可能不熟悉的領域。這些 tefillot（禱文）只在三大節期 (chag，חַג) 被吟唱。所以跟著我，享受這個新的 nusach——這些新的旋律。」 After the prayer, Pastor Moises drew the global audience into the liturgical heart of the closing — a brand-new nusach (נוּסָח, melodic setting). He said: “Now I'm taking you into some territory that may be unfamiliar to you. These tefillot (prayers) are only sung during the three festivals (chag, חַג). So follow along — enjoy this new nusach, these new melodies.”

「Nusach」這個詞在猶太禮儀中極為精準。它不只是「旋律」（melody）——它是整套禱告文本與旋律的傳承體系。世界上有多套 nusach：Nusach Ashkenaz（東歐傳統）、Nusach Sefarad（西班牙系統，但今天的「西班牙系」其實是 18 世紀哈西迪派發展出來的折衷版本）、Nusach Edot HaMizrach（東方猶太人系統）、Nusach Italki（義大利系統，可追溯至羅馬時期）等等。每一個 nusach 都是一個世代與世代之間的口傳鏈。 The word “nusach” is highly precise in Jewish liturgy. It is not merely “melody” — it is the entire transmitted system of prayer text and melody. Several historic nusachot exist: Nusach Ashkenaz (Eastern European), Nusach Sefarad (the “Spanish” system, though today's version is actually an 18th-century Hasidic synthesis), Nusach Edot HaMizrach (the Mizrahi system), Nusach Italki (the Italian system, traceable to Roman times), and others. Each nusach is an oral chain across generations.

摩西斯牧師（透過他的會堂與 Chazan 戈登）所引介的這個「新 nusach」是一個對傳統的當代貢獻——一個專門為彌賽亞會眾、為今天的節期所作的新旋律設定。這是一件極不平凡的事：在猶太禮儀史上，能寫一套新 nusach 並讓社群接受的人，屈指可數。從中世紀的 Eleazar HaKalir，到 19 世紀維也納的 Sulzer，再到 20 世紀的 Lewandowski——每一位都是一個世代的禮儀英雄。摩西斯牧師謙卑地把這個新 nusach 引介給全球的彌賽亞家庭，是一件歷史性的事。 The “new nusach” that Pastor Moises (with his congregation and Chazan Gordon) introduced here is a contemporary contribution to that tradition — a melodic setting purpose-written for Messianic congregations and the festivals of this hour. This is no small thing: in the history of Jewish liturgy, the figures who have written and seen a new nusach accepted by the community can be counted on one hand. From the medieval Eleazar HaKalir, to 19th-century Vienna's Sulzer, to 20th-century Lewandowski — each one a liturgical hero of his generation. For Pastor Moises to humbly offer this new nusach to the global Messianic family is a historic moment.

他特別提到：「我們會從 Anusim（被迫者）的背景中認出這段旋律。」這是一個小小的、但極具份量的線索。Anusim 是 1492 年被西班牙逼迫皈依的「隱藏猶太人」——他們在地下五百年保存了自己的旋律。摩西斯牧師的新 nusach 中編入了這些 Anusim 的旋律 DNA——這是一場跨越五個世紀的記憶之回返。一位來自巴塞隆納（1492 年驅逐令的中心地之一）的西班牙語牧師，在 5786 年把 Anusim 的旋律帶回到全球的節期禮儀中——這本身就是預言性的。 He noted specifically: “We'll know this melody from the background of the Anusim.” A small but enormously weighted clue. The Anusim are the “hidden Jews” forced to convert in 1492 — who preserved their melodies underground for five centuries. Pastor Moises' new nusach weaves the melodic DNA of those Anusim back in — a return of memory across five hundred years. A Spanish-speaking pastor from Barcelona (one of the epicentres of the 1492 expulsion) bringing the Anusim's melodies back into the global festival liturgy in 5786 — that itself is prophetic.

「我們會從 Anusim（被迫者，1492 年被驅逐的西班牙猶太人後裔）的背景中認出這段旋律。」——摩西斯牧師 “We'll know this melody from the background of the Anusim [the ‘forced ones,' descendants of the 1492-expelled Spanish Jews].”— Pastor Moises

六、Shavuot 與彌賽亞 ── 律法與聖靈的合一VI. Shavuot and the Messiah — The Union of Torah and Spirit

這場閉幕的禮儀核心是 Shavuot（שָׁבוּעוֹת，七七節，又稱五旬節）的 tefillot（禱文）。為什麼選擇 Shavuot？因為 Shavuot 在猶太傳統中是神在西奈山賜下妥拉的日子；而在使徒行傳 2 章中，它也是聖靈降臨在彌賽亞門徒身上的日子——西方教會稱之為「五旬節 Pentecost」（源自希臘文 pentēkostē，「第五十」，因為它在逾越節後第五十天）。 The liturgical heart of this closing is the tefillot for Shavuot (שָׁבוּעוֹת, Feast of Weeks, also called Pentecost). Why Shavuot? Because in Jewish tradition Shavuot is the day God gave the Torah at Mount Sinai; and in Acts 2 it is the day the Holy Spirit fell on the disciples of the Messiah — the Western church calls it “Pentecost” (from Greek pentēkostē, “fiftieth,” because it falls on the fiftieth day after Pesach).

這個雙重意涵極重要：同一個節期 (chag) 承載了「律法」與「靈」的兩個給予。對基督徒來說，使徒行傳 2 章不是一個「全新」的事件——它是 Shavuot 在彌賽亞裡的延伸與完滿。神在西奈山把妥拉寫在石版上；祂在五旬節把同一份妥拉寫在祂百姓的心版上（耶 31:33；來 8:10）。摩西斯牧師選擇用 Shavuot 的禱文來結束「新婦之夜」，是把整場節目定位在這個「律法與聖靈合一」的時刻。 The dual meaning is critical: the same festival (chag) carries two givings — Torah and Spirit. For Christians, Acts 2 is not a “new” event; it is Shavuot extended and consummated in the Messiah. At Sinai God wrote the Torah on tablets of stone; at Pentecost He wrote the same Torah on the tablets of His people's hearts (Jer 31:33; Heb 8:10). By choosing the Shavuot prayers to close the Night of the Bride, Pastor Moises is positioning the entire broadcast at this moment of Torah-and-Spirit union.

他吟唱的禱文中包含了一段極關鍵的話：「v'zikron Mashiach ben David avdecha」（「願祢記念祢的僕人——大衛之子彌賽亞」）。在傳統的猶太節期禱告中，這個祈求是為「將要來」的彌賽亞而說。在彌賽亞會堂的脈絡中，這個祈求帶著雙重張力：「祂已經來過（作為受苦的僕人耶書亞），祂將要再來（作為大衛之子君王）。」——這是彌賽亞猶太教獨特的「兩次降臨」(Mashiach ben Yosef + Mashiach ben David) 的神學。 A critical line in his chanted prayer: “v'zikron Mashiach ben David avdecha” (“and remembrance of Your servant, Messiah son of David”). In traditional Jewish festival liturgy this petition is for the “coming” Messiah. In a Messianic context it carries a double tension: “He has already come (as the suffering servant Yeshua), and He is coming again (as the Davidic King).” — the distinctive Messianic-Jewish theology of Mashiach ben Yosef and Mashiach ben David, the two advents.

「Yeshuah ben Atzeret」（「七七節的救恩」）——這個短語在傳統 Shavuot 禱文中出現，但在彌賽亞會堂中它變成了一個雙關：耶書亞（Yeshua）就是 Shavuot 的救恩 (Yeshuah)。希伯來文「Yeshua」（救恩）與耶書亞的名字是同一個詞。 “Yeshuah ben Atzeret” (“the salvation of Shavuot”) — this phrase appears in traditional Shavuot prayer, but in a Messianic congregation it becomes a pun: Yeshua (יֵשׁוּעַ) is the very “yeshua” (salvation) of Shavuot. In Hebrew, “Yeshua” the name and “yeshua” the noun “salvation” are the same word.

七、Amidah ── 站立的禱告，全球同步VII. The Amidah — The Standing Prayer, Globally Synchronized

禮儀的高潮是 Amidah（עֲמִידָה，「站立」），又稱 Shemoneh Esreh（十八祝禱，雖然今天通常是十九段）。這是猶太禱告的核心中的核心——每天三次（早、午、晚）站立著、面向耶路撒冷、靜默地或低聲地誦讀。 The liturgical climax is the Amidah (עֲמִידָה, “the Standing”), also called the Shemoneh Esreh (“the Eighteen Benedictions,” though today there are usually nineteen). It is the heart of the heart of Jewish prayer — recited three times a day (morning, afternoon, evening), standing, facing Jerusalem, in silence or undertone.

摩西斯牧師對全球觀眾說：「我現在邀請全世界一起加入戈登 Chazan 的 Amidah，我邀請你們大家與我們一同站立，作一段特別為 Shavuot 的 Amidah。請你們為所有那些社群站立。請為我們、為全球的提姆納餘民站立。」 Pastor Moises addressed the global audience: “I invite the entire world right now to join Chazan Gordon for the Amidah, and I invite you all to stand up with us for a special Amidah for Shavuot. Please stand for all those communities. Stand for us, for the Timna remnant worldwide.”

這個邀請從聆聽的姿態，轉變為參與的姿態。身體本身就是禱告——猶太禮儀的核心智慧之一就是：你的身體姿態、你的方位、你的呼吸節奏，都是禱告的本體，而不只是「附加品」。當摩西斯牧師邀請全球的觀眾「站立」時，他是在把整個世界的身體放入這場禱告裡——一個跨越時區、語言、文化的身體合一。 This invitation shifts the audience from listener to participant. The body itself is prayer — one of the core wisdoms of Jewish liturgy is that your posture, your direction, the rhythm of your breath are the substance of prayer, not its “add-ons.” When Pastor Moises invites the world to “stand,” he is placing the body of the entire world inside this prayer — a bodily unity across time zones, languages, and cultures.

禱告中包含了一段令人動容的祈求：「Ritze Adonai Eloheinu Be'amcha Yisrael tefilatam, Ve'ashevet avodah le'dvir vetecha」——「主啊，求祢悅納祢百姓以色列的禱告，求祢將敬拜歸回到祢殿宇的至聖所。」這是一個等候聖殿重建的禱告。在彌賽亞會眾的脈絡中，這個祈求帶著雙重盼望：等候彌賽亞回來，等候祂在耶路撒冷重建聖殿。 One especially moving petition: “Ritze Adonai Eloheinu Be'amcha Yisrael tefilatam, Ve'ashevet avodah le'dvir vetecha” — “Lord, accept the prayers of Your people Israel; restore the worship to the Holy of Holies of Your house.” This is a prayer for the rebuilt Temple. In the Messianic context this petition carries a double longing: for the Messiah to return, and for Him to restore the Temple in Jerusalem.

「主啊，求祢悅納祢百姓以色列的禱告。求祢將敬拜歸回到祢殿宇的至聖所。」——出自 Amidah 中的 Retzeh 祝禱 “Lord, accept the prayers of Your people Israel. Restore the worship to the Holy of Holies of Your house.”— from the Retzeh benediction of the Amidah

八、ברכה ── 全場直播的最後祝福VIII. The Berakhah — The Final Blessing of the Entire Broadcast

在禮儀的所有 tefillot 吟唱完之後，摩西斯牧師回到西班牙語，向全球的會眾送上這場 6 小時 17 分鐘廣播的最後一個祝福。他援用了一個希伯來字根：בְּרָכָה berakhah（祝福）——這個字與「בֶּרֶךְ berekh」（膝蓋）同源。祝福，本意是「使你彎下膝蓋」——讓你跪下，承認你不是源頭，你是承受者。 After all the chanted tefillot, Pastor Moises returned to Spanish to send forth, to the global congregation, the final blessing of this 6-hour-17-minute broadcast. He drew on the Hebrew root בְּרָכָה berakhah (blessing) — cognate with “בֶּרֶךְ berekh” (knee). To bless, at root, means “to make you bend your knee” — to bring you to kneel, to acknowledge that you are not the source, you are the receiver.

這個祝福沒有要把觀眾「打發回家」，相反地，它要把觀眾「打發出去做見證」。在猶太禮儀的最末，Kaddish（願頌讚之名被聖化）之後，會眾走出會堂時，他們不是「離開了神」，而是「被神差遣出去」。當摩西斯牧師說最後的 Amen 時，整場「新婦之夜 5786」沒有「結束」——它差遣了。 This blessing did not aim to “send the audience home.” It aimed to “send the audience out as witnesses.” At the very end of Jewish liturgy, after the Kaddish (“may His great Name be sanctified”), when the congregation leaves the synagogue, they are not “leaving God” — they are being sent forth by God. When Pastor Moises said the final Amen, the whole “Night of the Bride 5786” did not “end.” It commissioned.

「願主賜福你，保護你；願主使祂的臉光照你，賜恩給你；願主向你仰臉，賜你 שָׁלוֹם shalom（平安）。Hermanos, vayan en paz——弟兄姊妹，平安地去吧。願大衛之子彌賽亞快快回來。Am Yisrael Chai——以色列的百姓永遠存活。Amen v'Amen。」 “May the LORD bless you and keep you; may the LORD make His face shine upon you and be gracious to you; may the LORD lift up His countenance upon you and give you שָׁלוֹם shalom (peace). Hermanos, vayan en paz — brothers and sisters, go in peace. May Messiah son of David come quickly. Am Yisrael Chai — the people of Israel lives forever. Amen v'Amen.”
