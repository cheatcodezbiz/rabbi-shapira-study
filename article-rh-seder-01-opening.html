開場 — Shana Tova 與羔羊的生命冊 Opening — Shana Tova and the Lamb's Book of Life

全球同步的羅什哈沙拿之夜，在韓國的河中浸禮裡以希伯來文的問安 Shalom · Shana Tova 揭開：敬拜、合一，以及那卷在這一年之初為我們的名字而打開的——羔羊的生命冊。 The Worldwide Rosh HaShanah Seder opens from a river mikveh in Korea with the Hebrew greeting Shalom · Shana Tova — worship, unity, and the book that opens at the turn of the year for the names of His people: the Lamb's Book of Life.

Ahavat Ammi · Kol HaShofar 全球連線 · 韓語服事 Ahavat Ammi · Kol HaShofar Worldwide · Korean-Language Service
2026 · 5 · 26 · 約 9 分鐘閱讀~9 min read · ▶ 觀看 · 開場Watch · Opening ·

這一夜不是從講台開始的，而是從水裡開始的。在韓國一條河中，許多人浸入水中受彌賽亞式的浸禮 (mikveh)，主領者抬起頭，向著鏡頭、向著全世界說出那句古老的問安：「Shalom 各位，Shana Tova。」這是 5786 年——「羅什哈沙拿」רֹאשׁ הַשָּׁנָה（Rosh Hashanah，「年之首」）的開端，也是一場橫跨韓國、北京、美國、巴塞隆納，最終要在耶路撒冷會合的「全球羅什哈沙拿之夜 (Seder)」的第一道門。今夜，書要打開了。 This night does not begin at a pulpit. It begins in water. In a river in Korea, many step down into a Messianic immersion (mikveh); the host lifts his head toward the camera, toward the whole watching world, and speaks the ancient greeting: “Shalom, everyone. Shana Tova.” This is the threshold of 5786 — of Rosh Hashanah (רֹאשׁ הַשָּׁנָה, “the head of the year”) — and the first gate of a Worldwide Rosh HaShanah Seder that will travel from Korea to Beijing, the United States, Barcelona, and finally converge in Jerusalem. Tonight, the book opens.

一、Shalom · Shana Tova ── 一句問安裡的整個盼望I. Shalom · Shana Tova — A Whole Hope in One Greeting

這是一場韓語的服事。主領者用韓文向觀眾問安，但他口中第一個詞卻是希伯來文：שָׁנָה טוֹבָה Shana Tova，字面意思是「美好的一年」，是猶太人在新年彼此祝福的話。它的完整形式 Shana Tova U'Metukah 意為「願你有美好而甘甜的一年」——所以猶太人在這一夜會蘸蜜吃蘋果，求一年的甘甜。 This is a Korean-language service. The host greets his audience in Korean — but the first word out of his mouth is Hebrew: שָׁנָה טוֹבָה Shana Tova, literally “a good year,” the blessing Jews speak to one another at the new year. Its fuller form, Shana Tova U'Metukah, means “a good and sweet year” — which is why, on this night, apples are dipped in honey and eaten as a plea for a sweet twelvemonth.

「Shalom」(שָׁלוֹם) 也遠不只是「你好」。它的字根 sh-l-m 指向「完整、圓滿、和好、無虧欠」的境界。所以這兩個詞並排，不只是禮貌的招呼，而是把整個新年的盼望濃縮成一句話：願破碎的得以完整，願這一年是甘甜的。主領者特別強調：今年的羅什哈沙拿「與以往任何一年都完全不同」。 “Shalom” (שָׁלוֹם) is also far more than “hello.” Its root sh-l-m reaches toward wholeness, completeness, reconciliation, nothing-lacking. So the two words set side by side are not mere courtesy; they compress the whole hope of the new year into a single line: may what is broken be made whole, and may this year be sweet. The host underlines it: this Rosh Hashanah is “completely different from any that came before.”

二、河中的浸禮 ── 在水裡與以色列連合II. The River Mikveh — United with Israel in the Water

服事是從一場 מִקְוֶה mikveh（浸禮、潔淨之水）開始的。Mikveh 在猶太傳統裡是悔改、潔淨、過渡到新身分的記號；在彌賽亞信徒中，它也指向在水中與彌賽亞同死同復活的浸禮。主領者說：「許多人今天前來受浸，來到主面前。」在進入新的一年之前，人先把自己浸入水中——這不是偶然安排，而是整夜的鑰匙：你想要新的一年，就先讓舊的被水洗去。 The service begins with a מִקְוֶה mikveh — an immersion, the waters of purification. In Jewish tradition the mikveh marks repentance, cleansing, the passage into a new status; among Messianic believers it also points to immersion into the Messiah's death and resurrection. “Many came today to be immersed,” the host says, “to come before the Lord.” Before stepping into the new year, the people step down into the water first — not by accident, but as the key to the whole night: if you want a new year, let the old be washed away first.

但這場在韓國河中的浸禮有一層更深的意圖。主領者說：「我們在這韓國的土地上浸禮，是因為我們要與以色列連合。」他向觀眾發出一連串赤裸的問題：「你夠愛猶太人嗎？你願意與以色列連合嗎？你願意為了以色列得自由，連自己的神學都放下嗎？」這是這場服事反覆出現的主旋律：在水裡，列國與以色列成為一家。 But this river mikveh in Korea carries a deeper intent. “We immerse here on Korean soil,” the host says, “because here we are uniting ourselves with Israel.” He puts a string of bare questions to the watching world: “Do you love the Jewish people enough? Are you willing to unite yourself with Israel? Are you willing to lay down even your own theology so that the nation of Israel may be set free?” This is the recurring theme of the whole service: in the water, the nations become one house with Israel.

三、5786 ── 「連合之年」與全球連線III. 5786 — The Year of Unity, and a World Linked Together

主領者請觀眾留意這一年的數字：5、7、8、6。他從這幾個數字裡讀出一個信息——這是連合的一年，是把「餘民」連合起來的一年。他剛接到一通從北京打來的電話：那裡的弟兄姊妹正透過網路一同參與。「我們在韓國行羅什哈沙拿，他們在北京行羅什哈沙拿——而我們此刻是同時連結在一起的。」美國的觀眾要在十多個小時後才進入這一夜，巴塞隆納、印尼、非洲、拉丁美洲——全都要陸續加入。 The host asks his audience to notice this year's digits — 5, 7, 8, 6 — and reads a message in them: this is the year of unity, the year that binds the remnant together. He has just taken a phone call from Beijing, where brothers and sisters are joining online. “We keep Rosh Hashanah in Korea, they keep Rosh Hashanah in Beijing — and at this very moment we are connected together.” America enters the night some thirteen hours later; Barcelona, Indonesia, Africa, Latin America — all of them folding in, one after another.

他特別警告一種驕傲的試探：「不要說『這裡才是餘民教會，中國不重要、印尼不重要、非洲不重要』。」他堅持：我們都是同一個家的一部分。這正是 סֵדֶר Seder（「次序、安排」）這個字的含義——羅什哈沙拿之夜是一場有次序的禮儀晚宴，按著固定的步驟，把分散各地的人帶進同一個敬拜的節奏裡。今夜的 Seder 不在一張桌子上，而在整個地球上。 He warns sharply against a proud temptation: “Do not say, ‘Here is the remnant church, and China doesn't matter, Indonesia doesn't matter, Africa doesn't matter.’” He insists: we are all part of the same house. This is the meaning of סֵדֶר Seder — “order, arrangement.” A Rosh HaShanah Seder is a liturgical meal kept in a fixed order, a set sequence of steps that draws scattered people into one shared rhythm of worship. Tonight's Seder is not laid on a single table; it is laid across the whole earth.

四、羊角號的呼召 ── 回家，建造神的家IV. The Call of the Shofar — Come Home, and Build God's House

羅什哈沙拿在聖經裡又稱 Yom Teruah（吹角的日子，利未記 23:24）。整場服事的名字「100 道門 100 羊角號 (100 Gates · 100 Shofarot)」就出自這裡：羊角號 (שׁוֹפָר shofar) 在這一夜要被吹響上百次。主領者說，這號角的聲音是一個呼召——「把我們眾人都呼召回家」。他引用以賽亞書：「我的殿必稱為萬民禱告的殿」（賽 56:7），求羊角號的聲音從這韓國的土地上響起，傳到全世界。 In Scripture Rosh Hashanah is also called Yom Teruah, the Day of the Blast (Leviticus 23:24). The name of the whole service — “100 Gates · 100 Shofarot” — comes from here: the shofar (שׁוֹפָר, the ram's horn) will be sounded a hundred times this night. That sound, the host says, is a summons — “calling all of us home.” He reaches for Isaiah — “My house shall be called a house of prayer for all nations” (Isa 56:7) — and prays that the voice of the shofar, rising from this Korean ground, would be heard by all the world.

這裡有一個美麗的雙重動作：羊角號既呼召人回家，也呼召人去建造那個家。「你們聽見羊角號的聲音，就回家吧；然後我們一同建造神的家。」回家不是終點，而是起點——回到神的約裡，然後與從萬國回來的人一同，把那座「為萬民禱告的殿」建立起來。今夜的浸禮、敬拜、號角，都是這同一座殿的根基。 There is a beautiful double motion here: the shofar both calls people home and calls them to build that home. “When you hear the sound of the shofar, come home — and then let us build the house of God together.” Coming home is not the destination but the starting point: back into God's covenant, and then, alongside those returning from every nation, to raise up that “house of prayer for all peoples.” Tonight's immersion, worship, and horn-blasts are all foundations of that one house.

五、生命冊打開的這一夜 ── Sefer HaChayimV. The Night the Book Opens — Sefer HaChayim

然後，這場服事的心臟跳了出來。主領者放慢語氣說：「Shana Tova。願我們被記念，願我們被寫在羔羊的生命冊上。今夜，書要打開了。」猶太傳統教導，在羅什哈沙拿這一夜，天上有三本書被打開：義人的書、惡人的書，以及介於兩者之間之人的書。義人立刻被寫入生命之中；其餘的人則在隨後的「敬畏之日」(Yamim Nora'im) 中被審查，直到贖罪日 (Yom Kippur) 封定。 Then the heart of the service beats out into the open. The host slows his voice: “Shana Tova. May we be remembered; may we be written in the Lamb's Book of Life. Tonight, the book opens.” Jewish tradition teaches that on the night of Rosh Hashanah three books are opened in heaven: the book of the righteous, the book of the wicked, and the book of those in between. The righteous are inscribed at once unto life; the rest are weighed through the “Days of Awe” (Yamim Nora'im) that follow, until the seal falls on the Day of Atonement (Yom Kippur).

這就是「סֵפֶר הַחַיִּים Sefer HaChayim」——「生命冊」。它在希伯來聖經裡早有根據：摩西求神「從你所寫的冊上塗抹我的名」（出 32:32），詩篇說義人的名「不被塗抹」（詩 69:28）。而在新約裡，這卷冊有了一個名字：羔羊的生命冊（啟 21:27）。主領者把這兩條線織在一起——猶太新年的「生命冊」，與啟示錄中「羔羊的生命冊」，原是同一卷書。 This is the סֵפֶר הַחַיִּים Sefer HaChayim — “the Book of Life.” It is rooted deep in the Hebrew Scriptures: Moses begs God, “blot me out of the book You have written” (Exodus 32:32); the Psalm says the righteous are “not blotted out” (Psalm 69:28). And in the New Testament the book receives a name: the Lamb's Book of Life (Revelation 21:27). The host weaves the two threads into one — the “Book of Life” of the Jewish new year and the book of the Lamb in Revelation are, in the end, the same scroll.

於是這一夜的穿著也有了意義：「我們都穿上了白衣。」白衣是羅什哈沙拿與贖罪日的傳統,象徵罪得潔淨、被寫在生命之中的盼望。今夜被歡迎進來的,「沒有韓國人、沒有中國人、沒有義大利人、法國人、非洲人——我們都是一個了。」他們白衣束身,剛從水裡上來,一同站在那位將要審判全地的主面前,聽著號角,等候自己的名字被寫上。 And so even the clothing of the night means something: “We are all dressed in white.” White is the garment of Rosh Hashanah and Yom Kippur, a sign of sin washed clean and of the hope of being written unto life. Tonight all who are welcomed in — “no Korean, no Chinese, no Italian, French, African — we are all one” — stand together in white, fresh from the water, before the One who will judge all the earth, listening for the horn, waiting for their names to be written.

六、站在審判之日的天空下 ── 以大喜樂進入VI. Standing Under the Sky of the Day of Judgment — Entering with Great Joy

這一夜帶著一種莊嚴與喜樂並存的張力。主領者說：「接下來的十天，審查 (interrogation) 開始了。」這正是「敬畏之日」的核心——人站在審判的天空下，省察自己的生命。然而他並不是用恐懼結束,而是用確信:「我們屬於彌賽亞——你信嗎?」並為新的一年禱告:求主賜下「甘甜、良善、生命、祝福、供應,與自由」。 The night carries a tension in which solemnity and joy live together. “For the next ten days,” the host says, “the interrogation begins.” This is the very heart of the Days of Awe — to stand under the sky of judgment and examine one's life. Yet he ends not in fear but in assurance: “We belong to the Messiah — do you believe it?” — and prays over the new year for “sweetness, goodness, life, blessing, provision, and freedom.”

他也沒有忘記受苦的人。在禱告中,他為以色列被擄的人質代求,求主「不要忘記他們」,願他們不久就與家人團聚;他們白衣的會眾「替他們站立」。最後他向觀眾發出邀請:今夜是特別的一夜,眾人要一同進入敬拜,進入「100 個羊角號」,進入特別的禱告。「我們如今站在天空之下——在那審判的日子,我們要站立。所以讓我們以大喜樂,站在天的面前。」 Nor does he forget those who suffer. In prayer he intercedes for Israel's captive hostages, asking the Lord “not to forget them,” that soon they would be reunited with their families; his white-robed congregation “stands in their place.” And then he turns to invite the watching world: tonight is a special night, and all are to enter worship together, into the “hundred shofarot,” into special prayer. “We stand now beneath the open sky — and on that day of judgment we shall stand. So let us stand before heaven with great joy.”

「Shana Tova。願我們被記念,願我們被寫在羔羊的生命冊上。今夜,書要打開了。聽見羊角號的聲音,就回家吧——猶太君王將要回來。」 “Shana Tova. May we be remembered; may we be written in the Lamb's Book of Life. Tonight, the book opens. Hear the sound of the shofar, and come home — the Jewish King is returning.”
