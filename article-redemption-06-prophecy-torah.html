預言與妥拉 Prophecy and Torah

妥拉是不是一本「預言書」？拉姆班（Ramban，נַחְמָנִידֵס）在《救贖之書》(Sefer HaGeulah) 中給出一個微妙卻革命性的答案：妥拉並非預言之書——但它充滿極深的「預言價值」。而真正令人震驚的，是他如何看待預言的「條件」：神的應許必定成就，但什麼時候成就，取決於我們。 Is the Torah a “book of prophecy”? In Sefer HaGeulah, the Ramban (נַחְמָנִידֵס) gives a subtle but revolutionary answer: the Torah is not a book of prophecy — yet it carries the deepest prophetic value. And the startling part is how he treats prophecy's “condition”: God's promises must be fulfilled — but when they are fulfilled depends on us.

沙皮拉拉比博士 · Ahavat Ammi 事工 Rabbi Dr. Itzhak Shapira · Ahavat Ammi Ministries
2026 · 5 · 25 · 约 9 分钟阅读~9 min read · ▶ 觀看 · 第六課：預言與妥拉Watch · Lesson 6: Prophecy and Torah ·

「我們應當如何看待聖經？妥拉在我們生命中佔有什麼位置？我們又該如何學習看待預言？」拉比沙皮拉以這三個問題開啟《救贖之書》(Sefer HaGeulah，סֵפֶר הַגְּאֻלָּה) 的第六課。在上一課裡，拉姆班 (רַמְבַּ"ן Ramban) 似乎把妥拉與預言劃清了界線——妥拉是生活方式，不是一本占卜未來的工具書。但在這一課中，拉姆班「稍稍敞開了他的立場」：是的，妥拉不是一本完整的預言書；但妥拉之中，藏著極深的預言價值。而貫穿全課的那句震撼之語是：神在妥拉中的應許是無條件的——它必定成就；改變的不是「會不會」，而是「什麼時候」。 “How should we treat the Scriptures? What is the place of the Torah in our lives? And how should we learn to look upon prophecy?” With these three questions Rabbi Shapira opens Lesson 6 of Sefer HaGeulah (סֵפֶר הַגְּאֻלָּה, the Book of Redemption). In the previous lesson, the Ramban (רַמְבַּ"ן) seemed to draw a hard line between Torah and prophecy — the Torah is a way of life, not a manual for divining the future. But in this lesson the Ramban “opens his position just a bit”: yes, the Torah is not a complete book of prophecy; yet within it lies the deepest prophetic value. And the line that runs through the whole lesson is startling: God's promises in the Torah are unconditional — they will come to pass. What changes is not whether, but when.

一、為什麼這項研究是一個重擔I. Why This Study Is a Burden

拉比沙皮拉坦言，他之所以承擔《救贖之書》這項研究，是「一個極大的重擔與任務」。原因在於，這本由拉姆班（Nachmanides，נַחְמָנִידֵס）所寫的中世紀著作，觸及了一些對「信徒」而言最為根本的問題。 Rabbi Shapira admits that taking up this study of Sefer HaGeulah is “a great burden and task.” The reason is that this medieval work by Nachmanides (נַחְמָנִידֵס) tackles questions that are, in his words, “so fundamental to us as believers.”

那三個問題並不抽象，而是直指生命的核心：我們應當如何對待聖經？妥拉 (תּוֹרָה Torah) 在我們今天的生活中佔有什麼地位？我們又該如何學習看待預言 (נְבוּאָה nevuah)？整部《救贖之書》——尤其是這一課——就是繞著這三條軸線，逐字逐句地與拉姆班同行。 Those three questions are not abstract; they cut to the core of life: How should we treat the Scriptures? What place does the Torah (תּוֹרָה) hold in our lives today? And how do we learn to look upon prophecy (נְבוּאָה nevuah)? The whole of Sefer HaGeulah — and this lesson especially — walks word by word with the Ramban along these three axes.

二、拉姆班「稍稍敞開」的立場II. The Ramban “Opens His Position Just a Bit”

在上一課（標題為「妥拉」）中，拉姆班的立場非常獨特而嚴格：人研讀妥拉，是為了事奉神、為了催促祂的回歸，而不是把它當作一件預言的工具。他並且強調：我們絕不能把妥拉從摩西（Moshe Rabbeinu，我們的老師摩西）的處境中抽離出來——萬萬不可。 In the previous lesson (titled “Torah”), the Ramban's position was unique and strict: one studies Torah in order to serve God and to hasten His return, and not as a tool of prophecy. He also stressed that we must never lift the Torah out of the context of Moses (Moshe Rabbeinu, “Moses our teacher”) — God forbid.

但這一課，拉姆班「稍稍敞開了他的立場」。他用了一個分量極重的字——emet（אֱמֶת，「真理」）——來開場：「這是真理。」拉比沙皮拉把它逐句拆解：拉姆班承認，妥拉確實有時在「責備未來」、宣告刑罰；有時又在「給予安慰」；妥拉也「多次談到神蹟與奇事」。換句話說，雖然妥拉不像先知書，它不是一本完整的預言書——但妥拉之中，確有極深的預言價值。 But in this lesson the Ramban “opens his position just a bit.” He begins with a weighty word — emet (אֱמֶת, “truth”): “This is the truth.” Rabbi Shapira unpacks it line by line: the Ramban concedes that the Torah does at times rebuke about the future and speak of punishment; at times it gives a message of comfort; and many times it speaks of signs and wonders. In other words, although the Torah is not like the writings of the prophets — it is not a complete book of prophecy — there is, within the Torah, great prophetic value.

三、兩種藏在文本中的方式：remez 與 diburIII. Two Ways It Lives in the Text — Remez and Dibur

拉姆班說，預言價值在妥拉的文本中以兩種方式出現：ben b'remez 與 ben b'dibur。要理解這兩個字，拉比沙皮拉提醒我們猶太人讀經的「四層方法」（即 PaRDeS 之法）。 The Ramban says the prophetic value appears in the Torah's text in two ways: ben b'remez and ben b'dibur. To understand these, Rabbi Shapira recalls the “four layers” of Jewish interpretation (the method known as PaRDeS).

Remez（רֶמֶז，「暗示、線索」）是第二層——文本中暗藏的、需要被讀出的線索。拉姆班說：整部妥拉之中，到處都散佈著這樣關於未來的「線索」。Dibur（דִּבּוּר，「言說」）則接近第一層，也就是 pshat（פְּשָׁט，文本最直接、字面的意義）。拉姆班的意思是：就連在最直白的字面（pshat）裡，也藏著關於未來的暗示（remazim）。 Remez (רֶמֶז, “hint, clue”) is the second layer — the hidden clues woven into the text that must be drawn out. The Ramban says the whole Torah is dotted with such clues about the future. Dibur (דִּבּוּר, “speech”) is closer to the first layer — the pshat (פְּשָׁט, the plain, literal sense of the text). The Ramban's point: even in the plainest literal reading (pshat), there are hints (remazim) about the future.

所以拉姆班的兩面式結論在此凝結為一句話：是的，妥拉不該被當作一本預言書來看待——但妥拉確實承載著極深的預言價值。這兩句話並不矛盾；它們是同一枚硬幣的兩面。 So the Ramban's two-sided conclusion crystallizes into a single sentence: yes, the Torah should not be looked upon as a book of prophecy — and yet the Torah does carry a great prophetic value. The two statements are not in tension; they are two faces of one coin.

四、申命記 4:25 ── 妥拉「一語雙關」IV. Deuteronomy 4:25 — The Torah Speaks “With Two Faces at Once”

拉姆班接著給出例證，取自申命記。拉比沙皮拉指出，這段經文出自parashat va'etchanen（瓦埃特哈南段）。申命記 4:25 說：「ki tolid banim u'bnei banim」——「你在那地住久了，生子生孫……」拉姆班注意到，妥拉在此「用一種一語雙關的語言說話」(medaberet belashon she'meshamesh bishtei panim yachad，「以一種同時呈現兩面的語言說話」)。 The Ramban then gives an example, drawn from Deuteronomy — from parashat va'etchanen, as Rabbi Shapira notes. Deuteronomy 4:25 reads: “ki tolid banim u'vnei vanim” — “When you father children and grandchildren and have grown old in the land…” The Ramban observes that here the Torah “speaks in a language that serves two faces at once” (medaberet belashon she'meshamesh bishtei panim yachad).

這正是關鍵所在。同一節經文，既在預告未來（「你必生子生孫，必得救於那地」），同時又在告訴我們此刻當怎樣生活。經文後半段警告：「你若……雕刻偶像……行耶和華你神眼中看為惡的事，惹祂發怒……」拉比沙皮拉說：這就是妥拉的「短期應用」（今日的行為準則）與「長期應用」（未來的應許）並存於一句話中。 This is the crux. The same verse both foretells the future (“you will father children and grandchildren, you will be saved in the land”) and at the same time tells us how to live right now. The latter half of the passage warns: “…if you carve an idol… and do evil in the eyes of the LORD to provoke Him to anger…” Rabbi Shapira: this is the Torah's “short-term application” (today's way of life) and its “long-term application” (the future promise) standing together in a single sentence.

拉比沙皮拉強調這一點為何重要：拉姆班並不是說「妥拉只關乎今日」，也不是說「妥拉只關乎未來」。妥拉同時是今天的生活方式，也是對未來的預言。它兩者皆是。 Rabbi Shapira underscores why this matters: the Ramban is not saying “the Torah is only for today,” nor “the Torah is only for the future.” The Torah is at once a way of life for today and a prophecy for the future. It is both.

五、無條件的應許 ── 「不是 v'im」V. The Unconditional Promise — “It Does Not Say v'im”

接下來是這一課最尖銳、也最容易被誤解的轉折。拉姆班指出：妥拉中關於「你必生子生孫」的應許，是 blilit l'Torateinu——對我們的妥拉而言是無條件的。它必定發生。絕對無條件。 Here comes the lesson's sharpest — and most easily misread — turn. The Ramban points out that the Torah's promise “you will father children and grandchildren” is blilit l'Torateinu — unconditional for our Torah. It will happen. Absolutely unconditional.

這似乎與上一課的教導衝突。上一課說「預言是有條件的」；這一課卻說「不，並不是預言本身有條件。」拉比沙皮拉精準地化解了這個張力：有條件的是「時機」，而非「應許是否成就」。預言必定要被成就——正如拉姆班所言。 This seems to clash with the previous lesson, which taught that “prophecy is conditional.” Here it says: no, it is not that the prophecy itself is conditional. Rabbi Shapira resolves the tension precisely: what is conditional is the timing, not whether the promise is fulfilled. The prophecy must be fulfilled — just as the Ramban says.

拉姆班的證據藏在文法裡：經文沒有說 v'im（וְאִם，「如果」）。它不是說「如果你做這些事」，而是說「你必做這些事」。這一點至關重要。在同一處經文（申命記 4），妥拉既宣告了無條件的應許，也立刻給出我們當行的指引。 The Ramban's proof lies in the grammar: the verse does not say v'im (וְאִם, “if”). It does not say “if you do these things”; it says “you will do these things.” This is decisive. In the very same passage (Deuteronomy 4), the Torah declares an unconditional promise and immediately supplies the guidance for how we are to live.

六、申命記 4:29 ── 我們的工作如何成就預言VI. Deuteronomy 4:29 — How Our Work Fulfills Prophecy

拉姆班引用申命記 4:29：「但你們在那裡必尋求耶和華你的神。你盡心盡性尋求祂，就必尋見。」拉比沙皮拉指出，這不是一個有條件的應許——它必定要發生，因為摩西（一位 navi，נָבִיא，先知）這樣說了。凡摩西以預言之口所說的，必定成就。 The Ramban cites Deuteronomy 4:29: “But from there you will seek the LORD your God, and you will find Him, if you search after Him with all your heart and with all your soul.” Rabbi Shapira stresses that this is not a conditional promise — it will happen, because Moses (a navi, נָבִיא, “prophet”) said so. Whatever Moses speaks prophetically must come to pass.

這把我們帶到本課的核心鑰匙：妥拉是滿有 Musar（מוּסָר，倫理、品格的教導）的書。拉姆班說，妥拉應當在兩個層面被看待：第一，它是一本「Musar 之書」——一部倫理價值的教導，告訴我們當如何藉著遵行誡命（mitzvot）而活；第二，妥拉中也有某些地方，是在談論我們這份 Musar 在未來的成就。 This brings us to the lesson's central key: the Torah is a book full of Musar (מוּסָר, “ethical instruction, character”). The Ramban says the Torah should be seen on two levels: first, it is a “book of Musar” — a teaching of ethical value, showing us how to live by keeping the commandments (mitzvot); and second, there are places in the Torah that speak of the future fulfillment of that very Musar.

於是拉姆班道出那把關鍵之鑰：「因著我們去做這份工作，預言就被成就了。」拉比沙皮拉說，這是一種與眾極為不同的預言觀。 And so the Ramban turns the key: “Because we do the work, prophecies are being fulfilled.” This, Rabbi Shapira says, is a profoundly different way of looking at prophecy.

七、催促救贖 ── 不是當神預備好，而是當百姓預備好VII. Hastening the Redemption — Not When God Is Ready, but When the People Are

拉比沙皮拉直言：大多數今天研究預言的人，以為預言會「就那樣、像魔術一樣」自動實現。拉姆班卻說：不，不是這樣。預言被成就，不是當哈舍姆（Hashem，神）預備好的時候，而是當百姓預備好的時候。這才是按拉姆班所言、看待預言的正確方式。 Rabbi Shapira is blunt: most people who study prophecy today think it will happen “like that, magically.” The Ramban says: no, no. Prophecies are fulfilled not when Hashem (God) is ready, but when the people are ready. That, according to the Ramban, is the correct way to look at prophecy.

換句話說，我們藉著自己的行動，催促或拖延預言成就的時間。這正是 ziruz（זֵרוּז，「催促、加快」）的意思——我們的順服能為救贖 (גְּאוּלָּה geulah) 帶來催促，使以色列得以經歷申命記 4:29 那樣美好的應許。 In other words, by our own actions we hasten or delay the timing of a prophecy's fulfillment. This is the meaning of ziruz (זֵרוּז, “hastening, acceleration”) — our obedience brings a hastening to the redemption (גְּאוּלָּה geulah), so that Israel may experience the beautiful promises of Deuteronomy 4:29.

這裡有一個極其重要的分辨。我們不是為了「讓預言實現」才去遵行妥拉——那絕不是目的。目的是事奉哈舍姆。當我們讀申命記 4:29 時，不能說「我們為了這個緣故呼求」；不，我們事奉神，這就足夠了。而隨之而來的「附加的益處」是：全以色列都將尋求耶和華。 Here is a crucial distinction. We do not keep the Torah in order to “make prophecy come true” — that is never the goal. The goal is to serve Hashem. When we read Deuteronomy 4:29, we cannot say “we call out for this reason”; no — we serve God, and that is enough. The “added benefit” that follows is this: all of Israel will seek the LORD.

八、那麼，妥拉是一本預言書嗎？VIII. So — Is the Torah a Prophetic Book?

拉比沙皮拉把整課收束成一組清晰的問答。妥拉是一本預言書嗎？拉姆班會回答：絕對是。那麼，妥拉的本質就是預言嗎？拉姆班會回答：不是。那妥拉的本質是什麼？是妥拉的倫理與 Musar（מוּסָר）。 Rabbi Shapira gathers the whole lesson into a crisp pair of questions. Is the Torah a prophetic book? The Ramban would say: absolutely, yes. Is its essence prophecy? The Ramban says: no. Then what is its essence? The ethics and the Musar (מוּסָר) of the Torah.

這就是拉姆班獨特而精微的平衡：我們看待妥拉，承認它確有預言價值——但我們的目標不是那預言價值。我們的目標是成全誡命（mitzvah），而這正是我們敬拜哈舍姆的方式。預言價值是隨之而來的果子，而非我們攀摘的目的。 This is the Ramban's unique and delicate balance: we look upon the Torah and acknowledge that it does hold prophetic value — but our goal is not that prophetic value. Our goal is to fulfill the mitzvah, and that is how we worship Hashem. The prophetic value is the fruit that follows, not the prize we reach for.

對華人信徒而言，這項教導有著解放性的力量。它把我們從那種「被動觀望末世、為『預言應驗』暗自興奮」的姿態中釋放出來，轉向一條更安靜、也更有份量的道路：盡心事奉神、活出妥拉的倫理。應許必定成就——這一點神已經應許了；而什麼時候成就，神竟然交在我們順服的手中。 For Chinese believers, this teaching is liberating. It frees us from the posture of “passively watching the end times and quietly thrilling that ‘prophecy is being fulfilled,’” and turns us toward a quieter, weightier path: serving God with all our heart and living out the Torah's ethics. The promise will be fulfilled — God has already pledged that; and when it is fulfilled He has, astonishingly, placed into the hands of our obedience.

「妥拉是一本預言書嗎？絕對是。它的本質是預言嗎？不是——它的本質，是倫理與 Musar。所以我們持守妥拉，不是為了讓預言實現；我們事奉哈舍姆——而正是這份事奉，催促了救贖（geulah）的來到。」 “Is the Torah a prophetic book? Absolutely. Is its essence prophecy? No — its essence is the ethics and the Musar. So we keep the Torah not to make prophecy come true; we serve Hashem — and it is that very service which hastens the geulah, the redemption.”
