國度的光景 The State of the Kingdom

每年國家領袖會向人民發表「國情咨文」。但在這吹角節（Rosh Hashanah）為君王加冕的日子，麥基翁牧師邀請全世界一同檢視一件更重要的事：神國度的光景如何？答案是——這國度堅立、永遠長存，而君王即將回來。 Each year a nation's leader gives a “State of the Union.” But on this day of Rosh Hashanah — the coronation of the King — Pastor McKeown invites the whole world to take stock of something greater: what is the state of the Kingdom of God? The answer: it stands, it is forever, and the King is on His way.

麥基翁牧師 · Ahavat Ammi 事工 Pastor McKeown · Ahavat Ammi Ministries
2026 · 5 · 25 · 約 10 分鐘閱讀~10 min read · ▶ 觀看 · 國度的光景Watch · The State of the Kingdom ·

在麥基翁牧師的國家，憲法規定總統每年一月要向人民發表「國情咨文」(State of the Union)，述說國家現今的光景。但這天，全世界數百個城市同步連線，不是要聽某一個國家的光景，而是要明白一件更要緊的事：神國度（מַלְכוּת malchut）的光景如何？麥基翁牧師站在錫安所傳出的號角聲中，宣告一個既古老又迫近的答案——國度堅立、永遠長存，而君王正在路上，並且快要來了。今天，是我們甘心為君王加冕的日子。 In Pastor McKeown's country, the constitution requires the president to give a yearly “State of the Union” describing where the nation stands. But on this day, with hundreds of cities around the world linked together, the question is not the state of any one country, but something far greater: what is the state of the Kingdom of God (מַלְכוּת malchut)? Standing inside the sound of the shofar going out from Zion, Pastor McKeown declares an answer at once ancient and imminent — the Kingdom stands, it is forever, and the King is on His way, and He is coming soon. Today is the day we willingly crown the King.

一、國度的光景 ── 「國情咨文」之上的「國度咨文」I. The State of the Kingdom — A “State of the Union” Above All Unions

麥基翁牧師以一個對比開場：人間的領袖每年發表「國情咨文」，但全世界的信徒真正需要知道的，不是各自國家的光景，而是神國度的光景。而聖經自己給了答案。他一口氣引用了一連串經文，像在朗讀君王的就職宣言—— Pastor McKeown opens with a contrast: earthly leaders give a yearly “State of the Union,” but what believers across the world truly need to know is not the state of their own countries — it is the state of the Kingdom of God. And Scripture itself gives the answer. He reads out a cascade of verses like an inauguration proclamation —

「耶和華啊，尊大、能力、榮耀、強勝、威嚴都是你的；凡天上地下的都是你的；國度也是你的，並且你為至高，為萬有之首。」——歷代志上 29:11 “Yours, O LORD, is the greatness and the power and the glory and the majesty and the splendor, for everything in heaven and earth is yours. Yours, O LORD, is the kingdom; you are exalted as head above all.”— 1 Chronicles 29:11
「耶和華已經在天上立定寶座，祂的國統管萬有。」——詩篇 103:19 “The LORD has established his throne in the heavens, and his kingdom rules over all.”— Psalm 103:19

他又引但以理書 2:44——「天上的神必另立一國，永不敗壞……卻要存到永遠」；引撒迦利亞書 14:9——「耶和華必作全地的王；那日耶和華必為獨一無二的，祂的名也是獨一無二的」；又引詩篇 45:6——「神啊，你的寶座是永永遠遠的。」結論只有一句，他重複了兩遍：「國度的光景，極其美好。」而且國度是永遠的，並且快要來了。 He cites Daniel 2:44 — “the God of heaven will set up a kingdom that shall never be destroyed… and it shall stand forever”; Zechariah 14:9 — “the LORD will be king over all the earth; on that day the LORD will be one and his name one”; and Psalm 45:6 — “Your throne, O God, is forever and ever.” The conclusion is one line, repeated twice: “The state of the Kingdom is great.” And the Kingdom is forever — and it is coming soon.

二、君王降生 ── 政權必擔在祂的肩頭上II. A Child Is Born — The Government on His Shoulders

麥基翁牧師引用以賽亞書 9:6-7——這是會堂每個安息日都會誦讀的經文：「因有一嬰孩為我們而生，有一子賜給我們；政權必擔在祂的肩頭上……祂的政權與平安必加增無窮，在大衛的寶座上治理祂的國，以公平公義使國堅定穩固，從今直到永遠。」這位嬰孩，就是那位君王（מֶלֶךְ melech）。 Pastor McKeown turns to Isaiah 9:6-7 — the very passage read in the synagogue every Shabbat: “Unto us a child is born, to us a son is given, and the government shall be on his shoulders… Of the increase of his government and of peace there will be no end, on the throne of David and over his kingdom, to establish it and uphold it with justice and righteousness from this time forth and forevermore.” This child is the King — the melech (מֶלֶךְ).

國度其實早已啟動，只是這故事有一個次序。麥基翁牧師回到耶書亞所頒的「大使命」：先從耶路撒冷起，然後是撒瑪利亞，再到地極（徒 1:8）。這意味著——國度起初是賜給猶太人的，接著賜給混合猶太血統的撒瑪利亞人，最後賜給全地的列國。 The Kingdom was in fact set in motion long ago — but the story has an order. Pastor McKeown returns to the Great Commission Yeshua gave: first Jerusalem, then Samaria, then the ends of the earth (Acts 1:8). This means the Kingdom was given first to the Jewish people, then to the people of mixed Jewish heritage in Samaria, and at last to all the nations of the earth.

於是他向觀眾說出一句令人動容的話：在座大多數人——也是此刻收看的大多數人——都不是出於以色列的子民，我們是被接上的枝子（羅 11）。然而正是我們，得著了與猶太弟兄姊妹一同為以色列的君王加冕的尊榮。「這是何等奇妙的特權！」 Then he says something moving: most of those gathered — and most watching right now — do not come from the people of Israel. We are a grafted-in branch (Romans 11). And yet it is precisely we who are given the privilege of joining our Jewish brothers and sisters to crown the King of Israel. “How amazing that we have this privilege!”

三、加冕，還是再次戲弄？III. To Crown Him — or to Mock Him Again?

君王正在路上。然而神的仇敵想要戲弄祂。麥基翁牧師讓我們想起耶書亞受死時所受的戲弄：他們給祂戴的不是金冠，而是荊棘的冠冕；給祂披上紫袍來譏笑祂；把一根葦子放在祂手中當作權杖。但當我們的君王再來時——祂口中要出來一把利劍（啟 19:15），祂要擊潰神一切的仇敵。 The King is on His way. Yet the enemies of God want to mock Him. Pastor McKeown recalls the mockery Yeshua endured at His execution: they crowned Him not with gold but with a crown of thorns; they draped a robe on Him to scorn Him; they put a staff in His hand as a mock scepter. But when our King returns — a sword will come from His mouth (Revelation 19:15), and He will decimate the enemies of God.

今天的戲弄換了形式。麥基翁牧師指出：神的仇敵想要把君王從祂的猶太身分中剝離，想要把祂與聖經分割，想把君王變成某種「政治革命家」，拿祂為他們的宣傳服務。但他斬釘截鐵地說：「我們的君王不能被人利用為人的目的；是我們的君王使用我們，成就祂的目的。」 Today's mockery takes a different form. Pastor McKeown warns: the enemies of God want to detach the King from His Jewish identity, to separate Him from Scripture, to turn the King into some kind of “political revolutionary,” to use Him for their propaganda. But he says it plainly: “Our King cannot be used for human purposes. It is our King who uses us for His purposes.”

他把這話帶進當下的文化場景：今天在他的國家、在世界許多地方，人們為了某項主張而「單膝下跪」，自以為清醒覺悟；也有人說「我為國歌肅立」。但有一天，我們不再為某項運動、某個人、或某個人造的國家示威——我們要向萬王之王、萬主之主宣誓效忠。「萬膝必跪拜，萬口必稱頌」（賽 45:23）。 He brings this into the present cultural moment: today in his country, and across the world, people “take a knee” for a cause and think themselves awake; others say “I stand for the anthem.” But a day is coming when we will not demonstrate for a cause, a man, or a man-made nation — we will swear allegiance to the King of kings and Lord of lords. “To Me every knee shall bow, and every tongue swear allegiance” (Isaiah 45:23).

四、無人能廢的話 ── 君王不是被選出來的IV. A Word No One Can Tear — The King Is Not Elected

麥基翁牧師用他自己國家的政治，反襯出這位君王的獨一無二。在他的國家，人們投票選領袖；但君王不需要選票——無論人喜不喜歡，祂就是王。人說「人人都可以當總統」，但我們的君王是無人能與祂競選的，沒有人能站出來與祂對抗。祂不必受賄、不虧欠任何人；祂不能被彈劾、不能被推翻；祂不需要第二輪選舉，也不需要去迎合不屬於祂的文化——相反，祂邀請我們進入祂的文化。 Pastor McKeown uses his own nation's politics to set off the King's uniqueness. In his country, people vote for a leader; but there is no voting for the King — like it or not, He is the King. People say “anyone can grow up to be president,” yet our King runs unopposed; no one can stand against Him. He takes no bribes and is beholden to none; He cannot be impeached or overthrown; He needs no runoff election, and He does not pander to cultures not His own — instead, He invites us into His culture.

接著他講了兩個真實的對照。當美國總統發表國情咨文時，2009 年，一位反對黨議員約·威爾遜在演說中當眾喊出「你說謊！」——但我們君王的話是真實的。又如今年一月，當總統演說結束後，眾議院議長南希·裴洛西站在他身後，當眾撕毀了演說稿——但我們君王的話就是真理；天地都要廢去，祂的話卻一句不廢去（太 24:35）。沒有人能撕毀我們君王的話。 Then he draws two real contrasts. When the U.S. president gave the State of the Union in 2009, a congressman from the opposing party shouted “You lie!” during the speech — but our King's word is true. And when, this past January, the president finished his address, the Speaker of the House tore up her copy of the speech as she stood behind him — but our King's word is truth; heaven and earth will pass away before any of His words pass away (Matthew 24:35). No one tears up the word of our King.

五、甘心加冕 ── 「現在來敬拜」V. Crown Him Willingly — “Come, Now Is the Time to Worship”

麥基翁牧師把抉擇擺在我們面前：這個吹角節（Rosh Hashanah），我們有一個選擇——我們可以現在甘心為君王加冕，或者等祂來時被迫承認祂的王權。他引用一首詩歌〈來，現在是敬拜的時候〉中的一句歌詞：「有一天萬口都要承認你是神，有一天人人都要俯伏；但最大的賞賜，仍存留給那些如今甘心揀選你的人。」 Pastor McKeown sets the choice before us: this Rosh Hashanah, we have a choice — we can willingly crown the King now, or be forced to recognize His kingship when He comes. He quotes the song “Come, Now Is the Time to Worship”: “One day every tongue will confess You are God, one day every knee will bow; still, the greatest treasure remains for those who gladly choose You now.”

他提醒我們：就連鬼魔，最終也要咬牙切齒地承認耶書亞是主，將榮耀歸給父（腓 2:10-11）。所以，弟兄姊妹，讓我們甘心地為君王加冕。這是悔改回轉（תְּשׁוּבָה teshuvah）的呼召——不是等到被迫，而是趁今日，主動把自己交在君王的手中。 He reminds us: even the demons will one day confess, through clenched teeth, that Yeshua is Lord, to the glory of the Father (Philippians 2:10-11). So, brothers and sisters, let us crown the King willingly. This is the call of teshuvah (תְּשׁוּבָה, return) — not waiting to be forced, but today, of our own will, placing ourselves into the King's hands.

六、君王正在路上 ── 我們有國度的工要作VI. The King Is on His Way — We Have Kingdom Work to Do

如果君王正在路上，那麼你我就有工要作。麥基翁牧師引用施洗約翰的呼召：「預備主的道，修直祂的路」（賽 40:3；太 3:3）。他解釋：這不是說路是彎的——而是說路本是直的，卻有高低起伏，而你我有責任去修直君王的路。不僅如此，我們還要作君王的開路先鋒，跑在君王行列的前頭，宣告君王即將來臨。 If the King is on His way, then you and I have work to do. Pastor McKeown invokes John the Baptist's cry: “Prepare the way of the Lord, make His paths straight” (Isaiah 40:3; Matthew 3:3). He explains: this does not mean the path is crooked — it means the path is straight but has its ups and downs, and you and I have the responsibility to straighten the King's path. More than that, we are to be the heralds of the coming King, running ahead of the kingly procession to proclaim that the King is on His way.

他見證了當下的奇事：那一週，全世界驚奇地看著以色列簽署《亞伯拉罕協議》(Abraham Accords)，與阿拉伯聯合大公國、與巴林王國締結和平。人們驚訝於「幾個人竟能帶來和平」。麥基翁牧師說：若幾個人聚在一起，就能在地上促成和平，那位「和平的君」（שַׂר־שָׁלוֹם）來的時候，要為全世界帶來何等的和平！ He points to a present wonder: that week, the world watched in amazement as Israel signed the Abraham Accords, making peace with the United Arab Emirates and the Kingdom of Bahrain. People marveled that peace could come through the actions of a few men. Pastor McKeown says: if a few men meeting together can make peace on earth, what will the Prince of Peace (שַׂר־שָׁלוֹם, Sar Shalom) do when He brings peace to the entire world?

但他也帶著沙皮拉拉比的提醒，把話說得嚴肅：這日子被稱為「耶和華大而可畏的日子」（珥 2:31）。對一些人是大喜的日子，對另一些人卻是可怕的日子。不要忽略在你生命中為君王加冕——免得太遲。一旦太遲，你就再沒有甘心加冕祂的機會了。 But, echoing Rabbi Shapira, he speaks soberly: this day is called “the great and terrible day of the LORD” (Joel 2:31). For some it will be great; for others, terrible. Do not neglect to crown the King in your life — before it is too late. Once it is too late, you will no longer have the choice to crown Him willingly.

七、正確的問題 ── 「祂來時，會不會找到我們忠心？」VII. The Right Question — “Will He Find Us Faithful?”

麥基翁牧師指出我們這世代的問題：我們只是在等候君王。我們看著手錶、尋找各樣的兆頭，不停地問：「祂幾時來？祂幾時來？」但這不是正確的問題。正確的問題是：當祂真的來到時，會不會找到我們已經預備好？會不會找到我們忠心？ Pastor McKeown names the problem of our generation: we are merely waiting for the King. We look at our watch, search for signs, and keep asking, “When is He coming? When is He coming?” That is not the right question. The right question is: when He does come, will He find us ready? Will He find us faithful?

他直指更深的問題：在我們這世代，我們常常不是在為那國度作工，而是在為我們自己的小國度作工——我們憂心會眾的人數、憂心信息能不能「爆紅」。我們究竟是在為「我的國度」作工，還是在為「神的國度」作工？我們是在為君王作工，還是只為自己？這需要我們在神的靈這「顯微鏡」下，作一次誠實的自我省察。 He presses to a deeper issue: in our generation, we are often not working for the Kingdom but for our own little kingdoms — anxious about attendance in our congregations, anxious about our messages “going viral.” Are we working for OUR kingdom or for THE Kingdom? For the King, or only for ourselves? It calls for an honest self-examination under the “microscope” of God's Spirit.

他也加上一個盼望的維度：我們的君王不被瘟疫所拖延，不懼怕人的暴動，不被暴力所阻擋，不因全球疫情或經濟困頓而遲延。相反——按使徒彼得（西門·磯法 Shimon Kepha）所寫的，我們竟能「催促」君王的來臨（彼後 3:12）。所以問題就回到我們自己身上：我是在作國度的工，還是在旁邊袖手旁觀？ He adds a note of hope: our King is not delayed by pandemic, not afraid of people rioting, not deterred by violence, not held back by worldwide plagues or economic struggle. On the contrary — as the Apostle Peter (Shimon Kepha) writes, we can even “hasten” the coming of the King (2 Peter 3:12). So the question comes back to us: am I doing the work of the Kingdom, or sitting on the sidelines?

八、向萬民的呼召 ── 「萬名之上的名」VIII. A Call to the Nations — “The Name Above All Names”

麥基翁牧師轉向那些尚未跟隨這位彌賽亞的觀眾，語氣坦率而有憐憫。他說：我相信佛陀是個好人，他的教導裡也有一些真理，但那不是「那真理」。古蘭經中或許有一些正面的東西可說，但古蘭經不是妥拉（תּוֹרָה Torah），阿拉不是哈希姆（הַשֵּׁם HaShem），不是亞伯拉罕、以撒、雅各的神。各宗教「都一樣」的說法，不是真理。 Pastor McKeown turns to those watching who do not yet follow this Messiah, candid but compassionate. He says: I'm sure the Buddha was a kind man, and there is some truth in his teaching — but it is not the truth. There may be positive things one could say about the Quran, but the Quran is not the Torah (תּוֹרָה), and Allah is not HaShem (הַשֵּׁם), not the God of Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob. The claim that “all religions are equal” is not the truth.

他特別向穆斯林背景的人說話：在你的書中也提到一位，祂的希伯來名字是耶書亞（Yeshua），你的書甚至稱祂為彌賽亞——只是它說穆罕默德在祂之上。麥基翁牧師說：這不是真理。因為一聽見耶書亞的名，萬膝都要跪拜，萬口都要承認祂是君王、是彌賽亞——連穆罕默德也要向我們的君王下拜。這就是我們要傳開的、關於君王與祂將臨之國度的真實信息。 He speaks especially to those from a Muslim background: your book mentions one whose Hebrew name is Yeshua, and even calls Him the Messiah — but it places Muhammad above Him. Pastor McKeown says: that is not the truth. For at the name of Yeshua every knee will bow and every tongue confess that He is the King, the Messiah — even Muhammad will bow to our King. This is the true message of the King and His coming Kingdom that we must spread.

九、結語 ── 「我們歡迎你，奉主的名來的是應當稱頌的」IX. Closing — “Baruch HaBa B'Shem Adonai”

麥基翁牧師以禱告作結。他為美國的東岸——包括那座自誇為「屬世」、甚至自誇為「無神」的紐約城——禱告，宣告耶書亞是這一切城市的君王。他求神使我們不再戲弄君王的名、不再把荊棘的冠冕戴在祂頭上，而是獻上祂配得的君王的冠冕。「我們把自己從寶座上挪開，把我們國家的領袖從寶座上挪開」；願今日所聽見的羊角號聲（שׁוֹפָר shofar），從錫安一直到地的四極，喚醒我們的靈。 Pastor McKeown closes in prayer. He prays for the East Coast of the United States — including New York, a city that sometimes prides itself on being worldly, even godless — declaring that Yeshua is King over them all. He asks that we would no longer mock the King's name, no longer place a crown of thorns on His head, but give Him the kingly crown He deserves. “We take ourselves off the throne; we take our nation's leaders off the throne.” May the sound of the shofar (שׁוֹפָר) heard today wake our spirits, from Zion to the four corners of the earth.

「我們甘心俯伏在你面前，甘心為你加冕為王。我們說：『奉耶和華名來的，是應當稱頌的（בָּרוּךְ הַבָּא בְּשֵׁם יְהוָה Baruch HaBa B'Shem Adonai）。』我們今早為你加冕；我們說：『主耶書亞啊，我願你來！』你不單是我們的君王，更是那位拯救我們的主——願凡勞苦擔重擔的，都在你裡面得享安息。」 “We willingly bow before You; we willingly crown You as King. We say, ‘Baruch HaBa B'Shem Adonai — Blessed is He who comes in the name of the LORD’ (בָּרוּךְ הַבָּא בְּשֵׁם יְהוָה). We crown You this morning; we say, ‘Even so, come, Messiah Yeshua.’ You are not only our King but the One who saves us — and may all who are weary find rest for their souls in You.”
