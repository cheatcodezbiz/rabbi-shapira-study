準備新婦的三重聖潔 ── 時、地、行動 Preparing the Bride — Holy Time, Holy Place, Holy Action

「新婦預備好了嗎？」── 這是萬問之問。在「新婦之夜 5786」全球直播的核心 67 分鐘裡，沙皮拉拉比博士帶我們從西奈山的kolot（聲音）走到啟示錄 19 章的婚宴；從拉比 Akiva 的ketarim（字冠）走到拉比 Jonathan Sacks 的「天地之間的婚約」；從耶書亞「為他們的緣故，我將自己分別為聖」走到我們每一個人——猶太人、外邦人、彌賽亞猶太人——今晚就可以做的一個聖潔行動。 “Is the Bride ready yet?” — the question of all questions. In the 67-minute heart of the worldwide Night of the Bride 5786 broadcast, Rabbi Dr. Shapira walks us from the kolot (voices) at Mount Sinai to the marriage supper of Revelation 19; from Rabbi Akiva's ketarim (crowns on the letters) to the late Rabbi Jonathan Sacks' “marriage contract between heaven and earth”; from Yeshua's “for their sake, I consecrate Myself” to one concrete holy action that you — Jew, Gentile, Messianic Jew — can begin tonight.

「新婦預備好了嗎？」是萬問之問。這就是沙皮拉拉比博士在「新婦之夜 5786」(Milchemet HaChatunah，「婚禮之戰」) 全球直播核心 67 分鐘裡，要全世界 ── 從中國到韓國、從以色列到拉丁美洲、從非洲到澳洲 ── 一起跪下來問的問題。他帶我們從出埃及記 19 章的西奈山開始：人民「從遠方站立」(vaya'amdu merachok)，因為他們看見了聲音(ra'u et ha-kolot)。他帶我們經過拉比 Akiva 在每一個字母上看到的ketarim（字冠），帶我們聽到耶書亞在約翰福音 17 章親口說的「我將自己分別為聖」(Ani Akadesh)，再帶我們到啟示錄 19 章的婚宴 ── 那位身穿「細麻衣，光明潔白」的新婦。5786 年的這一個 Shavuot，新婦預備好了嗎？沙皮拉拉比的回答不是抽象的：是三個極具體的維度──聖時 (Zman Kodesh)、聖地 (Makom Kodesh)、聖行動 (Ma'aseh Kodesh)，以及一個今晚就可以做的、會被神看為聖潔行動的小小選擇。 “Is the Bride ready yet?” This is the question of all questions. It is the question Rabbi Dr. Itzhak Shapira asks the world — China, Korea, Israel, Latin America, Africa, Australia — at the heart of the 67-minute main teaching of the worldwide Night of the Bride 5786 (Milchemet HaChatunah, “the wedding war”). He walks us back to Exodus 19, to Mount Sinai, where the people “stood at a distance” (vaya'amdu merachok) because they saw the voices (ra'u et ha-kolot). He walks us through the ketarim — the crowns Rabbi Akiva read on every letter — and through Yeshua's own words in John 17, “I consecrate Myself” (Ani Akadesh). He carries us forward to Revelation 19 and the marriage supper of the Lamb, to the bride clothed in “fine linen, bright and clean.” On this Shavuot of 5786, is the Bride ready? Rabbi Shapira's answer is not abstract: three concrete dimensions — Holy Time (Zman Kodesh), Holy Place (Makom Kodesh), Holy Action (Ma'aseh Kodesh) — and one small, choosable act tonight that God will count as holy.

一、5786 的提問 ── 新婦預備好了嗎？I. The Question of 5786 — Is the Bride Ready Yet?

沙皮拉拉比是以一段不尋常的呼喊開場的：「今晚我們會有神蹟。我們會有奇妙的景象。我們會有神蹟。」(Tonight we will have signs. We will have wonders. We will have miracles.) 這不是煽情話，而是對 Shavuot 真正意義的回歸 ── 因為 Shavuot 在拉比傳統中不是一個普通的節期，它是婚禮日(Yom HaChatunah，יום החתונה)，是神與以色列在西奈山下立下永約的那一日。「新婦之夜」(Leil HaKallah，ליל הכלה) 這個名字本身就是這個婚禮神學的延伸：今晚的整個世界 ── 中國、韓國、澳洲、以色列、加薩戰火的邊緣、紐約曼哈頓的廚房 ── 都被邀請進入這個婚禮現場，一起對陛下(HaMelech) 說「我願意」。 Rabbi Shapira opens with an unusual cry: “Tonight we will have signs. We will have wonders. We will have miracles.” This is not theatre. It is a return to what Shavuot really is — because in rabbinic tradition Shavuot is not just another feast: it is the Wedding Day (Yom HaChatunah, יום החתונה), the day on which the LORD cut His eternal covenant with Israel at the foot of Mount Sinai. The very name “Night of the Bride” (Leil HaKallah, ליל הכלה) is an extension of this wedding theology: tonight the whole world — China, Korea, Australia, Israel, the edges of the Gaza warzone, a kitchen in Manhattan — is invited into the wedding hall to say “I do” to the King (HaMelech).

但拉比立刻把這個歡慶的場景轉成一個極銳利的問題。他引用拉比 Jonathan Elowitz 的一句話：「新婦預備好了嗎？」(Is the Bride ready yet?) ── 這句話不是禮節性的提問，而是核對。因為啟示錄 19:7 明明寫著：「羔羊婚娶的時候到了，新婦也自己預備好了」── 換句話說，新婦必須先自己預備好，婚禮才能舉行。沙皮拉拉比的整段教導，就是要逼著今晚每一位觀眾、每一位讀者問自己：在 5786 年的這個 Shavuot，我那一份「預備」做到了沒有？ But the rabbi immediately turns this celebratory scene into a sharp question. He invokes his student Rabbi Jonathan Elowitz's framing: “Is the Bride ready yet?” The line is not rhetorical; it is an audit. Revelation 19:7 says it plainly: “the marriage of the Lamb has come, and His bride has made herself ready.” In other words, the Bride must first ready herself — only then does the wedding take place. Rabbi Shapira's whole teaching is built to push every viewer, every reader, to ask one question of themselves on this Shavuot of 5786: have I done my part of the readying?

「羔羊婚娶的時候到了，新婦也自己預備好了。就賜她穿上光明潔白的細麻衣 ── 這細麻衣就是聖徒所行的義。」── 啟示錄 19:7-8 “The marriage of the Lamb has come, and His bride has made herself ready. It was given to her to clothe herself in fine linen, bright and clean — for the fine linen is the righteous deeds of God's holy people.”— Revelation 19:7-8

這段經文裡，「細麻衣」(byssinon) ＝ 「聖徒所行的義」(ta dikaiōmata tōn hagiōn)。也就是說：新婦的婚紗，不是別人替她穿上的某種神秘披風，而是她自己日復一日用聖潔的生活所織成的。「她也自己預備好了」── 這個「自己」(heautēn) 在希臘文裡是反身代名詞，強調「她親自」。神不會強迫祂的新婦穿衣服上轎；祂等候她自願、主動地把自己分別為聖。沙皮拉拉比一再強調：「預備」不是被動的等候，是一個動作 (ma'aseh)。 In this text, “fine linen” (byssinon) = “the righteous deeds of the saints” (ta dikaiōmata tōn hagiōn). That is: the Bride's wedding dress is not some mystical robe placed on her by someone else — it is what she herself weaves, day by day, out of a holy life. “She has made herself ready” — the Greek reflexive heautēn stresses “she, herself, personally.” God does not drag His bride to the chuppah; He waits for her to willingly, actively consecrate herself. Rabbi Shapira pounds this point: “readiness” is not passive waiting; it is an act (ma'aseh).

二、Shavuot 不是節期，是婚禮 ── 拉比 Sacks 的「天地之間的婚約」II. Shavuot Is Not a Festival — It Is a Wedding. Rabbi Sacks' “Marriage Contract Between Heaven and Earth”

沙皮拉拉比在這一段裡明確地引用了已故的拉比 Jonathan Sacks 爵士(z"l，זצ"ל，「願義人的記念蒙福」) ── 二十一世紀英語世界最重要的猶太思想家之一，前英國聯合希伯來會堂的首席拉比。拉比 Sacks 在他多年的妥拉教導中反覆地闡明：Shavuot 是「天地之間的婚約」(the marriage contract between heaven and earth)。整個西奈山下發生的事件 ── 雷聲、閃電、號筒、雲、火、人民的「我願意」(na'aseh v'nishma，「我們要遵行，且要聽從」) ── 不是一個普通的「立法日」，而是一場神聖婚禮的完整禮儀。 Rabbi Shapira explicitly invokes the late Rabbi Lord Jonathan Sacks (z"l, זצ"ל, “may the memory of the righteous be a blessing”) — one of the most important Jewish thinkers of the English-speaking world in the twenty-first century, and former Chief Rabbi of the United Hebrew Congregations of the Commonwealth. Across years of Torah teaching, Rabbi Sacks repeatedly framed it: Shavuot is “the marriage contract between heaven and earth.” Everything that happened at the foot of Mount Sinai — thunder, lightning, shofar, cloud, fire, the people's “I do” (na'aseh v'nishma, “we will do and we will hear”) — is not just some “lawgiving ceremony.” It is the full liturgy of a sacred wedding.

在 Sacks 的讀法裡，每一個元素都對應於一場真實的猶太婚禮： In Sacks' reading, every element corresponds to a real Jewish wedding:

西奈山＝chuppah (חוּפָּה)，婚禮的篷子（拉比們稱西奈山「像一個傘」覆蓋在以色列頭上）；
妥拉＝ketubah (כְּתוּבָּה)，婚禮的契約，新郎承諾於新娘的書面條件；
「na'aseh v'nishma」(נַעֲשֶׂה וְנִשְׁמָע)＝「我願意」，新娘對新郎所說的應允；
kolot 與閃電＝婚禮的音樂、號筒、舞蹈、與歡呼；
第三日的火與煙＝Shechinah，神的榮光降臨，把這場聯合「shamayim u'va-aretz」（在天上、也在地上）封印起來。 Mount Sinai = the chuppah (חוּפָּה), the wedding canopy (the sages say Sinai was held “like an umbrella” over Israel's head);
the Torah = the ketubah (כְּתוּבָּה), the marriage contract — the groom's written commitments to his bride;
“na'aseh v'nishma” (נַעֲשֶׂה וְנִשְׁמָע) = “I do”, the bride's assent;
the kolot and lightning = the wedding music, the shofar, the dancing, the shouts of joy;
the fire and smoke of the third day = the Shechinah, the divine glory sealing the union shamayim u'va-aretz — in heaven and on earth.

這個讀法的重要性是：整個聖經從西奈山到啟示錄 19 章是同一場婚禮的兩個時刻。西奈山是訂婚(erusin，אֵרוּסִין)；羔羊的婚宴是圓房(nissuin，נִשּׂוּאִין)。在猶太傳統中，這兩個時刻可以相隔多年。沙皮拉拉比說：我們現在就活在訂婚與圓房之間的這段時間裡 ── 也就是新婦預備自己的時間。每一個 Shavuot，神都在問祂的百姓同一個問題：「新婦預備好了嗎？」 Why this matters: the whole Bible from Sinai to Revelation 19 is two moments of the same wedding. Sinai is the betrothal (erusin, אֵרוּסִין); the marriage supper of the Lamb is the consummation (nissuin, נִשּׂוּאִין). In Jewish tradition these two moments can be separated by years. Rabbi Shapira says it directly: we live right now in the interval between the erusin and the nissuin — and that interval is precisely the bride's time of self-preparation. Every Shavuot, God asks His people the same question: “Is the Bride ready yet?”

拉比 Sacks 在他最後幾年的講道中提到一個極深的洞見：神的這場婚禮是有耐心的。神不跳過訂婚而直接強行圓房；祂等候新婦自願穿上「光明潔白的細麻衣」。5786 年的這個 Shavuot 也是一樣 ── 神不是強迫者，祂是等候者。而新婦的「預備」，按拉比沙皮拉的整段教導，就是三個維度：聖時、聖地、聖行動。 In his last years, Rabbi Sacks returned to one deep insight: God's wedding has patience. God does not skip the erusin and force the nissuin; He waits for the Bride to voluntarily clothe herself in the “fine linen, bright and clean.” The Shavuot of 5786 is the same — God is not a coercer; He is a waiter. And the Bride's “readying,” in Rabbi Shapira's whole teaching, is exactly three dimensions: Holy Time, Holy Place, Holy Action.

三、聖時 (Zman Kodesh) ── 「父啊，時候到了」III. Holy Time (Zman Kodesh) — “Father, the Hour Has Come”

沙皮拉拉比帶我們進入第一個維度：聖時 (Zman Kodesh，זְמַן קֹדֶשׁ)。這個觀念對華人讀者可能是最陌生的，因為我們的時間觀通常是線性的、世俗的 ── 一秒鐘是一秒鐘，一個小時是一個小時，沒有什麼分別。但聖經的時間觀不是這樣。在希伯來的世界裡，「時」本身可以是聖潔的。每週的 Shabbat、每年的 Shavuot、每四十九年的禧年 ── 這些都是被神「分別出來」的「moed」(מוֹעֵד，「神所定的會晤之時」)。 Rabbi Shapira leads us into the first dimension: Holy Time (Zman Kodesh, זְמַן קֹדֶשׁ). This is probably the strangest concept for Chinese readers, because our default time-sense is linear and secular — one second is one second, one hour is one hour, all equivalent. The biblical time-sense is not like that. In the Hebrew world, “time” itself can be holy. Every weekly Shabbat, every annual Shavuot, every forty-ninth-year Jubilee — each is a moed (מוֹעֵד, “an appointed time of meeting with God”) that God has personally set apart.

耶書亞自己活在這個時間觀裡。沙皮拉拉比直接引用約翰福音 17:1 ── 耶書亞「舉目望天」(nasa et eynav la-shamayim，נָשָׂא אֶת עֵינָיו לַשָּׁמַיִם) ── 然後祂說：「父啊，時候到了。」(Avi, ba'ah ha-sha'ah，אָבִי, בָּאָה הַשָּׁעָה)。希伯來文與希臘文 (elēluthen hē hōra，「那個時候已經來了」) 都用了一個極具體的「那個時辰」── 不是「某一段時間」，而是神在永恆中已經標記出來的那一刻。耶書亞知道那一刻，並且把祂自己對齊於那一刻。 Yeshua Himself lived inside this time-sense. Rabbi Shapira quotes John 17:1 directly — Yeshua “lifted up His eyes to heaven” (nasa et eynav la-shamayim, נָשָׂא אֶת עֵינָיו לַשָּׁמַיִם) — and said: “Father, the hour has come.” (Avi, ba'ah ha-sha'ah, אָבִי, בָּאָה הַשָּׁעָה). The Hebrew and the Greek (elēluthen hē hōra, “the hour has come”) both use a very specific “that hour” — not “some time,” but the moment God had marked in eternity. Yeshua knew that moment, and He aligned Himself with that moment.

這就是「聖時」的核心：不是你按你的日曆活，而是你按神的日曆活。Shavuot 之所以是「聖時」，是因為神親自把這一天分別出來，定為「陛下與祂的新婦相會的時辰」。而新婦預備自己的第一步，就是承認這個「時」是神的，進入這個「時」，把自己的日曆服在這個「時」之下。對華人基督徒來說，這個應用極為實際： This is the heart of Holy Time: you do not live by your calendar — you live by God's calendar. Shavuot is Holy Time because God Himself has set this day apart as “the appointed hour when the King meets His Bride.” The Bride's first step in readying herself is to recognise that this time belongs to God, to enter this time, to submit her own calendar to God's calendar. For Chinese Christians, the application is very practical:

學習在 Shabbat（每週五日落到週六日落）停下來。學習在 Shavuot（五旬節）為以色列守一個夜禱。學習注意 Pesach（逾越節）、Rosh HaShanah（吹角節）、Yom Kippur（贖罪日）、Sukkot（住棚節）── 因為這七個「moedim」(מוֹעֲדִים) 不是「猶太人的節日」，是陛下的婚禮邀請，發出給以色列、也發出給接到祂橄欖樹上的列國的。── 拉比沙皮拉的應用 Learn to stop on Shabbat (Friday sundown to Saturday sundown). Learn to keep a vigil on Shavuot for Israel. Pay attention to Pesach (Passover), Rosh HaShanah (Feast of Trumpets), Yom Kippur (Day of Atonement), Sukkot (Tabernacles) — because these seven moedim (מוֹעֲדִים) are not “Jewish festivals.” They are the King's wedding invitations, issued both to Israel and to the nations grafted into His olive tree.— Rabbi Shapira's application

四、聖地 (Makom Kodesh) ── 為什麼一定是西奈山？IV. Holy Place (Makom Kodesh) — Why It Had to Be Mount Sinai

第二個維度是聖地 (Makom Kodesh，מָקוֹם קֹדֶשׁ)。沙皮拉拉比帶我們回到出埃及記 19:1-2，注意一個極小的細節：「他們離了利非訂，來到西奈的曠野，就在那山下安營。」(出 19:2) 為什麼神非要把以色列帶到一個曠野、一個山下，才把妥拉給他們？為什麼不在埃及的王宮裡給？為什麼不在更舒適的肥沃之地給？ The second dimension is Holy Place (Makom Kodesh, מָקוֹם קֹדֶשׁ). Rabbi Shapira sends us back to Exodus 19:1-2, and asks us to notice a tiny detail: “They departed from Rephidim and came to the wilderness of Sinai, and camped there before the mountain.” (Ex 19:2). Why does God insist on bringing Israel into a wilderness, to the foot of a mountain, before giving them the Torah? Why not in Pharaoh's palace? Why not in some more comfortable, fertile land?

拉比們的答覆很美：因為「曠野」(midbar，מִדְבָּר) 是無主之地。曠野不屬於埃及、不屬於迦南、不屬於米甸 ── 它不屬於任何一個地上的王國。所以當神在那裡降臨時，妥拉就不屬於任何一個地上的王國，它屬於全人類。而「山」── 拉比 Akiva 與 Midrash Tanchuma 都讀到 ── 是神放下傘(kafa aleihem，「祂把山扣在他們頭上」) 的地方，是chuppah（婚禮篷）的雛形。地點本身在說話：「這是婚禮現場。」 The rabbinic answer is beautiful: because the “wilderness” (midbar, מִדְבָּר) is no-one's land. The wilderness belongs neither to Egypt, nor to Canaan, nor to Midian — it does not belong to any earthly kingdom. So when God descends there, the Torah does not belong to any earthly kingdom — it belongs to all humanity. And the “mountain” — as Rabbi Akiva and Midrash Tanchuma read it — is the place where God holds it like an umbrella (kafa aleihem, “He inverted the mountain over them”) — a prototype of the chuppah. The place itself is speaking: “This is the wedding venue.”

沙皮拉拉比延伸：耶書亞活的時候，祂選擇的地點從來不是偶然。客西馬尼園 (Gat Shemanim，「榨油」)是聖地，因為「義人在那裡被榨」。各各他 (Golgotha，「髑髏地」) 是聖地，因為拉比傳統把它與亞當被造之地連在一起 ── 第二個亞當在第一個亞當倒下的地方擔當咒詛。橄欖山 (Har HaZeitim) 是聖地，因為撒迦利亞書 14:4 應許彌賽亞要在那裡腳踏實地、改變地理。對神來說，地點是聖約的見證者。 Rabbi Shapira extends it: in Yeshua's life, the places He chose were never accidental. The Garden of Gethsemane (Gat Shemanim, “the oil press”) is holy ground — “the Righteous One is pressed there.” Golgotha (“place of the skull”) is holy ground, because rabbinic tradition links it to the place Adam was formed — the second Adam bears the curse on the very ground where the first fell. The Mount of Olives (Har HaZeitim) is holy ground, because Zechariah 14:4 promises the Messiah will set His feet there and change the geography. For God, place is a witness to covenant.

那對我們呢？拉比沙皮拉的應用：每一個家庭的飯桌都可以成為「mikdash me'at」(מִקְדָּשׁ מְעַט，「小聖所」) ── 一個小型的西奈山。當你在自己的飯桌上點起 Shabbat 的燭、念 HaMotzi（餅的祝福）、把一杯葡萄酒舉起來為 Kiddush（分別為聖）── 你的飯桌就在那一刻從「世俗」(chol，חוֹל) 變成「聖潔」(kodesh，קֹדֶשׁ)。你的家就成為橄欖樹的一根新枝，與西奈山同等地位。「聖地」不是地理問題，是奉獻問題。 And for us? Rabbi Shapira's application: every family's dinner table can become a mikdash me'at (מִקְדָּשׁ מְעַט, “a little sanctuary”) — a miniature Mount Sinai. When you light Shabbat candles, say the HaMotzi (the blessing on bread), lift a cup of wine for Kiddush (the act of setting-apart) — in that moment your table moves from “profane” (chol, חוֹל) to “holy” (kodesh, קֹדֶשׁ). Your home becomes a new branch of the olive tree, of equal standing with Mount Sinai. “Holy place” is not a question of geography — it is a question of dedication.

五、聖行動 (Ma'aseh Kodesh) ── 「為他們的緣故，我將自己分別為聖」V. Holy Action (Ma'aseh Kodesh) — “For Their Sake, I Consecrate Myself”

第三個 ── 也是最具突破性的 ── 維度，是聖行動 (Ma'aseh Kodesh，מַעֲשֵׂה קֹדֶשׁ)。沙皮拉拉比把我們直接帶到約翰福音 17:19 的耶書亞口中 ── 這是耶書亞被釘十架前夜的「大祭司禱告」： The third — and most explosive — dimension is Holy Action (Ma'aseh Kodesh, מַעֲשֵׂה קֹדֶשׁ). Rabbi Shapira leads us straight into Yeshua's mouth in John 17:19 — the “High Priestly Prayer” on the night before the crucifixion:

「我為他們的緣故，自己分別為聖 (Ani Akadesh et atzmi，אֲנִי אֲקַדֵּשׁ אֶת עַצְמִי)，叫他們也因真理 (emet，אֱמֶת) 成聖。」── 約翰福音 17:19 “For their sake I consecrate Myself (Ani Akadesh et atzmi, אֲנִי אֲקַדֵּשׁ אֶת עַצְמִי), so that they too may be sanctified in truth (emet, אֱמֶת).”— John 17:19

沙皮拉拉比讓我們停在這節經文上。他指出一個極深的神學重點：耶書亞本來就是聖潔的 ── Ukvar Kaddosh（「他已經是聖潔的」）── 那為什麼祂還要做一個「分別為聖」的動作？答案是：因為聖潔不是一個靜態的身分，是一個動詞。即使是已經聖潔的，也可以選擇更聖潔；可以進入「Kodesh Kodashim」(קֹדֶשׁ קֳדָשִׁים，「至聖所」、「聖中之聖」) 的維度。 Rabbi Shapira makes us stop on this verse. He flags a profound theological point: Yeshua was already holy — Ukvar Kaddosh (“He is already holy”) — so why does He still perform an act of consecration? Because holiness is not a static identity; it is a verb. Even what is already holy can choose deeper holiness; can enter the dimension of Kodesh Kodashim (קֹדֶשׁ קֳדָשִׁים, “the Holy of Holies,” “holy within the holy”).

這個關鍵詞 ── 「holy within holy」(Kodesh Kodashim) ── 在整段教導裡反覆出現。沙皮拉拉比說：聖經從來不把聖潔當作一個「是/不是」的開關。聖經把聖潔當作一個有層次的、可上升的、活的實體。會幕的設計本身就是這個神學的物理化： This phrase — “holy within holy” (Kodesh Kodashim) — recurs throughout the teaching. Rabbi Shapira says: Scripture never treats holiness as a binary “on/off.” Scripture treats holiness as a tiered, ascending, living reality. The physical architecture of the Tabernacle is the very embodiment of this theology:

外院(chatzer，חָצֵר) ── 已是聖潔。
聖所(ha-kodesh，הַקֹּדֶשׁ) ── 比外院更聖。
至聖所(kodesh ha-kodashim，קֹדֶשׁ הַקֳּדָשִׁים) ── 聖中之聖。 The Outer Court (chatzer, חָצֵר) — already holy.
The Holy Place (ha-kodesh, הַקֹּדֶשׁ) — holier than the court.
The Holy of Holies (kodesh ha-kodashim, קֹדֶשׁ הַקֳּדָשִׁים) — holy within the holy.

希伯來書 10:20 說，耶書亞的肉體就是那道幔子(parochet，פָּרֹכֶת) ── 隔開聖所與至聖所的那道幔子。也就是說，祂的身體本身就是從「聖」到「聖中之聖」的通道。當祂在約翰福音 17:19 說「我將自己分別為聖」時，祂是在做第二級的聖潔行動 ── 從已經聖潔升到至聖，為的是把祂的門徒、把今晚每一個聽眾，也帶進這個維度。 Hebrews 10:20 says Yeshua's flesh is the veil (parochet, פָּרֹכֶת) — the curtain dividing the Holy Place from the Holy of Holies. That is: His body itself is the passage from “holy” to “holy of holies.” When He says in John 17:19, “I consecrate Myself,” He is performing the second-tier holy action — moving from already-holy to holy-of-holies — so that His disciples, and every listener tonight, can also be carried into that dimension.

這個邏輯的應用對新婦來說極為震撼：你已經是被分別為聖的，這不代表你不能更被分別為聖。受洗了，不代表你不能再活出受洗的真實。受聖靈了，不代表你不能再被聖靈充滿。聖潔不是學歷，它不是一張你拿到手裡的證書；聖潔是一條走的路，每一天可以走得更深。沙皮拉拉比說：「我們可以在身體裡是聖潔的，但仍然可以變得更聖潔。」(We can be holy in the body, still we can become more holy.) 這就是新婦的預備。 The application for the Bride is staggering: you are already consecrated — that does not mean you cannot be more consecrated. You were baptised; that does not mean you cannot live out your baptism more fully. You received the Spirit; that does not mean you cannot be more filled with the Spirit. Holiness is not a credential; it is not a certificate in your hand. Holiness is a road you walk, and every day you can walk it deeper. Rabbi Shapira says it plainly: “We can be holy in the body, still we can become more holy.” This is the Bride's readying.

六、為什麼他們「從遠方站立」？── kolot 與被看見的聲音VI. Why Did They “Stand at a Distance”? — The Kolot and the Voices That Could Be Seen

沙皮拉拉比在這一段裡帶我們進入整段教導最深的一個謎：出埃及記 20:18 ── 「眾百姓看見雷轟 (kolot，קוֹלוֹת)、閃電、號角聲，並山上冒煙；他們就站在遠處」(vaya'amdu merachok，וַיַּעַמְדוּ מֵרָחֹק)。為什麼他們在這個應該是圓房的時刻、新郎終於降臨的時刻、最該挨近的時刻 ── 反而後退？ Rabbi Shapira leads us into the deepest puzzle of the entire teaching: Exodus 20:18 — “All the people saw the thunderings (kolot, קוֹלוֹת), the lightnings, the sound of the shofar, and the mountain smoking; and they stood at a distance” (vaya'amdu merachok, וַיַּעַמְדוּ מֵרָחֹק). Why did they, at the moment that was supposed to be the consummation — the moment the Groom finally descended, the moment of all moments to draw near — step back instead?

注意經文裡的兩個極特殊的細節，是拉比 Akiva 與 Kli Yakar 等中世紀注釋家反覆地解開的： Notice two highly unusual details in the text — the very details Rabbi Akiva and the medieval commentator Kli Yakar repeatedly worked open:

「看見聲音」──不是「聽見」聲音 (shamu)，是「看見」聲音 (ra'u)。
「kolot」── 在希伯來文裡少了一個 vav (kolt, קֹלֹת)，是「缺寫」(chaser) 的形式。 “They saw the voices” — not “heard” (shamu), but “saw” (ra'u).
“kolot” — in Hebrew, missing one vav (kolt, קֹלֹת) — the “defective” (chaser) spelling.

Kli Yakar 解釋：「kolot」在西奈山這一節裡缺一個 vav，是要告訴我們：這些「聲音」不是自然的聲音。自然的聲音會減弱── 你越走遠，越聽不見。但這些「kolot」越來越強── 出埃及記 19:19 說「號角聲漸漸地高而又高」(holech v'chazek me'od)。物理的聲音不會這樣做。這是超自然的聲音 ── 是shefa（神聖湧流，שֶׁפַע）── 是Shechinah本身。 Kli Yakar explains: the missing vav in “kolot” at Sinai is there to tell us that these “voices” were not natural sounds. Natural sounds weaken with distance — the further away, the fainter. But these kolot kept growing — Exodus 19:19 says “the sound of the shofar grew louder and louder and louder” (holech v'chazek me'od). Physical sound does not behave that way. These were supernatural voices — they were shefa (divine effluence, שֶׁפַע) — they were the Shechinah itself.

而「看見聲音」── 拉比們解：因為這時的「kolot」是可見的閃光，是光的形式，是從神口中出來的字本身穿過空氣化作可見的火焰。Midrash Mekhilta 比喻：「就像鐵匠的火花迸開到四面八方」── 每一個字都燃燒著，每一個 vav 都發光。這就是拉比 Akiva 一生研究的 ketarim（字母上的「冠」） ── 他在每一個字母的上方讀出整個律法的奧秘。耶書亞自己在馬太福音 5:18 提到這個傳統：「律法的一點一畫也不能廢去」── 「一點」原文是 iota，對應希伯來的最小字母 yod；「一畫」原文是 keraia，正是拉比 Akiva 的 keter（字冠）。 And “seeing the voices” — the rabbis answer: because at that moment the kolot were visible flashes, forms of light, the very words from God's mouth crossing the air and becoming visible flame. The Mekhilta compares: “like sparks from a blacksmith's hammer flying in every direction.” Every word burning, every vav radiating. This is the ketarim Rabbi Akiva spent his life on (the “crowns” on the letters) — reading the whole secret of the Law in the strokes above each letter. Yeshua Himself invokes this tradition in Matthew 5:18: “not one iota, not one stroke shall pass from the Law” — “iota” = the smallest Hebrew letter yod; “stroke” (keraia) = precisely Rabbi Akiva's keter (the crown).

所以「人民從遠方站立」── 不是因為他們不愛神。沙皮拉拉比說：恰恰相反。他們從遠方站立，是因為神聖的接近開始讓他們承受不住。Or HaChaim 引用詩篇 63:4：「祢的慈愛比生命更好」(tov chasdecha me-chaim，טוֹב חַסְדְּךָ מֵחַיִּים) ── 拉比們的解釋是：那時的kedusha（聖潔）這麼濃，新婦如果再挨近，她會因為神的慈愛而死。她退後，不是逃避新郎，而是在承認新郎的份量。 So “the people stood at a distance” — not because they did not love God. Rabbi Shapira says: the opposite. They stood at a distance because the divine nearness was beginning to overwhelm them. Or HaChaim quotes Psalm 63:4 — “Your loving-kindness is better than life” (tov chasdecha me-chaim, טוֹב חַסְדְּךָ מֵחַיִּים) — and the rabbis read: the kedusha in that moment was so dense that if the Bride drew any closer, she would die of the very mercy. Her stepping back was not avoidance of the Groom; it was an acknowledgement of the Groom's weight.

這也解釋了為什麼以色列接著對摩西說：「你親近主，把祂的話告訴我們。我們所求的，求祢說，神不要對我們親自說，恐怕我們死亡。」(出 20:19) 這不是不信，這是敬畏。沙皮拉拉比說：新婦預備好的標誌，不是「我什麼都不怕」── 而是「我終於知道我面對的是誰」。 This also explains why Israel then says to Moses: “You speak to us, and we will listen. But let not God speak with us, lest we die.” (Ex 20:19) This is not unbelief — it is awe. Rabbi Shapira: the mark of a ready Bride is not “I'm afraid of nothing.” It is, “I finally know whose face I am standing in front of.”

七、急切等候 ── 「催促」(speudontas) 至日的到來VII. Earnest Hastening — “Speudontas,” Accelerating the Day of the LORD

沙皮拉拉比在這一段裡引用彼得後書 3:12 ── 在原文裡的一個極受爭議、極有份量的希臘字：speudontas（σπεύδοντας）。和合本譯為「切切仰望」，但這個字的主要意義是「催促、加快」。如 NIV 直譯：「look forward to and hasten the coming of the day of God」(等候並催促神之日的到來)。 Rabbi Shapira reaches here for 2 Peter 3:12 and the much-debated, weighty Greek word in the original: speudontas (σπεύδοντας). Many translations render it “earnestly desiring,” but the primary sense is “hastening, accelerating.” As the NIV puts it: “look forward to and hasten the coming of the day of God.”

「切切仰望神的日子來到（speudontas tēn parousian tēs tou Theou hēmeras，σπεύδοντας τὴν παρουσίαν τῆς τοῦ Θεοῦ ἡμέρας）。」── 彼得後書 3:12 “Looking for and hastening the coming of the day of God (speudontas tēn parousian tēs tou Theou hēmeras, σπεύδοντας τὴν παρουσίαν τῆς τοῦ Θεοῦ ἡμέρας).”— 2 Peter 3:12

彼得 ── 那位Shimon Bar Yonah，那位漁夫使徒 ── 在明確地告訴我們：義人有份於「催促」(l'maher，לְמַהֵר) 神之日（Yom Hashem，יוֹם ה' ）的到來。這不是說我們可以「強迫」神，而是說：我們的禱告、我們的悔改、我們的聖潔行動，在神的天平上有份量，可以提前那一日。 Peter — Shimon Bar Yonah, the fisherman apostle — is telling us plainly: the righteous have a share in “hastening” (l'maher, לְמַהֵר) the Day of the LORD (Yom Hashem, יוֹם ה'). He is not saying we can “force” God; he is saying that our prayers, our repentance, our holy actions weigh on God's scale, and can bring forward that Day.

這個觀念在希伯來思想裡叫「l'maher」(לְמַהֵר，「催促」) ── 也是 Talmud 與 Midrash 反覆出現的主題。Talmud Sanhedrin 98a 直接記載 ── 「以色列若全部悔改一日，Mashiach 即至。」沙皮拉拉比在這個邏輯裡注入了基督徒從未充分理解的份量：當你今晚為以色列禱告、當你今晚把一位朋友（不論是猶太人、外邦人、彌賽亞猶太人）邀請到你的飯桌、當你今晚做一個聖潔的選擇 ── 那一個動作在神的時間軸上是「speudontas」── 是加速陛下回來的婚禮。 In Hebrew thought this idea is l'maher (לְמַהֵר, “to hasten”) — a recurring theme in the Talmud and the Midrash. Talmud Sanhedrin 98a says it directly: “If Israel were to repent for a single day, the Messiah would come immediately.” Rabbi Shapira pours into this an emphasis Christians have rarely grasped: when you pray for Israel tonight, when you bring a friend — Jew, Gentile, or Messianic Jew — to your Shabbat table tonight, when you make one holy choice tonight — that act registers on God's timeline as speudontas. It is accelerating the King's wedding return.

這也是沙皮拉拉比對所有「災前被提」(pre-tribulation Rapture) 末世觀的回應：聖經不把我們描寫成被動的「觀眾」，也不把我們邀出歷史的舞台。聖經把我們放進舞台，請我們參與那一日的到來。所有受造之物 ── 從星辰到星座，從信徒到非信徒 ── 都在「同聲歎息、勞苦」（羅 8:22）等候Yom Hashem。而新婦的義行 ── 啟示錄 19:8 的「細麻衣」── 就是這個歷史宇宙進程中真正可以加速的那一部份。 This is also Rabbi Shapira's reply to every pre-tribulation Rapture eschatology: Scripture does not cast us as passive “spectators,” nor does it extract us from history's stage. Scripture places us on the stage and invites us to participate in the arrival of the Day. All creation — from stars to constellations, from believers to unbelievers — is “groaning together” (Rom 8:22) for Yom Hashem. And the Bride's righteous deeds — Revelation 19:8's fine linen — are the part of this cosmic process that can actually be accelerated.

八、外院的洗濯 ── 為什麼「洗衣服」是聖約禮儀VIII. Washing in the Outer Court — Why “Wash Your Clothes” Is Covenant Liturgy

沙皮拉拉比把我們的注意力拉回出埃及記 19:10-11 ── 神對摩西說：「你往百姓那裡去，叫他們今天明天自潔，又叫他們洗衣服。到第三天要預備好了，因為第三天耶和華要在眾百姓眼前降臨在西奈山上。」這一段在西奈山婚禮的外面的兩天，是新婦的「沐浴與穿衣」(mikveh u-malbush)。 Rabbi Shapira pulls us back to Exodus 19:10-11 — God says to Moses: “Go to the people and consecrate them today and tomorrow, and let them wash their clothes; let them be ready for the third day, for on the third day the LORD will come down on Mount Sinai before the eyes of all the people.” These two days before the Sinai wedding are the Bride's “bath and dressing” (mikveh u-malbush).

為什麼「洗衣服」？這不就是外面的事嗎？拉比沙皮拉的答覆是極希伯來的：「外面的洗濯，是為了把你帶到內裡的洗淨。」(External washing is to bring you to internal cleansing.) 在猶太傳統裡，mikveh（潔淨之池）不是清潔水的問題 ── 它是意圖(kavanah，כַּוָּנָה) 的問題。如果你帶著正確的kavanah進入 mikveh，外面的水就把你裡面的心一同洗濯。 Why “wash your clothes”? Isn't that just an external matter? Rabbi Shapira's answer is profoundly Hebrew: “External washing is to bring you to internal cleansing.” In Jewish tradition the mikveh (immersion pool) is not about hygienic water — it is about intention (kavanah, כַּוָּנָה). If you enter the mikveh with the right kavanah, the outer water does simultaneously cleanse the inner heart.

這是耶書亞在馬太福音 23:25-26 對法利賽人的真正批評：「你們洗淨杯盤的外面，裡面卻盛滿了勒索和放縱」── 祂不是說「不要洗外面」，祂是說「外面的洗，不能取代裡面的洗」。當外面與裡面同時被洗，這就是 kodesh。當外面被洗、裡面卻沒被洗，這就是偽善(tzevi'ut，צְבִיעוּת)。 This is Yeshua's actual critique of the Pharisees in Matthew 23:25-26: “You clean the outside of the cup, but inside it is full of greed and self-indulgence.” He is not saying “don't clean the outside.” He is saying, “the outside cleaning does not replace the inside cleaning.” When both inside and outside are washed at once — that is kodesh. When the outside is washed but the inside is not — that is hypocrisy (tzevi'ut, צְבִיעוּת).

新婦的洗衣服對 5786 年的我們來說，可以是極具體的事： The Bride's “washing of clothes” for us in 5786 can be very concrete:

● 今晚實際上洗一個澡，但在水裡說一個禱告：「神啊，求祢用祢的chesed洗淨我的心，像今晚我的身體被洗。」
● 今晚清理你的飯桌、你的廚房、你的小聖所；外在的清理，就是內在預備的雛形。
● 今晚原諒一個你還沒原諒的人；這是新婦最深的「洗衣服」── 因為赦免就是把那件污染的衣服從你身上脫下來。
● 今晚道歉一個你還沒道歉的人；這也是「洗衣服」── 是讓你明天可以站在西奈山下而不羞愧。 ● Tonight actually take a bath, but in the water say a prayer: “God, cleanse my heart with Your chesed as You cleanse my body tonight.”
● Tonight tidy your dinner table, your kitchen, your little sanctuary — the outer ordering is a prototype of the inner readying.
● Tonight forgive one person you have not forgiven; that is the Bride's deepest “washing of clothes” — because forgiveness is taking the contaminated garment off your back.
● Tonight apologise to one person you have not apologised to; that, too, is “washing of clothes” — so that tomorrow you can stand at the foot of Sinai without shame.

九、Ahdut ── 一個的同心 ── 是新婦的衣裳IX. Ahdut — One Accord — Is the Bride's Garment

沙皮拉拉比在後半段引出他的學生拉比 Jonathan Elowitz（一位第二代的彌賽亞猶太信徒，即將被按立為彌賽亞猶太拉比）── 他在新婦的「預備」上加進一個極關鍵的維度：「Ahdut」(אַחְדוּת，「合一」)。 Rabbi Shapira hands the second half of the teaching to his student Rabbi Jonathan Elowitz — a second-generation Messianic Jewish believer about to be ordained as a Messianic Jewish rabbi — who adds a key dimension to the Bride's readying: “Ahdut” (אַחְדוּת, “oneness, unity”).

在出埃及記 19:8 ── 摩西把神的話告訴以色列之後 ── 以色列的回應極特殊：「百姓都同聲回答說：『耶和華所吩咐的，我們都要遵行。』」(vaya'anu kol ha-am yachdav，וַיַּעֲנוּ כָל הָעָם יַחְדָּו)。「yachdav」（一同、同心、合一） ── 這個字本身就是Ahdut的字根。這就是新郎下到山上之前，新娘必須先做到的事：她必須先合一。 In Exodus 19:8 — after Moses reports God's words to Israel — Israel's response is striking: “And all the people answered together, ‘All that the LORD has spoken we will do.’” (vaya'anu kol ha-am yachdav, וַיַּעֲנוּ כָל הָעָם יַחְדָּו). “Yachdav” (together, in one accord) — this word is the very root of Ahdut. This is what the Bride must do before the Groom descends on the mountain: she must first be one.

拉比 Elowitz 與沙皮拉拉比把這個「合一」拉到一個極為直接的應用：若我們對自己的鄰舍懷有仇恨 (sinat chinam，שִׂנְאַת חִנָּם，「無故的仇恨」)，我們就還沒有預備好。Talmud 中明確指出第二聖殿被毀就是因為sinat chinam。而 Talmud Yoma 同樣指出 ── 若要重建第三聖殿，必須先有ahavat chinam（אַהֲבַת חִנָּם，「無故的愛」）。 Rabbi Elowitz and Rabbi Shapira drive this “oneness” into a direct application: if we hold hatred toward our neighbour (sinat chinam, שִׂנְאַת חִנָּם, “baseless hatred”), we are not yet ready. The Talmud says outright that the Second Temple was destroyed because of sinat chinam. And the Talmud Yoma teaches that to rebuild the Third Temple there must first be ahavat chinam (אַהֲבַת חִנָּם, “baseless love”).

「你不能恨你的鄰舍而事奉神。你不能拿著仇恨而站在西奈山下。新婦預備自己的衣裳，最大的一條，就是『愛鄰如己』。」── 拉比 Jonathan Elowitz “You cannot hate your neighbour and worship God. You cannot stand at the foot of Sinai while carrying hatred. The biggest single thread in the Bride's wedding garment is ‘love your neighbour as yourself.’”— Rabbi Jonathan Elowitz

耶書亞自己在馬太福音 5:23-24 重複這個原則：「所以你在祭壇上獻禮物的時候，若想起弟兄向你懷怨，就把禮物留在壇前，先去同弟兄和好，然後再來獻禮物。」── 在西奈山的kodesh意義上，這節經文是說：連神都暫停接受你的祭物，直到你先把跟人的關係修好。新娘不能戴著一條撕裂的面紗走進婚禮。 Yeshua Himself repeats this principle in Matthew 5:23-24: “If you are offering your gift at the altar and there remember that your brother has something against you, leave your gift there before the altar and first go and be reconciled to your brother, and then come and offer your gift.” In the Sinai kodesh sense, this verse is saying: even God pauses His acceptance of your offering until you first repair your relationship with the person. The Bride cannot walk down the aisle wearing a torn veil.

對華人基督徒，Ahdut 有幾個極具體的應用：跟你的父母和好（很多華人信徒的最大未解開的結就在這裡）；跟你的配偶謙卑下來（在拉比的傳統裡，丈夫週五晚上要起身為妻子讀箴言 31 章的「才德之婦」）；跟你的教會或會堂裡的某一位弟兄姊妹去喝一杯咖啡，把那段久遠的誤會講開。這些都是新婦的衣裳。 For Chinese Christians, Ahdut has several concrete applications: reconcile with your parents (many Chinese believers' biggest unresolved knot is right here); humble yourself before your spouse (in rabbinic tradition the husband on Friday night stands and reads Proverbs 31's Eshet Chayil, “a woman of valour,” over his wife); go have a coffee with that one brother or sister in your church or synagogue and finally walk through the long-buried misunderstanding. These are all the Bride's garment.

十、今晚的小行動 ── 把一個人請到你的 Shabbat 飯桌X. Tonight's Small Action — Bring One Person to Your Shabbat Table

在 67 分鐘教導的最後，沙皮拉拉比的呼召極為樸素而具體 ── 他不是叫所有人去開個事工、做大事；他叫每一個聽眾今晚做一件很小的事： At the close of his 67-minute teaching, Rabbi Shapira's call is unusually modest and concrete — he does not ask everyone to start a ministry or do something grand. He asks each listener to do one very small thing tonight:

「從這個 Shavuot 到下一個 Shavuot，每兩個月把一個人請到你的家裡。一起做Kiddush（葡萄酒的祝福）。一起念Eshet Chayil（才德之婦）。一起說Malachei HaShalom（平安天使的歌）。把聖中之聖帶到一個人的心裡。不必把整本妥拉砸到他頭上。用希伯來文做，讓他自己問：『這是什麼意思？』── 然後神的手就會自己向他啟示。以賽亞書 53:1 說：『我們所傳的有誰信呢？耶和華的膀臂向誰顯露呢？』── 是耶和華的膀臂顯露，不是你的膀臂。你只負責把他帶進來、讓他吃好、跟他作伴、跟他過 Shabbat、跟他享平安。願主用智慧充滿你，奉耶書亞之名建立祂的國，使榮耀歸於祂。Am Yisrael Chai。」 “From this Shavuot to the next Shavuot, every two months bring one person into your home. Do Kiddush (the blessing of the wine) together. Recite Eshet Chayil (the woman of valour) together. Sing Malachei HaShalom (the angels of peace) together. Carry holy-within-holy into one person's heart. Do not throw the whole Torah on his head. Do it in Hebrew so he asks, ‘What does this mean?’ — and the hand of the LORD will reveal itself to him. Isaiah 53:1 says, ‘Who has believed our report? To whom has the arm of the LORD been revealed?’ — the arm of the LORD is revealed, not your arm. Your job is to bring him in, feed him well, be in fellowship, keep Shabbat, be at peace. May the Lord fill you with wisdom, in the name of Yeshua, to build His Kingdom and bring glory to His Name. Am Yisrael Chai.”

沙皮拉拉比加了一句極為重要的提醒：「不要把這變成妥拉的課堂。」(Don't make this into a Torah class.) 這就是聖中之聖的奧秘：你不是教導對方，你是跟他一同站在西奈山下。你不是炫耀你的kavanah，你是分享你的Shechinah。你不是把對方變成猶太人，你是把對方介紹給以色列的神。 Rabbi Shapira adds a crucial caution: “Don't make this into a Torah class.” This is the secret of holy-within-holy: you are not teaching the other person — you are standing with them at the foot of Sinai. You are not showing off your kavanah — you are sharing your Shechinah. You are not making the other into a Jew — you are introducing the other to the God of Israel.

這個應用對華人基督徒尤其美。在華人文化裡，「請吃飯」── 請人到家裡吃飯 ── 本來就是建立關係最深的方式之一。沙皮拉拉比是在告訴華人信徒：你們文化裡已經有的「請客」的恩典，正是新婦預備自己最具體的維度。把這個文化恩賜 放在 Shabbat 的時間框裡（聖時）、放在你的家裡（聖地）、放上 HaMotzi 與 Kiddush（聖行動）── 三個維度同時對齊，你的飯桌就成為西奈山。 This application is especially beautiful for Chinese Christians. In Chinese culture, “inviting someone to dinner” — having them in your home for a meal — is already one of the deepest forms of relationship-building. Rabbi Shapira is telling Chinese believers: the gift of hospitality your culture already has is precisely the most concrete dimension of the Bride's readying. Place that cultural gift inside the time-frame of Shabbat (Holy Time), in your own home (Holy Place), with HaMotzi and Kiddush on the table (Holy Action) — three dimensions aligned at once, and your dinner table becomes Mount Sinai.

十一、結語 ── 從遠處走近、從多日走進這一日XI. Closing — From Afar Drawing Near, From Many Days Into This One Day

沙皮拉拉比的整段 67 分鐘教導，最終可以濃縮成一段話：5786 年的這一個 Shavuot，神不在西奈山上等候祂的新婦 ── 祂在你的飯桌上等候。祂不要你走得遠 ── 祂要你走近。祂不要你顫抖到後退 ── 祂要你顫抖到更深地敬拜。祂不要你「看見聲音」之後逃走 ── 祂要你「看見聲音」之後說：「Hineni（הִנֵּנִי，我在這裡），父啊，時候到了。」 The whole 67-minute teaching can finally be distilled into one sentence: on this Shavuot of 5786, God is not waiting for His Bride on Mount Sinai — He is waiting on your dinner table. He does not want you to stand far off — He wants you to draw near. He does not want you to tremble and retreat — He wants you to tremble and worship more deeply. He does not want you to “see the voices” and run — He wants you to “see the voices” and say, “Hineni (הִנֵּנִי, ‘Here I am’), Father, the hour has come.”

而那一刻的「Hineni」── 是亞伯拉罕在創世記 22:1 的「我在這裡」，是摩西在出埃及記 3:4 的「我在這裡」，是撒母耳在撒上 3:4 的「我在這裡」，是以賽亞在以賽亞書 6:8 的「我在這裡，請差遣我」── 也是耶書亞自己在希伯來書 10:7 的「看哪，我來了，為要照祢的旨意行」。新婦的Hineni，就是新郎走下西奈山的鑰匙。 That “Hineni” moment is Abraham in Genesis 22:1's “Here I am.” It is Moses in Exodus 3:4's “Here I am.” It is Samuel in 1 Samuel 3:4's “Here I am.” It is Isaiah in Isaiah 6:8's “Here I am — send me.” It is Yeshua Himself in Hebrews 10:7's “Behold, I have come to do Your will.” The Bride's Hineni is the key that turns the lock of the Groom's descent from Sinai.

所以今晚 ── 沙皮拉拉比說 ── 不要只是「觀看」這場直播。請現在停下來。請現在說一個禱告：「父啊，求祢讓我這個 Shavuot 不只是聽見，而是看見。求祢讓我這個 Shavuot 不只是遠遠站著，而是把我自己分別為聖。求祢讓我的時間、我的地點、我的行動 ── 在這個 5786 的婚禮日 ── 都成為祢的聖。Hineni，父啊，時候到了。奉耶書亞的名。」 So tonight — Rabbi Shapira says — do not merely “watch” this broadcast. Stop right now. Pray right now: “Father, let this Shavuot be not only a hearing but a seeing. Let this Shavuot be not only a standing-at-a-distance but a consecrating-of-myself. Let my time, my place, and my action — on this wedding day of 5786 — all be set apart for You. Hineni, Father, the hour has come. In the name of Yeshua.”
