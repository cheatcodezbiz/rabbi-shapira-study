新婦的智慧：在盟約旅程中平衡準備與支持 The Wisdom of the Bride: Balancing Preparation and Support in the Covenant Walk

布倫特·伍茲牧師從伯恩斯坦拉比那句「永遠是伴娘，從不當新婦」開始，帶我們穿過馬太福音 25 章十童女的比喻，進入薩克斯拉比論「נַעֲשֶׂה וְנִשְׁמָע — 我們願做，我們願聽」的盟約秩序，最終落在納曼拉比「中道」的智慧上：準備與支持必須同時被持守，新婦才能在主來的那一夜，燈裡有油。 Pastor Brent Woods begins from Rabbi Bernstein's saying — “Always the bridesmaid, never the bride” — walks us through Matthew 25's ten virgins, into Rabbi Jonathan Sacks on נַעֲשֶׂה וְנִשְׁמָע / na'aseh v'nishma (“we will do and we will hear”), and lands on Rabbi Nachman's path of balance: preparation and support must be held together if the bride is to have oil in her lamp on the night the Bridegroom comes.

在莫雷牧師簡短而溫暖的歡迎之後，布倫特·伍茲牧師走上講台，把今夜的主題濃縮成一個極為樸素的圖像：一位伴娘、一位新婦，與一場註定要來的חַתוּנָה (chatunah)——婚禮。他引用伯恩斯坦拉比那句聽起來像玩笑、卻深得令人安靜的話——「永遠是伴娘，從不當新婦」——並立刻把它翻轉過來：伴娘從來不是「失敗的新婦」；伴娘是使新婦能成為新婦的人。新婦 (כַּלָּה, kallah) 是獨一的、不可替代的、被聖約所定義的；但若沒有願意預備、不爭競、舉燈陪行的伴娘群體，新婦的那一夜永遠到不來。整場 93 分鐘的教導從這一張圖像展開，借薩克斯拉比論「נַעֲשֶׂה וְנִשְׁמָע（我們願做，我們願聽）」的盟約秩序，與納曼拉比「中道—אִיזוּן (izun)—平衡」的牧養智慧，把一個常被冷處理的比喻（馬太福音 25：十童女）重新點燃，讓我們在油快要燒乾的時刻，問一個非常簡單的問題：我，今夜，是聰明的童女，還是愚拙的童女？ After Pastor Morey's brief, warm welcome, Pastor Brent Woods steps to the podium and reduces tonight's theme to one disarmingly simple image: a bridesmaid, a bride, and a חַתוּנָה (chatunah) — a wedding — that is coming. He quotes Rabbi Bernstein's seemingly throwaway line — “Always the bridesmaid, never the bride” — and immediately flips it: the bridesmaid is never a “failed bride”; she is the one who makes it possible for the bride to be the bride. The bride (כַּלָּה, kallah) is singular, irreplaceable, covenant-defined; but without a company of bridesmaids willing to prepare, refuse rivalry, and walk with lamps lifted, the bride's night never arrives. From this single image the full 93-minute teaching unfolds — borrowing Rabbi Sacks on the covenantal grammar of נַעֲשֶׂה וְנִשְׁמָע (na'aseh v'nishma — “we will do, and we will hear”), and Rabbi Nachman's pastoral wisdom of אִיזוּן (izun — balance), to re-ignite a parable Christians often handle too coolly (Matthew 25, the ten virgins) and to press one very simple question at the hour the oil runs low: tonight, am I a wise virgin, or a foolish one?

一、開場 ── 莫雷牧師的「歡迎」與一場跨大洲的חַתוּנָהI. Opening — Pastor Morey's Welcome and a Chatunah Across Continents

莫雷牧師以一句溫柔的話為布倫特·伍茲牧師開門：「弟兄們、姊妹們，請與我一同歡迎我所敬愛的布倫特·伍茲牧師。」這個介紹本身就承載了今夜的神學重量——「歡迎」(welcome) 是一個盟約的動詞，是新婦家裡的成員為另一位準備好的成員開門。在「婚禮之戰 / Milchemet HaChatunah」這個跨大洲、跨語言的 Shavuot 守夜中，伍茲牧師的位置是第五位——意味著他並非單獨站立，而是接過前面幾位拉比與牧者的火炬，把它再傳給下一位。 Pastor Morey opens the door for him with a tender line: “Brothers and sisters, please join me in welcoming my beloved Pastor Brent Woods.” The introduction itself carries the night's theology — “welcome” is a covenantal verb, a member of the bride's household opening the door for another prepared member. In this cross-continental, multi-lingual Shavuot vigil — “The War for the Wedding / Milchemet HaChatunah” — Pastor Woods stands fifth in the lineup, which is to say: he is not alone. He receives the torch from the rabbis and pastors who preceded him and will hand it on to the next.

伍茲牧師上台的第一句話是「Chag Sameach!」（חג שמח，「節期快樂」）。這不僅僅是一句問候——它是進入希伯來節期框架的鑰匙。Shavuot 是「賜律法的節期」，也是傳統上被理解為「神與以色列訂婚之日」的節期。所以當伍茲牧師對全球的觀眾說「Chag Sameach」時，他實際上是把每一位收看的弟兄姊妹也領進了訂婚的帳棚。今夜不是觀禮——今夜，我們就是當事人。 Pastor Woods's first words on the platform are “Chag Sameach!” (חג שמח — “Joyous Festival!”). This is not mere greeting — it is the key that opens the Hebrew festival frame. Shavuot is the festival of the giving of the Torah, and in Jewish tradition it is read as the day of God's betrothal to Israel. So when Pastor Woods says “Chag Sameach” to a worldwide audience, he is in fact ushering every viewer into the betrothal tent. Tonight is not spectating; tonight, we are the parties to the covenant.

「歡迎」這個詞，是新婦的家裡才會說的話。陌生人對你說「Hello」；只有家人對你說「歡迎進來」。—— 引自伍茲牧師開場的牧者神學 “Welcome” is a word only spoken inside the bride's house. Strangers say “hello.” Only family says “come in.” — After Pastor Woods's opening pastoral theology

二、伯恩斯坦拉比的那句話 ── 「永遠是伴娘，從不當新婦」II. Rabbi Bernstein's Line — “Always the Bridesmaid, Never the Bride”

伍茲牧師從伯恩斯坦拉比那句俏皮話開始：「永遠是伴娘，從不當新婦 (Always the bridesmaid, never the bride)。」在世俗的語境裡，這是一句帶著嘆息的話——好像「伴娘」是「沒輪到的人」、是「次一級的角色」。但伍茲牧師立刻把它翻過來：在聖經的婚禮意象裡，伴娘並不是新婦的對手，而是新婦的使者、同伴、守燈者。 Pastor Woods opens with Rabbi Bernstein's wry line: “Always the bridesmaid, never the bride.” In secular ears it carries a sigh — as if the bridesmaid were “the one whose turn never came,” a second-tier role. Pastor Woods flips it immediately: in the biblical wedding image, the bridesmaid is not the bride's rival. She is the bride's messenger, her companion, the lamp-bearer who walks beside her into the night.

這個翻轉至關重要。因為在福音派的群體中，常有一個隱性的競爭：誰才是「被揀選的」、誰才是「真正的新婦」、誰才是「主特別愛的那一位」。伍茲牧師說：這種競爭本身就是把新婦的婚禮拆毀。在希伯來的חַתוּנָה (chatunah) 圖景裡，新婦只有一位，但她是被整個社群送進婚房的——每一位伴娘的存在，都是新婦能被認出來的條件。 The flip matters enormously. In evangelical circles a hidden competition often runs underneath: who is “the chosen one,” who is “the real bride,” who is “the Lord's special one.” Pastor Woods says: that very competition dismantles the bride's wedding. In the Hebrew picture of a חַתוּנָה (chatunah), there is only one bride — but she is escorted to the bridal chamber by the entire community. The presence of every bridesmaid is what makes the bride recognisable as bride.

伍茲牧師於是給出一個極具牧者氣息的應用：如果你今天感覺自己「不是核心人物」、「不是台上的那一位」、「不是被聚光燈照到的那一個」——好。因為新婦的婚禮，正是因伴娘的隱藏服侍才得以成形。從新婦的角度看，伴娘的「永遠」並不是悲嘆——是盟約的忠誠。 Pastor Woods then offers a deeply pastoral application: if today you feel you are “not the central figure,” “not the one on the platform,” “not the one in the spotlight” — good. The bride's wedding is shaped precisely by the hidden service of the bridesmaids. From the bride's vantage point, the bridesmaid's “always” is not a lament — it is covenantal loyalty.

三、十童女的比喻 ── 馬太福音 25 與「油」的神學III. The Parable of the Ten Virgins — Matthew 25 and the Theology of “Oil”

從伴娘的圖像，伍茲牧師很自然地進入馬太福音 25 章的十童女比喻——這是新約裡最直接把信徒描繪為「新郎來臨之夜的伴娘」的一段經文。十位童女，五位聰明，五位愚拙；不同之處只有一樣：聰明的童女隨身帶了油，愚拙的童女沒有帶。 From the bridesmaid image, Pastor Woods moves naturally into the parable of the ten virgins in Matthew 25 — the New Testament's most direct portrayal of believers as bridesmaids on the night the Bridegroom arrives. Ten virgins, five wise, five foolish. The only difference: the wise ones carried oil with them; the foolish did not.

「愚拙的對聰明的說：『請分點油給我們吧，因為我們的燈要滅了。』聰明的回答說：『恐怕不夠你我用的；不如你們自己到賣油的那裡去買吧。』」—— 馬太福音 25:8-9 “The foolish said to the wise, ‘Give us some of your oil, for our lamps are going out.’ But the wise answered, ‘Since there will not be enough for us and for you, go rather to the dealers and buy for yourselves.’”— Matthew 25:8-9

伍茲牧師指出一個常被忽略的細節：聰明的童女拒絕分油給愚拙的童女。這個拒絕並不是冷酷——而是因為油不能被轉讓。在拉比解經傳統裡，「油」(שֶׁמֶן, shemen) 是內在生命的象徵——是聖靈所充滿的、是經年累月的祈禱、是默默無聲的מִצְוָה (mitzvah) 所積攢的內在儲備。沒有人可以替你過你的盟約生活。沒有牧者、沒有教會、沒有配偶、沒有屬靈媽媽可以在最後一刻替你「補上你沒有預備的那一夜」。 Pastor Woods highlights a detail often glossed over: the wise virgins refuse to share their oil. This refusal is not cold — it is because oil cannot be transferred. In rabbinic exegesis, oil (שֶׁמֶן, shemen) is a symbol of inner life — Spirit-filling, years of quiet prayer, the silent reserve built up by unseen מִצְוָה (mitzvah). No one can live your covenant life for you. No pastor, no church, no spouse, no spiritual mother can, at the last moment, “make up for the night you did not prepare for.”

這段話伍茲牧師講得很慢、很重。他說，這不是恐嚇——這是慈愛。「主耶書亞並不是在威脅你；祂是在告訴你怎樣不錯過婚禮。」這位牧者用一個非常細膩的姿態，把這段常被當作「末日警告」的經文，重新解讀為「準備指南」——主來的時刻你能夠已經有油，因為祂今天就在邀請你開始預備。 Pastor Woods slows down and lets these lines land. He insists: this is not a threat — it is mercy. “The Lord Yeshua is not menacing you; He is telling you how not to miss the wedding.” With remarkable pastoral tenderness, he re-reads a passage often heard as “end-times warning” into a “preparation manual” — at the hour He arrives, you can have oil already, because He is inviting you, today, to begin preparing.

四、納曼拉比的中道 ── אִיזוּן (izun) 與婚禮之夜的平衡IV. Rabbi Nachman's Middle Path — אִיזוּן (izun) and the Balance of the Wedding Night

為了不讓「準備」變成焦慮的律法主義，伍茲牧師此時引入布雷斯洛夫的納曼拉比 (Rabbi Nachman of Breslov) 的「中道」教導——אִיזוּן (izun)，即「平衡」。納曼拉比說：靈性的健康不在於走極端，而在於同時持守兩端——同時是「行動者」與「聆聽者」，同時是「準備自己」與「支持他人」，同時是「敬畏顫驚」與「歡欣鼓舞」。 So that “preparation” will not collapse into anxious legalism, Pastor Woods now introduces Rabbi Nachman of Breslov and his teaching of the middle path — אִיזוּן (izun) — “balance.” Rabbi Nachman insists: spiritual health is not found in extremes but in holding both poles at once — being simultaneously “doer” and “listener,” simultaneously “preparing oneself” and “supporting others,” simultaneously “trembling” and “rejoicing.”

這個「平衡」對基督徒尤其重要。我們在福音派的傳統裡，有時把「在恩典裡安息」單獨抬高，反過來就把「努力預備」當作「行為主義」加以排斥。但比喻中那五位愚拙的童女——伍茲牧師指出——並非沒有信仰；她們有燈、有期盼、有同伴、甚至預備好等待。她們唯一沒有做的，就是主動把油裝進瓶子裡帶在身邊。她們安息得太徹底，以至於忘了預備。 This balance matters especially for Christians. In evangelical tradition we sometimes elevate “resting in grace” alone and react against “diligent preparation” as if it were works-righteousness. But the five foolish virgins in the parable, Pastor Woods points out, were not faithless — they had lamps, they had hope, they had companions, they were even prepared to wait. The one thing they did not do was actively pour oil into a flask and carry it with them. They rested so thoroughly that they forgot to prepare.

納曼拉比的אִיזוּן於是為這個比喻打開了一道新的門：聰明的童女既安息也預備；既等待新郎也採取行動把油準備好。她們的智慧不是「比愚拙的童女更努力」，而是「在等待與行動之間保持平衡」。這正是「我們願做，我們願聽」——נַעֲשֶׂה וְנִשְׁמָע——的盟約秩序。 Rabbi Nachman's izun opens a new door into the parable: the wise virgins both rest and prepare; they both wait for the Bridegroom and act to have the oil ready. Their wisdom is not “they tried harder than the foolish ones”; it is “they held the balance between waiting and acting.” This is precisely the covenantal grammar of “We will do, and we will hear” — נַעֲשֶׂה וְנִשְׁמָע (na'aseh v'nishma).

不是更熱心的人贏，而是持守平衡的人留下來。新婦的婚禮，是給既預備、又安息的伴娘的。—— 從納曼拉比的אִיזוּן引申 It is not the more zealous who wins; it is the one who keeps the balance who remains. The bride's wedding is given to bridesmaids who both prepare and rest. — After Rabbi Nachman's izun

五、薩克斯拉比論נַעֲשֶׂה וְנִשְׁמָע ── 「我們願做，我們願聽」V. Rabbi Jonathan Sacks on נַעֲשֶׂה וְנִשְׁמָע — “We Will Do, and We Will Hear”

伍茲牧師此時把整個教導推進到本夜的神學高峰：薩克斯拉比 (Rabbi Jonathan Sacks, ז״ל) 對נַעֲשֶׂה וְנִשְׁמָע (na'aseh v'nishma)——「我們願做，我們願聽」——這個出埃及記 24:7 中以色列在西奈山下的回答的解讀。傳統的智慧者問了一個尖銳的問題：怎麼可以先承諾「做」，再說「聽」？難道不應該先「聽明白」才能「去做」嗎？ Pastor Woods now lifts the entire teaching to its theological peak: Rabbi Jonathan Sacks (ז״ל, of blessed memory) on the answer Israel gives at the foot of Sinai in Exodus 24:7 — נַעֲשֶׂה וְנִשְׁמָע (na'aseh v'nishma), “We will do, and we will hear.” The classical sages asked a sharp question: how can one promise to do first and only afterwards to listen? Shouldn't one first understand in order to obey?

薩克斯拉比的回答是極為深刻的：在一段盟約的婚姻中——而西奈山下的關係正是這樣一段婚姻——「做」必須先於「完全聽明白」。因為若一個人只在自己「完全理解」之後才願意「做」，那他就不是新婦，他只是顧客。新婦的承諾是：「我先把自己交給你，再一輩子去明白你。」這就是נַעֲשֶׂה וְנִשְׁמָע。 Rabbi Sacks's answer is profound: in a covenantal marriage — and Sinai is precisely such a marriage — “doing” must precede “full hearing.” For a person who waits to fully understand before they act is not a bride; they are a customer. The bride's vow is: “I give myself to you first, and I will spend a lifetime coming to know you.” This is נַעֲשֶׂה וְנִשְׁמָע.

伍茲牧師把這個盟約語法直接套在十童女的比喻上：聰明的童女「做」了——她們在還不知道新郎何時來、不知道為何要等這麼久、不知道結局會是什麼的情況下，就先把油裝進了瓶子裡。她們的「做」並不是源於完全的「聽明白」，而是源於對盟約的信任。她們的「聽」是發生在「做」之後、發生在新郎來的那一刻——當門打開，新郎呼喊她們的名字時，她們才完全「聽明白」當初為什麼要裝油。 Pastor Woods maps this covenantal grammar directly onto the ten virgins: the wise ones “did” — they filled their flasks with oil without knowing when the Bridegroom would come, why the wait was so long, or how the story would end. Their “doing” was not grounded in complete “hearing” but in trust in the covenant. Their “hearing” comes after the doing, at the very moment the Bridegroom arrives — when the door opens and He calls their names, only then do they fully hear why they had carried the oil all along.

六、伴娘的事工 ── 不是「補位」而是「託付」VI. The Bridesmaid's Ministry — Not “Filling In” but “Entrustment”

回到伴娘的圖像，伍茲牧師此時開始給今夜的聽眾一個非常具體的應用：「你的伴娘事工是什麼？」每一位信徒——無論你的天賦、職分、年齡、地理位置如何——都被分派了一份具體的伴娘之工：你要幫助哪一位「新婦」預備？你要為哪一個家庭、哪一位姊妹、哪一位即將跌倒的弟兄舉燈陪行？ Returning to the bridesmaid image, Pastor Woods now gives the audience a very concrete application: “What is your bridesmaid ministry?” Every believer — regardless of gift, office, age, or geography — has been assigned a specific bridesmaid's work: which “bride” are you helping prepare? Which family, which sister, which brother on the edge of stumbling are you walking beside, lamp lifted?

他特別強調：伴娘的事工不是「補位」——即「新婦累了我替她上場」——伴娘的事工是「託付」，即「我所擁有的，是要交在新婦手中、為了那一夜」。這個區別至關重要。許多教會中的「服侍」實際上是一種競爭的補位——「如果牧師不在，我來」、「如果這位姊妹做得不好，我來」。但伍茲牧師說：這不是聖經中的伴娘形象。聖經中的伴娘從不想成為新婦；她只想看見新婦的那一夜。 He underlines: the bridesmaid's ministry is not “filling in” — “if the bride gets tired I will step into her place.” The bridesmaid's ministry is entrustment — “what I have is to be placed into the bride's hand, for that night.” The distinction matters. Much of what passes for “service” in our churches is in fact competitive substitution — “if the pastor isn't there, I will be”; “if that sister isn't doing it well, I will.” Pastor Woods insists: this is not the bridesmaid of Scripture. The bridesmaid of Scripture never wants to be the bride; she wants only to see the bride's night come.

這個應用直接接上薩克斯拉比的נַעֲשֶׂה וְנִשְׁמָע：伴娘「做」——她預備、她舉燈、她走在前面為新婦點亮道路——而她「聽」會在新郎到達的那一刻，當她聽見新郎對新婦說「進來」時，她才完全明白自己幾十年無聲的服侍是為了這一夜。בעזרת השם (b'ezrat haShem)——願這就是我們的事工。 This application connects directly back to Rabbi Sacks on נַעֲשֶׂה וְנִשְׁמָע: the bridesmaid “does” — she prepares, she lifts her lamp, she walks ahead lighting the path for the bride — and her “hearing” happens at the very moment the Bridegroom arrives, when she hears Him say “Come in” to the bride, and only then does she fully understand that her decades of silent service were for this one night. בעזרת השם (b'ezrat haShem) — may that be our ministry.

七、為華人信徒的應用 ── 「我們願做，我們願聽」在普通生活裡VII. For Chinese Believers — “We Will Do, and We Will Hear” in Ordinary Life

第一，華人教會的群體文化裡，常常有一種「主導者—跟隨者」的二元劃分——好像不是站台上的人，就是台下的人。伍茲牧師今夜的圖像為我們打開第三條路：「伴娘」。伴娘不在台上，也不只是台下；她是在新婦旁邊。對華人姊妹特別重要：你不必成為「下一位講員」、「下一位主任牧師」、「下一位被注視的人」，才有事工。你今晚就有事工——你身邊的那一位「新婦」是誰？ First, Chinese-church culture often runs a binary of “leader / follower” — as though one is either on the platform or in the seats. Pastor Woods's image opens a third path: the bridesmaid. She is not on the platform, and not merely in the seats; she is beside the bride. This is especially important for Chinese sisters: you do not need to become “the next preacher,” “the next senior pastor,” “the next person watched” in order to have a ministry. You have a ministry tonight — who is the “bride” standing next to you?

第二，נַעֲשֶׂה וְנִשְׁמָע的盟約秩序，挑戰了華人文化中常見的「完全理解之後才行動」的傾向。我們受儒家影響極深，「思而後行」幾乎是一種文化反射。但盟約的婚姻說：「先把自己交出去，再用一生明白」。這對華人信徒的洗禮、奉獻、宣教呼召、婚姻立約都有極為直接的應用。你不需要在「完全聽明白」之後才委身——委身本身就是聽見的一部分。 Second, the covenantal grammar of נַעֲשֶׂה וְנִשְׁמָע challenges a deep Chinese-cultural reflex: “fully understand before acting.” Confucian instinct presses us toward “think first, then act.” But the covenantal marriage insists: “give yourself first, and spend your life coming to know.” The implication touches baptism, financial offering, missions call, and marriage covenant directly. You do not need to “fully hear” before committing — commitment itself is part of how you come to hear.

第三，אִיזוּן (izun)——「平衡」——對華人信徒的牧養特別重要。我們的福音派傳統有時偏向「努力」（行為、紀律、查經、奉獻），有時偏向「安息」（恩典、被動、等候）。納曼拉比說：兩者都要。既預備油，也信任新郎。既「做」，也「聽」。既「準備自己」，也「支持新婦」。既「聰明」，也「柔軟」。這就是基督教與彌賽亞猶太教共同的牧者神學。 Third, אִיזוּן (izun) — “balance” — is especially important for the pastoral care of Chinese believers. Our evangelical inheritance sometimes leans toward “effort” (works, discipline, Bible study, giving), sometimes toward “rest” (grace, passivity, waiting). Rabbi Nachman insists: both. Both prepare the oil and trust the Bridegroom. Both “do” and “hear.” Both “prepare yourself” and “support the bride.” Both “wise” and “tender.” This is the shared pastoral theology of Christian and Messianic Jewish faith.

八、結語 ── 「今夜，你的瓶子裡有沒有油？」VIII. Closing — “Tonight, Is There Oil in Your Flask?”

伍茲牧師臨近結尾時，把整場 93 分鐘的教導收攏在一個極為樸素、極為個人的問題上：「今夜，你的瓶子裡有沒有油？」他不問「你會不會被提」、不問「你在教會的職分」、不問「你的神學立場」——他問你瓶子裡有沒有油。這個問題只有你自己能回答；這個問題不能被任何人替你回答。 Approaching the end, Pastor Woods gathers the full 93 minutes into one disarmingly personal question: “Tonight, is there oil in your flask?” He does not ask “Will you be raptured?” He does not ask “What is your office in the church?” He does not ask “What is your theological position?” He asks whether there is oil in your flask. Only you can answer this question. No one can answer it for you.

而他補上一句使全場安靜的話：「但好消息是——現在還有時間。」主耶書亞還沒有到。今夜——這一夜——你還可以走出去買油。你還可以禱告。你還可以與一位你曾經得罪過的弟兄和好。你還可以對你失聯多年的女兒打那一通電話。你還可以把那筆你藏起來不肯奉獻的錢交出去。「我們願做」——今夜你就可以做；「我們願聽」——你一輩子都會繼續聽。 And he adds the line that quiets the whole room: “But the good news is — there is still time.” The Lord Yeshua has not yet arrived. Tonight — this night — you can still go out and buy oil. You can still pray. You can still reconcile with the brother you wronged. You can still make the phone call to the daughter you have not spoken with in years. You can still release the offering you have been hiding. “We will do” — you can do it tonight; “we will hear” — you will go on hearing for a lifetime.

「永遠是伴娘，從不當新婦——但她仍然有重要的工作要做。那就是幫助新婦為最終的婚禮日做準備。今夜，去把你的油預備好。חַתוּנָה (chatunah) 要來了——而כַּלָּה (kallah)，新婦——是被聖約所揀選的，她的婚禮，是因你的נַעֲשֶׂה וְנִשְׁמָע而得以成形。」 “Always the bridesmaid, never the bride — but she still has an important job to do: to help the bride prepare for the eventual wedding day. Tonight, get your oil ready. The חַתוּנָה (chatunah) is coming — and the כַּלָּה (kallah), the bride, is the one the covenant has chosen. Her wedding is shaped by your נַעֲשֶׂה וְנִשְׁמָע.”
