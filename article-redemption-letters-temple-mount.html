橄欖山的劈裂 ── 弥赛亚脚踏之处的活水与寶藏 The Cleaving of the Mount of Olives — Living Waters and Hidden Treasures Beneath His Feet

沙皮拉拉比博士站在橄欖山（Har HaZeitim，הר הזיתים）——撒迦利亚书第十二章预言弥赛亚要降临的那座山。他指出最近以色列发生的强震、地下早已被勘测出的巨大水庫、以及拉比传统所说的「弥赛亚一脚劈山到地中海」——是同一份應许在我们这世代的脚步。 Rabbi Dr. Shapira stands on the Mount of Olives (Har HaZeitim, הר הזיתים) — the very mountain Zechariah 12 names as the place where the Messiah will stand. He links Israel's recent strong earthquake, the immense underground reservoir already mapped beneath the mountain, and the rabbinic tradition that the Messiah will cleave this mountain to the Mediterranean Sea — into one converging sign for our generation.

沙皮拉拉比博士 · Ahavat Ammi 事工 Rabbi Dr. Itzhak Shapira · Ahavat Ammi Ministries

这是「赎回的字母」(Otiyot HaGeulah，אותיות הגאולה) 节目里一段非常特别的实地教导。沙皮拉拉比博士没有坐在书房裡，而是站在橄欖山上、面对著圣殿山——身后正是末后第三圣殿(Beit HaMikdash，בית המקדש) 将要重建之处。他指出三件正在同时发生的事：撒迦利亚书与以西结书所预言的「大地震」、橄欖山地下经地质勘测已确认的活水水庫、以及拉比传统所说的「弥赛亚的脚踏在橄欖山上，必从此处一直劈到地中海，长达四十五至五十英里」。这不是说教，这是站在现场对著鏡头说：「快了。」 This is a striking on-location teaching from the series Otiyot HaGeulah (אותיות הגאולה, “Letters of Redemption”). Rabbi Dr. Shapira is not seated in a study — he stands on the Mount of Olives, looking out at the Temple Mount, with the future site of the third Beit HaMikdash (בית המקדש, “Temple”) directly behind him. He points to three converging realities: the great earthquake prophesied in Zechariah and Ezekiel, the immense living-water reservoir already mapped by geologists beneath the mountain, and the rabbinic tradition that the Messiah's foot will cleave this mountain all the way to the Mediterranean — a fissure roughly 45-50 miles long. This is not a sermon. It is a man on location, looking into the camera, saying: “Soon.”

一、「我们脚下，正是弥赛亚要站立之处」I. “Where We Stand Is Where the Messiah Will Stand”

拉比沙皮拉一开场就指明位置：他所站的橄欖山 (Har HaZeitim，הר הזיתים)，根据《撒迦利亚书》第十四章 4 节，是耶和华最後审判时祂的脚要站立的地方：「那日，他的脚必站在耶路撒冷前面朝东的橄欖山上；这山必从中间分裂，自东至西，成为极大的谷……」 Rabbi Shapira opens by naming the place. The Mount of Olives (Har HaZeitim, הר הזיתים), according to Zechariah 14:4, is where the Lord's feet stand in the day of judgement: “On that day His feet shall stand on the Mount of Olives, which lies before Jerusalem on the east, and the Mount of Olives shall be split in two from east to west by a very wide valley…”

「我们现在所站的地方，就是弥赛亚将要站立的地方。」——沙皮拉拉比博士 “The very place where we are standing now is where the Messiah will stand.”— Rabbi Dr. Itzhak Shapira

这就是为什麼橄欖山在拉比传统裡被视为「以色列最神圣的墓园」——历代敬虔的犹太人选择安葬在这裡，是因为传统认为：当弥赛亚来、当死人復活时（Techiat Ha-Metim，תחיית המתים），橄欖山上的人将是第一批从地裡出来的。这不是迷信，是末世希望以一种具体的、有地址的方式被表达。 This is why the Mount of Olives is, in rabbinic tradition, “the most sacred cemetery in Israel.” Generations of devout Jews chose to be buried here because tradition holds that when the Messiah comes and the dead are raised — Techiat Ha-Metim (תחיית המתים) — those buried on this mountain will be the first to rise. This is not superstition. It is eschatological hope given a concrete address.

二、东门、穆斯林墓园，与必要的地震II. The Eastern Gate, the Muslim Cemetery, and the Necessary Earthquake

弥赛亚必须从东门进入圣殿山 (《以西结书》43:1-5)。但东门在 16 世纪被苏莱曼大帝下令封闭，门外又设了一片穆斯林墓园——根据犹太礼法 (Halakha) 一位Kohen（祭司）是不能经过墓地的，这是有意的政治阻挠。 The Messiah must enter the Temple Mount through the Eastern Gate (Ezekiel 43:1-5). But the Eastern Gate was sealed in the 16th century by Suleiman the Magnificent — and a Muslim cemetery was placed in front of it. Under Jewish halakha, a Kohen (priest) may not pass through a graveyard. This was a deliberate political block.

拉比指出，先知书自己已经给了答案：「将有大地震」。《撒迦利亚书》14:5 说：「你们要从我山的谷中逃跑，因为山谷必延到亚萨；你们逃跑，必如犹大王乌西雅年间的人逃避大地震一样。」《以西结书》38:19-20 在歌革之战中也说：「我的忿怒如火发作……以致海中的鱼、天空的鸟、田野的兽……都在我面前震动。」 Rabbi Shapira points out that the prophets themselves give the answer: “There will be a great earthquake.” Zechariah 14:5: “You shall flee by the valley of My mountains … as you fled from the earthquake in the days of Uzziah king of Judah.” Ezekiel 38:19-20, in the Gog war: “My wrath shall blaze … the fish of the sea, the birds of the air, the beasts of the field … shall quake at My presence.”

「问题是：如果那裡现在是一片穆斯林墓园，这件事将如何发生？我相信撒迦利亚书和以西结书裡都提到，将会发生一次巨大的地震，对吗？」——沙皮拉拉比博士 “The question is: if that area is now a Muslim cemetery, how will this happen? I believe both Zechariah and Ezekiel say there will be a great earthquake — yes?”— Rabbi Dr. Itzhak Shapira

三、四十五英里的山谷 ── 一週之内以色列刚刚强震III. A 45-Mile Valley — And an Earthquake in Israel This Very Week

拉比传统说：当弥赛亚把脚踏在橄欖山上时，会形成一条从橄欖山一直延伸到地中海的山谷，长约 四十五至五十英里（约 72-80 公里）。这并不是浪漫的修辞——这正是《撒迦利亚书》14:4 所说「极大的谷」的延伸解读。 Rabbinic tradition holds that when the Messiah's foot strikes the Mount of Olives, a valley will open from the mountain to the Mediterranean Sea, roughly 45 to 50 miles long (72-80 km). This is not romantic rhetoric. It is the extended reading of Zechariah 14:4's “a very wide valley.”

就在录製这段影片的同一週，以色列刚刚发生了一次强烈的地震——震央距离橄欖山大约三十到五十英里。拉比沙皮拉提醒大家：「记住一件事——以色列就坐落在断层线上。」（这是地质学上确凿的事实：地中海板块、阿拉伯板块、非洲板块在这一带交会，所谓「死海裂谷带」(Dead Sea Transform) 就贯穿其中。）所以摆在我们面前的有两种可能： The same week this video was filmed, Israel experienced a strong earthquake — its epicenter roughly 30-50 miles from the Mount of Olives. Rabbi Shapira reminds the camera: “Remember one thing — Israel sits on a fault line.” (Geologically uncontested: the Mediterranean, Arabian, and African plates meet in this region along the so-called Dead Sea Transform.) Two possibilities lie before us:

可能一：一次极大的地震会先把这一区域夷为平地，移开穆斯林墓园、打开东门，为弥赛亚的到来预备道路——正如《以赛亚书》40:3 所说的：「在旷野预备耶和华的路。」
可能二：弥赛亚亲自降临，祂的脚一踏上橄欖山，山就当下劈开，从橄欖山一直裂到地中海。
拉比沙皮拉说：这两种可能性都是正确的——而且很可能同时发生。 Possibility 1: a great earthquake first levels the region, removing the Muslim cemetery and opening the Eastern Gate — preparing the way for the Messiah's coming, as Isaiah 40:3 says: “Prepare in the wilderness the way of the Lord.”
Possibility 2: the Messiah Himself descends, His foot strikes the Mount of Olives, and the mountain splits in that instant — from the summit to the sea.
Rabbi Shapira says: both possibilities are correct — and they may well happen at once.

四、地下的活水 ── 撒迦利亚的应许已经在我们脚下IV. The Living Waters Underground — Zechariah's Promise Already Beneath Our Feet

《撒迦利亚书》14:8 说：「那日，必有活水从耶路撒冷出来，一半往东海流，一半往西海流；冬夏都是如此。」（希伯来文：v'yatzu mayim chayim mi'Yerushalayim，ויצאו מים־חיים מירושלם；mayim chayim，מים חיים，「活水」）。 Zechariah 14:8: “On that day living waters shall flow out from Jerusalem, half of them to the eastern sea and half to the western sea; it shall continue in summer as in winter.” (Hebrew: v'yatzu mayim chayim mi'Yerushalayim, ויצאו מים־חיים מירושלם; mayim chayim, מים חיים, “living waters.”)

拉比指出一件少有人知道的现代事实：地质勘测早已确认，在橄欖山与圣殿山的正下方，存在著一个规模极大的地下水庫。这不是猜测，不是传说——这是现代仪器测出来的。换句话说，活水的水源已经在那裡了；它只等著从地下涌出来。 Rabbi Shapira points to a modern fact that is rarely advertised: geological surveys have confirmed that directly beneath the Mount of Olives and the Temple Mount lies an immense underground water reservoir. This is not speculation, not legend — modern instruments have mapped it. In other words, the source of the living water is already there. It is only waiting to be released.

「我们百分之百可以确定：这个地方的深处，地下有水。记住撒迦利亚先知所说，将有活水涌出。我们非常清楚地知道，在正下方，确实存在著一个规模巨大的地下水庫。」——沙皮拉拉比博士 “We can be 100% certain: deep beneath this place, there is water underground. Remember what the prophet Zechariah said — living waters shall flow forth. We know very clearly that directly below us is a reservoir of immense proportions.”— Rabbi Dr. Itzhak Shapira

对基督徒读者而言，这一段经文有一份更深的迴响。《约翰福音》7:38 耶书亚说：「信我的人，就如经上所说，从他腹中要流出活水的江河来。」希伯来文的 mayim chayim（活水），不只是「能喝的水」，它是「带著生命的水」、「从神的本源出来的水」。橄欖山下那个尚未被开啟的水庫，是一道实物的预表——那位站在井旁对撒玛利亚妇人说话的耶书亚，已经在橄欖山的脚下藏好了祂要赐下的活水。 For a Christian reader, this passage carries a deeper echo. In John 7:38, Yeshua says: “He who believes in Me, as the Scripture has said, out of his belly shall flow rivers of living water.” The Hebrew mayim chayim is not merely drinkable water — it is water carrying life, water issuing from the divine source. The unreleased reservoir under the Mount of Olives is a physical type. The Yeshua who stood by the well speaking to the Samaritan woman has already stored, beneath this very mountain, the living water He is about to give.

五、隐藏的寶藏 ── 米德拉什所说的「脚一踏，藏物都现」V. Hidden Treasures — “When His Foot Touches, All Is Revealed”

拉比沙皮拉提到另一条流传久远的犹太传统：约柜 (Aron HaBrit，ארון הברית) 可能就藏在圣殿山地下。这条传统的来源之一，是《玛喀比二书》2:4-8 对耶利米将约柜藏入山中的记载，另一是《巴比伦塔木德》(Yoma 53b-54a) 论圣殿地下藏物的讨论。如今圣殿山下许多区域因政治与宗教原因不能挖掘，但这条传统仍然鲜活。 Rabbi Shapira mentions another long-running Jewish tradition: the Ark of the Covenant (Aron HaBrit, ארון הברית) may be hidden underground beneath the Temple Mount. One source for this is 2 Maccabees 2:4-8 — Jeremiah's hiding of the Ark in the mountain — and another is the Babylonian Talmud (Yoma 53b-54a) discussing hidden objects beneath the sanctuary. Today many areas of the Temple Mount cannot be excavated for political and religious reasons. But the tradition lives.

米德拉什 进一步说：当弥赛亚的脚踏在橄欖山上时，一切隐藏在地下的寶藏都将被显露出来。这是米德拉什的浪漫吗？也许；但它也契合保罗所说的：「那时被隐藏在黑暗中的事，都要彰显出来。」（《哥林多前书》4:5）弥赛亚的来临不是把光带到一片空白的房间，而是把光照进一间已经堆满圣物、却长期被封死的圣殿地窖。 The Midrash goes further: when the Messiah's foot strikes the Mount of Olives, every hidden treasure beneath the ground shall be revealed. Is this midrashic romance? Perhaps. But it resonates with Paul's word: “He will bring to light the things now hidden in darkness.” (1 Corinthians 4:5). The coming of the Messiah is not light entering an empty room. It is light entering a sealed Temple cellar already filled with holy objects.

「如果弥赛亚来到、将祂的脚踏在橄欖山上，会怎样？所有隐藏在地下的寶藏，都将被显露出来。这正是米德拉什所说的。」——沙皮拉拉比博士 “If the Messiah comes and places His foot on the Mount of Olives — what then? All the treasures hidden underground shall be revealed. This is precisely what the Midrash says.”— Rabbi Dr. Itzhak Shapira

六、关於分辨，与最后的呼召VI. A Word on Discernment — and the Final Call

在结束之前必须说一句诚实话：「弥赛亚的脚一踏，橄欖山从此处一直裂到地中海四十五英里」——这是Drash（讲道层次的解读），不是P'shat（字面解读）。《撒迦利亚书》14:4 的字面层只说「橄欖山要从中间分裂」；那个「四十五英里」的延伸、那个「约柜在地下」的推论、那个「本週的地震就是」的应用——是拉比与米德拉什的解读，不是经文的直接陈述。读者宜分辨：哪些是经文，哪些是拉比传统，哪些是当代应用。 An honest note before closing. “The Messiah's foot will cleave the mountain forty-five miles to the sea” is drash (homiletical), not p'shat (plain reading). Zechariah 14:4 says, at the surface, only that “the Mount of Olives shall be split in two.” The extension to “forty-five miles,” the inference that “the Ark is below,” and the application that “this week's earthquake is the sign” — these are interpretations from the rabbis and the Midrash, not direct statements of the text. A wise reader distinguishes scripture, tradition, and contemporary application.

但即使我们对每一个细节保留谨慎，更大的图景仍然是经文本身在说话：那位站在橄欖山上的弥赛亚要来；祂的脚要使大地震动；祂要从耶路撒冷的中心释放活水；祂要使一切隐藏的事被彰显。这些不是推测——这是先知与使徒、新旧两约共同的呼喊。本週的地震、地下的水庫、被封的东门——它们或许是记号，或许是提醒，或许只是使我们仰望那真正之日的预兆。但「祂必再来」这一点，是确定的。 Yet even with the proper caution on each detail, the larger picture is Scripture's own: the Messiah who stands on the Mount of Olives is coming; His foot will shake the earth; He will release living water from the heart of Jerusalem; He will bring every hidden thing into the open. These are not speculations. They are the joint cry of prophets and apostles, of both Testaments. This week's earthquake, the underground reservoir, the sealed Eastern Gate — they may be signs, they may be reminders, they may be merely pointers that make us look up. But that He is coming back is certain.

对每一位爱以色列的基督徒，这段五分钟的实地教导留下了一个非常明确的姿态：不要把弥赛亚的再来当作遥远的神学条目，要把它当作即将到来的客人来准备。当那一日真的到来——当橄欖山真的裂开、当活水真的涌出、当东门真的被打开——我们要在场，要站在那群欢迎祂的人中间。 For every Christian who loves Israel, this five-minute on-location teaching leaves one very clear posture: do not treat the Messiah's return as a distant theological article. Treat it as a guest about to arrive. When that day truly comes — when the Mount of Olives truly splits, when the living water truly flows, when the Eastern Gate is truly opened — we are to be present, standing among those who welcome Him.

「我们正在等待这一天，愿弥赛亚快快到来。我们所要盼望的，是何等不可思议的事。朋友们——弥赛亚正在来到，来到耶路撒冷，来到以色列，祂要住在这裡，在祂赐予我们的何等应许中。Shalom。」 “We are waiting for this day. May the Messiah come quickly. What an incredible thing we have to look forward to. Friends — the Messiah is coming. Coming to Jerusalem. Coming to Israel. He will dwell here, in the promise He has given us. Shalom.”
