《利百加余民》
研习指南 The Rivkah Remnant
A Christian's Study Guide

八堂课程，从基督教与犹太教的双重视角，伴你读懂沙皮拉拉比的著作。 Eight lessons. A companion for Christians who love the Jewish people, reading Rabbi Shapira's book through both lenses — to understand, to question, and to learn.

基于沙皮拉拉比博士《利百加余民》(2025) Based on The Rivkah Remnant by Rabbi Dr. Itzhak Shapira (2025)
8 堂课程8 Lessons · 约 45 分钟通读~45 min read-through · 自助 / 小组皆宜Self-paced or small group · 📖 购买原书Get the Book ·

这份指南不是要替你「读完」沙皮拉拉比的《利百加余民》，而是要陪你认真地读它。这是一本由犹太弥赛亚拉比写给当代世界的书——他向教会和会堂同时发出挑战，用米德拉什（犹太释经法）打开《创世记》中利百加的故事，将其呈现为「末后日子的余民」之预言模型。如果你是一位爱犹太人的基督徒——并且想真正听懂一位犹太拉比在说什么、并以诚恳的提问深入理解他的观点——这份学习指南就是为你而设的。 This guide is not a shortcut around Rabbi Shapira's The Rivkah Remnant — it is a companion for reading it carefully. The book is written by a Messianic Jewish rabbi who challenges both the Church and the Synagogue, opening the Genesis narrative of Rebekah through Midrash (the Jewish art of interpretation) to present her as the prophetic prototype of the end-times remnant. If you are a Christian who loves the Jewish people — and you want to honestly hear what a rabbi is saying, and ask the kind of questions that make you understand his viewpoint more deeply — this guide is for you.

本指南共 八堂课，每一堂围绕拉比书中的一个核心教导。每一堂都包含五个部分： The guide is structured as eight lessons, each centered on a core teaching from the book. Every lesson follows the same five-part rhythm:

① 拉比说什么—— 用拉比自己的语境概述他的论点；

א  ·  01

为什么基督徒要读一位拉比？ Why Should a Christian Read a Rabbi?

① 拉比说什么 ① What the Rabbi Says

沙皮拉拉比在序言（由麦基翁牧师执笔）中讲述一个故事：一对犹太朋友受邀在他的教会唱诗，礼拜结束后，一位会众居高临下地问他们：「你接受耶稣作你个人的救主了吗？」拉比由此提出本书的核心论点——会堂与教会都辜负了寻索之人。教会失去了它的犹太根基；会堂则把寻求基督的犹太人推为外人。「我的弟兄们，这就是我们迫切需要这本书的原因。两个群体都负有责任。」 In his foreword Pastor Matt McKeown tells of inviting his Jewish friends to play music at his church — only for a man at the back to corner them with the question, "Have you accepted Jesus as your personal Lord and Savior?" From this story Rabbi Shapira launches the book's central claim: both the Synagogue and the Church have failed the seeker. The Church has been cut off from its Jewish root; the Synagogue treats Jews who seek Yeshua as outsiders. "My brothers and sisters, this is why we urgently need this book. Both communities bear responsibility."

② 关键经文 ② Key Scripture

「我的子民因无知识而灭亡。」 "My people are destroyed for lack of knowledge." 《何西阿书》4:6 · Hosea 4:6

③ 希伯来语之眼 ③ Through Hebrew Eyes

שׁוּבוּ · Shuvu——「回归」、「回转」。这也是拉比所创办的「犹太回归经学院」(Yeshivat Shuvu) 的名字。在希伯来语里，「回转」与「悔改」(teshuvah, תשובה) 是同一个词根。基督徒所熟悉的「悔改」(metanoia) 在原始的犹太语境里，从来不只是情绪上的懊悔，而是「回到原本应该站立的位置」。 שׁוּבוּ · Shuvu — "Return." It is also the name Rabbi Shapira gave his yeshiva, Yeshivat Shuvu. In Hebrew, "return" shares its root with teshuvah (תשובה) — repentance. The Christian word metanoia, in its original Jewish setting, is never merely emotional remorse — it is "returning to the place where you were always supposed to stand."

④ 基督徒视角 ④ A Christian Lens

使徒保罗本人就是法利赛人门下的拉比，他在罗马书 11 章用「橄榄树」的比喻警告外邦信徒：「你不是托住根，乃是根托住你。」沙皮拉拉比所说的「教会失去犹太根」并不是指基督徒要去归化犹太教，而是指——你所信的那位耶稣（Yeshua），祂的祷文、祂的安息日、祂引用的经文，全都来自希伯来世界。要听懂祂，就必须学会听祂母语的回声。 The apostle Paul was himself a rabbi trained at the feet of Gamaliel. In Romans 11 he warns Gentile believers using the olive-tree metaphor: "You do not support the root, but the root supports you." When Rabbi Shapira speaks of "the Church losing its Jewish root," he is not asking Christians to convert to Judaism. He is saying that the Yeshua you trust — His prayers, His Sabbath, the Scriptures He quoted — all came out of a Hebrew world. To hear Him fully, you have to learn to hear the echo of His mother tongue.

⑤ 诚实的提问 ⑤ Honest Questions

  • 如果拉比说「教会失去了犹太根」，那么我自己的教会是把这「根」补回来了，还是仍然在切断？我从哪里可以分辨？If the rabbi claims "the Church has lost its Jewish root," is my own church reattaching to that root — or still cutting it off? How would I tell the difference?
  • 在那位会众身上，我看到的是「传福音」，还是某种我也曾经做过的事？我能否用拉比所示的优雅来回应别人？In the man at the back of the church, was I seeing "evangelism" — or something I have done myself? Could I respond with the grace the rabbi's friends showed?
  • 我读《新约》时，是否已经习惯于把它当作「基督教书」？如果它本是犹太人写给犹太人的，我会读出什么不同？When I read the New Testament, do I read it as "the Christian book"? If it is in fact Jewish writers writing to a Jewish audience, what changes?
ב  ·  02

先知预言的根基——五项解读原则 The Foundations of Prophecy — Five Reading Principles

① 拉比说什么 ① What the Rabbi Says

在写预言性的书之前，拉比先用半本书的篇幅给我们「立规矩」。他援引迈蒙尼德 (Rambam) 的教导——「凡试图算尽末日的，都将受咒诅」——并提出读预言的五项原则：(1) 字面意思 (peshat) 永远是基础；(2) 妥拉中的事件具有循环重演性，先父们所走的路是后代的「预言镜子」；(3) 预言的应验取决于人的回应，不是注定不可改变；(4) 必须用整本经文读经文，不可断章；(5) 任何与摩西相矛盾的预言都不成立。预言不是「未来的剧本」，而是给人选择的呼召。 Before writing a prophetic book, the rabbi spends almost half the volume teaching us how to read one. He cites Maimonides — "Cursed are those who calculate the end" — and lays out five principles: (1) the plain meaning (peshat) is always the foundation; (2) Torah events cycle — the steps of the patriarchs are a "prophetic mirror" for later generations; (3) the fulfilment of prophecy depends on human response, not fixed fate; (4) Scripture must be read with Scripture, never in isolation; (5) any prophecy that contradicts Moses cannot stand. Prophecy is not "a script of the future" — it is a call requiring a choice.

② 关键经文 ② Key Scripture

「以色列中再也没有出现过像摩西一样的先知，他是我主面对面所拣选的。」 "And there has not arisen in Israel a prophet like Moses, whom the LORD knew face to face." 《申命记》34:10 · Deuteronomy 34:10

③ 希伯来语之眼 ③ Through Hebrew Eyes

פַּרְדֵּ"ס · PaRDeS——犹太释经的四层。Pshat（字面）、Remez（暗示）、Drash（讲道）、Sod（奥秘）。注意：每一层都从字面 (peshat) 开始，没有人可以跳过字面直奔奥秘。这恰好抑制了基督徒和犹太人共同的诱惑——把经文当作占卜末日的密码本。 פַּרְדֵּ"ס · PaRDeS — the four layers of Jewish interpretation: Pshat (plain), Remez (hint), Drash (homily), Sod (mystery). Note the order: every reading begins at peshat. No one is permitted to leap to mystery without first standing on the plain. This single rule disciplines a temptation common to Christians and Jews alike — treating Scripture as a cipher for the end times.

④ 基督徒视角 ④ A Christian Lens

许多福音派基督徒读《启示录》或但以理书时，已经默认采用「预言 = 注定的剧本」的读法。拉比的第三条原则——预言取决于人的回应——会让你停顿。这与《约拿书》尼尼微的悔改完全吻合：神宣告四十天就要倾覆这城，结果人悔改了，神就「后悔不降所说的灾」。这并非神改变心意，而是预言原本就把人放在方程式里。 Many evangelical readers of Revelation or Daniel default to a "prophecy = fixed script" reading. The rabbi's third principle — prophecy depends on human response — should give you pause. It fits perfectly with the book of Jonah: God announces forty days to overthrow Nineveh, the people repent, and "God relented from the disaster He had said He would do." This is not God changing His mind. It is prophecy that always had the human response inside the equation.

⑤ 诚实的提问 ⑤ Honest Questions

  • 我对末世的理解，多少来自「字面释经 (peshat)」，多少来自电影、小说和讲台上的图表？How much of my end-times framework comes from peshat — and how much from films, novels, and pulpit charts?
  • 如果预言会因人的回应而调整，这是否意味着信徒今天的祷告与悔改可以真正改变未来？这令我觉得不安还是被授权？If prophecy moves with human response, does that mean a believer's prayer and repentance today can actually shape what comes? Does that unsettle me, or empower me?
  • 拉比要求所有预言必须与摩西一致——耶稣自己说「摩西指着我所写的话」。我读摩西五经够多吗？The rabbi insists all prophecy must agree with Moses. Yeshua Himself said, "Moses wrote of me." Have I read the five books of Moses enough to test it?
ג  ·  03

两位弥赛亚——约瑟之子与大卫之子 The Two Messiahs — Ben Yosef and Ben David

① 拉比说什么 ① What the Rabbi Says

这是本书最让基督徒震动的一章。拉比说，犹太传统从《塔木德》开始就承认两位弥赛亚的存在：约瑟之子弥赛亚 (Mashiach ben Yosef)——受苦、被拒绝、为以色列死；以及大卫之子弥赛亚 (Mashiach ben David)——得胜、登宝座、复兴万国。沙皮拉拉比的论证是：拿撒勒人耶稣恰好以「约瑟之子」的身份显现——被自己的兄弟们卖掉、下到外邦人之地、最后才显露身份——而祂还要再来，那一次是以「大卫之子」的身份。两位弥赛亚不是两个人，而是同一位的两次显现。 This is the chapter that most unsettles Christian readers. The rabbi argues that Jewish tradition — going back to the Talmud — has long recognized two Messiahs: Mashiach ben Yosef (the Suffering One — rejected, dying for Israel) and Mashiach ben David (the Reigning One — enthroned, restoring the nations). Shapira's claim: Yeshua of Nazareth came precisely as Ben Yosef — sold by His brothers, descending into Gentile lands, only revealing His identity at the end — and He will come again, this time as Ben David. The Two Messiahs are not two people. They are one Person, in two appearances.

② 关键经文 ② Key Scripture

「他们必仰望我，就是他们所扎的；必为我悲哀，如丧独生子……」 "They will look on Me, the One they have pierced, and they will mourn for Him as one mourns for an only child…" 《撒迦利亚书》12:10 · Zechariah 12:10

③ 希伯来语之眼 ③ Through Hebrew Eyes

מָשִׁיחַ בֶּן יוֹסֵף · Mashiach ben Yosef。这个词最早出现在《巴比伦塔木德》Sukkah 52a——比基督教成型早。它不是基督徒「发明」给自己用的辩护，而是犹太教自己内部的一条线。沙皮拉拉比是说：当我们指着耶稣是受苦的弥赛亚时，我们不是在用基督教反对犹太教——我们在用犹太教自己的语言，说出犹太教自己的盼望。 מָשִׁיחַ בֶּן יוֹסֵף · Mashiach ben Yosef. The phrase appears in the Babylonian Talmud, Sukkah 52a — older than the formation of Christianity. It is not a Christian invention pressed back onto Jewish texts in self-defense; it is a thread internal to Judaism itself. What Shapira is saying is this: when we point to Yeshua as the suffering Messiah, we are not using Christianity against Judaism — we are speaking Judaism's own language to name Judaism's own hope.

④ 基督徒视角 ④ A Christian Lens

基督教以《使徒信经》定下「祂被钉、死、葬、复活、升天、还要再来」——这本身就暗含两次显现。但许多基督徒只熟悉第一次受苦的耶稣，对那位骑白马的得胜君王反而陌生。拉比的「两位弥赛亚」框架不是要否定耶稣，而是邀请基督徒——认真预备「第二次」。一位犹太拉比在告诉你，他正在等候王回来。 The Christian creeds already confess two appearances — "He suffered… was buried… and shall come again." But many Christians know the suffering Jesus far better than the returning King on the white horse. The rabbi's "Two Messiahs" framework does not deny Yeshua — it invites Christians to take the second appearance seriously. A Jewish rabbi is telling you he is waiting for the King to come back.

⑤ 诚实的提问 ⑤ Honest Questions

  • 如果两千年前的犹太拉比们已经在期待两位弥赛亚，为什么主流犹太教今天不接受耶稣是其中之一？拉比对此本身有解释吗？（提示：他写道「以色列要先被洁净」。）If rabbis two thousand years ago already expected two messianic appearances, why does mainstream Judaism today not accept Yeshua as one of them? Does the rabbi himself address this? (Hint: he writes that Israel must first be refined.)
  • 我所相信的耶稣，是「已经来过那位救赎主」，还是「还要再来那位君王」？两者我都同等地预备好了吗？Is the Jesus I trust mostly "the Savior who already came" or also "the King who is coming back"? Am I equally ready for both?
  • 如果犹太人正在期待大卫之子弥赛亚，而我正在期待主再来——那我们其实在等同一位。我能从这个共同的盼望开始一段对话吗？If Jews are waiting for Mashiach ben David, and I am waiting for the second coming — we are waiting for the same Person. Could that shared longing be the starting point of a conversation?
ד  ·  04

基督教内被连根拔起的余民 The Uprooted Remnant Within Christianity

① 拉比说什么 ① What the Rabbi Says

拉比毫不留情地写道：今天基督教内部正在打一场战争——一边是渴望回到犹太根基的人，另一边是抓着「空洞的旧根基」不放的体制。拉比并不要基督徒变成犹太人。他相信「以扫」——基督教世界——本身要经历tikkun（修正），并从其中产生一群「得胜的余民」。这是雅各（犹太人）整体丰满的关键。简单地把所有基督徒归为一类是危险的。 The rabbi writes bluntly: a battle is being fought inside Christianity itself — between those longing to return to the Jewish root and an institution clinging to "an empty old foundation." He is not asking Christians to become Jews. He believes "Esau" — the Christian world — itself must undergo tikkun (correction), and that out of it will rise a victorious remnant. This is decisive for the fullness of "Jacob" — the Jewish people. To lump all Christians together, he warns, is dangerous.

② 关键经文 ② Key Scripture

「就是现今的时候，也是这样，照着拣选的恩典，还有所留的余数。」 "So too at the present time there is a remnant chosen by grace." 《罗马书》11:5 · Romans 11:5

③ 希伯来语之眼 ③ Through Hebrew Eyes

תִּקּוּן · Tikkun——「修正」、「修复」。在犹太神秘传统中，世界因堕落而破碎，每一个人的呼召是参与tikkun olam「修复世界」。拉比把它用在以扫身上：以扫不是要被毁灭，而是要被修正。这与基督徒所讲的「拯救」不同——它强调的不是免除审判，而是被还原到原本的呼召。 תִּקּוּן · Tikkun — "rectification," "repair." In Jewish mystical tradition the world is broken at the fall, and every soul is called to tikkun olam — repair of the world. The rabbi applies it to Esau: Esau is not to be destroyed but to be rectified. This is a different accent from the Christian word salvation — it focuses less on escape from judgment and more on restoration to original calling.

④ 基督徒视角 ④ A Christian Lens

福音派常以「教会 vs. 世界」为框架。但拉比给我们一个更细微的图景：教会内部本身有真假之分。这与耶稣自己讲的麦子与稗子比喻一致（《马太福音》13章）。拉比并不在指责基督徒——他在邀请那些愿意听的基督徒「认领」自己的余民身份。这是一封拉比写给「教会内寻求者」的情书——只是写得不像情书。 Evangelical Christians often think in terms of "Church vs. World." The rabbi gives a sharper picture: there is a true and a false within the Church itself. This is fully in line with Yeshua's own parable of the wheat and tares (Matthew 13). The rabbi is not condemning Christians — he is inviting those willing to listen to claim their identity as part of the remnant. It is a love letter to "seekers inside the Church" — written in the voice of a rabbi who refuses to flatter.

⑤ 诚实的提问 ⑤ Honest Questions

  • 在我自己的教会传统里，什么是「真根」、什么是「空洞的旧根基」？我能不带防御地分辨吗？In my own church tradition, which parts are "the true root" and which are "the empty old foundation"? Can I tell the difference without becoming defensive?
  • 如果以扫的呼召不是「被毁灭」而是「被修正」，那我对那些和我神学不一致的基督徒——会不会带上更多耐心？If Esau's calling is not "destruction" but "rectification," does that change how patient I am with Christians whose theology I disagree with?
  • 作为基督徒，我有没有为以色列祷告——不只是「让他们得救」，而是「让以色列丰满」？这两个祷告是同一个吗？Have I ever prayed for Israel — not only "may they be saved," but "may Israel come to fullness"? Are those the same prayer, or different?
ה  ·  05

利百加——预备好的新妇 Rivkah — The Prototype Bride

① 拉比说什么 ① What the Rabbi Says

本书的核心比喻在这里。《创世记》24章——亚伯拉罕的仆人去远方，为以撒带回新妇利百加。拉比读出整段经文的预言性：以撒 = 犹太民族；仆人 = 圣灵；利百加 = 从列国出来、归入以色列家的余民。利百加的故事不是结婚的浪漫故事；它是「外邦人如何被预备、如何快步走向以色列，最终生出弥赛亚」的预言性蓝图。 Here is the book's central metaphor. In Genesis 24, Abraham's servant travels far to bring back a bride — Rivkah — for Isaac. The rabbi reads the whole passage prophetically: Isaac = the Jewish people; the servant = the Spirit; Rivkah = the remnant out of the nations who joins the household of Israel. Her story is not a romance. It is a prophetic blueprint of how the Gentile is prepared, how she hurries toward Israel, and how through that union the Messiah is born.

② 关键经文 ② Key Scripture

「这少女美丽动人，是一位从未有男人亲近的处女 (betulah)。她下到泉水边，取满罐子，然后上来。」 "The young woman was very beautiful, a virgin (betulah) — no man had ever lain with her. She went down to the spring, filled her jar, and came up." 《创世记》24:16 · Genesis 24:16

③ 希伯来语之眼 ③ Through Hebrew Eyes

בְּתוּלָה / עַלְמָה · betulah / alma。利百加同时被称为这两个词——betulah「处女」和 alma「年轻女子」。基督徒会立刻想到：这正是《以赛亚书》7:14 中那个充满争议的词——「必有 alma怀孕生子」。拉比在此指出：利百加是后来弥赛亚处女降生的预言性原型。她生雅各，雅各生约瑟——而耶稣被称为约瑟之子，由处女所生。这并非偶然。 בְּתוּלָה / עַלְמָה · betulah / alma. Rivkah is called by both words — betulah, "virgin," and alma, "young woman." Any Christian will immediately recall the contested word in Isaiah 7:14: "Behold, the alma shall conceive." The rabbi's point: Rivkah is the prophetic prototype of the later virgin who bears the Messiah. Through her come Jacob, then Joseph — and Yeshua is called the "son of Joseph," born of a virgin. This is not accidental.

④ 基督徒视角 ④ A Christian Lens

基督徒一向视利百加是「忠信的妻」，但极少把她读为「从外邦出来、归入以色列、生出弥赛亚的预言新妇」。这把基督徒所熟悉的「羔羊的婚筵」（《启示录》19）放回了希伯来圣经的根上——新妇并非凭空出现，她有一位预表她的「祖母」叫利百加。 Christians have always honored Rivkah as a faithful wife. They have rarely read her as the prophetic bride who comes out from the nations, joins Israel, and brings forth Messiah. This grounds the Christian image of "the wedding of the Lamb" (Revelation 19) back into the Hebrew Scriptures — the Bride does not appear from nowhere; she has a "grandmother" named Rivkah.

⑤ 诚实的提问 ⑤ Honest Questions

  • 如果作为外邦基督徒，我的「身份原型」是利百加，那我从今天起应该如何活——更像她、更像谁？If my identity-prototype as a Gentile believer is Rivkah, how should I live differently today — more like her, more like whom?
  • 利百加是被仆人「带」回去的——她不是自己跑过去的。在我的属灵生命里，我让圣灵「带」我到我以为自己永远不会去的地方了吗？Rivkah was brought by the servant — she did not arrive on her own. In my spiritual life, am I letting the Spirit lead me to places I would never have chosen?
  • 基督教传统经常争论alma是否真的是「处女」。读了拉比对利百加的解读后，我会怎样回应那些说「alma只是『年轻女子』」的人？Christian tradition has long argued whether alma truly means "virgin." After reading the rabbi on Rivkah, how would I now respond to someone who says "alma simply means 'young woman'"?
ו  ·  06

「我们必要行，我们必要听」 "We Will Do, We Will Hear"

① 拉比说什么 ① What the Rabbi Says

当亚伯拉罕的仆人问利百加「你愿不愿意走？」她不是说「让我考虑一下」。她说「我们必要行」。在《出埃及记》24:7，整个以色列在西奈山下也说出同一句话——na'aseh v'nishma，「我们必要行，我们必要听」。注意这个顺序：先行动，后听见。这与现代基督教中「先把所有问题都搞清楚再委身」的习惯刚好相反。利百加之所以能成为余民的原型，是因为她放弃了自己十二个月的「适应期」——立刻就走。 When Abraham's servant asks Rivkah, "Will you go?" she does not say "Let me think." She says "I will go." At Sinai in Exodus 24:7 all Israel says the same words — na'aseh v'nishma, "We will do, and we will hear." Note the order: act first, then understand. This is the opposite of the modern Christian instinct to "resolve all my questions before committing." Rivkah becomes the remnant prototype precisely because she gives up her customary twelve months of adjustment — and goes at once.

② 关键经文 ② Key Scripture

「凡耶和华所吩咐的，我们都必行，都必听从。」 "All that the LORD has spoken we will do, and we will hear." 《出埃及记》24:7 · Exodus 24:7

③ 希伯来语之眼 ③ Through Hebrew Eyes

נַעֲשֶׂה וְנִשְׁמָע · Na'aseh v'nishma——「我们必要行，我们必要听」。在拉比传统中，nishma不仅是「听」，也是「明白」、「领会」。所以这句话的意思是：「我们先去做，做着做着就懂了。」这条原则与希腊—西方思维（先理解再行动）截然相反，是希伯来式信心的核心。 נַעֲשֶׂה וְנִשְׁמָע · Na'aseh v'nishma — "We will do, and we will hear." In rabbinic tradition nishma means not only "hear" but "understand," "grasp." The phrase therefore means: "We will act, and the understanding will come as we act." This principle — opposite to the Greek-Western "understand first, act second" — is at the heart of Hebraic faith.

④ 基督徒视角 ④ A Christian Lens

耶稣的呼召几乎全部是这种顺序——「来跟从我」，而不是「先听完登山宝训再决定要不要跟随」。彼得在水面上行走，是「先迈步，再质疑」。希伯来书 11 章的信心英雄们都是「因着信，亚伯拉罕……出去的时候，还不知往哪里去」。利百加是这条线索的开端。na'aseh v'nishma 不是反智，而是属灵的次序。 Yeshua's call is almost always in this order — "Follow Me", not "Listen to the Sermon on the Mount and decide whether you'd like to come along." Peter walks on water by stepping before doubting. Hebrews 11 commends Abraham who "went out, not knowing where he was going." Rivkah stands at the head of this line. Na'aseh v'nishma is not anti-intellectual — it is a spiritual sequence.

⑤ 诚实的提问 ⑤ Honest Questions

  • 在我自己生命中，是否有一件事，神已经清楚地呼召我「做」——而我却用「我还没明白」当作不动手的理由？Is there something in my life God has clearly called me to do — that I have postponed under the cover of "I haven't fully understood yet"?
  • 如果作为利百加余民的呼召意味着「放弃我的十二个月适应期」，对我来说，那个「适应期」具体是什么？If the call of Rivkah's remnant means "giving up my twelve-month adjustment period" — what, in my case, is that adjustment period?
  • na'aseh v'nishma 与「先因信称义」是否冲突？拉比是否在讲一个不同的层次？Does na'aseh v'nishma conflict with "justification by faith first"? Or is the rabbi speaking on a different layer altogether?
ז  ·  07

从骆驼上摔下——面纱落下的时刻 Falling from the Camel — The Veil Drops

① 拉比说什么 ① What the Rabbi Says

利百加的旅程在快要见到以撒时出现一个奇怪的小细节：她从骆驼上摔了下来，然后蒙起面纱。米德拉什让这一刻爆炸开来：骆驼（gamal）在希伯来传统里是「兼具洁净与不洁特征」的动物——它有一项洁净的标记（反刍）但没有另一项（分蹄）。利百加从骆驼上摔下，象征她放下「混合的身份」。蒙面纱代表谦卑、专注，准备好与新郎相会。这是从「列国之女」到「余民之母」的转折点。 Just before meeting Isaac, Rivkah's journey hits an oddly specific moment — she falls from the camel, and then covers herself with a veil. Midrash explodes this scene: the camel (gamal) in Hebrew tradition is a creature of "mixed signs" — possessing one mark of cleanness (chewing the cud) but missing the other (split hoof). Rivkah's fall represents her laying down a mixed identity. Veiling herself signifies humility, focus, readiness for the bridegroom. This is the turning point from "daughter of the nations" to "mother of the remnant."

② 关键经文 ② Key Scripture

「利百加举目看见以撒，就从骆驼上下来，问那仆人：『这田间走来迎接我们的是谁？』……利百加就拿帕子蒙上脸。」 "Rivkah lifted her eyes, and when she saw Isaac, she dismounted from the camel… and she took her veil and covered herself." 《创世记》24:64-65 · Genesis 24:64-65

③ 希伯来语之眼 ③ Through Hebrew Eyes

גָּמָל · gamal——骆驼。和这个词同根的g-m-l意为「断奶」、「成熟」。米德拉什的智慧是：从骆驼上「摔下」就是「断奶」——告别仍带有混合本质的旧身份。基督徒会立刻想到保罗的话：「既作了人，就把孩子的事丢弃了。」（林前13:11） גָּמָל · gamal — camel. The same root g-m-l means "to wean," "to mature." The Midrashic insight: "falling" from the camel is being weaned — released from an old identity still carrying mixed traces. Christians will hear Paul: "When I became a man, I gave up childish things" (1 Cor 13:11).

④ 基督徒视角 ④ A Christian Lens

在基督教文化里，许多新信徒带着「混合的」属灵习惯——一半是福音，一半是民俗、商业、政治。利百加的「摔下」不是说外邦的一切都是污秽，而是说真正的相遇要求脱去混合。当她将要见到那位为她而来的人时，她蒙起面纱——这不是隐藏，而是专注。基督徒所等候的，与利百加预备见的，是同一位。 Many Christians come to faith carrying "mixed" spiritual habits — half gospel, half folk culture, half commerce, half politics. Rivkah's fall does not mean everything Gentile is unclean. It means the real meeting requires shedding the mixture. When she is about to meet the one who came for her, she veils herself — not to hide, but to focus. The One Christians wait for and the One Rivkah was about to meet are the same Person.

⑤ 诚实的提问 ⑤ Honest Questions

  • 我属灵生命里，有没有「混合的骆驼」——看上去虔诚却仍混杂着我尚未承认的东西？Are there "mixed camels" in my spiritual life — things that look devout but still carry mixtures I have not named?
  • 利百加蒙面纱不是为了隐藏，而是为了「专注」。我是否能区分这两种动机——逃避与专注？Rivkah veils not to hide but to focus. Can I tell the difference between these two motives — avoidance and focus?
  • 如果我每一天都是「即将与新郎相见」的那一天，我会先放下什么？If today I were on the brink of meeting the Bridegroom, what would I let go of first?
ח  ·  08

今天加入余民 Joining the Remnant Today

① 拉比说什么 ① What the Rabbi Says

本书在尾声引《出埃及记》39-40章——会幕被造好后，零散的部件抬到摩西面前，但没有人能把它立起来，因为板子太重。摩西说：「人怎么能立起它？」神回答：「你尽你的力量。」拉什解释：会幕看起来是摩西立起来的，实际上是会幕自己立起来的。这是拉比留给我们的最后一幅图：余民的工作不是靠人完成的，但人必须先伸出手。神并不要求完美，祂要求真实地伸手。 The book closes by citing Exodus 39-40 — when the parts of the Mishkan are brought to Moses, no one can raise it, the boards are too heavy. Moses asks God, "How can a man raise this?" God answers, "Put your strength to it." Rashi explains: it appeared Moses raised the tabernacle — but in truth the tabernacle raised itself. This is the rabbi's closing image: the work of the remnant is not done by human strength alone — but a human must put out his hand first. God does not demand perfection. He demands that you genuinely reach.

② 关键经文 ② Key Scripture

「要恶恶好善，在城门口秉公行义；或者耶和华万军之上帝向约瑟的余民施恩。」 "Hate evil, love good, and establish justice in the gate; perhaps the LORD God of Hosts will be gracious to the remnant of Joseph." 《阿摩司书》5:15 · Amos 5:15

③ 希伯来语之眼 ③ Through Hebrew Eyes

שְׁאֵרִית · Sh'erit——「余民」。在希伯来圣经中这个词从不指失败者剩下的——它是种子，是「专门为下一代留下的精华」。沙皮拉拉比想留给读者的最后一句话是：你不必是大多数；你只需要是真正的那一点。 שְׁאֵרִית · Sh'erit — "remnant." In the Hebrew Scriptures the word never carries the sense of "what is left over from defeat." It is seed — the deliberately preserved essence kept for the next generation. The rabbi's final word to the reader is this: you do not have to be the majority. You only have to be the real few.

④ 基督徒视角 ④ A Christian Lens

耶稣讲过一个很相似的图景——「不要怕，你们这小群」（路12:32）。基督教在数字增长的诱惑里太久了。拉比的「余民」并不在数谁多——它在数谁真。这与新约里「窄门」「少数」「余种」的语言完全一致。如果你读完这本书后觉得自己站在两个传统之间，既不被会堂完全接纳、也不被教会完全理解——拉比的回答是：你本来就该站在那里。利百加的脚步就是在两个家中间。 Yeshua said something almost identical: "Fear not, little flock" (Luke 12:32). Christianity has lived too long under the temptation of numerical growth. The rabbi's "remnant" is not counting who is many — it is counting who is true. This aligns precisely with the New Testament's language of the "narrow gate," the "few," the "seed." If after reading this book you feel you stand between the two traditions — not fully received by the Synagogue, not fully understood by the Church — the rabbi's answer is: you were meant to stand there. Rivkah's steps are between two houses.

⑤ 诚实的提问 ⑤ Honest Questions

  • 读完这八堂课，我对一位犹太拉比的看法有没有改变？我对自己作为基督徒的身份呢？Having walked through these eight lessons, has my sense of who a Jewish rabbi is shifted? What about my sense of who I am as a Christian?
  • 如果我「伸出手」，神就让会幕自己立起来——那我今天能伸出的那一只手是什么？If God will raise the tabernacle when I "put out my hand" — what is the one hand I can put out today?
  • 我是否愿意带着这本书继续——不只是读完，还是与一位拉比、一位犹太朋友、一位牧师谈论它？Am I willing to carry this book further — not just to read it, but to discuss it with a rabbi, a Jewish friend, or a pastor?

同行祝福 A Blessing for the Road

「愿耶和华向你仰脸，赐你平安。」

愿你做了，听见了；摔下了，蒙脸了；走完了远路，进入了家。

愿你在两个传统之间不孤单——因为利百加曾走过这条路，而那位预备好的新郎正在田间走出来迎接。 "The LORD lift up His countenance upon you, and give you peace."

May you do, and hear. May you fall, and be veiled. May you finish the long road, and enter the house.

May you not feel alone between the two traditions — for Rivkah has walked this road, and the Bridegroom is already coming out into the field to meet you.
