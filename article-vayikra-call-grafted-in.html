Vayikra ── 那個微小的 Aleph 與基督徒的呼召 Vayikra — The Tiny Aleph and the Call to Christians

與爱我民國際 (Ahavat Ammi) 的麥基翁牧師同站，沙皮拉拉比博士論證：今天教會內部正在進行一場「我民信仰之爭」(Milchemet Emunah)，外邦基督徒正被神親自呼召——藉由那個寫得格外微小的希伯來字母 Aleph。 Standing with Pastor Matt McKeon of Ahavat Ammi International, Rabbi Dr. Shapira argues that a “war of faith” (Milchemet Emunah) is now under way inside the Church — and that Gentile Christians are being personally summoned by God through the curiously small Hebrew letter Aleph.

沙皮拉拉比博士 · Ahavat Ammi 事工 Rabbi Dr. Itzhak Shapira · Ahavat Ammi Ministries

妥拉的第三卷書名叫 Vayikra（וַיִּקְרָא，「祂呼喚」）。在古老的羊皮卷上，這個字最後一個字母 Aleph（א）被刻意寫得異常微小——猶太傳統說，這是摩西自己的謙卑。在沙皮拉拉比看來，這個微小的 Aleph 不只是古代抄寫的細節：它是一個發給今日所有基督徒的個人呼召。在一個反猶聲浪重新席捲西方教會、自稱基督徒的人公開散播「血祭誣告」與「以色列種族滅絕」謊言的時代，拉比與麥基翁牧師同聲指出：真正的基督徒此刻必須謙卑站起來，作為「彌賽亞的弟兄」與祂的百姓並肩。 The third book of the Torah is called Vayikra (וַיִּקְרָא, “And He called”). On the ancient scroll, the final letter of this word — the Aleph (א) — is deliberately written unusually small. Jewish tradition teaches: that miniature letter is Moses' own humility. For Rabbi Shapira, that tiny Aleph is more than a scribal detail. It is a personal summons issued today to every Christian. In an age when antisemitism is sweeping back into the Western Church and self-styled Christian voices are openly trafficking in blood libel and “Israel-genocide” lies, the rabbi and Pastor McKeon name what is happening together: the genuine Christian must stand up, in humility, as the “brother of the Messiah,” shoulder to shoulder with His people.

一、「我民信仰之爭」── 教會內部的屬靈戰爭I. Milchemet Emunah — A War of Faith Inside the Church

中東有一場肉眼可見的戰爭——飛彈、砲火、警報。但拉比沙皮拉指出：在這場「物理戰爭」之外，教會內部正在進行一場看不見的戰爭，希伯來文叫 Milchemet Emunah（מלחמת אמונה，「信仰之戰」）。爭點是什麼？基督徒究竟要不要與以色列同站？ There is a visible war in the Middle East — missiles, artillery, sirens. But Rabbi Shapira points out: behind that physical war, an invisible war is being waged inside the Church. Hebrew calls it Milchemet Emunah (מלחמת אמונה, “war of faith”). The question at stake: will Christians stand with Israel, or not?

證據觸目驚心：自稱基督徒的網紅與牧者開始散播「血祭誣告」(blood libel)、宣稱「以色列在加薩犯下種族滅絕」、傳播「阿什肯納茲猶太人不是真猶太人」這類陰謀論。耶書亞 (Yeshua) 在馬太福音 25 章所說的「分別綿羊與山羊」此刻正以一種我們未曾預料的方式，發生在教會自身內部。 The evidence is stark: self-styled Christian influencers and pastors are now spreading blood libel, the “Israel-genocide” narrative about Gaza, and conspiracy theories like “Ashkenazi Jews aren't real Jews.” The “separation of sheep and goats” Yeshua spoke of in Matthew 25 is unfolding right now — in a way we did not expect — inside the Church itself.

二、Vayikra 與 Vayikar ── 神聖呼召 vs 偶然遇見II. Vayikra vs Vayikar — Holy Call vs Chance Encounter

希伯來文裡有兩個非常相近、但意義截然不同的字。Vayikra（וַיִּקְרָא）意思是「祂呼喚」——這是一個有意的、神聖的、親密的召喚。Vayikar（וַיִּקָּר）則是「祂偶然遇見」——一個沒有計劃、沒有親密度的偶然碰面。 Hebrew has two words that look almost identical and mean radically different things. Vayikra (וַיִּקְרָא) means “He called” — an intentional, holy, intimate summons. Vayikar (וַיִּקָּר) means “He happened upon” — an unplanned, impersonal encounter.

Vayikar 是民數記 23 章中神對待先知巴蘭 (Balaam) 的方式——巴蘭是一個被雇來咒詛以色列的先知，神「偶然遇見」他、糾正他，但並沒有與他建立親密關係。Vayikra 則是神對摩西的方式——「主從會幕中呼喚 (Vayikra) 摩西」（利未記 1:1）——這是一個面對面、有名有姓的、邀請進入聖殿核心的召喚。 Vayikar is the way God meets the false-prophet Balaam in Numbers 23 — Balaam was hired to curse Israel; God “happened upon” him to correct him, but with no intimacy. Vayikra is the way God meets Moses — “The LORD called (Vayikra) Moses out of the tent of meeting” (Leviticus 1:1) — a face-to-face, name-by-name summons into the holy of holies.

拉比沙皮拉的應用尖銳：今天，神不是「Vayikar」地對待愛祂的外邦基督徒——祂是 Vayikra。祂在呼喚你的名。問題只是：你是否聽見？ Rabbi Shapira's application is sharp: today, God is not Vayikar-ing the Gentile believers who love Him. He is Vayikra-ing them. He is calling you by name. The only question: do you hear?

三、那個微小的 Aleph ── 摩西的謙卑III. The Tiny Aleph — Moses' Humility

在猶太抄經傳統中，每一卷妥拉羊皮卷上，Vayikra 這個字的最後一個字母——Aleph（א）——必須被寫得明顯比其他字母小。為什麼？拉比傳統有兩個解釋，都指向「謙卑」： In the Jewish scribal tradition, on every Torah scroll the final letter of Vayikra — the Aleph (א) — must be written noticeably smaller than the surrounding letters. Why? The rabbinic tradition gives two explanations, both pointing to humility:

（1）摩西不願寫「神呼喚了我」(Vayikra) 這樣顯赫的字眼，他想寫「神偶然遇見了我」(Vayikar)，把自己降到巴蘭的層次。神堅持要寫 Vayikra，但允許摩西把那個 Aleph 寫得很小——作為謙卑的記號。 (1) Moses did not want to write that God “called me” (Vayikra) — such an honoured form. He wanted to write “He happened upon me” (Vayikar) — lowering himself to Balaam's level. God insisted on Vayikra, but allowed Moses to make that Aleph tiny — as a mark of humility.

（2）神親密呼喚的聲音是低沉、隱密、需要彎下身才能聽見的。微小的 Aleph 是這個「需要彎身才能聽見的呼喚」的視覺記號。 (2) The voice of God's intimate calling is quiet, hidden, requires you to bend low to hear it. The miniature Aleph is the visual sign of a summons you have to stoop to receive.

「外邦基督徒，你正被神呼喚——但呼喚的形式像那個微小的 Aleph：謙卑、隱密、需要你彎下身。不要對以色列誇口，不要把猶太人看作『沒接受耶書亞所以渺小』。彎下身。聽。」——沙皮拉拉比博士 “Gentile Christian, you are being called by God — but the call comes in the shape of that tiny Aleph: humble, hidden, requiring you to stoop. Do not boast over Israel. Do not view the Jewish people as ‘small’ because they have not accepted Yeshua. Stoop. Listen.”— Rabbi Dr. Itzhak Shapira

四、馬太福音 25:40 中「我的弟兄」究竟是誰？IV. Who Are “My Brothers” in Matthew 25:40?

馬太福音 25 章中，耶書亞描述末日的審判：「我實在告訴你們，這些事你們既做在我這弟兄中一個最小的身上，就是做在我身上了。」(馬太福音 25:40) 多數西方解經把「我這弟兄中一個最小的」泛指為「窮人」、「邊緣人」。但拉比沙皮拉指出：耶書亞作為一位猶太拉比，當祂用「我的弟兄」這個詞時，祂指的是祂血肉的同胞——猶太人。 In Matthew 25, Yeshua describes the final judgement: “Truly I tell you, whatever you did for one of the least of these brothers of mine, you did for me.” (Matt 25:40) Most Western readings take “the least of these my brothers” to mean simply “the poor,” “the marginalised.” But Rabbi Shapira observes: Yeshua was a Jewish rabbi, and when a Jewish rabbi says “my brothers,” He means His own flesh-and-blood — the Jewish people.

這個讀法並不削弱「服事貧窮人」的教導——它把這段經文先放回它的字面背景：神在末日要根據人們如何對待祂的猶太弟兄來分別綿羊與山羊。為以色列禱告、為猶太人挺身、在反猶聲浪中發聲——這些不是「政治選擇」，按耶書亞的話，這是對祂自己的對待方式。 This reading does not weaken the teaching of serving the poor — it restores the verse to its first, plain context: at the end, God will divide the sheep from the goats by how they treated His Jewish brothers. Praying for Israel, standing up for the Jewish people, speaking out against antisemitism — these are not “political choices.” In Yeshua's own words, they are how you treat Him.

五、羅馬書 11:18 ── 「不要向舊枝誇口」V. Romans 11:18 — “Do Not Boast Against the Natural Branches”

保羅在羅馬書 11 章用橄欖樹的比喻：以色列是天然的橄欖樹，外邦信徒是「被接上去」(grafted in) 的野橄欖枝。然後他發出警告：「你不要向舊枝誇口；若是誇口，當知道不是你托著根，乃是根托著你。」(羅馬書 11:18) Paul, in Romans 11, uses the olive-tree image: Israel is the natural olive tree; Gentile believers are wild branches grafted in. Then he issues a warning: “Do not boast over the branches. If you do, consider this: you do not support the root, but the root supports you.” (Romans 11:18)

麥基翁牧師強調這一點：今天許多教會偷偷地、有時公開地把這個次序顛倒了——彷彿教會是「真以色列」，而猶太人是「被切斷的舊枝」。這是誇口。這是反猶主義的神學形式。它違背使徒行傳所傳的彌賽亞信仰——那個信仰中，外邦人被接上以色列，而不是相反。 Pastor McKeon presses the point: many churches today have quietly — and sometimes loudly — inverted this order, as if the Church were “the true Israel” and the Jewish people the “severed old branches.” This is boasting. This is the theological form of antisemitism. It contradicts the Messianic faith of Acts itself, in which Gentiles are grafted into Israel, never the other way around.

六、走進以色列的力量VI. The Power of Walking on the Land Itself

兩位拉比與牧師都注意到：許多最尖銳地批評以色列的人，從未去過以色列。他們的判斷建立在媒體片段、社交平台短片與意識形態框架之上，而不是建立在親身踏上那片土地的經驗上。 Both the rabbi and the pastor observe: many of the loudest critics of Israel have never been there. Their judgements are built on media clips, social-platform shorts, and ideological frames — not on personal experience of standing on that ground.

親自走進耶路撒冷、走進耶書亞所走過的加利利、走進西牆腳下，會徹底改變一個基督徒讀聖經的方式。這不只是觀光——對華人基督徒而言，這是朝聖，也是當代信仰最缺乏的功課之一。 Walking through Jerusalem yourself, walking the Galilee Yeshua walked, standing at the foot of the Western Wall — these change the way a Christian reads the Bible, completely. This is not tourism. For the Chinese Christian, it is pilgrimage — and one of the most neglected disciplines of contemporary faith.

七、彌賽亞回來時 ── 一個猶太國度VII. When Messiah Returns — A Jewish Kingdom

拉比沙皮拉指出一個許多基督徒沒有正面思考的事實：當彌賽亞回來時，祂要建立一個猶太國度——耶路撒冷有一位猶太王，坐在大衛的寶座上。這不是次要的細節，這是新約反覆預言的核心圖景（路加福音 1:32-33；啟示錄 22:16；撒迦利亞書 14；以西結書 40-48）。 Rabbi Shapira raises a point many Christians have never squarely faced: when Messiah returns, He will establish a Jewish kingdom — a Jewish King in Jerusalem on David's throne. This is not a side detail. It is the recurring core picture of the New Testament itself (Luke 1:32–33; Revelation 22:16; Zechariah 14; Ezekiel 40–48).

那一刻來臨時，那些把耶書亞重新塑造成「西方版本」的基督徒會驚訝——他們會發現新婦的妝飾是猶太的、聖殿的祭儀是猶太的、君王是猶太的。今天這場「我民信仰之爭」的部分意義，就是讓基督徒在那一刻來臨之前，先學會看見這個事實。 When that moment arrives, Christians who have re-cast Yeshua in a Western image will be startled to discover that the bride's adornment is Jewish, the temple liturgy is Jewish, the King is Jewish. Part of the meaning of today's Milchemet Emunah is to teach Christians, before that moment arrives, to learn to see this.

八、對基督徒的呼召 ── 回到使徒行傳的信仰VIII. The Call to Christians — Return to the Faith of Acts

拉比與麥基翁牧師的呼召不是「成為猶太人」，而是回到使徒行傳所描述的彌賽亞信仰——一個猶太人與外邦人在彌賽亞裡合一的信仰，一個耶書亞自己日復一日所實踐的猶太信仰。 The rabbi's and the pastor's call is not “become Jewish.” It is return to the Messianic faith described in Acts — a faith of Jews and Gentiles united in Messiah, a Jewish faith that Yeshua Himself practised day in and day out.

具體而言：學一點希伯來文。讀一讀拉什 (Rashi) 對某段經文的解讀。理解一個猶太節日的內在含義。明白「橄欖樹」的比喻並非裝飾，而是末世論的核心。對華人基督徒來說，這個學習不必是學術性的——它可以從「為以色列禱告、買一本好的猶太背景聖經、跟隨一兩位真正能教導希伯來根源的老師」這樣簡單的步驟開始。 Concretely: learn a little Hebrew. Read what Rashi says on a passage. Understand the inner meaning of one Jewish feast. Grasp that the “olive tree” of Romans 11 is not decoration but the core of eschatology. For Chinese Christians this study need not be academic — it can begin from steps as simple as praying for Israel, buying a good Hebrew-roots study Bible, following one or two genuine Hebrew-root teachers.

九、關於分辨IX. A Word on Discernment

把馬太福音 25:40 的「我的弟兄」嚴格限定為「猶太人」是一個解經選擇——它有古代教父與一些當代學者的支持，但並不是所有基督徒解經家都同意。一個更穩妥的讀法是：「我的弟兄」首先指猶太人（按字面），並且擴展包括所有屬於彌賽亞的人。兩個層次都成立，後者不取消前者。 Restricting “my brothers” in Matthew 25:40 strictly to “Jewish people” is an interpretive choice — it has support from some ancient Fathers and some contemporary scholars, but not all Christian commentators agree. A safer reading is: “my brothers” first means the Jewish people (the plain Jewish sense), and extends to include all who belong to Messiah. Both layers stand; the second does not erase the first.

同樣，「不要向舊枝誇口」（羅 11:18）是聖經明文，不是解經。任何把教會抬到「真以色列」、把猶太人壓到「被切斷者」的神學，都直接違反這節經文。這不是Drash——這是P'shat。 Equally, “do not boast over the branches” (Rom 11:18) is plain text, not interpretation. Any theology that raises the Church to “the true Israel” and pushes Jewish people down to “the severed branches” directly contradicts this verse. That is not drash — it is p'shat.

十、結語 ── 彎下身，聽那個呼召X. Closing — Stoop, and Hear the Call

妥拉抄寫員在每一卷羊皮卷上，都把那個 Aleph 寫得格外微小——三千年來，沒有人改變這個傳統。它在那裡等著你彎下身去看。 Every Torah scribe, on every scroll, writes that Aleph deliberately small — three thousand years and no one has changed it. It is waiting there for you to stoop and see.

「外邦基督徒——你被呼召了。不是被『偶然遇見』，是被親密呼喚。彎下身。聽見。回到使徒行傳的信仰，與彌賽亞的弟兄們同站。」 “Gentile Christian — you are called. Not happened upon; summoned, by name. Stoop. Hear. Return to the faith of Acts, and stand beside the brothers of the Messiah.”
