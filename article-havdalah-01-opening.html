何謂事奉？── 開場與哈夫達拉禮 What Is Avodah? — Opening & the Havdalah Ceremony

在一場跨越全球的安息日尾聲聚會中，敬拜以歡迎與見證揭開序幕，隨即進入哈夫達拉（הַבְדָּלָה）禮——那分別「聖」與「俗」的祝禱。當會眾齊唱〈以利亞先知〉時，他們心中所等候的，正是大衛之子彌賽亞、耶書亞。 As a global family gathers at the close of Shabbat, worship opens with welcome and testimony, then moves into the Havdalah (הַבְדָּלָה) ceremony — the blessing that separates the holy from the common. As the congregation sings Eliyahu HaNavi, the longing in the room is for Mashiach ben David, Yeshua.

Morah Giborah McDonald · Ahavat Ammi 事工 Morah Giborah McDonald · Ahavat Ammi Ministries

安息日將盡，世界各地的弟兄姊妹——從牙買加到韓國——透過螢幕聚在一起。聚會的開場不是高深的教義，而是歌唱、歡迎與見證：一首取自巴蘭祝福的〈Ma Tovu〉，一句溫暖的「Shavua Tov」（שָׁבוּעַ טוֹב，「願你有美好的一週」）。然後，主領者帶領眾人進入這場聚會真正的核心——哈夫達拉（הַבְדָּלָה）禮：那分別「聖」與「俗」的祝禱。本文記述這頭十六分鐘所展開的，是一個關於分別、盼望與彌賽亞的故事。 As Shabbat draws to a close, a family scattered across the world — from Jamaica to Korea — gathers through their screens. The opening is not high doctrine but song, welcome, and testimony: a Ma Tovu drawn from Balaam's blessing, a warm “Shavua Tov” (שָׁבוּעַ טוֹב, “a good week to you”). Then the host leads everyone into the true heart of the gathering — the Havdalah (הַבְדָּלָה) ceremony, the blessing that separates the holy from the common. What unfolds in these first sixteen minutes is a story about separation, longing, and the Messiah.

一、開場 ── 從巴蘭的祝福到「Shavua Tov」I. The Opening — From Balaam's Blessing to “Shavua Tov”

聚會以一首歌開場。一位來自牙買加、名叫 Jimmy 的弟兄帶領眾人唱起〈Ma Tovu〉（מַה־טֹּבוּ，「你的帳棚何其華美」）。這首詩句出自一個出人意料的源頭——先知巴蘭（Balaam）。在民數記中，巴蘭原本受僱去咒詛以色列，但每次他開口，從他口中出來的卻是祝福。 The gathering opens with a song. A brother from Jamaica named Jimmy leads everyone in Ma Tovu (מַה־טֹּבוּ, “How goodly are your tents”). The line comes from an unexpected source — the prophet Balaam. In the book of Numbers, Balaam was hired to curse Israel, yet every time he opened his mouth, what came out was blessing.

主領者笑著把這個故事說得很白：巴蘭「以為自己很壞，其實他做了好事，因為他祝福了以色列」。這個小小的開場，藏著一個極深的真理：神的旨意能勝過人的惡意。就連一個想要咒詛的口，神也能扭轉成讚美的器皿。以這樣的歌開場，等於在說：今晚，我們聚在恩典之下。 The host tells the story plainly, with a smile: Balaam “thought he was bad, but he was really good, because he blessed Israel.” In that small opening hides a deep truth: God's will overrides human malice. Even a mouth that meant to curse, God can turn into an instrument of praise. To open with such a song is to say: tonight, we gather under grace.

接著，主領者 Maury Jackson Edwards 正式歡迎眾人來到 Ahavat Ammi International 的哈夫達拉聚會，並預告當晚的重頭戲：來自韓國的 Morah Giborah McDonald 將帶來一段教導，題目是「何謂事奉？（What Is Avodah?）」——談點燈、Shalom Aleichem、Kiddush（祝聖）等安息日的事奉細節。在進入教導之前，他先邀請眾人一同進行哈夫達拉。 The host, Maury Jackson Edwards, then formally welcomes everyone to the Havdalah service at Ahavat Ammi International and previews the evening's main teaching: Morah Giborah McDonald, joining from Korea, will teach on the theme “What Is Avodah?” — on candle lighting, the Shalom Aleichem, the Kiddush, and the other details of Shabbat service. But first, he invites everyone into the Havdalah.

這個開場本身就值得我們停下來細想。帶領詩歌的，是一位遠從牙買加而來的弟兄；當晚的主要教導，來自韓國；接下來的聚會，將在法國與比利時舉行。一首取自妥拉的古老希伯來詩歌，被一群來自天涯海角、語言文化各異的人同聲唱出。這不是偶然——這正是列國被接進以色列這棵橄欖樹的活生生畫面。 The opening itself is worth pausing over. The one leading the song is a brother from Jamaica; the evening's main teaching comes from Korea; the gatherings that follow will be held in France and Belgium. An ancient Hebrew song drawn from the Torah is sung in one voice by people from the ends of the earth, of every language and culture. This is no accident — it is a living picture of the nations grafted into the olive tree of Israel.

二、哈夫達拉 ── 分別「聖」與「俗」II. Havdalah — Separating the Holy from the Common

「哈夫達拉」（הַבְדָּלָה）這個希伯來字的字根意思就是「分別、區隔」。它是安息日結束時的一個簡短儀式，標記著神聖的安息日時光與接下來六個工作日的分界。會眾翻到《Avat HaOlam Siddur（禱文書）》第 87 頁，一同吟誦這串古老的祝禱。 The Hebrew word Havdalah (הַבְדָּלָה) comes from a root meaning “to separate, to divide.” It is a short ceremony marking the close of Shabbat — the boundary between the sacred time of the Sabbath and the six working days that follow. The congregation turns to page 87 of the Avat HaOlam Siddur and chants the ancient blessings together.

哈夫達拉禮通常包含三樣祝謝：一杯酒（生命的喜樂）、香料（besamim，安慰即將離去的安息日）、以及一支編織的蠟燭（ha'eish，火）。每一樣都以「Baruch atah Adonai Eloheinu melech haolam」（祢是應當稱頌的，耶和華我們的神，宇宙的君王）起頭。 The Havdalah service traditionally includes three blessings: a cup of wine (the joy of life), spices (besamim, to comfort the soul as Shabbat departs), and a braided candle (ha'eish, fire). Each begins with the words “Baruch atah Adonai Eloheinu melech haolam” — “Blessed are You, LORD our God, King of the universe.”

儀式的核心，是那句定義整個哈夫達拉的祝禱： The heart of the ceremony is the blessing that defines the whole of Havdalah:

הַמַּבְדִּיל בֵּין קֹדֶשׁ לְחֹל
Ha'mavdil ben kodesh lechol
「那分別聖（kodesh）與俗（chol）的主」 הַמַּבְדִּיל בֵּין קֹדֶשׁ לְחֹל
Ha'mavdil ben kodesh lechol
“He who separates the holy (kodesh) from the common (chol)”

這句話不只是描述一個動作；它在宣告神的一個屬性。分別聖與俗的，是神自己。祝禱完整地說：祂分別光明與黑暗、以色列與列國、第七日與工作的六日。哈夫達拉因此不是「結束」，而是一次清醒的劃分——提醒我們，神所設立的界線是真實的、是要被守護的。 This is not merely the description of an action; it is a declaration of who God is. It is God Himself who separates the holy from the common. The full blessing names the separations: light from darkness, Israel from the nations, the seventh day from the six days of labour. Havdalah, then, is not simply an “ending” but a sober act of distinction — a reminder that the boundaries God has set are real, and meant to be guarded.

三、以利亞先知 ── 為彌賽亞開路III. Eliyahu HaNavi — Preparing the Way for Messiah

祝禱之後，眾人齊聲喊出「Shavua Tov!」（שָׁבוּעַ טוֹב，「願你有美好的一週！」），然後唱起一首特別的詩歌——〈以利亞先知〉（אֵלִיָּהוּ הַנָּבִיא，Eliyahu HaNavi）。主領者特意解釋了為什麼此刻要唱這首歌。 After the blessing, everyone cries out “Shavua Tov!” (שָׁבוּעַ טוֹב, “a good week!”) and breaks into a special song — Eliyahu HaNavi (אֵלִיָּהוּ הַנָּבִיא, “Elijah the Prophet”). The host deliberately explains why this song belongs at this moment.

「我們一起唱〈以利亞先知〉。我們知道，在逾越節（Pesach）我們會走到門口尋找以利亞——而在以利亞先知的身後，我們所尋找的，是大衛之子耶書亞（Yeshua ben David），大衛之子彌賽亞（Mashiach ben David）。」—— 哈夫達拉主領者 “We are singing Eliyahu HaNavi together. As we know, during Pesach we go to the door and look for Eliyah — and behind Eliyahu HaNavi, we are looking for Yeshua ben David, Mashiach ben David.”— the Havdalah host

在猶太傳統中，先知以利亞是彌賽亞時代的先驅。瑪拉基書 4:5 應許：「看哪，耶和華大而可畏之日未到以前，我必差遣先知以利亞到你們那裡去。」因此在逾越節的家宴上，猶太家庭會為以利亞留一杯酒、開一扇門——期盼著那位為彌賽亞開路的先知。 In Jewish tradition, the prophet Elijah is the forerunner of the Messianic age. Malachi 4:5 promises: “Behold, I will send you Elijah the prophet before the great and awesome day of the LORD comes.” So at the Passover table, Jewish families set out a cup for Elijah and open a door — longing for the prophet who prepares the way for Messiah.

而這正是這場彌賽亞信仰聚會的關鍵轉折：他們唱〈以利亞先知〉，眼睛卻越過以利亞，望向大衛之子彌賽亞（מָשִׁיחַ בֶּן דָּוִד，Mashiach ben David）——耶書亞。歌詞反覆唱著「願祂快快臨到我們，與彌賽亞同來」。先知的傳統，在此被直接接上那位已經來過、且必再來的猶太君王。 This is the decisive turn of a Messianic gathering: they sing Eliyahu HaNavi, but their eyes look past Elijah toward Mashiach ben David (מָשִׁיחַ בֶּן דָּוִד, “Messiah, son of David”) — Yeshua. The lyric repeats, “may he come quickly to us, with the Messiah.” The prophetic tradition is joined directly to the Jewish King who has come, and who is coming again.

值得留意的是，這份盼望在哈夫達拉的每一個元素中都已暗暗鋪陳。那杯滿溢的酒，是默西亞宴席的預嚐；那香料的芬芳，安慰著因安息日離去而暫感失落的靈魂；那編織的火光，則指向「世界的光」。當會眾在週末的尾聲齊喊「Shavua Tov」，他們不是在道別，而是在向前張望——望向那將一切「俗」都轉化為「聖」的日子。 It is worth noticing that this hope is quietly woven into every element of Havdalah. The brimming cup is a foretaste of the Messianic banquet; the fragrance of the spices comforts a soul momentarily bereft as Shabbat departs; the braided flame points to the “light of the world.” When the congregation cries “Shavua Tov” at the close of the weekend, they are not saying goodbye but looking forward — toward the day when all that is “common” will be made “holy.”

四、一個全球家庭 ── 韓國、巴黎與蓬勃的事工IV. A Global Family — Korea, Paris, and a Growing Ministry

在祝禱之間，主領者分享了幾項報告，讓人看見這個彌賽亞信仰群體跨越國界的樣貌。接下來兩週，Noah 與沙皮拉拉比將在法國巴黎的伯特利（Beit El）；隨後在比利時有安息日聚會，並發行《以東的陷落（Fall of Edom）》荷蘭文版。七月還有 Shuvu 青年營（7 月 24–27 日）。 Between the blessings, the host shares several announcements that reveal a Messianic community reaching across borders. Over the next two weeks, Noah and Rabbi Shapira will be in Paris, France at Beit El; then in Belgium for a Shabbaton and the release of the Fall of Edom in Dutch. In July there is also the Shuvu youth camp (July 24–27).

這段教導本身則錄自韓國——當晚稍後，Morah Giborah McDonald 將從韓國坐下來，講述安息日前夕（Erev Shabbat）所發生的事，特別是事奉（Avodah）的意義。當她受到熱情款待時，她由衷地說：「西方可以從韓國文化、從你們的待客之道，學到很多。」一句問候「Shabbat Shalom」橫跨了大陸——這正是一個沒有國界的屬靈家庭。 The teaching itself was recorded in Korea — later that evening, Morah Giborah McDonald sits down from Korea to recount what happened on Erev Shabbat, especially the meaning of Avodah. Received with warm hospitality, she says from the heart: “The West can learn a lot from Korean culture, and from your hospitality.” A single greeting, “Shabbat Shalom,” crosses continents — this is a spiritual family without borders.

這些報告看似只是「事務性公告」，卻恰好呼應了哈夫達拉的精神。一本書被翻成荷蘭文、一場聚會在巴黎舉行、一群青年在七月相聚——這些都是把「聖」帶進日常、把分別出來的時光延伸到一整週的具體行動。事奉（Avodah）並不終止於蠟燭熄滅的那一刻；它從安息日的桌前，一路走進世界的各個角落。 These announcements may sound like mere “housekeeping,” yet they echo the very spirit of Havdalah. A book translated into Dutch, a gathering held in Paris, a group of young people coming together in July — each is a concrete act of carrying the “holy” into the ordinary, of stretching the set-apart time across a whole week. Avodah does not end the moment the candle is extinguished; it travels from the Shabbat table out into every corner of the world.

五、對華人信徒的意義 ── 學習「分別」V. What This Means for Chinese Believers — Learning to Separate

第一，學習為神「分別」時間與空間。哈夫達拉的核心，是「分別聖與俗」。對在現代忙碌生活中奔波的華人信徒而言，這是一個強而有力的操練：刻意地把一段時間、一個空間分別出來歸給神，正如那支哈夫達拉的蠟燭標記著一段神聖時光的結束與另一段的開始。神聖，往往始於一個清醒的界線。 First, learn to “separate” time and space for God. The heart of Havdalah is the separation of holy from common. For Chinese believers carried along by the rush of modern life, this is a powerful discipline: to intentionally set apart a time and a space for God, just as the Havdalah candle marks the end of one sacred time and the beginning of another. Holiness often begins with a sober boundary.

第二，事奉（Avodah）是主動的，不是被動的。聖潔不會自己發生。Morah Giborah 即將教導的點燈、祝聖、禱告，都提醒我們：敬拜需要用心、刻意的參與。我們的行動必須扎根於神聖的源頭，無論四圍多麼喧囂。 Second, Avodah (service) is active, not passive. Holiness does not happen on its own. The candle lighting, the Kiddush, the prayers that Morah Giborah is about to teach all remind us: worship requires mindful, deliberate participation. Our actions must be rooted in a sacred source, however loud the surrounding chaos.

第三，懷著期盼的心而活。那杯為以利亞而留的酒、那扇敞開的門，都在訴說一種盼望——大衛之子彌賽亞必要再來。當會眾唱「願祂快快臨到」時，他們不是在演一場儀式，而是在催促那日子。願華人信徒也學會抬起頭，望向那位將要回來的猶太君王。 Third, live with an expectant heart. The cup set out for Elijah, the door left open — both speak of a longing: that Mashiach ben David will surely return. When the congregation sings “may he come quickly,” they are not performing a ritual; they are hastening the day. May Chinese believers also learn to lift their eyes toward the Jewish King who is coming back.

六、結語 ── 願你有美好的一週VI. Closing — May You Have a Good Week

「הַמַּבְדִּיל בֵּין קֹדֶשׁ לְחֹל ── 分別聖與俗的，是神自己。今晚我們唱〈以利亞先知〉，眼睛卻望向那位在他身後的——大衛之子耶書亞。為自己預備吧，因那猶太君王將要回來。Shavua Tov，願你有美好的一週。」 “Ha'mavdil ben kodesh lechol — it is God Himself who separates the holy from the common. Tonight we sing Eliyahu HaNavi, yet our eyes are on the One behind him — Yeshua ben David. Prepare yourself, for the Jewish King is returning. Shavua Tov — may you have a good week.”
