律法如呼吸 ── 內化妥拉與歸回的呼召 The Torah as Breath — Internalizing the Law and the Call to Return

尼澤明篇（申命記 29–30）站在約的門檻上：以色列全會眾「站立」在神面前。沙皮拉拉比博士提醒我們，妥拉不能只停在「遵守」的層面——它必須內化，成為我們的本能、我們的呼吸、我們的血液。這就是תְּשׁוּבָה teshuvah（歸回）的心：因為「這話離你甚近，就在你口中、在你心裡，使你可以遵行」（申 30:14）。 Parashat Nitzavim (Deuteronomy 29–30) stands at the threshold of the covenant: all Israel “standing” before God. Rabbi Dr. Shapira reminds us that the Torah cannot stay at the level of “observance” — it must be internalized until it becomes our instinct, our breath, our blood. This is the heart of תְּשׁוּבָה teshuvah (return): for “the word is very near you, in your mouth and in your heart, that you may do it” (Deut 30:14).

「各位，大家好。在我們學習妥拉的這段時間裡，我很高興能與大家一起走過這段旅程。」沙皮拉拉比博士這樣開始這篇尼澤明篇（נִצָּבִים Nitzavim，「站立」）的短講。在這卷申命記第 29–30 章的經文裡，摩西召聚全以色列——首領、長老、官長、男人、女人、孩子、寄居的，連砍柴打水的——都「站立」(nitzavim) 在耶和華面前立約。而拉比要我們聽見的，不只是「站著守約」，而是一個更深的呼召：把妥拉內化，直到它成為你的本能、你的呼吸、你的血液。 “Friends, everyone. During this time that we are studying the Torah, I am delighted to walk this journey with all of you.” So Rabbi Dr. Shapira opens this short teaching on Parashat נִצָּבִים Nitzavim (“standing”). In Deuteronomy 29–30 Moses gathers all Israel — heads, elders, officers, men, women, children, the sojourner, even the woodcutter and the water-drawer — all of them “standing” (nitzavim) before the LORD to enter the covenant. And what the rabbi wants us to hear is not merely “stand and keep the rules,” but a deeper call: internalize the Torah, until it becomes your instinct, your breath, your blood.

一、站立在門檻上 ── נִצָּבִיםI. Standing at the Threshold — נִצָּבִים

尼澤明篇的第一個字就是它的名字：「你們今日都站立在耶和華你們神的面前」（申 29:10，希伯來文 29:9）。希伯來文 נִצָּבִים nitzavim 不是「隨便站著」，而是「堅立、就位、嚴陣以待」——像士兵在指揮官面前列隊，像見證人在法庭上就位。摩西在他生命的最後幾天，把整個民族帶到約的門檻上。 The opening word of the portion is its name: “You are standing today, all of you, before the LORD your God” (Deut 29:10, Heb. 29:9). The Hebrew נִצָּבִים nitzavim is not “loitering” — it is “firmly stationed, taking one's post, standing at attention” — like soldiers drawn up before a commander, like witnesses taking their place in court. In the last days of his life, Moses brings the whole nation to the threshold of the covenant.

這個「站立」有一個極重要的細節：經文刻意把每一個階層都點名——「首領、眾支派、長老、官長」與「你們的妻子、兒女，並你營中寄居的，以及為你劈柴挑水的人」（申 29:10-11）。在神的約面前，沒有人是旁觀者。最尊貴的與最卑微的，並肩站立。這就是尼澤明篇的第一課：約不是給一小群「宗教專家」的，而是給整個會眾、給每一個人的。 There is a crucial detail in this “standing”: the text deliberately names every layer — “your heads, your tribes, your elders and your officers” together with “your little ones, your wives, and the sojourner in your camp, from the one who cuts your wood to the one who draws your water” (Deut 29:10–11). Before the covenant of God, no one is a spectator. The highest and the lowliest stand side by side. This is the first lesson of Nitzavim: the covenant is not for a small guild of “religious experts,” but for the whole congregation — for every single person.

「你們今日都站立在耶和華你們神的面前……為要你順從耶和華你神今日與你所立的約。」——申命記 29:10, 12 “You are standing today, all of you, before the LORD your God … so that you may enter into the covenant of the LORD your God, which He is making with you today.”— Deuteronomy 29:10, 12

二、不能只停在「遵守」的層面II. We Cannot Stop at “Observance”

拉比說，在我們學習了妥拉之後，我們學會了如何過一個有意義的生活——「如何敬畏神，如何愛我們的鄰舍，如何遵守神的旨意，如何過一個安穩、有秩序的生活。」這是妥拉所帶給我們的真實恩典。但我們不能只停留在「遵守」的層面。 After studying the Torah, the rabbi says, we have learned how to live a meaningful life — “how to fear God, how to love our neighbor, how to keep God's will, how to live a stable and ordered life.” This is the genuine gift the Torah gives us. But we cannot remain merely at the level of “observance.”

這正是尼澤明篇的核心張力。許多人把誡命 (מִצְוֺת mitzvot) 當作一張外在的清單：做完了就交差了。但摩西在這裡所要的，遠不止於此。下一章他會說：神要「使你心裡和你後裔心裡受割禮」（申 30:6）——不是手上的記號，而是心 (לֵב lev) 的更新。外在的順服若沒有內在的轉化，就只是空殼。 This is the very tension at the heart of Nitzavim. Many treat the commandments (מִצְוֺת mitzvot) as an external checklist: do them, and you are done. But what Moses wants here is far more. In the next chapter he says God will “circumcise your heart and the heart of your offspring” (Deut 30:6) — not a mark on the hand, but a renewal of the heart (לֵב lev). Outward obedience without inward transformation is only a shell.

所以拉比的呼籲是：「我們必須將這些律法，內化為我們的生命。」不是把妥拉記在腦中、掛在門框上就夠了——而是要讓它住進我們裡面，重塑我們的渴望、我們的反應、我們本能的方向。 So the rabbi's appeal: “We must internalize these laws into our very lives.” It is not enough to memorize the Torah in the mind or hang it on the doorpost — it must move in and dwell within us, reshaping our desires, our reactions, the very bent of our instincts.

三、律法如呼吸 ── 妥拉成為你的血液III. The Torah as Breath — When It Becomes Your Blood

這篇短講最有力的一段，是拉比三次重複的呼喊。它把妥拉從「學術的、法律的」領域，搬到了「生命的、存在的」領域： The most powerful passage of this short teaching is a threefold cry from the rabbi. It moves the Torah out of the “academic, legal” realm and into the “biological, existential” realm:

「我們必須讓律法，成為我們的本能。我們必須讓律法，成為我們的呼吸。我們必須讓律法，成為我們的血液。這才是真正的『活出律法』。」——沙皮拉拉比博士 “We must allow the Torah to become our instinct. We must allow the Torah to become our breath. We must allow the Torah to become our blood. This is what it means to truly ‘live the Torah.’”— Rabbi Dr. Shapira

這個意象並非憑空而來。尼澤明篇的高峰正是申命記 30:14：「這話離你甚近，就在你口中、在你心裡，使你可以遵行。」希伯來文 כִּי־קָרוֹב אֵלֶיךָ הַדָּבָר מְאֹד（ki-karov elecha ha-davar me'od，「這話離你甚近」）——妥拉不是「在天上」、不是「在海外」（申 30:12-13），叫人遙不可及；它已經在你的口、在你的心裡。這正是「成為呼吸、成為血液」的聖經根據：神所要的順服，是發自一顆被祂的話充滿的心。 This image does not come from nowhere. The summit of Nitzavim is Deuteronomy 30:14: “The word is very near you, in your mouth and in your heart, that you may do it.” The Hebrew כִּי־קָרוֹב אֵלֶיךָ הַדָּבָר מְאֹד (ki-karov elecha ha-davar me'od, “the word is very near you”) — the Torah is not “in heaven,” nor “beyond the sea” (Deut 30:12–13), out of reach; it is already in your mouth and in your heart. This is the scriptural ground for “becoming breath, becoming blood”: the obedience God seeks flows from a heart already filled with His word.

這也呼應了שְׁמַע Shema（「以色列啊，你要聽」）的命令：「我今日所吩咐你的話都要記在心上……也要繫在手上為記號，戴在額上為經文」（申 6:6-8）。Shema 從來不是「聽過就算」，而是「聽進去、刻在心版上、活出來」。尼澤明篇把這個命令推到極致：妥拉要近到成為你的本能反應。 This also echoes the command of the שְׁמַע Shema (“Hear, O Israel”): “These words that I command you today shall be on your heart … bind them as a sign on your hand, let them be frontlets between your eyes” (Deut 6:6–8). The Shema was never “hear it and move on” — it is “take it in, engrave it on the tablet of the heart, and live it out.” Nitzavim pushes that command to its furthest point: the Torah should be so near that it becomes your instinct itself.

四、不是外在的規則，而是內在的指引IV. Not an External Rule, but an Internal Guide

在這裡，拉比的語氣轉為更具哲學思辨性，他給出了整篇教導的論題句： Here the rabbi's tone shifts to something more contemplative, and he gives the thesis statement of the whole teaching:

「我們必須明白，律法不是一個外在的規則。律法，它是一個內在的指引。它告訴我們，我們應該成為什麼樣的人；它告訴我們，我們應該如何對待這個世界。」——沙皮拉拉比博士 “We must understand that the Torah is not an external set of rules. The Torah is an internal guide. It tells us what kind of people we ought to become; it tells us how we ought to treat this world.”— Rabbi Dr. Shapira

這個轉變極其重要。一本「規則書」只回答「我可不可以做這件事」；一本「內在的指引」卻回答更深的問題——「我該成為怎樣的人」。妥拉不只規範行為，它塑造品格。拉比因此呼籲：「我們必須學會，將律法的精神，帶入到我們生活的每一個角落；在每一次的選擇中，都能體現出律法的智慧；在每一次的互動中，都能體現出律法的慈悲。」 This shift matters enormously. A “rulebook” only answers “may I do this thing?”; an “internal guide” answers the deeper question — “who am I to become?” The Torah does not merely regulate behavior; it forms character. So the rabbi urges: “We must learn to bring the spirit of the Torah into every corner of our lives; to manifest the wisdom of the Torah in every choice we make; to manifest the compassion of the Torah in every interaction.”

這與尼澤明篇的應許完全相合。摩西說，當神「使你心裡受割禮」之後，「你必歸回，聽從耶和華的話，遵行祂一切的誡命」（申 30:8-10）——順服不再是咬牙忍耐的重擔，而是從一顆被更新的心裡自然流出的。智慧與慈悲，不是外加的要求，而是內化之妥拉的果子。 This fits Nitzavim's own promise exactly. Moses says that when God “circumcises your heart,” then “you shall again obey the voice of the LORD and do all His commandments” (Deut 30:8–10) — obedience is no longer a teeth-gritted burden, but something that flows naturally from a renewed heart. Wisdom and compassion are not external demands bolted on; they are the fruit of an internalized Torah.

五、歸回 ── תְּשׁוּבָה 的呼召V. The Call to Return — תְּשׁוּבָה

接著，拉比的語氣轉為對「歸回」的呼喚——而這正是尼澤明篇的神學心臟。希伯來文 תְּשׁוּבָה teshuvah（「歸回／悔改」）來自動詞 שׁוּב shuv（「轉回、回轉」）。尼澤明篇連續用了七次這個字根：「你必回轉歸向耶和華……耶和華也必使你回轉」（申 30:2-3）。歸回是雙向的：人轉向神，神也轉向人。 Then the rabbi's tone turns to a call for “Return” — and this is the very theological heart of Nitzavim. The Hebrew תְּשׁוּבָה teshuvah (“return / repentance”) comes from the verb שׁוּב shuv (“to turn back, to return”). Nitzavim uses this root seven times in a row: “you shall return to the LORD … and the LORD will restore you” (Deut 30:2–3). The return is two-directional: the people turn to God, and God turns to them.

「我們必須學會『歸回』。回歸到律法的源頭。回歸到神的旨意。回歸到我們最初的純淨狀態。我們必須學會，放下我們所有的雜念、所有的慾望、所有的驕傲。」——沙皮拉拉比博士 “We must learn ‘Return.’ To return to the source of the Torah. To return to God's will. To return to our original state of purity. We must learn to let go of all our distractions, all our desires, and all our pride.”— Rabbi Dr. Shapira

拉比把歸回描述為一個「卸下」的過程：放下雜念、慾望、驕傲。這正是猶太傳統中 teshuvah 的精神——不是賺取，而是剝除；不是堆積更多宗教成就，而是除去那些擋在你與神之間的東西，「像一個孩子一樣，謙卑地回到神的懷抱」。而尼澤明篇給出的最大安慰是：這條歸回的路永遠開著。哪怕以色列被趕散到天涯（申 30:1, 4），神仍應許要把他們招聚回來。沒有一個人遠到不能歸回。 The rabbi describes return as a process of “letting go”: releasing distraction, desire, pride. This is precisely the spirit of teshuvah in Jewish tradition — not earning, but stripping away; not piling up more religious achievement, but removing what stands between you and God, “returning to God's embrace with the humility of a child.” And the great comfort Nitzavim offers is this: the road home is always open. Even if Israel is scattered to the ends of the heavens (Deut 30:1, 4), God still promises to gather them back. No one is too far gone to return.

六、把妥拉當作「活著的指南」VI. Treat the Torah as a “Living Guide”

教導的結尾，拉比的語氣轉為鼓勵與期許。他給會眾一個最終、可實行的功課——不要把妥拉當作一本書來讀。 The teaching closes with encouragement and expectation. The rabbi gives the congregation one final, actionable task — do not read the Torah as a mere book.

尼澤明篇本身就把這個抉擇擺在我們面前。在 30:11-14 確認妥拉「離你甚近」之後，30:15-19 摩西宣告：「看哪，我今日將生與福、死與禍，陳明在你面前……所以你要揀選生命，使你和你的後裔都得存活。」（申 30:19）妥拉不是一份要被歸檔的古老文件——它是每一天擺在你面前的活的抉擇。每一個清晨，「揀選生命」這個呼召都重新臨到。 Nitzavim itself sets this choice before us. After confirming in 30:11–14 that the Torah is “very near you,” in 30:15–19 Moses declares: “See, I have set before you today life and good, death and evil … therefore choose life, that you and your offspring may live” (Deut 30:19). The Torah is not an ancient document to be filed away — it is a living choice set before you every single day. Each morning the call to “choose life” comes again.

這也正是「妥拉珍珠」這個系列的心意：每週的妥拉段落不是要我們讀過去，而是要我們活進去。拉比的願望很樸素，也很深：讓妥拉指引你的每一步，照亮你心裡的每一個房間，把它的精神帶進你每一個平凡的日子。 This is exactly the heart of the “Pearls of the Torah” series: each week's portion is not meant to be read past, but lived into. The rabbi's wish is simple and deep: let the Torah guide your every step, illuminate every chamber of your heart, and bring its spirit into each of your ordinary days.

七、關於分辨VII. A Word on Discernment

這篇教導大部分是 derash（דְּרַשׁ，講道式的、屬靈的引申），而非逐字的釋經。「讓妥拉成為你的血液、你的呼吸」這樣的語言是有力的、詩意的、高度比喻性的——它表達的是「願神的話充滿並更新我整個人」的屬靈渴望，而不是字面的生理或律法主張。應當把它讀作屬靈的勸勉，而不是新的教義。 Much of this teaching is derash (דְּרַשׁ, homiletical, spiritual extension) rather than verse-by-verse exegesis. Language like “let the Torah become your blood, your breath” is powerful, poetic, and highly metaphorical — it voices the spiritual longing for God's word to fill and renew the whole person, not a literal physiological or legal claim. It should be read as a spiritual exhortation, not a new doctrine.

話雖如此，這篇短講所站立的根基——尼澤明篇的明文——是P'shat（字面、平實的意思）：妥拉「離你甚近，在你口中、在你心裡」（申 30:14）；神應許「使你心裡受割禮」（申 30:6）；歸回的路永遠敞開（申 30:2-3）；生命就擺在你面前，要你揀選（申 30:19）。拉比所做的，是把這些平實的經文，吹進今日聽者的心裡。 That said, the ground on which this short teaching stands — the plain text of Nitzavim — is p'shat (the literal, straightforward sense): the Torah is “very near you, in your mouth and in your heart” (Deut 30:14); God promises to “circumcise your heart” (Deut 30:6); the road of return is always open (Deut 30:2–3); life is set before you to choose (Deut 30:19). What the rabbi does is breathe these plain verses into the hearts of today's hearers.

八、對華人信徒的意義VIII. What This Means for Chinese Believers

第一，「內化」這個主題，對深受「信心 vs. 行為」之爭影響的華人教會是一帖良藥。尼澤明篇告訴我們：神所要的，從來不是「外在守規矩」與「內在有信心」二選一，而是一顆被祂的話更新、以致順服自然流出的心。妥拉「在你心裡」——這與保羅在羅馬書 10:8 引用申命記 30:14 來描述「信道之話」，是同一條線。 First, the theme of “internalization” is medicine for a Chinese church often caught in a “faith vs. works” tug-of-war. Nitzavim tells us God has never asked us to choose between “outward rule-keeping” and “inward faith,” but seeks a heart renewed by His word so that obedience flows from it. The Torah “in your heart” — this is the same thread Paul picks up when he quotes Deuteronomy 30:14 in Romans 10:8 to describe “the word of faith.”

第二，teshuvah（歸回）的應許，對任何覺得自己「走得太遠、回不去」的人，都是巨大的盼望。尼澤明篇明說：哪怕被趕散到天邊，歸回的門仍然開著。這對華人信徒——無論身在何地、無論曾經如何偏離——都是同一個應許：沒有一個人遠到神的手搆不著。 Second, the promise of teshuvah (return) is enormous hope for anyone who feels they have “gone too far to come back.” Nitzavim says plainly: even scattered to the ends of the heavens, the door of return stays open. For Chinese believers — wherever they are, however far they may have wandered — it is the same promise: no one is too far for God's hand to reach.

第三，「揀選生命」是每日的功課，不是一次性的決定。把神的話「當作一本活著的指南」，意味著每個清晨重新站立 (nitzavim) 在祂面前，重新揀選。這正是「妥拉珍珠」邀請我們一起操練的生活方式——讓古老的話，成為今天的呼吸。 Third, “choosing life” is a daily discipline, not a one-time decision. To treat God's word as “a living guide” means standing (nitzavim) before Him afresh each morning and choosing again. This is precisely the way of life “Pearls of the Torah” invites us to practice together — letting the ancient word become today's breath.

九、結語 ── 站立，並歸回IX. Closing — Stand, and Return

「所以，各位，我期盼大家，不要只是將妥拉當作一本書來讀；我期盼大家，將它當作一本『活著的指南』。讓它指引你們的每一步，照亮你們心裡的每一個房間。讓我們一起，把妥拉內化成我們的呼吸、我們的血液——並一同歸回。因為這話離你甚近，就在你口中、在你心裡，使你可以遵行。」 “So, friends, I hope you will not merely read the Torah as a book; I hope you will treat it as a ‘living guide.’ Let it guide your every step and illuminate every chamber of your heart. Let us together internalize the Torah until it becomes our breath, our blood — and let us return. For the word is very near you, in your mouth and in your heart, that you may do it.”
