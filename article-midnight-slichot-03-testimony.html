見證 ── 以色列之歌與羔羊之歌 A Testimony — The Song of Israel and the Song of the Lamb

一通急切的電話，一趟說走就走的飛行；一位八十五歲、淚流滿面的正統派猶太長者；五十五年寫下的詩歌樂譜，作為一份「傳承」交在外邦人手中。在這份見證裡，以色列之歌與羔羊之歌合而為一——成了猶太人與外邦人一同進入敬拜的活畫面。 An urgent phone call, a flight taken at a moment's notice; an eighty-five-year-old Orthodox Jewish man with tears in his eyes; fifty-five years of melodies handed over as a “legacy” into a Gentile's hands. In this testimony, the song of Israel and the song of the Lamb become one — a living picture of Jew and Gentile entering worship together.

這不是一篇講章，而是一個見證——在午夜的悔罪服事（Slichot）裡，講者放下大綱，講起一件真實發生在他身上的事：一通急切的電話、一趟連夜起飛的航班、一位八十五歲的正統派猶太長者、五十五年寫成的詩歌樂譜。故事的核心是一個小小的、卻又無比巨大的神蹟——以色列之歌與羔羊之歌，在一位外邦人的手中合而為一。它是一幅活的圖畫：猶太人與外邦人，一同站在敬拜裡，一同等候那要回家的日子。 This is not a sermon but a testimony. In the midnight penitential service (Slichot) the speaker sets his outline aside and tells something that actually happened to him: an urgent phone call, an overnight flight, an eighty-five-year-old Orthodox Jewish elder, fifty-five years of melodies written down by hand. At the heart of the story is a small — yet immense — miracle: the song of Israel and the song of the Lamb, joined together in the hands of a Gentile. It is a living picture: Jew and Gentile, standing together in worship, together awaiting the day of the homecoming.

一、那通電話 ── 「現在就上飛機」I. The Phone Call — “Get On a Plane Right Now”

見證從一通電話開始。講者認識那座城市，於是他打電話到當地的會堂，把一張照片傳給那裡的拉比。三十分鐘後，回話來了：「我們找到了他的孫子。他八十五歲。他問你能不能現在就上飛機，立刻來見他。」 The testimony begins with a phone call. The speaker knew the city, so he called the local synagogue and sent a photograph to the rabbi there. Thirty minutes later the answer came back: “We found his grandson. He is eighty-five years old. He is asking whether you can get on a plane right now and come to meet him immediately.”

沒有遲疑，沒有討價還價。「第二天早上，我就上了那班飛機。」講者飛越半個地球，抵達澳洲。他來了，為要見一個他從未謀面、卻早已等候著他的人。 No hesitation, no negotiating. “The next morning, I got on that plane.” The speaker flew halfway around the world and arrived in Australia — to meet a man he had never seen, yet who had long been waiting for him.

二、Baruch Haba ── 含淚的歡迎II. Baruch Haba — A Welcome Through Tears

門開了。一位八十五歲、完全正統派的猶太長者迎接他——一個身材矮小的老人，雙手微微顫抖。他伸出那雙抖動的手，用希伯來文說出最古老的歡迎：בָּרוּךְ הַבָּא（Baruch Haba，「奉主名來的，是應當稱頌的／歡迎你」）。 The door opened. An eighty-five-year-old, fully Orthodox Jewish man received him — a small man whose hands trembled. He reached out those shaking hands and spoke the most ancient of welcomes in Hebrew: בָּרוּךְ הַבָּא (Baruch Haba, “Blessed is the one who comes — welcome”).

講者描述那一刻：「當他看見我的時候，我能看見他眼中的淚水。」這不是禮貌性的寒暄，而是一場等候已久的相認。長者把他帶回自己的家。他妻子的名字叫以斯帖（Esther）——而講者母親的名字，也叫以斯帖。一個小小的細節，像一條暗線，把兩個素不相識的家庭縫在了一起。 The speaker describes the moment: “When he saw me, I could see tears in his eyes.” This was no polite greeting but a long-awaited recognition. The elder brought him home. His wife's name was Esther — and the speaker's own mother is also named Esther. A small detail, like a hidden thread, stitched two strangers' households together.

三、五十五年 ── 一位 chazzan 的傳承III. Fifty-Five Years — The Legacy of a Chazzan

長者問了一個問題：「你知道我這過去五十五年都在做什麼嗎？」講者向他提起自己的兒子，說孩子想成為一位cantor（領唱者）。長者聽了，回答說：「我就是這會堂的指揮、領唱的大師。」 The elder asked a question: “Do you know what I have been doing these past fifty-five years?” The speaker mentioned his own son, saying the boy wanted to become a cantor. The elder answered: “I am the master conductor and cantor of this synagogue.”

在猶太傳統裡，這位「領唱者」就是חַזָּן（chazzan，「領唱／頌禱者」）——會堂中帶領全會眾以歌聲向神禱告的人。他不是表演者，而是把整個群體的呼求、悔罪、與盼望，化作旋律托上去的那一位。長者一生五十五年所做的，就是這件事：為神的子民譜寫、保存、領唱那一首又一首的שִׁיר（shir，「歌」）。 In Jewish tradition this “cantor” is the חַזָּן (chazzan, “cantor, the one who leads in prayer-song”) — the one who leads the whole congregation in singing its prayers to God. He is not a performer; he is the one who takes the cries, the repentance, and the hope of an entire community and carries them up as melody. What the elder had done for fifty-five years was exactly this: to compose, preserve, and lead song after song — שִׁיר (shir, “song”) — for the people of God.

接著，長者說：「我有東西要給你。」他轉身離開，回來時手裡捧著一疊紙。「這些是我所寫下的全部樂譜、全部的曲譜。我要把它們送給你。這是我今天留給你、也留給你兒子諾亞（Noach）的傳承。」 Then the elder said: “I have something for you.” He turned away and came back holding a bundle of papers. “These are all the musical notes I have written, all the scores. I want to give them to you. This is the legacy I leave to you today — and to your son, Noach.”

「這些是我寫下的全部樂譜。我要把它們送給你。這是我留給你、也留給你兒子的傳承。」—— 那位八十五歲的 chazzan “These are all the scores I have written. I want to give them to you. This is the legacy I leave to you and to your son.”— The eighty-five-year-old chazzan

講者說，這份禮物「是一個神蹟，讓我接連哭了好多天」。長者帶他走進會堂，指給他看：就在這裡，他曾經唱過這一切美麗的歌。一個人五十五年的事奉、一整座會堂的歌聲記憶，就這樣被親手交付出去——交在一個外邦人手中，交給一個願意把它傳下去的兒子。 The speaker says the gift “was a miracle that made me weep for many days.” The elder walked him into the synagogue and showed him: here, in this place, he had sung all these beautiful songs. Fifty-five years of one man's service, the whole sung memory of a synagogue, was handed over by hand — placed into the hands of a Gentile, given to a son who would carry it on.

四、以色列之歌與羔羊之歌IV. The Song of Israel and the Song of the Lamb

講者把這件事轉向在場的教會，並指向使徒保羅。「教會啊，你知道保羅為什麼寫下那些話嗎？因為他知道——他知道有一天會來到：你要從正門走進以色列的家，而你將會被授予一首新歌。」 The speaker turns from the story to the gathered Church, and points to the Apostle Paul. “Church, do you know why Paul wrote what he wrote? Because he knew — he knew a day was coming when you would walk in through the front door into the house of Israel, and you would be handed a new song.”

這「新歌」就是שִׁיר חָדָשׁ（shir chadash，「新歌」）——詩篇一再呼喚的那首歌（詩 96:1：「你們要向耶和華唱新歌」）。而在啟示錄裡，這首歌有了它最完滿的形態：得勝的人「唱神僕人摩西的歌，和羔羊的歌」（啟示錄 15:3）。講者把那位 chazzan 的樂譜，與這節經文疊在一起：以色列之歌（摩西之歌）與羔羊之歌，不是兩首互相取代的歌，而是要在同一個敬拜裡一同響起的歌。 This “new song” is שִׁיר חָדָשׁ (shir chadash, “new song”) — the song the Psalms call for again and again (Psalm 96:1, “Sing to the LORD a new song”). And in Revelation it reaches its fullest form: the overcomers “sing the song of Moses, the servant of God, and the song of the Lamb” (Revelation 15:3). The speaker lays the cantor's handwritten scores over this verse: the song of Israel (the song of Moses) and the song of the Lamb are not two rival songs that replace one another, but one worship in which both are meant to sound together.

那一疊樂譜，因此成了一個活的比喻：一位正統派的猶太 chazzan，把以色列五十五年的歌，親手交給一個外邦家庭，要他們的兒子繼續唱下去。「這就是聖靈，」講者說，「祂已經在你裡面歌唱了。你正在被興起，正在被喚醒。」外邦人不是來奪走以色列的歌，而是被邀請走進那扇正門，與以色列一同歌唱。 That bundle of scores becomes a living parable: an Orthodox Jewish chazzan handing fifty-five years of Israel's song, by his own hand, to a Gentile family so their son might keep singing it. “This is the Spirit,” the speaker says, “already singing inside of you. You are being raised up; you are being awakened.” The Gentile does not come to seize Israel's song, but is invited through the front door to sing together with Israel.

五、三重代禱 ── 回家、和好、上耶路撒冷V. Three Movements of Intercession — Homecoming, Reconciliation, Jerusalem

講者隨即邀請會眾進入三個「代禱的階段」，並吹響了號角（shofar）。這三聲，是這份見證從個人故事流向群體呼召的橋。 The speaker then invites the congregation into three “movements of intercession,” sounding the shofar. These three blasts are the bridge by which the testimony flows from a personal story into a corporate calling.

第一聲——回家。講者以猶太人的身分站立，像他那位等候了五十年的「表親」一樣說話：「我們已經等你兩千年了。我們預備好了，等你回家。回來吧，歡迎你。」第一聲，是聖經話語的聲音：站立得穩，認識主眷顧的時候——「Adonai 你的主正在對你說話：回來、回來、回家。家很大。」 The first blast — come home. The speaker stands as a Jew and speaks like the “cousin” who has waited fifty years: “We have been waiting for you for two thousand years. We are ready for you to come home. Come back — welcome.” The first sound is the sound of the word of Scripture: stand firm, and know the time of the Lord's visitation — “Adonai your Lord is saying to you: come back, come back, come home. The house is large.”

第二聲——和好。「我們是破碎的、被割裂的，已經彼此遠離了太久。」猶太人與外邦人一同哭泣：為教會哭，為以色列的家哭。「主啊，求你使我們合一，使我們和好。」講者說，眼淚會打開那些把我們隔開的門。讓這聲音響起，在聖靈裡打開那些門。 The second blast — reconciliation. “We are broken, cut apart; we have been far from one another for too long.” Jew and Gentile weep together: weeping for the Church, weeping for the house of Israel. “Lord, unite us; let us be reconciled.” The speaker says: tears open the doors that separate us. Let this sound go out, and in the Spirit, let those doors swing open.

第三聲——上耶路撒冷。會眾為自己的咒詛、黑暗與驕傲求赦免，並求神「憐憫你的會眾」。彌賽亞的身體選擇「今天就上耶路撒冷去」——不是作為征服者，而是作為悔改、歸回、與以色列同站的人。 The third blast — go up to Jerusalem. The congregation asks forgiveness for its curses, its darkness, and its pride, and pleads with God to “have mercy on your congregation.” The body of the Messiah chooses “to go up to Jerusalem today” — not as a conqueror, but as those who repent, return, and stand together with Israel.

六、悔罪文 ── 「求你按你名的緣故而行」VI. The Slichot Prayer — “Act for the Sake of Your Name”

隨後，這份見證融入古老的悔罪禮文。會眾呼求神「十三屬性」的憐憫——這是出埃及記 34:6-7 所啟示的神的名：「Adonai、Adonai，有憐憫、有恩典的神，不輕易發怒，並有豐盛的慈愛和誠實……赦免罪孽、過犯和罪惡。」 The testimony then dissolves into the ancient penitential liturgy. The congregation calls on the “thirteen attributes” of God's mercy — the name of God revealed in Exodus 34:6-7: “Adonai, Adonai, a God merciful and gracious, slow to anger and abounding in steadfast love and faithfulness … forgiving iniquity and transgression and sin.”

「Adonai，求你按你名的緣故而行，因為我們是奉你的名而來。求你按你名的美善與榮耀而行，因為你的名滿有恩典與憐憫。求你赦免我們的罪孽，因為它甚大。」—— 悔罪文（Selichot） “Adonai, act for the sake of Your name, for we have come depending on Your name. Act for the goodness and the glory of Your name, for gracious and merciful is Your name, O God. Pardon our iniquity, for it is great.”— from the Selichot

禮文以但以理書 9 的呼求作結：「我的神啊，求你側耳而聽，睜眼而看……我們在你面前懇求，原不是因自己的義，乃因你的大憐憫。主啊，求你垂聽；主啊，求你赦免；主啊，求你應允而行。」悔罪不是為了贏得辯論，而是為了被神的憐憫尋見。 The liturgy closes with the cry of Daniel 9: “O my God, incline Your ear and hear; open Your eyes and see … we do not present our pleas before You because of our righteousness, but because of Your great mercy. O Lord, hear; O Lord, forgive; O Lord, pay attention and act.” Repentance is not about winning an argument; it is about being found by the mercy of God.

這就是這份見證最終的形狀：一位 chazzan 含淚交出的樂譜，在午夜的悔罪聲中，化作了整個身體——猶太人與外邦人——一同俯伏、一同求赦、一同歌唱的「新歌」。以色列之歌與羔羊之歌，終於在同一個敬拜裡合而為一。 This is the final shape of the testimony: the scores a chazzan handed over in tears, amid the midnight sound of repentance, become the whole body — Jew and Gentile — bowing together, asking pardon together, singing together the “new song.” The song of Israel and the song of the Lamb, at last, joined as one worship.

「教會啊，你要從正門走進以色列的家——你將會領受一首新歌：以色列之歌、摩西之歌、與羔羊之歌。聖靈已經在你裡面歌唱了。回家吧，歡迎你。Baruch Haba。」 “Church, walk in through the front door into the house of Israel — and you will be handed a new song: the song of Israel, the song of Moses, and the song of the Lamb. The Spirit is already singing inside of you. Come home — welcome. Baruch Haba.”
