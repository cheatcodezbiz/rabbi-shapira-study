100 道門 · 100 羊角號100 Gates · 100 Shofarot

第三道門 The Third Door

在大節期，我們聽見羊角號響起一百零一次，求神藉每一聲號角為我們開啟一百道門。但拉比沙皮拉博士說：還有最後一道門——「第三日的門」。它通往國度，一旦跨過，就再也無法回頭。在這個悔改與吹角的季節，天庭正注視著一件事：誰在開門，誰正走過去？ In this High Holy Days season we hear the shofar sound a hundred and one times, asking God to open a hundred gates through each blast. But Rabbi Dr. Shapira says there is one final door — “the door of the third day.” It opens into the Kingdom, and once you cross it there is no way back. In this season of repentance and the shofar, heaven is watching one thing: who is opening the door, and who is walking through it?

沙皮拉拉比博士 · Ahavat Ammi 事工 Rabbi Dr. Itzhak Shapira · Ahavat Ammi Ministries

羊角號正在響起——不是一百次，而是一百零一次——我們向 Hashem（הַשֵּׁם，那名）祈求：藉著每一聲號角，開啟一百道門。但拉比沙皮拉要我們把目光集中在最後一道門上。在這個 Elul（אֱלוּל）與大節期的季節，整個猶太世界都在吹角、都在 תְּשׁוּבָה teshuvah（悔改、回轉）。而拉比說：我們的一生，其實就是從一道門走向另一道門。當你走進一道門，另一道門就會在你面前出現。今天，神已經把門打開了——問題不在於門開不開，而在於：你願不願意走過去？ The shofar is sounding — not a hundred, but a hundred and one times — and we are asking Hashem (הַשֵּׁם, “the Name”) to open a hundred gates through each blast. But Rabbi Shapira wants our eyes fixed on the final door. In this season of Elul (אֱלוּל) and the High Holy Days, the whole Jewish world is blowing the shofar and doing תְּשׁוּבָה teshuvah — repentance, return. And the rabbi says: our whole life is a walk from one door to the next. The moment you walk through one door, another appears before you. Today, God has already opened the door — the question is not whether it is open, but: will you walk through it?

一、旋轉的門 ── 從一道門走向另一道門I. Revolving Doors — From One Door to the Next

這個 Shabbat 我們讀到 עֲקֵדָה Akedah（捆綁以撒）的故事，而拉比注意到一個耐人尋味的細節：在亞伯拉罕的第十次、也是最後一次試驗裡，神重複了祂起初就對他說過的話——「לֶךְ־לְךָ lech lecha」（你去）。等一等——亞伯拉罕不是早就走過「lech lecha」了嗎？神為什麼要再給他一次同樣的試驗？ This Shabbat we read the story of the עֲקֵדָה Akedah — the binding of Isaac — and the rabbi notices a striking detail: in Abraham's tenth and final trial, God repeats the very words He had spoken at the beginning — “לֶךְ־לְךָ lech lecha” (go forth). Wait a moment — hadn't Abraham already walked through “lech lecha”? Why would God give him the same trial twice?

拉比的回答是：那些門是旋轉的門。你一走進一道門、跨進新的一層，另一道門就立刻出現。我們常自以為：「我認識主了，我認識彌賽亞了，日光之下對我已沒有新事。」但事實正好相反——我們的整個生命、整個信仰之路、整個存在，都在於一道門接一道門地往前走。雅歌用美麗的方式描述這件事：我們是從一座山走向另一座山、從一個高崗走向另一個高崗。 The rabbi's answer: those doors are revolving doors. The second you walk through one door and step into a new level, another door appears. We tend to think, “I know the Lord, I know the Messiah; there is really nothing new under the sun for me.” But the opposite is true — our entire life, our entire walk, our entire existence is about walking from door to door. The Song of Songs says it beautifully: we are moving from hill to hill, from one mountain to another.

「Rosh Hashanah（רֹאשׁ הַשָּׁנָה，新年）正是要提醒我們：要往前跳躍。不要回頭看身後的門——要看你面前的門。」 “Rosh Hashanah (רֹאשׁ הַשָּׁנָה, the New Year) reminds us to leap forward. Don't look at the doors behind you — look at the door ahead of you.”

二、דֶּלֶת Delet ── 「門」這個字II. Delet — The Word for “Door”

拉比要我們停下來，思想一個希伯來字：דֶּלֶת delet——「門」。今天，我們向神呼求：「讓我們過去！讓我們過去！」但真正的問題是：我們要怎麼走過這些門？門已經開了，神，是祢開了門——但人如何走進下一個層次？人如何走進一個新的約？ The rabbi asks us to stop and consider one Hebrew word: דֶּלֶת delet — “door.” Today we are crying out to God, “Let us through! Let us through!” But the real question is: how do we go through those doors? The door is open; God, You opened it — but how does one walk into the next level? How does a person walk into a new covenant?

這裡有一個深刻的字根遊戲。希伯來文「fullness（豐滿、成全）」一字——hitgadlut（הִתְגַּדְּלוּת，「長大、成熟、被擴大」）——它的字尾正是 delet（門）。拉比要我們默想這件事：人唯一能走過新門的方式，就是走進自己的「豐滿」。用基督教的術語來說，這叫「走進你的呼召」、「走進你完全的潛能」。這正是此刻擺在我們面前那道門的核心。 Here is a profound play on roots. The Hebrew word for fullness — hitgadlut (הִתְגַּדְּלוּת, “growing up, maturing, being enlarged”) — ends in delet, the door. Meditate on this, the rabbi says: the only way one can walk through the new doors is by walking into one's fullness. In Christian terms we call it “walking into your calling,” “walking into your full potential.” This is the essence of the door standing in front of us right now.

拉比坦率地說：我們站在一個彌賽亞運動之中，而這個運動「從來沒有長大」。作為一個彌賽亞運動，我們一直想停留在嬰兒的尿布裡；列國想停在尿布裡；許多歸向彌賽亞的猶太人成了「名義上的猶太人」，大多不再持守猶太生活。但以色列的大能者正在末後的日子做一件事——祂在呼召我們，走進豐滿、走過那道門。 The rabbi is candid: we stand in a Messianic movement that “never grew up.” As a Messianic movement we wanted to stay in diapers; the nations wanted to stay in diapers; many Jewish people who came to the Messiah became nominal Jews who no longer practice Judaism. But the Mighty One of Israel is doing something in these last days — He is calling us to walk into fullness, and walk through the door.

三、為什麼神祝福一個「未完成」的工程III. Why God Blesses an “Unfinished” Project

當以色列建造 מִשְׁכָּן Mishkan（會幕）時，他們做了三個月，然後來到摩西面前說：「摩西，你看，這東西要散架了，一團亂。」但摩西看了他們的工、看了他們的 עֲבוֹדָה avodah（事奉/工作），就祝福了他們。拉比問：摩西怎麼能祝福一個失敗的工程？會幕都還沒立起來呢。 When Israel built the מִשְׁכָּן Mishkan (the Tabernacle), they worked on it for three months, then came to Moses and said, “Moses, look — this thing is falling apart, it's a mess.” But Moses looked at their work, saw their עֲבוֹדָה avodah (their labor, their service), and blessed them. How, the rabbi asks, could Moses bless a failed project? The Tabernacle wasn't even standing yet.

拉比把這幅圖景對準今天的彌賽亞猶太運動：他看見一個充滿混亂、充滿分裂、充滿一個個「小王國」、卻沒有真正國度的運動。神為什麼要祝福這樣的東西？答案是：神祝福會幕（它是 מַלְכוּת malchut——王權、國度的圖像），是因為祂看見了他們的心。摩西說：你們把所有的都獻上了——所以我要祝福你們。然後神才准許亞倫開始就職的事奉、開始那設立之禮。 The rabbi turns this picture on today's Messianic Jewish movement: he sees a movement full of chaos, full of division, full of little “kingdoms” — yet without a real Kingdom. Why would God bless something like that? The answer: God blessed the Tabernacle (a picture of מַלְכוּת malchut — kingship, the Kingdom) because He saw their hearts. Moses said: you gave it all you had — so I will bless you. Only then did God grant that Aaron begin the inauguration service.

「沒有百姓，就沒有君王；但沒有 כֹּהֵן kohen（祭司）也不行。Mashiach（מָשִׁיחַ，彌賽亞）來，不只作君王，更作 כֹּהֵן גָּדוֹל Kohen Gadol（大祭司）——而祂若沒有會幕，就不能來。」 “There is no king without a people — but there is no king without a כֹּהֵן kohen, a priest, either. Mashiach (מָשִׁיחַ, Messiah) comes not only as King but as כֹּהֵן גָּדוֹל Kohen Gadol, the High Priest — and He cannot come if there is no Mishkan.”

四、從下面被喚醒 ── Tishrei 的救贖IV. Awakened from Below — The Redemption of Tishrei

會幕要從上面來，還是從下面來？拉比的回答是：兩者都有，但有一個關鍵的次序。這也正是 Rosh Hashanah 我們吹 שׁוֹפָרוֹת shofarot（複數的羊角號）的原因。猶太傳統中有一個 מַחְלֹקֶת machloket（爭辯）：那 גְּאֻלָּה Geulah（救贖）究竟是從上面來、還是從下面來？答案是：在 Nisan（尼散月），救贖從上面來；但在 Tishrei（提斯利月，即大節期所在的月份），救贖要在我們喚醒諸天的時候才臨到。 Is the Tabernacle to come from above or from below? The rabbi's answer: both — but in a crucial order. This is precisely why on Rosh Hashanah we blow שׁוֹפָרוֹת shofarot (shofars, plural). There is a מַחְלֹקֶת machloket (a rabbinic dispute) over whether the גְּאֻלָּה Geulah — the redemption — comes from above or from below. The answer: in Nisan it comes from below; in another sense from above; but in Tishrei, the month of the High Holy Days, it comes when we awaken the heavens.

那麼「喚醒諸天」是什麼意思？拉比說：我們在這裡，就是要喚醒諸天。這意味著我們必須通過最後一個試驗——而這，我的朋友，是「第三日的試驗」。我們不能拖延。天庭說過：當我們在地上拖延這座建造的工程時，那是何等令神憂傷的事。你我都是 קַבְּלָן（承包商）——我們是這國度的建造者，而拖延正是「彌賽亞的腳步聲」被延遲的原因。你恨惡拖延；告訴你，神也恨惡在建立祂國度的事上拖延。 So what does it mean to awaken the heavens? The rabbi says: we are here to awaken the heavens. It means we must go through one last test — and this, friend, is the test of the third day. We cannot drag our feet. Heaven has said how grievous it is when we drag the construction project here on earth. You and I are contractors — builders of the Kingdom — and our delay is the very reason “the footsteps of the Messiah” are delayed. You hate delay; let me tell you, God hates delay in building His Kingdom.

五、第三日 ── 試煉、妥拉、復活V. The Third Day — Trial, Torah, Resurrection

拉比要我們留意妥拉中圍繞著 Rosh Hashanah 的一個模式。我們剛讀了 Akedah 的故事，經文說：亞伯拉罕走進的那個試驗，發生在第三日（創世記 22:4）。而且他「清早起來」，滿懷期待地起身——他沒有拖延。 The rabbi points to a pattern in the holy Torah around Rosh Hashanah. We have just read the story of the Akedah, and the text says the trial Avraham walked into happened on the third day (Genesis 22:4). And he “rose early” in expectation — he did not drag his feet.

這個「第三日」的模式貫穿整本聖經：亞伯拉罕在第三日受試驗；妥拉在第三日被賜下（出埃及記 19:16）；而拉比最珍愛的一處——何西阿書第六章說，到第三日，我們必被復活（何西阿書 6:2）。讓我再說一次這個原則：每當有試煉，必定總有一條出路；總會有得勝的機會。神不會給你一個試煉、不會給你一項建造的任務——哪怕是像這樣艱鉅的任務——而不給你一條出路。這叫恩典；這叫憐憫。 This “third day” pattern runs through all of Scripture: Avraham was tested on the third day; the Torah was given on the third day (Exodus 19:16); and — the rabbi's favorite — Hosea chapter 6 says that on the third day we will be resurrected (Hosea 6:2). Let me say the principle again: whenever there is a trial, there is always going to be a way out; there is always a chance for victory. God will not give you a trial, will not give you a task to build something — even something like this — without a way out. It is called grace; it is called mercy.

「第三日不只是妥拉被賜下的日子，更是 Mashiach 復活的日子。你看見這連結了嗎？你和我，今天早晨正在被復活——好叫我們走過第三道、也是最後一道門。」 “The third day is not just the day the Torah was given — it is the day Mashiach was resurrected. Do you see the connection? You and I are being resurrected this morning — so that we will go through the third and final door.”

六、跨過去 ── Ivri，那「越過」的人VI. Crossing Over — Ivri, the One Who Crosses

這是一道通往國度的門；一旦走進去，除了建造國度之外，別無他路。它像機場裡那種單向門——一旦進去，就出不來了。拉比把這話直白地交給我們、也交給列國：仔細想清楚——因為你若走過這道門，就沒有回頭路了。神對亞伯拉罕說「你去」的時候，也是說「沒有回頭路了」。這就是為什麼亞伯拉罕差遣他的僕人去——因為他自己不能回去。 This is a door that leads into the Kingdom; once you walk in, there is no other way but to build the Kingdom. It is like one of those one-way doors at the airport — once you come in, you cannot go back out. The rabbi hands this plainly to us, and to the nations: think carefully — because if you walk through this door, there is no way back. When God said “go forth” to Abraham, He also said there is no coming back. That is why Abraham sent his servant — because he himself could not go back.

走過這道門、開這道門的行動，希伯來文叫做「跨過去 / 越過」。當保羅說「真猶太人是在內心做的，不在外表」，他真正的意思是：那願意越過、進入祂的維度、進入祂國度的人。希伯來人——עִבְרִי Ivri——這個字的意思就是「那越過去的人」。越過去做什麼？越過去建造國度。以色列有以色列的任務，列國有列國的任務。 The act of crossing through and opening this door is, in Hebrew, “to cross over.” When Paul said “one is a Jew inwardly, not outwardly,” what he really meant is: the one willing to cross over and enter into His dimension, His Kingdom. A Hebrew — עִבְרִי Ivri — means “the one who crosses over.” Crosses over to do what? To build the Kingdom. Israel has a task, and the nations have a task.

「我若今天與神玩弄宗教，對祂說：『神啊，我曉得這事重要，但我不太確定……』——讓我告訴你：你不但無法跨過那道門，你還會在跨越的途中跌倒。」 “If you play religion with God today and say, ‘God, I know it's important, but I'm not quite sure…’ — let me tell you: not only will you be unable to cross the door, you will fall as you cross over.”

七、向旅人敞開的門 ── 以色列的責任VII. The Door Open to the Sojourner — Israel's Responsibility

拉比引用約伯記 31 章作結：「沒有寄居的旅客在街上過夜，我也曾向過路的人敞開我的門。」(約伯記 31:32) חֲזַ"ל Chazal（歷世的賢者）問：你為什麼向大路敞開門？而拉什 Rashi 解釋：我家的門不是封閉在門房裡的。彌賽亞猶太信仰不是被召作一個封閉的邪教——我們不是邪教；彌賽亞猶太信仰是被召要有一道敞開的門。 The rabbi concludes from Job 31: “No sojourner spent the night in the street; I opened my doors to the road.” (Job 31:32) חֲזַ"ל Chazal — the sages — ask: why open your door to the road? And Rashi explains: the doors of my house were not enclosed in a gatehouse. Messianic Judaism is not called to be an enclosed cult — we are not a cult; Messianic Judaism is called to have an open door.

但「敞開」不等於「來者不拒」。拉比警告：「不要試圖進入以色列之家，除非你走那道正門。」我們運動今天有太多問題——希伯來字根派、聖名運動、第二聖殿、第三聖殿……追根究底，都是「從錯誤的門進來」的問題。只有一道門，這道門已被信託、交付給猶太民族。經文說：向「寄居的旅人」敞開門——不是向任何人都敞開。但拉什這樣作結：要向十字路口敞開門，使凡真心願意進來的人都能進來。 But “open” does not mean “anyone may barge in.” The rabbi warns: “Do not try to come to the house of Israel unless you go through the main door.” Our movement has too many problems today — Hebrew-roots factions, sacred-name movements, second-house, third-house — and at the end of the day it is all the problem of coming in through the wrong doors. There is only one door, and that door has been entrusted to the Jewish people. The text says: open the door to the sojourner — not to just anybody. But Rashi concludes: open the doors to the crossroads, so that whoever truly wishes to come may do so.

這寄居的「旅人」——גֵּר ger——這個字源自「居住者」：一個尋求成為以色列之家一份子的人。你不必是猶太人，才能說「我願意住在以色列的家中」。拉比為今天許多趕著去「歸正入教」的人感到不安，他說：你已經有了邀請；除了這邀請以外，你不需要別的。羅馬書 3 章與 9 章都說：神的聖言、神的諸約，已經被託付給猶太人——為什麼？「免得有一個寄居的旅人在外露宿過夜。」 This “sojourner” — גֵּר ger — comes from the word “dweller”: one who seeks to be part of the house of Israel. You don't have to be Jewish to say, “I want to dwell here in the house of Israel.” The rabbi is troubled by how many today rush to do conversions, and says: you already have the invitation; you need nothing more than the invitation. Romans 3 and Romans 9 both say: the oracles of God and the covenants have been entrusted to the Jews — why? “So that no sojourner will spend the night in the open.”

八、票券、彌賽亞、與末後的江河VIII. The Ticket, the Messiah, and the River of the Last Days

「黑夜」代表什麼？拉比說：黑夜代表這世上沒有彌賽亞的時候，也就是我們稱為 גָּלוּת galut（流亡、被擄）的時候。約伯說「我向大路敞開我的門」——彌賽亞猶太信仰必須成為一個帳棚；帳棚是會幕的圖像，會幕是神國度的圖像，而神的國度是敞開的。世界需要知道：這國度——也就是我所稱的「以色列之家」——是敞開的；今天它向你敞開，但它不是向任何人隨意敞開，它是向那「願意進來居住的人」敞開。 What does the “night” represent? The night, the rabbi says, is the time we are without Messiah in the world — the time we call גָּלוּת galut, the exile. Job says, “I opened my doors to the road” — Messianic Judaism must become a tent; the tent is a picture of the Mishkan, the Mishkan is a picture of the Kingdom of God, and the Kingdom of God is open. The world needs to know that the Kingdom — what I call the house of Israel — is open; it is open to you today, but not to just anyone: it is open to the one who longs to come and dwell.

拉比把話講得極其清楚，以免有人誤會：Yeshua（יֵשׁוּעַ，耶書亞）就是那張票券。但人們常忽略一件事——當他們對耶書亞說「是」的時候，耶書亞並不是替你走過那道門。是你要走過那道門。耶書亞自己在使徒行傳第一章對門徒說的，正是這個意思。門徒想說：「來吧，我們跳 hava nagila，唱起歌來，耶書亞在這裡，我們的患難結束了！」耶書亞卻看著他們說：慢著。我來是要給你們一樣東西——一張票券，這票券名叫聖靈、Ruach HaKodesh（רוּחַ הַקֹּדֶשׁ）。現在，你需要去做工。 The rabbi makes it unmistakably clear, lest anyone misunderstand: Yeshua (יֵשׁוּעַ) is the ticket. But here is what people miss — when they say yes to Yeshua, Yeshua is not the one walking through the door for you. It is you who must walk through the door. This is exactly what Yeshua told His disciples in Acts chapter 1. They wanted to say, “Come, let's do the hava nagila, let's sing — Yeshua is here, our troubles are over!” But Yeshua looked at them and said: Not so fast. I came to give you something — a ticket, called the Holy Spirit, Ruach HaKodesh (רוּחַ הַקֹּדֶשׁ). Now you need to do the work.

誰做了這工？是神做的嗎？拉比說：不——是我們做的，是我們與神同工。「חָבֵר chaver」這個字不是「朋友」的意思；它源自 חִבּוּר chibur，意思是「連結、聯合」——我與彌賽亞連結，我緊緊依附彌賽亞，從那位 צַדִּיק tzaddik（義者）領受能力，正如約瑟、正如摩西把約瑟的骸骨帶上、緊緊抓住、從中支取力量。我們有地方可以從彌賽亞那裡領取票券——但還有兩件事必須發生：以色列必須為列國開門，而列國必須甘心走過那門。 Who did this work? Did God do it? No, the rabbi says — we did; we and God worked together. The word “חָבֵר chaver” does not mean “friend”; it comes from חִבּוּר chibur, “joining, union” — I am joined to the Messiah, I cleave to the Messiah, drawing power from the צַדִּיק tzaddik, the righteous one, just as Joseph, just as Moses took the bones of Joseph and cleaved and drew strength from him. We have a place to get the ticket from the Messiah — but two more things must take place: Israel must open the door for the nations, and the nations must be willing to walk through it.

九、結語 ── 天庭正注視著那道門IX. Closing — Heaven Is Watching the Door

拉比說，我們講了許多關於諸天、關於天庭注視的事。但你以為天庭究竟在注視什麼？他們只注視一件事：誰在開門，誰正走過那門。這就是此刻天庭正在觀看的——誰開門，誰進來。先知說：在末後的日子，必有極大的人潮湧過這門，像一條江河、像奔流的活水。這要成為一場 רֵוַיְוַל（復興）。拉比相信：彌賽亞猶太復興已經開始了，而它的明證，就是此刻正在發生的事——來自八十多個國家、超過四萬人，此刻正一同跟隨。 We talk so much, the rabbi says, about the heavens and heaven watching. But what do you think the heavens are watching, exactly? They are watching one thing: who is opening the door, and who is walking through it. This is what heaven is watching right now — who opens, and who comes through. The prophet says: in the last days there will be such a massive flocking through the door that it will be like a river, like gushing water. It is going to be revival. And the rabbi believes the Messianic Jewish revival has already begun — the testimony of it is what is happening at this very moment, with more than forty thousand people from over eighty nations following along together.

這道「第三日的門」有什麼特徵？第一，這門裡有試煉——你若選擇跨過去，必受試驗，正如亞伯拉罕走過十次試煉。第二，這門通往一個妥拉的生命，因為妥拉正是在第三日賜下的。第三，這門通往復活，因為彌賽亞在第三日復活了。太多人以為自己走進了以色列的家，但他們的生命卻一團混亂——那不是國度的門。唯有那通往復活的門，才是第三道門；也唯有在第三日，新約才賜給你。你一跨過去，就進入一個新約；而當你進入這新約時，你生命中其餘的一切都已如同過去、都已被遮蓋——我們稱之為 כִּפּוּרִים kippurim。十天之後，我們就要守 Yom HaKippurim（贖罪日）。 What do we know about this “door of the third day”? First, there are trials in this door — if you choose to cross over, you will be tested, just as Abraham went through ten trials. Second, the door leads to a Torah life, because the Torah was given on the third day. Third, the door leads to resurrection, because the Messiah was resurrected on the third day. Too many people think they have walked into the house of Israel, yet their lives are in chaos — that is not the door of the Kingdom. Only the door that leads to resurrection is the third door; and only on the third day is the new covenant given to you. The second you cross over, you enter a new covenant; and as you enter it, everything else in your existence becomes as though it is gone, covered over — what we call כִּפּוּרִים kippurim. In ten days we keep Yom HaKippurim, the Day of Atonement.

拉比為猶太人發出一個悔改的呼求：主啊，求祢饒恕我們這些猶太人，因我們把那名叫「以色列之家」的門開得如此狹小；我們是有罪的。今天，我們要為此補贖。他祈求許多人——許多猶太人、正統派、甚至彌賽亞信徒——一同為列國開門，不是說「你可以勉強進來一點點」，而是說「全然進來吧！」當列國回歸，以色列就要得救（羅馬書 11:25-26）——因為到那時，以色列終於完成了自己的工作：把門開了。 The rabbi raises a cry of repentance for the Jewish people: Lord, forgive us as Jews for making the door called the house of Israel so small; we are guilty. Today we are making up for it. He prays that many — many Jews, Orthodox, even Messianic — will open the door for the nations, not saying “you can sort of, kind of come in,” but “come fully in!” When the nations return, Israel will be saved (Romans 11:25–26) — because by then Israel has finally done its job: it has opened the door.

「天庭正注視著一件事：誰在開門，誰正走過那門。今天你有一個問題必須回答——你願不願意跨過去，願不願意開啟那最後一道門，那我稱之為『第三日之門』的門？願列國得著勇氣，在這個時候跨過去；願 Mashiach 的羊角號聲，護送你進入。以色列之家是敞開的，滿了邀請。Am Yisrael Chai——以色列的民活著。Amen。」 “Heaven is watching one thing: who is opening the door and who is walking through it. Today you have a question to answer — will you cross over, will you open the final door, the one I call the door of the third day? May the nations be given courage to cross over in this time; may the shofar of the Mashiach escort you in. The house of Israel is open, full of invitation. Am Yisrael Chai — the people of Israel live. Amen.”
