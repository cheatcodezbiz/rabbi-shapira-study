奉耶書亞之名的讚美與禱告 Praise and Prayer in the Name of Yeshua

在南北韓邊境的帕州，一場韓語的全球羅什哈沙拿之夜正在進行。會眾向耶書亞獻上讚美，奉祂的名一同禱告，齊聲說「阿們」——把全世界說韓語的家人，連同猶太人與列國，一同聚集在君王面前，迎接 5786「合一之年」。 On the Korea border at Paju, a worldwide, Korean-language Rosh HaShanah night is underway. The congregation lifts praise to Yeshua, prays together in His name, and answers with one “Amen” — gathering the worldwide Korean-speaking family, with Jew and Gentile, before the King to welcome 5786, the “year of union.”

「奉耶書亞（יֵשׁוּעַ Yeshua）的名禱告。阿們，阿們。」——這場全球韓語的羅什哈沙拿之夜，就是這樣開場的。在南北韓邊境的帕州，一小群「戰士」聚集在離北韓不遠的地方；同時，全世界透過直播加入：以色列、北美、南美、上海、北京的中國弟兄姊妹，以及散居各地說韓語的家人。他們先以詩篇五十一篇的認罪、אָבִינוּ מַלְכֵּנוּ（「我們的父，我們的王」）的呼求向神俯伏，再奉耶書亞之名，把 5786 這個「合一之年」帶到君王面前。 “We pray in the name of Yeshua (יֵשׁוּעַ). Amen, amen.” That is how this worldwide, Korean-language Rosh HaShanah night begins. On the Korea border at Paju, a small band of “warriors” gathers not far from North Korea; at the same moment the whole world joins by livestream — Israel, North and South America, Chinese brothers and sisters in Shanghai and Beijing, and the Korean-speaking family scattered across the earth. First they bow before God with the confession of Psalm 51 and the cry of אָבִינוּ מַלְכֵּנוּ (“Our Father, our King”); then, in the name of Yeshua, they carry 5786 — the “year of union” — before the King.

一、奉耶書亞之名 ── 開場的禱告I. In the Name of Yeshua — The Opening Prayer

整個崇拜的第一句話，就奠定了一切的根基：「奉耶書亞的名禱告，阿們，阿們。奉神的名禱告。」תְּפִלָּה tefillah（「禱告」）在這裡不是儀式上的開場白，而是把整個夜晚交託給那一位名字本身就是「神拯救」的彌賽亞。「יֵשׁוּעַ Yeshua」——這個名字在每一段禱告的起頭與結尾反覆出現，像一條金線把讚美、認罪、代求串在一起。 The very first words of the service lay the foundation for everything: “We pray in the name of Yeshua — amen, amen. We pray in the name of God.” Here תְּפִלָּה tefillah (“prayer”) is no ritual preamble; it entrusts the whole night to the Messiah whose very name means “God saves.” “יֵשׁוּעַ Yeshua” recurs at the opening and the close of every prayer, a golden thread binding praise, confession, and intercession together.

帶領者隨即進入詩篇五十一篇——大衛在拿單先知指出他與拔示巴的罪之後所寫的悔罪詩。「神啊，求你按你的慈愛憐恤我，按你豐盛的慈悲塗抹我的過犯⋯⋯求你為我造清潔的心，使我裡面重新有正直的靈。」在羅什哈沙拿這個審判與新生的日子，會眾選擇以「認罪」作為讚美的入口：先承認自己的不潔，才能舉手讚美那位潔淨人的神。 The leader moves at once into Psalm 51 — David's penitential psalm, written after Nathan the prophet confronted him over Bathsheba. “Be gracious to me, O God, according to your loving-kindness; according to the multitude of your mercies blot out my transgressions… Create in me a clean heart, O God, and renew a steadfast spirit within me.” On Rosh HaShanah, a day of judgment and new beginning, the congregation makes confession the doorway to praise: only after owning their own uncleanness do they lift their hands to the God who makes clean.

二、「主啊，求你開我的嘴唇」── 認罪與 Al ChetII. “O Lord, Open My Lips” — Confession and the Al Chet

禱告轉入會堂傳統中最為人熟知的一句：「אֲדֹנָי שְׂפָתַי תִּפְתָּח——主啊，求你開我的嘴唇，我的口便傳揚讚美你的話。」（詩 51:15）這句經文同時是《阿米達》（站立禱）的開端：唯有神先打開人的口，人才配得讚美祂。讚美不是出於人的本能，而是神所賜的恩典。 The prayer turns to one of the best-loved lines of the synagogue tradition: “אֲדֹנָי שְׂפָתַי תִּפְתָּח — O Lord, open my lips, and my mouth shall declare your praise” (Ps 51:15). This same verse opens the Amidah, the standing prayer: only when God first opens our mouths are we fit to praise Him. Praise is not human instinct; it is God-given grace.

接著會眾誦念羅什哈沙拿與贖罪日的「Al Chet」（「為這罪⋯⋯」）認罪文，一條一條地數算群體的罪：「為我們在你面前所犯的罪——以言語、以毀謗閒言、以不尊重父母與師長、以不潔的嘴唇、以明知故犯與不知不覺所犯的罪⋯⋯」每一段都以同一句收束：「וְעַל כֻּלָּם אֱלוֹהַּ סְלִיחוֹת, סְלַח לָנוּ, מְחַל לָנוּ, כַּפֶּר לָנוּ——為這一切，赦免的神啊，求你赦免我們、寬恕我們、為我們贖罪。」 Then the congregation recites the Al Chet (“For the sin…”) confession of Rosh HaShanah and Yom Kippur, naming the community's sins one by one: “For the sin we have committed before you — in speech, by gossip and tale-bearing, by disrespect for parents and teachers, with unclean lips, wittingly and unwittingly…” Each strophe closes with the same refrain: “וְעַל כֻּלָּם אֱלוֹהַּ סְלִיחוֹת, סְלַח לָנוּ, מְחַל לָנוּ, כַּפֶּר לָנוּ — For all these, O God of forgiveness, forgive us, pardon us, grant us atonement.”

這份認罪表是複數的、群體的：「我們所犯的罪」。沒有人以個人之姿單獨站立；全球的家人一同認罪，一同被赦免。每一輪認罪之後，會眾再次回到那句話：「奉耶書亞的名禱告。阿們，阿們。」אָמֵן Amen（「誠然如此」）成了把認罪封印在彌賽亞贖罪之工裡的印記。 This confession is plural and corporate: “the sin we have committed.” No one stands alone as an individual; the worldwide family confesses together and is forgiven together. After each round the congregation returns to the same words: “We pray in the name of Yeshua. Amen, amen.” אָמֵן Amen (“so be it, it is firm”) becomes the seal that locks the confession inside the Messiah's atoning work.

三、「我的心等候主」── 從認罪到盼望III. “My Soul Waits for the Lord” — From Confession to Hope

認罪過後，禱告轉向盼望。會眾誦念詩篇一三〇篇：「我等候耶和華，我的心等候；我也仰望祂的話。我的心等候主，勝於守夜的等候天亮，勝於守夜的等候天亮。以色列啊，你當仰望耶和華！因祂有慈愛，有豐盛的救恩。祂必救贖以色列脫離一切的罪孽。」 After confession, the prayer turns to hope. The congregation recites Psalm 130: “I wait for the LORD, my soul waits, and in His word I hope. My soul waits for the Lord more than those who watch for the morning — more than those who watch for the morning. Let Israel hope in the LORD, for with the LORD there is loving-kindness, and with Him is plentiful redemption; and He shall redeem Israel from all her iniquities.”

然後是羅什哈沙拿崇拜的高峰之一——「אָבִינוּ מַלְכֵּנוּ Avinu Malkenu」（「我們的父，我們的王」）。會眾一句句呼求：「我們的父，我們的王，我們在你面前犯了罪⋯⋯求你將我們記在生命的冊上⋯⋯記在救贖與拯救的冊上⋯⋯記在赦免與寬恕的冊上⋯⋯求你叫救恩（יְשׁוּעָה yeshua）速速臨近⋯⋯求你為你偉大、大能、可畏之名的緣故待我們。」「父」與「王」兩個稱呼在同一口氣中並列：祂既是審判的君王，又是憐憫的父親。 Then comes one of the peaks of the Rosh HaShanah service — “אָבִינוּ מַלְכֵּנוּ Avinu Malkenu” (“Our Father, our King”). Line by line the congregation cries out: “Our Father, our King, we have sinned before you… inscribe us in the book of life… in the book of redemption and salvation… in the book of pardon and forgiveness… let salvation (יְשׁוּעָה yeshua) spring up soon… act toward us for the sake of your great, mighty, and awesome name.” “Father” and “King” stand side by side in one breath: He is both the Judge-King and the merciful Father.

四、合一之年 ── 為列國與分裂之地禱告IV. The Year of Union — Praying for the Nations and the Divided Land

禱告之後，帶領者轉向一個極具時代意義的時刻。就在那一天，北韓傳出突發消息：金正恩宣告「統一已不存在，將有兩個國家、兩個民族」。帶領者把這話當作一個屬靈的呼召：「無論我們的仇敵定了什麼計謀，我們都要把它打破。猶太人與外邦人要合一，南韓與北韓要合一——一切都當合一，一切的牆都當倒塌。」 After the prayers, the leader turns to a moment heavy with significance. That very day, breaking news came from North Korea: Kim Jong-un declared that “unification is no more — there will be two states, two peoples.” The leader takes this as a spiritual summons: “Whatever scheme our enemies have laid, we will break it. Jew and Gentile shall be one; North and South Korea shall be one — everything must be united, every wall must fall.”

站在離北韓不遠的邊境，會眾承認主既是北韓的主、也是南韓的主，更是耶路撒冷的王、全世界的王。他們奉「耶書亞，בֶּן אֱלֹהִים בֶּן יִשְׂרָאֵל（神的兒子、以色列的兒子）」之名，宣告那分隔南北的惡者權勢必要倒塌，正如柏林牆的倒下。「就在這羅什哈沙拿，我們此刻宣告：所有的信徒都要合一，所有信神的人都要合一，所有的家庭都要合一。」 Standing on the border not far from North Korea, the congregation confesses the Lord as Lord of the North and Lord of the South, King of Jerusalem and King of all the earth. In the name of “Yeshua, בֶּן אֱלֹהִים בֶּן יִשְׂרָאֵל (Son of God, Son of Israel),” they declare that the power of the evil one dividing North from South must collapse, as the Berlin Wall fell. “On this very Rosh HaShanah we now declare: all believers shall be united, all who believe God shall be united, all families shall be united.”

五、Shanah Tovah ── 向全世界的家人問安V. Shanah Tovah — Greeting the Worldwide Family

這場崇拜的心跳，是它全球的家。帶領者向美洲、向南美、向以色列、向所有透過直播加入的人問安：「Shanah Tovah！」——「願你新年蒙福。」他特別提到此刻正與他們一同慶祝的、在上海與北京的中國弟兄姊妹，並舉起一份從中國帶來的禮物（松子），說：「這是主帶來的。願榮耀歸給神。」 The heartbeat of this service is its worldwide household. The leader greets the Americas, South America, Israel, and all who join by livestream: “Shanah Tovah!” — “a good and blessed new year.” He singles out the Chinese brothers and sisters in Shanghai and Beijing celebrating with them at that very hour, lifting up a gift carried from China (pine nuts) and saying, “This the Lord has brought. Glory be to God.”

他們也讀出實時從以色列傳來的祝福——「חַג שָׂמֵחַ，Shanah Tovah，他祝福你們，我們的家人。」帶領者對著直播鏡頭說：「你是韓國，你是中國，你們是美好的子民，你們領受了真正美麗的祝福。」הַלְלוּיָהּ Halleluyah（「你們要讚美耶和華」）此刻不是一句口號，而是一整個橫跨語言與大陸的家庭，一同向同一位神舉起的同一個聲音。 They read aloud a blessing arriving in real time from Israel — “חַג שָׂמֵחַ, Shanah Tovah; he blesses you, our family.” To the livestream camera the leader says: “You are Korea, you are China, you are a wonderful people, and you have received a truly beautiful blessing.” הַלְלוּיָהּ Halleluyah (“Praise the LORD”) is here no slogan but one voice — a single household across languages and continents lifted to the one God.

六、為人質設一張空椅 ── 代求的記號VI. An Empty Chair for the Hostages — A Sign of Intercession

在喜樂的問安之中，帶領者刻意停下，作一個先知性的舉動：請人搬來一張空椅，放在會眾中間，「為我們的人質而設」——求הַשֵּׁם（哈閃，「那名」，即神的名）使他們速速歸回。他呼籲線上的人點開連結，為人質奉獻、代求。在讚美與歡慶之中，會眾沒有忘記那些仍在受苦、仍未歸家的人。 In the midst of joyful greetings, the leader deliberately pauses for a prophetic act: he asks that an empty chair be brought and set among the congregation, “for our hostages” — praying that הַשֵּׁם (HaShem, “the Name”) would bring them home swiftly. He calls those online to open the link, to give and to intercede for the captives. Amid praise and celebration the congregation does not forget those still suffering, still not yet home.

這是這段崇拜的神學核心：真正的讚美從不是逃避現實的狂喜，而是在現實的傷口中舉起的禱告。會眾一面歡慶羅什哈沙拿，一面為被擄的人、為分裂的民族、為散居的家人代求——讚美與代求並非兩件事，而是同一份「奉耶書亞之名」的禱告的兩面。 This is the theological heart of the service: true praise is never an escapist ecstasy but prayer lifted up within the wounds of reality. Even as they celebrate Rosh HaShanah, the congregation intercedes for the captive, for a divided people, for a scattered family — praise and intercession are not two things but two faces of the same prayer offered “in the name of Yeshua.”

七、為何稱為「合一之年」── 羅什哈沙拿的意義VII. Why “The Year of Union” — The Meaning of Rosh HaShanah

帶領者解釋為何 5786 是「合一之年」：因為這年份的數字 5-7-8-6 含著「5、6、7、8」的次序——一個逐步邁向合一的記號。羅什哈沙拿（רֹאשׁ הַשָּׁנָה Rosh Hashanah，「年之首」）又稱「יוֹם תְּרוּעָה Yom Teruah」（吹角節）與「Yom HaZikaron」（記念之日）。羊角號（shofar）與「記念」相連：彌賽亞要以一個身體、一群子民歸回。 The leader explains why 5786 is the “year of union”: the digits 5-7-8-6 carry within them the sequence “5, 6, 7, 8” — a sign of moving step by step toward oneness. Rosh HaShanah (רֹאשׁ הַשָּׁנָה Rosh Hashanah, “head of the year”) is also called “יוֹם תְּרוּעָה Yom Teruah” (the day of blowing) and “Yom HaZikaron” (the day of remembrance). The shofar is bound to remembrance: the Messiah returns to one body, one people.

因此在羅什哈沙拿，會眾發出邀請：邀請彼此合一，邀請自己被接納進那合一裡，好叫眾人能一同迎接祂。「沒有比這更好的方式來開始我們的Seder（席序）了。」神的國度總有秩序：祂創造宇宙時，凡事各按其序——而創造的中心、最後被造的，正是「我們」。今夜的席序，要把所有人重新帶回神的秩序裡：彼此之間的秩序、與彌賽亞之間的秩序、猶太人與列國之間的秩序。 So on Rosh HaShanah the congregation issues an invitation: to be united with one another, and to be received into that union, so that together they may welcome Him. “There is no better way to begin our Seder (order) than this.” God's kingdom always has order: when He created the universe, everything came in its sequence — and at the center of creation, made last of all, was “us.” Tonight's Seder means to bring everyone back into God's order: order with one another, order with the Messiah, order between the Jewish people and the nations.

八、點燈與和好 ── Siman Aleph 的第一個祝福VIII. Lighting and Reconciliation — The First Blessing, Siman Aleph

席序的第一個記號（Siman Aleph，「第一標記」）是點燈的祝福。今夜帶領者邀請一對夫妻來點燈：大衛達（Davida，韓國人）與小路（Koji，日本人）。帶領者特別點出這對夫妻的記號意義——小路多年前移居韓國，不僅是為了娶妻，更是為了日本與韓國的和好。在歷史傷痕深重的兩國之間，這一對「Tikkun（修復）的家庭」本身就是一個活的講章。 The first sign of the Seder (Siman Aleph, “the first marker”) is the blessing over the lights. Tonight the leader invites a couple to kindle them: Davida (Korean) and Koji (Japanese). He draws out the couple's significance as a sign — Koji moved to Korea years ago not merely to find a wife but for the reconciliation of Japan and Korea. Between two nations carrying deep historical wounds, this “family of Tikkun (repair)” is itself a living sermon.

兩支蠟燭也承載著更大的象徵：丈夫與妻子，以及以色列與列國。「如果我們自己正在被列國接納，那麼以色列也必被接納。願我們得以與祂豐盛的全然一同前行。」帶領者也感謝預備這整場席序的團隊——大衛達親手擺出完美的席盤，盤中每一樣食材都要被吃下，每一樣都描繪著「我們一同進入秩序」。讚美在此化為具體的款待：一張桌子、兩支蠟燭、一個跨越世仇而和好的家。 The two candles also carry a larger symbol: husband and wife, and Israel and the nations. “If we ourselves are being received among the nations, then Israel too shall be received. May we go forward together with the fullness of His all-in-all.” The leader thanks the team who prepared the whole Seder — Davida set out a perfect plate, every element on it to be eaten, every one depicting “our entering into order together.” Praise here becomes concrete hospitality: a table, two candles, a household reconciled across an ancient enmity.

九、結語 ── 一個身體，一位君王IX. Closing — One Body, One King

這段崇拜從一句「奉耶書亞之名」開始，也將以同樣的話收束。會眾透過認罪、盼望、問安、代求，把散居全球的韓語家人連同列國，一同帶到審判與憐憫的君王面前。讚美與禱告在此不是兩個分開的環節，而是同一個動作：俯伏、認罪、舉手、合一。 This service begins with one phrase — “in the name of Yeshua” — and it will close with the same. Through confession, hope, greeting, and intercession, the congregation carries the worldwide Korean-speaking family, together with the nations, before the King of judgment and mercy. Praise and prayer here are not two separate movements but one act: to bow, to confess, to lift our hands, to be made one.

「奉יֵשׁוּעַ 耶書亞的名禱告。阿們，阿們。願全世界的信徒都合一，願所有的家庭都合一。在這合一之年，全彌賽亞的身體必要合一。הַלְלוּיָהּ 哈利路亞——榮耀歸給神。」 “We pray in the name of יֵשׁוּעַ Yeshua. Amen, amen. May all believers across the world be united, and may every family be made one. In this year of union the whole body of Messiah shall be one. הַלְלוּיָהּ Halleluyah — glory be to God.”
