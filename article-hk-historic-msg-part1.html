希伯来字母里的神圣时间表 ── 香港现场第一集 God's Calendar Hidden in the Hebrew Letters — Hong Kong Live, Part 1

沙皮拉拉比博士第一次踏足中国大陆，竟刚好落在《树木的新年》(Tu B'Shevat) 的前夜。他用希伯来字母的数值(Gematria) 与犹太曆的四个新年，为这场两集系列奠下基础：「神的时间表是完美的。」 Rabbi Dr. Shapira sets foot in mainland China for the first time — and the date falls exactly on the eve of Tu B'Shevat, the New Year of the Trees. Using Hebrew letter values (Gematria) and the four New Years of the Jewish calendar, he lays the foundation for a two-part Hong Kong series with a single thesis: “God's timing is perfect.”

这是一段「奠基」式的信息。沙皮拉拉比博士在香港现场，对着来自蒙古、中国大陆、台湾、香港各地的听众，用一种近乎家常的语调开场——但每一句都是后面整个周末预言教导的基石。他将公布的新书《新哈瑪斯》(New Hamas) 谈到末世的反犹与列国对以色列的围攻；在中国大陆，他将开讲使徒保罗 (Shaul)；而就在主日晚九点，他第一次跨入中国国门，那一刻正是 Shevat 月十五日的开始——犹太人称之为《树木的新年》。他笑着说：「神的时间表是完美的。」这并非寒暄。这是他正要拆开来给我们看的论题。 This is a foundation teaching. Speaking live in Hong Kong to listeners gathered from Mongolia, mainland China, Taiwan, and Hong Kong, Rabbi Dr. Shapira opens in an almost domestic tone — but every sentence is a load-bearing stone for the weekend of prophecy teaching that follows. He announces his new book New Hamas on end-time antisemitism and the gathering of the nations against Israel; in mainland China he will teach on the Apostle Paul (Shaul); and at 9pm Sunday — the precise moment he crosses into mainland China for the first time — the 15th of Shevat begins. The Jewish people call it the New Year of the Trees. He smiles and says: “God's timing is perfect.” This is not pleasantry. It is the thesis he is about to unfold.

一、一位拉比在香港 ── 这不是一场普通的开场I. A Rabbi in Hong Kong — Not an Ordinary Opening

拉比沙皮拉在香港的第一晚以一句温暖的问候开场：「Shalom, beloved Chinese.」他对会众说，他「难以相信自己又到了中国」，并要在这趟旅程中发布新书、开设中文的「回归精舍」(Beit Teshuvah，意为「悔改之家」) 课程。但他随即把话题从「行程」转到「时刻」——而这正是这整集教导的关键：他在中国的这几天，不是地理上的行程，而是神在历史日历上一个被预定的时刻。 Rabbi Shapira opens his first night in Hong Kong with a warm “Shalom, beloved Chinese.” He tells the room he can hardly believe he is back in China; he will be releasing a new book on this trip and launching a Mandarin Beit Teshuvah (בית תשובה, “House of Return / Repentance”) study. Then he shifts almost immediately from itinerary to timing — and this shift is the whole point of the night: his days in China are not a geographical itinerary. They are an appointed moment on God's historical calendar.

他特别向蒙古、中国大陆、台湾、香港来的弟兄姊妹致谢——「谢谢你让我看到了你们美好的民族的一面。」这一句听起来像客套，但拉比沙皮拉的整个事工建基于一个信念：外邦列国中有一群人，被神拣选来与以色列同行；他们不是观众，他们是这部末世剧的一部分。下文我们会看到，这一份「使命感」并非他个人的修辞——它直接出自先知书与Shevat 月的属灵意义。 He thanks the believers who have come from Mongolia, mainland China, Taiwan, and Hong Kong: “Thank you for being such a beautiful people.” It sounds like a courtesy. It is more than that. Rabbi Shapira's entire ministry rests on a conviction: among the Gentile nations, God has set apart a people to walk with Israel — they are not spectators of the end-time drama; they are inside it. As we will see, this sense of vocation is not personal rhetoric. It is drawn straight from the prophets, and from the spiritual meaning of the month of Shevat.

「Shalom，亲爱的中国人，我难以相信我又回到了中国。我已经习惯了中国的时间表，并且我爱这件事。来吧，参与我们的课程——希望这对你也是一个很好的祝福。」——沙皮拉拉比博士 “Shalom, beloved Chinese. I can hardly believe I'm back here. I'm getting used to China time — and I love that. Come to our classes. I hope this will be a great blessing for you.”— Rabbi Dr. Itzhak Shapira

二、希伯来文 ── 一个数学与音律同行的语言II. Hebrew — A Language Where Math and Music Walk Together

在拉比开始解构这个夜晚的核心字「Tishvat」之前，他先停下来说明一件每一位想认真读希伯来圣经的人都该认识的事：希伯来文不只是一种语言，它是一套三位一体的符号系统——每一个字母同时承载音 (sound) 、意 (meaning) 、数 (numerical value) 。这种现象在现代语言里几乎无可比拟。在中文里，「一」是一个字，它的笔画不带数值；但在希伯来文里，Aleph（א）这个字母本身就是「一」。 Before Rabbi Shapira unpacks the central word of the night — Tishvat — he pauses to make a point every serious reader of the Hebrew Bible should know: Hebrew is not merely a language. It is a threefold sign-system in which each letter carries sound, meaning, and numerical value at once. There is almost nothing like it in any modern language. In Chinese, the character 一 (“one”) has no built-in number value beyond its meaning. But in Hebrew, Aleph (א) is itself the number one.

「希伯来文是一个很奇特的语言。每一个字母它有一个音符。同时也有一个数值。」——沙皮拉拉比博士 “Hebrew is a very unique language. Each letter has a musical note. And each letter also represents a numerical value.”— Rabbi Dr. Itzhak Shapira

拉比把这套字母-数值的对应称为Gematria（גימטריה，「数值学」）——这是从《密西拿》(Mishnah) 时代起，犹太智者就用来发现经文之间隐藏关联的解经方法之一。最基本的一套是：Aleph（א）=1，Bet（ב）=2，Gimel（ג）=3，一直数到 Yud（י）=10；之后是 10, 20, 30, 40……到 Qof（ק）=100；再之后是 100, 200, 300，最末的Tav（ת）=400。 Rabbi Shapira calls this letter-number correspondence Gematria (גימטריה) — a method Jewish sages have used since the time of the Mishnah to find hidden links between scriptural words. The basic scheme: Aleph (א) = 1, Bet (ב) = 2, Gimel (ג) = 3 … through Yud (י) = 10. Then 10, 20, 30, 40 … to Qof (ק) = 100. Then 100, 200, 300 — ending with Tav (ת) = 400.

他特别强调：「Gematria 不只有一套」——犹太传统里至少有十几种不同的计算公式（Mispar Hechrachi、Mispar Katan、Mispar Siduri……）。今晚他选最简单的那一套，因为他要带听众做的不是一道数学题，而是一个属灵的辨识：神在一个字里藏了一个日期。 He stresses that there is more than one Gematria system — Jewish tradition contains a dozen calculation methods (Mispar Hechrachi, Mispar Katan, Mispar Siduri, and more). Tonight he uses the simplest, because he is not asking the room to do a math exercise. He is asking them to recognize something spiritual: God has hidden a date inside a word.

三、一个把自己日期藏在自己名字里的节日III. A Holiday That Hides Its Own Date Inside Its Own Name

犹太人把Shevat 月第十五日称为「Tu B'Shevat」（ט"ו בשבט）。Tu（ט״ו）并不是一个普通的希伯来词——它由两个字母组成：Tet（ט）和 Vav（ו）。 The Jewish people call the fifteenth of Shevat by a strange name: Tu B'Shevat (ט״ו בשבט). Tu (ט״ו) is not a normal Hebrew word. It is composed of two letters — Tet (ט) and Vav (ו).

Tet（ט）的数值是 9。Vav（ו）的数值是 6。九加六，等於十五。而这个节日，就是在 Shevat 月的第十五日庆祝。换句话说，Tu B'Shevat 这个名字本身就告诉你它什麼时候发生——它把日期编码在它的名字里。 Tet (ט) has a value of 9. Vav (ו) has a value of 6. Nine plus six equals fifteen. And this holiday is celebrated on the fifteenth day of Shevat. In other words, the name Tu B'Shevat itself tells you when it happens — the date is encoded inside the name.

「九加六，是 15。你什麼时候过这个树的节呢？就是 Shevat 月份的第 15 天。所以很巧的——这个 Shevat 第 15 天就是这个礼拜的週日开始。神的时间表是完美的。」——沙皮拉拉比博士 “Nine plus six is fifteen. When do you celebrate the festival of the trees? On the 15th day of Shevat. And how coincidental — the 15th of Shevat begins this Sunday. God's timing is perfect.”— Rabbi Dr. Itzhak Shapira

为什麼这种「把日期藏在名字里」的设计如此重要？因为它呈现了希伯来圣经的一个深层属灵原则：神所创造的语言本身就是一份记号系统——文字、数字、时间，是同一个圣旨用三种方式说出来。当一位犹太拉比和一群中国信徒在Tu B'Shevat 的前夕坐在同一个房间里——这件事本身，就是希伯来文那种「文字、数字、时间合一」的活的样本。 Why does this design — date encoded in name — matter so much? Because it shows a deep spiritual principle in the Hebrew Bible: the language God created is itself a sign-system. Word, number, and time are one decree spoken three ways. When a Jewish rabbi and a room of Chinese believers sit together on the eve of Tu B'Shevat, that gathering itself is a living example of that same letter-number-time unity.

四、四个新年 ── 犹太曆的循环式生命观IV. Four New Years — The Cyclical Soul of the Jewish Calendar

对大部分华人基督徒来说，「新年」就是一年的第一天——以色列的Rosh Hashanah（ראש השנה，「年首」）就是这个意思。但拉比沙皮拉指出一个让很多人惊讶的事实：犹太教里其实有四个新年。这一点来自《密西拿》(Mishnah Rosh Hashanah 1:1) 的明文： For most Chinese Christians, “New Year” means the first day of the year — and Israel's Rosh Hashanah (ראש השנה, “head of the year”) is taken to be exactly that. But Rabbi Shapira points to a fact that startles most listeners: in Judaism there are four New Years. This is explicit in the Mishnah (Rosh Hashanah 1:1):

「有四个新年——尼散月一日，是君王和节期的新年；以禄月一日，是牲畜十一的新年；提斯利月一日，是年岁、安息年与禧年的新年……示巴特月一日（依希列学派的说法），是树木的新年——但希列学派说是十五日。」——《密西拿·罗什·哈沙拿》1:1 “There are four New Years. On the first of Nisan — the New Year for kings and festivals. On the first of Elul — for the tithe of cattle. On the first of Tishrei — for years, sabbatical and jubilee years … On the first of Shevat — the New Year of the trees, according to the School of Shammai; the School of Hillel says: the fifteenth thereof.”— Mishnah Rosh Hashanah 1:1

拉比沙皮拉说：「在犹太教里，所有的东西都是循环的。我们的曆日是循环的——不是一次，不是两次，不是三次，而是四次。」这种「四个新年」的设计不是行事曆上的便利，它是一种属灵地图：神并不只用一条直线来度量历史，祂用多重的循环——王权的循环、收成的循环、灵魂的循环、生命的循环——彼此交织，构成一个完整的救赎图景。 Rabbi Shapira says: “In Judaism, everything is cyclical. Our calendar is cyclical — not once, not twice, not three times, but four times.” The four New Years are not a calendrical convenience. They are a spiritual map: God does not measure history along one straight line. He measures it through multiple overlapping cycles — the cycle of kingship, of harvest, of soul, of life — woven together into one full picture of redemption.

Tu B'Shevat 是其中那个最容易被忽略的——「树木的新年」。它不是关于国家或圣殿；它关乎生命悄悄萌芽的那一刻。在以色列，那是冬末的雨季，地下的根开始苏醒，但树枝上还看不见果子。它是一个记号性的节日——记号说：「看不见，不等於没有发生。」 Tu B'Shevat is the most easily overlooked of the four — the New Year of the trees. It is not about the kingdom or the Temple. It is about the silent moment when life begins to stir. In Israel it falls at the end of the winter rains, when roots underground are waking but no fruit is yet visible on the branch. It is a sign-festival, and its sign reads: “What you cannot yet see is not the same as what is not happening.”

五、四杯酒 ── 逾越节裡的「数字解经法」V. The Four Cups — Pesach as a Mirror of Gematria

为了帮听众感受到「数字与节期同行」这件事并非孤例，拉比问：「你们有歡庆逾越节吗？在逾越节你喝几杯酒？」答：四杯。这四杯酒并不是仪式上的随机数字——它们对应《出埃及记》6:6-7 中神所说的四个救赎动词：「我要救你们脱离 (וְהוֹצֵאתִי)、我要拯救你们 (וְהִצַּלְתִּי)、我要救赎你们 (וְגָאַלְתִּי)、我要选取你们作我的百姓 (וְלָקַחְתִּי)。」 To help the room feel that this “numbers walking with the festivals” is not an isolated quirk, the rabbi asks: “Have you ever celebrated Passover? How many cups of wine do you drink at Pesach?” Answer: four. The four cups are not a ritual whim. They correspond to the four verbs of redemption in Exodus 6:6-7: “I will bring you out (וְהוֹצֵאתִי), I will deliver you (וְהִצַּלְתִּי), I will redeem you (וְגָאַלְתִּי), I will take you to Me as a people (וְלָקַחְתִּי).”

四个救赎动词、四杯逾越节的酒、四个犹太新年——这绝非巧合。拉比沙皮拉提醒听众：犹太教的数字不是装饰；它们是结构。每当圣经反覆出现一个数字，那个数字本身往往就是一道解经的钥匙。Tu B'Shevat 的「九加六等於十五」属於同一道钥匙的另一齿。 Four verbs of redemption, four cups of Pesach, four New Years — this is not coincidence. Numbers in Judaism are not decoration. They are structure. Whenever the Scripture repeats a number, that number is often itself a key to interpretation. Tu B'Shevat's “nine plus six equals fifteen” is one more tooth on that same key.

「在犹太教里，所有的东西都是循环的。我们的曆日是循环的——不是一次，不是两次，不是三次，而是四次。」——沙皮拉拉比博士 “In Judaism, everything is cyclical. Our calendar is cyclical — not once, not twice, not three times, but four times.”— Rabbi Dr. Itzhak Shapira

六、神的时间表是完美的 ── 一位拉比第一次跨入中国的那一刻VI. “God's Timing Is Perfect” — The Hour a Rabbi First Steps Into China

拉比沙皮拉对会众说：「这週日晚上九点，我们就要去广州。那一刻——正是Shevat 月十五日的开始。」这不是巧合。对一个相信「希伯来文里字母即数字，数字即时间」的人而言，「我刚好在Tu B'Shevat 开始的那一刻跨入中国」与「这件事在神的日历上早已被预定」之间，没有距离。 Rabbi Shapira tells the room: “This Sunday at 9pm we will enter Guangzhou. That hour is the beginning of the fifteenth of Shevat.” This is not coincidence. For someone who believes that in Hebrew the letters are numbers and the numbers are time, the statement “I happen to step into China the moment Tu B'Shevat begins” and the statement “this was appointed on God's calendar from the beginning” are not two statements. They are one.

为什麼是Tu B'Shevat？为什麼神会选择「树木的新年」作为一位犹太拉比第一次正式跨入中国国门的那个晚上？拉比沙皮拉在第一集没有展开答案——他把这个问题留下来，作为接下来一整个週末教导的悬念。但他给出了一道线索：「树」在希伯来圣经里从来不只是植物。《诗篇》第一篇说义人「就像一棵栽在溪水旁的树」(k'etz shatul al palgei mayim，כעץ שתול על־פלגי־מים)。《耶利米书》17:8 几乎重复同样的语言。Tu B'Shevat 庆祝的，并不是植物学，而是生命在乾枯之地被栽种这件事。 Why Tu B'Shevat? Why would God choose “the New Year of the Trees” as the night a Jewish rabbi crosses formally into mainland China? Rabbi Shapira does not yet open the answer in Part 1. He leaves it as a held question for the whole weekend of teaching to follow. But he gives one clue: “tree” in the Hebrew Bible is never just botany. Psalm 1 calls the righteous person “like a tree planted by streams of water” (k'etz shatul al palgei mayim, כעץ שתול על־פלגי־מים). Jeremiah 17:8 repeats nearly the same image. What Tu B'Shevat celebrates is not botany. It is life being planted in dry ground.

「就是週日晚上九点，我们就已经进入大陆——而那一刻就是 Shevat 第十五日的开始。神的时间表是完美的。」——沙皮拉拉比博士 “Sunday at 9pm we will already be in mainland China — and that hour is the start of the fifteenth of Shevat. God's timing is perfect.”— Rabbi Dr. Itzhak Shapira

七、未来几日的预告 ── 《新哈瑪斯》、保罗、与預言VII. The Days Ahead — New Hamas, the Apostle Paul, and Prophecy

在打好基础之后，拉比沙皮拉用一段非常实务的话告诉会众接下来几天会发生什麼——而这份「预告」本身值得我们注意，因为它显示出他对当下时局的判断： After laying the foundation, Rabbi Shapira gives a very practical preview of what is coming over the next few days. The preview is itself worth our attention — because it reveals how he reads the present moment:

第一，他将发布新书《新哈瑪斯》（The New Hamas）。Hamas（חמס）这个词在希伯来文里不只是某一个组织的名字——它是《创世记》6:11 用来形容洪水之前那个世代的字：「地上满了强暴 (hamas)」。拉比把今天的反犹浪潮、列国对以色列的围攻、和挪亚日子的那种普世性的暴力，看作同一种灵的两次显现。 First, he will release his new book The New Hamas. The Hebrew word chamas (חמס) is not merely the name of one organization — it is the word Genesis 6:11 uses to describe the generation before the Flood: “the earth was filled with chamas” (violence). Rabbi Shapira reads today's antisemitism, the gathering of nations against Israel, and the worldwide violence of Noah's day as two appearances of the same spirit.

第二，週末的香港聚会主题将是预言。这与他多年的教导路线一致：先知书 (Nevi'im) 不是过去式，它是为「我们这正活在末后日子的世代」而写的活的剧本。 Second, the weekend in Hong Kong will be on prophecy. This is consistent with his long teaching line: the Nevi'im (Prophets) are not a past-tense library. They are a living script written for “us upon whom the end of the ages has come.”

第三，週日晚进入中国大陆之后，他将开讲使徒保罗（希伯来名Shaul，שאול）。这是一个非常刻意的安排——保罗是一位被差派给外邦人的犹太拉比。在Tu B'Shevat 这个「植根」的节日里、在中国这片「外邦的土地」上，由一位现代犹太拉比开讲那位古代犹太拉比——这本身就是一幅图：「橄欖树将外邦的枝子接在自己原本的根上。」（《罗马书》11:17-24） Third, after entering mainland China on Sunday night, he will teach on the Apostle Paul (Hebrew: Shaul, שאול). The choice is deliberate. Paul is a Jewish rabbi sent to the nations. On Tu B'Shevat — the festival of rooting — on Chinese soil — the soil of the nations — a modern Jewish rabbi will teach about that ancient Jewish rabbi. This itself is a picture: the olive tree grafting the wild branches back into its own natural root (Romans 11:17-24).

八、对华人信徒，这意味着什麼？VIII. What This Means for Chinese Believers

第一，这一集教导邀请华人基督徒看待自己的信仰，不是借来的西方文化，而是一棵从希伯来根上长出来的树。当你了解 Tet 加 Vav 等於十五的时候，你读《诗篇》、读《以赛亚书》、读《启示录》的眼光会被改变。希伯来文不是一道学者才可以跨过的高墙——它是神留给每一位愿意俯身去看的人的钥匙。 First, this teaching invites Chinese Christians to see their faith not as borrowed Western culture but as a tree growing out of a Hebrew root. Once you have seen that Tet plus Vav is fifteen, your reading of the Psalms, of Isaiah, of Revelation will change. Hebrew is not a wall only scholars may climb. It is a key God has left for anyone willing to stoop and pick it up.

第二，「神的时间表是完美的」这句话不是属灵口号——它是一道宣称：你的人生坐标，不在你自己的行事曆上，而是在神的循环裡。如果一位拉比的脚步可以精准落在 Shevat 月十五日的零点零分，那麼你的生命也在祂的时钟之上。你不必焦虑你「迟到」了什麼——只要你在祂的循环裡，该来的果子，会在它该结的那一天结出来。 Second, “God's timing is perfect” is not a devotional cliché. It is a claim: your life's coordinates are not on your own calendar. They are inside God's cycles. If a rabbi's footsteps can land precisely at midnight on the 15th of Shevat, your own life is also on His clock. You do not need to panic that you have “fallen behind.” While you stay inside His cycle, the fruit that is meant to come will appear on the day it is meant to appear.

第三，Tu B'Shevat（树的新年）选中你的城市作为发生地点，这件事本身值得停下来思想。在中国这片土地上、在数代被分裂被压抑被嘲笑的属灵生命之上，神在 2026 年的这个冬末，决定差遣一位犹太拉比来宣告「现在是栽种的季节」——「树」的新年。这不一定是一个最显眼的神蹟，但它非常像祂的工作方式：祂不打雷开场，祂从根上、从一寸微小的萌芽开始。 Third, the fact that Tu B'Shevat — the New Year of the Trees — chose your city as its venue is worth a long pause. Over the Chinese soil, after generations of fractured, suppressed, and mocked spiritual life, in the late winter of 2026 God appointed a Jewish rabbi to step in and announce: “This is the season of planting.” The New Year of the Tree. It is not a flashy miracle. It is very like how He works — He does not open with thunder. He opens from the root, with a single inch of emerging shoot.

九、关於分辨IX. A Word on Discernment

如果这篇文章给人一种印象：拉比沙皮拉「凭著 Gematria 解出神在 2026 年要做的事」——那是对他、对读者都不公平的呈现。Gematria 在犹太传统中属於「Drash」（讲道层次的解读），不是「P'shat」（字面解读）。它是一种属灵的对照法，不是一种预测系统。即使是最敬虔的拉比也不会把 Gematria 用来确立教义；它用来显出已经在经文裡的东西，不是用来制造经文之外的东西。 If this article leaves the impression that Rabbi Shapira used Gematria to “decode what God is doing in 2026,” that is unfair to him and to the reader. Gematria in Jewish tradition is drash (the homiletical layer) — not p'shat (the plain reading). It is a method of spiritual correspondence, not a system of prediction. Even the most pious rabbi would not use Gematria to establish doctrine. It is used to reveal something already in the text — not to invent something beyond the text.

话虽如此，「神的时间是完美的」这件事，本身就是经文。「凡事都有定期，天下万务都有定时」（《传道书》3:1，希伯来文 la-kol zeman v'et l'khol chefetz tachat ha-shamayim，לכל זמן ועת לכל־חפץ תחת השמים）。我们可以谨慎地、不滥用 Gematria，但「神在祂的循环里行事，祂的时间是精准的」——这不是推测，这是圣经。 That said, “God's timing is perfect” is itself a biblical claim. Ecclesiastes 3:1: “For everything there is a season, and a time for every purpose under heaven” (Hebrew: la-kol zeman v'et l'khol chefetz tachat ha-shamayim, לכל זמן ועת לכל־חפץ תחת השמים). We may be cautious about Gematria without abandoning the deeper truth — that God acts inside His cycles, and His timing is precise. This is not speculation. It is Scripture.

十、给华人弟兄姊妹的呼召X. The Call to the Chinese Brethren

拉比沙皮拉以一段几乎像家书的话作结。这段话不是一段神学声明，而是一份邀请——对每一位读到这裡的华人信徒： Rabbi Shapira closes with a passage that reads almost like a letter home. It is not a theological declaration. It is an invitation — for every Chinese believer reading this:

「我爱我们的中国朋友。谢谢你让我看到你们美好的民族。听主的声音。我会在中国大陆、在这里的香港，与你们再见。这週末的相聚将是奇妙的。现在报名还来得及。在香港这个安息日，我们见。Shabbat Shalom。」 “I love our Chinese. Thank you for being such a beautiful people. Listen to the voice of the Lord. I'll see you — in mainland China, and here in Hong Kong. The weekend gathering is going to be amazing. Registration is still open. I'll see you this Shabbat in Hong Kong. Shabbat Shalom.”
