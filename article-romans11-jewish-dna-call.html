And China, in the rabbi's picture, is the largest of the “peoples from afar” Isaiah 49 spoke of. The spiritual bloodline of the Chinese remnant already carries the summons of that Jewish DNA. This article is the long arc of that two-hour live teaching.

一、「这是应许的日子，是救恩的日子」── 一场宣告式的开场I. “This Is the Day of Promise, the Day of Salvation” — A Declarative Opening

拉比沙皮拉博士在中国现场直播的开头，没有按常规先做学术介绍——他先宣告。他从韩国问安：「Shalom. Shalom. Shalom.」然后转身向中国的听众说：「这是日子，是应许的日子，是宣告主的日子。」紧接着是一句关键的连结：「这是旷野的声音，是耶和华的声音。」 Rabbi Dr. Shapira does not open the China livestream with an academic preamble — he opens with a declaration. He greets the room from Korea: “Shalom. Shalom. Shalom.” Then he turns toward the Chinese audience and says: “This is the day — the day of promise, the day of proclaiming the Lord.” Then the crucial link: “This is the voice from the wilderness, the voice of YHVH.”

「这是日子，是应许的日子，是宣告主的日子。这是旷野的声音，是耶和华 (YHVH) 的声音。这是应许的日子，是救恩的日子。」——沙皮拉拉比博士 “This is the day, the day of promise, the day of proclaiming the Lord. This is the voice from the wilderness, the voice of YHVH. This is the day of promise, the day of salvation.”— Rabbi Dr. Itzhak Shapira

「旷野的声音」直接呼应以赛亚书 40:3：「在旷野有人声 (Kol, קוֹל) 喊着说：『当预备耶和华的路…』」希伯来文 Kol（קוֹל）意为「声音、呼喊」。新约里施洗约翰自称是这「旷野的声音」（约翰福音 1:23）——是弥赛亚的开路先锋。当拉比沙皮拉对中国信徒说「这是旷野的声音」，他不是文学修辞，他是在说：中国正在听见一个开路先锋的声音；这个声音指向那即将再来的弥赛亚。 “The voice from the wilderness” echoes Isaiah 40:3: “A voice (Kol, קוֹל) cries in the wilderness: prepare the way of the LORD…” Hebrew Kol (קוֹל) is “voice, cry, sound.” In the New Testament John the Baptist names himself as this voice in the wilderness (John 1:23) — the forerunner of the Messiah. When Rabbi Shapira tells the Chinese audience “this is the voice from the wilderness,” he is not stretching a metaphor. He is saying: China is now hearing a forerunner-voice; the voice points to the Messiah who is about to return.

二、「犹太基因」── 保罗在罗马书十一章实际写的是什么II. “Jewish DNA” — What Paul Actually Wrote in Romans 11

拉比沙皮拉的著作《罗马书十一章中的犹太基因》(Romans 11: Jewish DNA) 的核心命题来自一段大多数基督徒读过、但很少有人停下来逐字咀嚼的经文：「若果子是初熟的，麵团也是圣洁的；树根若是圣洁的，树枝也是圣洁的。」(罗马书 11:16) 紧接着是嫁接的图像：野橄榄枝被嫁接进入「好橄榄根的肥汁」(罗 11:17) ——希腊文是 πιότητος τῆς ῥίζης，「根的肥汁」。 Rabbi Shapira's book Romans 11: Jewish DNA opens at a passage most Christians have read but few stop to chew word by word: “If the firstfruit is holy, the lump is also holy; and if the root is holy, so are the branches.” (Romans 11:16) The image proceeds at once into grafting: a wild branch is grafted into “the richness of the olive root” (Rom 11:17) — Greek πιότητος τῆς ῥίζης, “the fatness of the root.”

请注意：保罗没有说「枝子是圣洁的」(然后从枝子下推到树根)。他说「树根是圣洁的，所以树枝也是圣洁的」——圣洁是从根向枝子遗传的。这正是「DNA」的逻辑：DNA 不是从外部贴上去的标签，而是从源头流入、塑造每一根枝叶的内在结构。 Notice: Paul does not say “the branches are holy” and deduce the root from them. He says “the root is holy, therefore the branches are too” — holiness is inherited from root to branch. This is precisely the logic of DNA: it is not a label glued on from outside; it is a code that flows from the source and shapes every branch and leaf from within.

「保罗讲的不是一个『感觉好』的比喻。他讲的是一个遗传学的事实：当一根野枝被嫁接进入圣根，圣根的『汁液』(肥汁) 就开始流入这根野枝。这汁液——就是『犹太基因』。它不是一个种族标签；它是从亚伯拉罕、以撒、雅各的应许之根所流出的、神圣的属灵血脉。」——沙皮拉拉比博士 “Paul is not preaching a feel-good metaphor. He is teaching a fact of inheritance: when the wild branch is grafted into the holy root, the sap of the holy root begins to flow into the wild branch. That sap — that is what we call Jewish DNA. It is not a racial label. It is a sacred spiritual bloodline flowing from the root of the promises to Abraham, Isaac and Jacob.”— Rabbi Dr. Itzhak Shapira

对一个华人读者，这一点必须再三强调：「犹太基因」不是种族意义上的「犹太血统」——它是「属灵 DNA」，是亚伯拉罕之约里所应许的、流向万国的那道「祝福的源」(创世记 12:3)。换言之，你不需要变成犹太人，你已经被嫁接进入犹太基因。 For a Chinese reader this must be said and said again: “Jewish DNA” is not racial Jewishness — it is spiritual DNA, the stream of blessing promised to flow from Abraham to all the families of the earth (Gen 12:3). In other words: you do not need to become a Jew; you have already been grafted into the Jewish DNA.

三、为何「旷野的声音」此刻在中国回响III. Why “the Voice in the Wilderness” Now Echoes in China

拉比一再重复「这是应许的日子，是救恩的日子」——为什么是「日子」？因为希伯来圣经里，「那日」(Yom HaHu, יום ההוא) 是一个固定的先知用语——它指着耶和华的日子 (Day of the LORD)，一个具体的、被神标记的历史节点。「应许的日子」=「那日」=「救恩的日子」=「弥赛亚显现的日子」。这一组语意在希伯来先知传统中是同义反覆。 The rabbi repeats “this is the day of promise, the day of salvation.” Why “the day”? In the Hebrew Bible, “that day” (Yom HaHu, יום ההוא) is a fixed prophetic phrase — it points to the Day of the LORD, a concrete and divinely-marked node of history. “The day of promise” = “that day” = “the day of salvation” = “the day Messiah is revealed.” In the Hebrew prophetic tradition, this set of phrases is synonymous.

拉比对中国听众说「这是那日」是一个极重的宣告。它意味着：神在中国并没有迟到。许多华人信徒成长在一种「西方早醒、东方迟醒」的叙事里——彷佛中国是末后赶上来的那一群。拉比的图景刚好颠倒：中国不是赶上来的；中国是被神留到「那日」的——她出场的时机本身，就是先知的记号。 When the rabbi tells the Chinese audience “this is that day,” the weight is enormous. He is saying: God is not late in China. Many Chinese believers have grown up in a narrative of “the West woke up first; the East catches up.” The rabbi's picture inverts that. China is not catching up. China was held back for “that day” — her timing of appearance is itself a prophetic sign.

以赛亚书 49:1：「众海岛 (iyyim, איים) 啊，当听我言！远方的众民 (ammim merachok, עמים מרחוק) 哪，留心而听！」拉比所说的「远方的众民」之中，最大的一群——按地理与人口——就是中国。她不是先知名单上的「附注」；她是其中的「最大一群」。 Isaiah 49:1: “Listen, O coastlands (iyyim, איים) — give heed, you peoples from afar (ammim merachok, עמים מרחוק)!” Among the “peoples from afar” the rabbi names, the largest in geography and population is China. She is not a footnote on the prophet's list. She is the largest line on it.

四、嫁接已经发生 ── 中国信徒的真实身份IV. The Graft Has Already Happened — the Chinese Believer's Real Identity

罗马书十一章的嫁接动作用的是希腊文 ἐγκεντρίζω（enkentrizō，「嵌入、嫁接」）。它是一个被动语态——不是「你嫁接自己」，而是「你被嫁接」。当一个中国信徒在某一日相信耶书亚是弥赛亚的那一刻，他没有在做一个「跨进基督教」的动作——按拉比的图像，他正在被园丁亲手插入那棵从亚伯拉罕生长出来的橄榄树。从那一刻起，「树根的肥汁」开始流入他。 Romans 11's grafting verb is Greek ἐγκεντρίζω (enkentrizō, “to graft in”). It is passive — not “you graft yourself,” but “you are grafted.” When a Chinese believer, on some specific day, believes that Yeshua is the Messiah, he is not doing the action of “crossing into Christianity.” On the rabbi's picture, he is being slipped — by the Gardener's own hand — into the olive tree that grows out of Abraham. From that moment, the “fatness of the root” begins to flow into him.

「这是关键：你不是在选择一个宗教。嫁接是被动语态。园丁拿起你，把你嵌入。当你信耶书亚的那一刻，你已经被植入到亚伯拉罕、以撒、雅各的橄榄树里，你的血脉已经被改写。」——沙皮拉拉比博士 “Here is the key: you are not choosing a religion. Engrafting is in the passive voice. The Gardener picks you up and slips you in. The moment you believed in Yeshua, you were planted into the olive tree of Abraham, Isaac and Jacob. Your bloodline was rewritten.”— Rabbi Dr. Itzhak Shapira

这就是为何拉比将其著作命名为Jewish DNA：它不是一个隐喻，也不是一种「精神上的认同」。它是说——你已经携带这套属灵 DNA。它在你里面运行——在你呼唤耶书亚之名的时候、在你为以色列祷告的时候、在你抵挡反犹声浪的时候、在你心被希伯来圣经所点燃的时候——那不是你的「选择」；那是 DNA 在运作。 This is why the rabbi titles his book Jewish DNA: it is not a metaphor, nor a “spiritual identification.” It is a claim that you carry this spiritual DNA. It runs in you — when you call on the name of Yeshua, when you pray for Israel, when you stand against antisemitism, when your heart is set on fire by the Hebrew Scriptures — that is not your “preference”; that is the DNA at work.

五、「先知性呼召」── 究竟在召你做什么？V. The “Prophetic Call” — What Exactly Are You Being Called To?

拉比在这场两小时的直播里反覆使用一个短语：「先知性呼召」(prophetic call, קריאה נבואית, Kri'ah Nevu'it)。希伯来文 Kri'ah（קריאה，「呼喊、呼召、读」）来自动词 kara（קרא），与利未记 1:1 的「Vayikra」(וַיִּקְרָא，「祂呼喚」) 同根。「先知性呼召」不是一个泛泛的「热心」邀请——它指的是神具体地、按你的名、向你发出的呼喊。 Across the two hours, the rabbi keeps using one phrase: “the prophetic call” (קריאה נבואית, Kri'ah Nevu'it). Hebrew Kri'ah (קריאה, “a calling, a cry, a reading”) is from the verb kara (קרא) — the same root as Vayikra (וַיִּקְרָא, “He called”) in Leviticus 1:1. A prophetic call is not a general invitation to be enthusiastic. It is a cry that God specifically, by name, has issued to you.

在「犹太 DNA」的框架里，这个「先知性呼召」具体内容是什么？拉比列出三层：
（一）站立 (Amad, עמד)：在反以色列声浪席捲全球的时代，站立就是一种属灵动作。 Inside the “Jewish DNA” frame, what is the prophetic call concretely calling you to? The rabbi names three layers:
(1) To stand (Amad, עמד): in an age when anti-Israel sentiment sweeps the world, standing is itself a spiritual act.

（二）祝福 (Barach, ברך)：「为耶路撒冷求平安」(诗篇 122:6)——这不是一个软弱的礼貌话，它是亚伯拉罕之约里的一个直接命令：「为你祝福的，我必赐福与他。」(创世记 12:3) 中国信徒为以色列祝福的时候，是在亚伯拉罕之约的内圈里运作的。 (2) To bless (Barach, ברך): “pray for the peace of Jerusalem” (Ps 122:6) is not soft courtesy. It is a direct clause of the Abrahamic covenant: “I will bless those who bless you” (Gen 12:3). When a Chinese believer blesses Israel, he is operating inside the inner ring of the Abrahamic covenant.

（三）传扬 (Higgid, הגיד)：把你所看见的传出去。在「众端之后」(Acharei Aklimot)、外壳已经掰开的时刻，沉默不再是一种选项。 (3) To proclaim (Higgid, הגיד): pass on what you have seen. In the moment of Acharei Aklimot (“after the climaxes”), with the shells cracked open, silence is no longer an option.

「先知性呼召不是叫你『感觉被感动』。它是叫你站立、祝福、传扬。这三个动作是先知传统里 Avraham、Yitzchak、Ya'akov（亚伯拉罕、以撒、雅各）所传下来的——你既被嫁接进他们的根，他们的动作就是你的动作。」——沙皮拉拉比博士 “The prophetic call is not telling you to feel moved. It is telling you to stand, bless, proclaim. These three motions come down from Abraham, Isaac and Jacob in the prophetic tradition — and once you are grafted into their root, their motions are your motions.”— Rabbi Dr. Itzhak Shapira

六、警告 ── 不要向天然的枝子夸口VI. A Warning — Do Not Boast Against the Natural Branches

保罗在罗马书十一章 18 节立刻发出一个尖锐的警告：「你就不可向旧枝子夸口；若是夸口，当知道不是你托着根，乃是根托着你。」(罗 11:18) 希腊文 katakauchaomai（κατακαυχάομαι，「夸口、傲慢」）暗示的是「我比你更高」的姿态。 Paul, in Romans 11:18, immediately fires a sharp warning: “Do not boast against the branches; if you do boast, remember it is not you who supports the root, but the root that supports you.” (Rom 11:18) The Greek katakauchaomai (κατακαυχάομαι, “to boast against”) carries the posture of “I am higher than you.”

拉比沙皮拉作为弥赛亚犹太人，对这条警告的份量感受很深。他在这场直播里几次回到这一点：基督教历史上有过「替代神学」(Replacement Theology, Supersessionism)的时代——教会自以为已经「取代」了以色列，从而把「神的应许」据为己有。拉比指出：那不是嫁接，那是「拔掉旧枝、塞自己进去」——是傲慢，是断根。 As a Messianic Jew, Rabbi Shapira feels this warning's weight personally. He returns to it several times during the broadcast: Christianity has had eras of Replacement Theology (Supersessionism), in which the Church imagined it had replaced Israel and could pocket the promises. The rabbi names that plainly: that is not engrafting — it is “pulling out the old branch and plugging yourself in.” It is pride, and it is cutting the root.

「华人信徒，听清楚：你不是在取代以色列。你是被嫁接进入以色列。你的根不在你自己里面；你的根在亚伯拉罕的应许里。所以不要再说『教会就是新以色列』这种话——那不是嫁接的语言，那是断根的语言。」——沙皮拉拉比博士 “Chinese believer, hear this: you are not replacing Israel. You are grafted into Israel. Your root is not in yourself; your root is in the promise to Abraham. So stop saying ‘the Church is the new Israel’ — that is not the language of engrafting. That is the language of cutting the root.”— Rabbi Dr. Itzhak Shapira

七、奥秘 ── 「以色列全家都要得救」VII. The Mystery — “All Israel Shall Be Saved”

罗马书十一章 25-26 节：「弟兄们，我不愿意你们不知道这奥秘 (mystērion, μυστήριον)，恐怕你们自以为聪明：就是以色列人有几分是硬心的，等到外邦人的数目添满了；于是以色列全家都要得救。」(罗 11:25-26) Romans 11:25-26: “Brothers, I do not want you to be ignorant of this mystery (mystērion, μυστήριον), lest you be wise in your own conceits: a partial hardening has come upon Israel, until the fullness of the Gentiles has come in; and so all Israel will be saved.” (Rom 11:25-26)

「外邦人的数目添满」希腊文是 πλήρωμα τῶν ἐθνῶν（plērōma tōn ethnōn，「外邦人的丰满数目」）。拉比在这场直播里大胆提出：当中国的余民——「最远方的众民」中最大的一群——被嫁接进来的时候，「外邦数目添满」的最后一段正在被填满。换言之，中国不是外邦数目里的一笔零头；中国是「数目添满」里的最后一个大块。 The phrase “the fullness of the Gentiles” in Greek is πλήρωμα τῶν ἐθνῶν (plērōma tōn ethnōn, “the full count of the nations”). In this broadcast the rabbi makes a bold claim: when the remnant of China — the largest line among the “most distant peoples” — is grafted in, the final segment of the plērōma is being filled in. In other words: China is not a small remainder in the Gentile count. China is the last great block of the “fullness.”

如果这个判断为真——那么中国的余民在末世历史里的角色就极为巨大：她不是末班车，她是那一节使整列火车开动的车厢。当「外邦数目添满」的时候，紧接着的事情是「以色列全家都要得救」。也就是说，华人余民的觉醒，在保罗的图景里，是触发以色列得救之奥秘的最后一击。 If that judgement is true, then China's remnant in end-time history plays an enormous role: she is not a late-arriving caboose. She is the carriage whose coupling sets the whole train moving. When the “fullness of the Gentiles” is reached, the very next clause is “all Israel will be saved.” That is: in Paul's picture, the awakening of the Chinese remnant is the last strike that triggers the mystery of Israel's salvation.

八、遗传 ── 犹太基因传给你的，究竟是什么？VIII. Inheritance — What Does the Jewish DNA Actually Transmit to You?

拉比在这场直播里详细列出「犹太 DNA」在被嫁接者身上的具体表达。这部分对华人信徒尤其实用，因为它把抽象的「DNA」翻译成可观察的属灵特征。 In the broadcast the rabbi lays out the concrete expressions of the “Jewish DNA” in the grafted-in. This part is especially practical for the Chinese believer because it translates the abstract “DNA” into observable spiritual traits.

(一) 对妥拉的胃口 (Ta'avat Torah, תאוות תורה)——一个被嫁接进入的人会发现自己对希伯来圣经突然饥饿。不是出于学术兴趣，而是出于一种饥饿，仿佛体内有一个器官长期没被喂养。 (1) A hunger for Torah (Ta'avat Torah, תאוות תורה) — the grafted-in suddenly find themselves starving for the Hebrew Scriptures. Not for academic interest, but a hunger like an organ in the body that has gone unfed for a long time.

(二) 对以色列的疼爱 (Ahavat Yisrael, אהבת ישראל)——一种无法解释的、为以色列流泪的心。在地缘政治上你与以色列毫无关联，但你的心却疼她。这就是 DNA 在运作。 (2) Love for Israel (Ahavat Yisrael, אהבת ישראל) — an inexplicable heart that weeps for Israel. You have no geopolitical stake; yet your heart aches for her. That is the DNA at work.

(三) 对以色列的弟兄之爱 (Ahavat Ammi, אהבת עמי，「对我民的爱」)——这是拉比的事工名称。它的意思是：在你心里，犹太人不再是「他们」；而是「我民」。 (3) Love for His people (Ahavat Ammi, אהבת עמי, “love for My people”) — this is the name of the rabbi's ministry. It means: in your heart, the Jewish people are no longer “they.” They are “My people.”

(四) 对耶书亚的认识 (Da'at Yeshua, דעת ישוע)——但这一项是反方向的：被嫁接者对耶书亚的认识，不取代犹太根，而是因为接到根之后才完全开花。 (4) Knowledge of Yeshua (Da'at Yeshua, דעת ישוע) — but this one is asymmetric: the grafted-in's knowledge of Yeshua does not replace the Jewish root; it flowers only when joined to the root.

「如果你检查自己内心，发现你对妥拉饥饿、为以色列流泪、对犹太人有疼爱、对耶书亚有深认识——这四样同时出现——朋友，那不是你的『信仰风格』，那是你的 DNA 在运作。你已经被嫁接了。」——沙皮拉拉比博士 “If you look inside yourself and find a hunger for Torah, tears for Israel, tenderness for the Jewish people, and deep knowledge of Yeshua — all four together — friend, that is not your ‘spiritual style.’ That is your DNA at work. You have been grafted in.”— Rabbi Dr. Itzhak Shapira

九、关于分辨IX. A Word on Discernment

本文呈现拉比沙皮拉关于「犹太基因」的论证，但读者仍当保留几处必要的分辨。第一，「DNA」是生物学的术语；拉比用它作为属灵的比喻——这是一个有力的、但毕竟是讲道层次的解读 (drash)，而非字面层次的解读 (p'shat)。罗马书十一章的「圣根托着枝子」是字面的；「DNA 在你血脉里流动」是应用。两者份量不同，不应混淆。 This article carries Rabbi Shapira's argument about “Jewish DNA,” but the reader should hold several discernment points. First, “DNA” is a biological term; the rabbi uses it as a spiritual analogy — powerful, but this is a drash-level reading, not p'shat. The Romans 11 claim “the holy root supports the branches” is literal; “the DNA flows in your bloodline” is application. The two carry different weights and should not be conflated.

第二，把以赛亚书 49:1中「远方的众民」直接读为「中国」是一种drash 式的应用。先知本人未必脑中浮现一张中国地图。但「中国是远方诸民中最大的一群」这件事——按今日的地理与人口——是事实。所以这条应用并非随意，但读者应清楚区分先知所说的原意与拉比所做的应用。 Second, reading Isaiah 49:1's “peoples from afar” directly as China is a drash-style application. The prophet did not necessarily have a Chinese map in his mind. But that “China is the largest line among distant peoples” — by today's geography and population — is a fact. The application is not arbitrary, but the reader should still distinguish the prophet's original sense from the rabbi's application.

第三，「中国是『外邦数目添满』的最后一大块」是一个有力的预言性判断，但它是判断，不是命题。它不可能被人口统计直接证伪或证立。它的功用是：给中国信徒一种历史份量的自我意识——你不是过客，你是触发器的一部分。但若你按这话去等候「某年某月以色列得救」的具体时刻，那你已经走出了拉比的本意。 Third, “China is the last great block of the fullness of the Gentiles” is a powerful prophetic judgement, not a verifiable proposition. Census data cannot disprove or confirm it. Its function: to give the Chinese believer a sense of historical weight — you are not a passer-by; you are part of the trigger. But to use it to set a date for “all Israel will be saved” is to step outside the rabbi's intent.

然而更大的图景——华人作为被嫁接进入希伯来根的肢体、携带犹太基因的呼召、活在「众端之后」的时刻——这个图景是圣经的，不是推测。它在罗马书十一章。它在以赛亚书 49 章。它在以赛亚书 60 章。它就是十字架与橄榄树的图景。 Yet the larger picture — Chinese believers as a grafted-in body of the Hebrew root, carrying the call of Jewish DNA, living in the moment of Acharei Aklimot — this picture is biblical, not speculation. It is in Romans 11. It is in Isaiah 49. It is in Isaiah 60. It is the picture of the cross and the olive tree.

十、抉择 ── 「这是应许的日子，是救恩的日子」X. The Choice — “This Is the Day of Promise, the Day of Salvation”

在两小时十分钟的直播末尾，拉比沙皮拉博士并不收尾于一段精致的总结——他回到那句开场的宣告，把它变成结尾的钉子：「这是应许的日子，是救恩的日子。」他对每一位华人听众发出一个个人的、按名字的呼召。 At the end of the two-hour-ten-minute broadcast, Rabbi Dr. Shapira does not close with a polished summary. He returns to the opening declaration and nails it to the end: “This is the day of promise, the day of salvation.” He issues a personal, name-by-name call to every Chinese listener.

「这是日子」——不是「将要是」，是「已经是」。「这是应许的日子」——亚伯拉罕之约的应许已经流到你这里。「这是救恩的日子」——耶书亚的救恩此刻就在你站立的地方。「这是旷野的声音」——开路先锋的声音今天就在你心里回响。 “This is the day” — not “will be,” but “already is.” “This is the day of promise” — the Abrahamic promise has now flowed to you. “This is the day of salvation” — Yeshua's salvation is right where you stand. “This is the voice from the wilderness” — the forerunner's voice echoes today inside your own heart.

「华人弟兄姊妹，听清楚：你的名字写在亚伯拉罕的橄榄树上。你的 DNA 已经被改写。今天就站立、祝福、传扬——这是应许的日子，这是救恩的日子。Am Yisrael Chai，中国，shine！」 “Chinese brothers and sisters, hear this clearly: your name is written into the olive tree of Abraham. Your DNA has been rewritten. Today — stand, bless, proclaim. This is the day of promise. This is the day of salvation. Am Yisrael Chai — China, shine.”
