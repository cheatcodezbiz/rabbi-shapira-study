哈夫達拉 · 何謂事奉Havdalah · What Is Avodah?

秩序的聖潔：每一項任務都是神聖的事奉 The Holiness of Order: Every Task Is Sacred

會幕的事奉由亞倫第四子以塔瑪 (Ithamar) 督理，因為哈舍姆 (Hashem) 是一位秩序 (seder) 的神——祂在意敬拜中的每一個細節，從最大的結構到最小的繩索。利未人肩上的重擔 (masa)，竟成了屬靈高升的隱喻。無論我們是寫一本書，還是修剪草坪，一切忠心的工作都是獻給神的事奉 (Avodah)。 The Tabernacle service was supervised by Aaron's fourth son, Ithamar, because Hashem is a God of seder — order — who cares about every detail of worship, from the largest structure to the smallest cord. The Levites' masa, their burden, becomes a metaphor for spiritual elevation. Whether we write a book or mow the lawn, all faithful work is Avodah unto God.

哈夫達拉 (Havdalah) 的整場教導，在這最後一段裡收束於一個看似平凡、卻足以改變人一生的問題：「何謂事奉 (Avodah)？」吉波拉·麥克唐納教師沒有把答案留在聖殿的祭壇上，也沒有把它鎖進拉比的書房裡。她把它放回你我的雙手——放回我們的書桌、廚房、辦公室與後院的草坪。會幕的搬運由亞倫的第四子以塔瑪 (Ithamar) 督理，因為哈舍姆 (Hashem) 是一位有秩序 (seder) 的神：祂在意每一根繩索、每一個鉤環、每一件器皿被誰抬、怎麼抬。而利未人肩上沉重的負擔 (masa)，正是這段教導翻轉的核心——它不是咒詛，而是高升。當你明白你是為誰而做、靠誰的力量而做，你生命中就再沒有一項任務「太卑微」或「太沉重」。 The whole Havdalah teaching gathers, in this closing section, around a question that looks ordinary and yet can reorder a life: “What is Avodah?” Morah Giborah McDonald does not leave the answer on the altar of the Temple, nor lock it in a rabbi's study. She places it back into our own hands — onto our desks, our kitchens, our offices, and the grass of the backyard. The carrying of the Tabernacle was supervised by Aaron's fourth son, Ithamar, because Hashem is a God of seder, of order: He cares about every cord, every clasp, every vessel — who carries it, and how. And the Levites' heavy masa, their burden, is the very thing this teaching turns inside out — not a curse, but an elevation. When you understand for whom you labor and in whose strength, no task in your life is ever “too menial” or “too heavy” again.

一、以塔瑪與秩序之神I. Ithamar and the God of Order

會幕拆卸、搬運、重新立起的整個流程，並不是隨意分配的。它被交付給亞倫的第四子以塔瑪 (אִיתָמָר, Ithamar) 督理。為什麼是以塔瑪？為什麼聖經要費筆墨記下「誰負責哪一件器皿、誰抬哪一根杠、誰收哪一條繩索」這些看似瑣碎的細節？因為哈舍姆 (הַשֵּׁם, Hashem) 是一位有秩序 (סֵדֶר, seder) 的神。 The dismantling, carrying, and re-erecting of the Tabernacle was not assigned at random. It was placed under the supervision of Aaron's fourth son, Ithamar (אִיתָמָר). Why Ithamar? Why does Scripture bother to record who was responsible for which vessel, who bore which pole, who gathered which cord — details that look so trivial? Because Hashem (הַשֵּׁם) is a God of order — of seder (סֵדֶר).

吉波拉教師強調這一點：神所看重的，從最大的結構到最小的繩索，無一不在祂的眼中。我們很容易以為，神只在意「大事」——只在意至聖所、只在意約櫃、只在意大祭司。但會幕的記載卻把鉤環、釘樁、罩棚的搭扣、器皿的杠，一一列名清點。對神而言，沒有一個細節是「太小」而不值得用心去做的。 Morah Giborah presses the point: nothing escapes God's eye — from the largest structure to the smallest cord. We easily assume God cares only about the “big things” — the Holy of Holies, the Ark, the High Priest. Yet the Tabernacle account names and counts the clasps, the pegs, the loops of the covering, the carrying-poles of the vessels, one by one. To God, no detail is ever “too small” to be done with care.

這份對秩序的看重，本身就是一種神學。一位隨意、混亂、漫不經心的神，不會在意誰抬哪一根杠；但聖經所啟示的神，卻把秩序織進了敬拜的每一寸結構裡。當我們學會在最小的任務上忠心、有序、用心，我們其實是在效法那位創造時把光暗、晝夜、海陸一一分開的神。 This regard for order is itself a theology. A careless, chaotic, indifferent god would not care who carries which pole; but the God revealed in Scripture weaves order into every inch of worship. When we learn to be faithful, orderly, and attentive in the smallest task, we are imitating the very God who, at creation, separated light from dark, day from night, sea from land, one act at a time.

這也是為什麼督理之責落在以塔瑪身上有其深意。亞倫的四個兒子中，拿答 (Nadav) 與亞比戶 (Avihu) 因獻上「凡火」——擅自、無秩序的敬拜——而倒下；存留下來的以塔瑪與以利亞撒 (Elazar) 則承接了井然有序的事奉。以塔瑪所監管的，不是壯觀的場面，而是清點、登記、交接這些不起眼的細工。神把最瑣碎的後勤，託付給最懂得秩序的人——因為在神眼中，忠心於小事的人，才配得管理大事。 There is depth, too, in why the oversight fell to Ithamar. Of Aaron's four sons, Nadav and Avihu fell for offering “strange fire” — presumptuous, disordered worship; the surviving Ithamar and Elazar carried on the orderly service. What Ithamar supervised was not spectacle but the unglamorous work of counting, recording, handing over. God entrusts the most tedious logistics to the one who best understands order — for in His sight, only the one faithful in little is fit to be set over much.

二、馬撒 ── 重擔成為高升II. Masa — When the Burden Becomes Elevation

會幕的器皿都是利未人用肩扛、用手抬的。希伯來文用一個詞描述這份工作：馬撒 (מַשָּׂא, masa)——「重擔、負荷」。表面上看，這只是一份吃力、繁重、甚至卑微的體力活。但拉比尼·貝哈亞 (Rabbeinu Bechaya) 對這個詞作了一個翻轉性的詮釋：masa 不僅是「擔起的服務」，更是「高升的服務」（希伯來文 hitnasut，הִתְנַשְּׂאוּת）。 The vessels of the Tabernacle were carried on the shoulders and in the hands of the Levites. Hebrew has a single word for this labor: masa (מַשָּׂא) — “burden, load.” On the surface it is merely heavy, grueling, even lowly physical work. But Rabbeinu Bechaya gives the word a transformative reading: masa is not only “the service of carrying” but “the service of lifting up” — hitnasut (הִתְנַשְּׂאוּת) in Hebrew.

利未人肩上的勞動，被設計來同時提升兩樣東西：一面提升會幕物質結構的尊榮，把它高舉到神聖的層次；另一面，也提升搬運者自己的靈魂。那沉重的負擔，被轉化成屬靈的功德、贖罪與淨化。貝哈亞解釋說：這份勞動為整個群體贖罪，也藉著努力與奉獻，淨化了工作者的靈魂。 The Levites' labor was designed to lift up two things at once: on one side, the honor of the Tabernacle's physical structure, raised to a divine level; on the other, the soul of the carrier himself. That heavy burden was transformed into spiritual merit, atonement, and purification. Bechaya explains that this labor atoned for the whole community, and through effort and devotion purified the soul of the worker.

這是整段教導裡最美的翻轉：你以為你只是在搬東西，其實神是在用這份重擔高升你。你生命中那些沉重的、重複的、看不見光彩的責任——照顧年邁的父母、清晨擠出時間禱告、把同一個禱告獻上第一百次——在神的經濟裡，每一份 masa 都是 hitnasut，每一份重擔都在把你往上抬。 This is the most beautiful turn in the whole teaching: you think you are merely carrying something — but God is using that very burden to lift you up. Those heavy, repetitive, unglamorous duties in your life — caring for an aging parent, carving out time to pray before dawn, offering the same petition for the hundredth time — in God's economy, every masa is a hitnasut; every burden is lifting you higher.

三、何謂事奉？ ── Avodah 的意義III. What Is Avodah? — The Meaning of the Word

那麼，事奉 (עֲבוֹדָה, Avodah) 究竟是什麼意思？字面上看，它是「工作的工作、服務的服務」。這個詞有極深厚的歷史：聖經用它描寫雅各為拉班所作的工，也用它描寫以色列人在埃及的奴役；它還是一個禮儀詞彙，指獻祭與祈禱。是的——禱告就是 Avodah，禱告是一種工作。 So what does Avodah (עֲבוֹדָה) actually mean? Literally, “work of work, service of service.” The word carries a powerful history: Scripture uses it for the labor Jacob performed for Laban, and for the enslavement of Israel in Egypt; it is also a liturgical word for sacrifice and prayer. Yes — prayer is Avodah; prayer is a work.

吉波拉教師提醒我們一個讀經的原則：當你要明白一個詞，永遠回到它在妥拉 (Torah) 裡第一次出現的地方。因為詞語的意義是在妥拉裡被定義的，而那意義會幫助你明白《新約》(Brit Khadashah)。Avodah 的詞根第一次出現，是在《創世記》（Bereshit）——神把亞當安置在伊甸園，要他「修理、看守」那地。希伯來文 ovdah 與 avodah 同根。 Morah Giborah reminds us of a principle of reading: when you want to understand a word, always go back to the first place it appears in the Torah. Because the meaning of words is defined in the Torah, and that meaning unlocks the Brit Khadashah (the New Covenant). The root of Avodah first appears in Bereshit (Genesis) — God places Adam in the Garden of Eden to “till and tend” it. The Hebrew ovdah shares the same root as avodah.

「耶和華神將那人安置在伊甸園，使他修理看守。」——創世記 2:15 “The LORD God took the man and put him in the garden of Eden to work it and keep it.”— Genesis 2:15

注意這裡的雙重意義：神呼召亞當的，不只是在物質上照料土地，更是在工作中服事、敬拜祂。當亞當耕地時，他要一面讚美、一面敬拜哈舍姆。從人類存在的第一刻起，工作與敬拜就是同一件事。Avodah 一個詞，同時承載了「勞動」與「崇拜」——這不是巧合，這是神對人受造目的的啟示。 Note the double meaning: God calls Adam not only to physically care for the land, but also to serve and worship Him in the work. As Adam tilled the soil, he was to praise and worship Hashem. From the very first moment of human existence, work and worship were one and the same thing. That a single word, Avodah, carries both “labor” and “worship” is no accident — it is God's revelation of why humanity was made.

四、事奉的廣度 ── 一切為了他人IV. The Breadth of Avodah — All of It for Others

Avodah 與修復世界 (Tikkun Olam, תיקון עולם) 的概念緊緊相連：人相信，藉著我們事奉的行為，可以促成一個更公義、更和諧的社會。因此 Avodah 常與慈善的慈惠 (Tzedakah, צְדָקָה) 交織在一起——因為兩者都是運用自己的資源與技能去造福他人，而不是造福自己。 Avodah is bound tightly to the idea of Tikkun Olam (תיקון עולם), repairing the world: the belief that through our acts of service we can bring about a more just and harmonious society. So Avodah is often interwoven with charitable Tzedakah (צְדָקָה) — for both use one's resources and skills to benefit others, not oneself.

這正是 Avodah 與一般「工作」分別的地方。世人衡量工作，看的是「我能得著什麼」——薪水、名聲、成就感；但 Avodah 衡量工作，看的是「我能給出什麼」。同一雙手、同一份勞苦，當它的指向從「自己」轉為「他人與神」時，那份工作的本質就被改變了——它從謀生變成了敬拜，從負擔變成了功德。不是任務本身決定它是否神聖，而是你做它時心向著誰。 This is precisely where Avodah parts ways with ordinary “work.” The world measures labor by “what can I get” — wages, reputation, a sense of achievement; Avodah measures it by “what can I give.” The same hands, the same toil — once their aim turns from self toward others and God, the very nature of the work is changed: it becomes worship instead of a wage, merit instead of a burden. It is not the task that makes the labor holy, but the One your heart is turned toward as you do it.

許多誡命 (mitzvot) 都活出了 Avodah 的精神：探望病人 (Bikur Cholim)、尊重他人的尊嚴 (Kavod HaBriyot)、款待客旅 (Hachnasat Orchim)、什一奉獻。這些全都是 Avodah 的行為，而且全都是為了幫助別人。吉波拉教師說得直白： Many mitzvot embody Avodah: visiting the sick (Bikur Cholim), honoring the dignity of others (Kavod HaBriyot), welcoming the stranger (Hachnasat Orchim), tithing. These are all acts of Avodah — and they are all done to help others. Morah Giborah puts it bluntly:

「我們不看自己能從中得著什麼。我來，拉比也來，不是為了我們自己。我們來，是要幫助你。我們不是來幫助自己——我們來，是要做幫助你的那份事奉。」——吉波拉·麥克唐納教師 “We don't look at what we can get out of it. I don't come here, nor does the rabbi, for ourselves. We come here to help you. We don't help ourselves — we come to do the Avodah of helping you.”— Morah Giborah McDonald

而當拉比與教師前來事奉時，那是心的事奉 (Avodat HaLev, עֲבוֹדַת הַלֵּב)。這種屬靈的工作與奉獻，是透過對哈舍姆的信靠 (Bitachon, בִּטָּחוֹן) 展現出來的——學習完完全全、徹徹底底地信靠神。我們都會經歷與 Bitachon 搏鬥的時刻：「神啊，祢吩咐我的我都做了，為什麼還沒有成？」但神的時機，加上我們的禱告，必在恰當的時候成就那事。 And when the rabbi and the teacher come to serve, that is Avodat HaLev (עֲבוֹדַת הַלֵּב) — the service of the heart. This spiritual work and devotion is shown through Bitachon (בִּטָּחוֹן), trust in Hashem — learning to rely on Him completely and utterly. We all know the seasons of wrestling with Bitachon: “God, I have done everything You asked — why has it not worked out yet?” Yet His timing, together with our prayer, will bring it to pass at the right time.

五、為甚麼一切的目的是救贖V. Why the End of It All Is Redemption

這一切 Avodah 的終極目的，是促成最終的救贖 (Geula, גְּאוּלָה)。真正的事奉是無私的：我們工作不是為了個人的得失，而是為了幫助他人、為猶太民族積攢功德，使耶書亞 (Yeshua) 得以再來。從會幕的時代起，神的事奉就需要資源來維繫與美化——百姓帶來奉獻支持曠野中的會幕，美化與維繫這兩樣並行的需要，至今對每一個新興的群體仍然至關重要。 The ultimate purpose of all Avodah is to bring about the final redemption — Geula (גְּאוּלָה). True service is selfless: we work not for personal gain, but to help others and to bring merit to the Jewish people, that Yeshua may return. From the days of the Tabernacle, the service of God required resources to sustain and beautify it — the people brought offerings to support the Tent of Meeting in the wilderness. Those twin needs, beautification and sustenance, remain vital for every emerging community today.

吉波拉教師把這個原則落到當下：當你在首爾、釜山、濟州島建立彌賽亞群體時，你需要 Avodah。你需要學會一起工作、彼此幫助、支持你的領袖。當有一天你擁有自己的妥拉捲軸，你需要齊心建造聖櫃、學習如何使用它——你永遠不能用手直接觸摸經卷，這正是它能流傳千年的原因。它不是普通的紙，而是經過反覆查核、確保毫無錯誤的聖物。所以你必須一起工作，必須學會彼此相愛——無論彼此有怎樣的瑕疵與皺紋。 Morah Giborah brings the principle into the present: as you build Messianic communities in Seoul, Busan, or Jeju Island, you will need Avodah. You will need to learn to work together, to help one another, to support your leaders. When one day you have your own Torah scroll, you will need to build an ark together and learn to handle it — you never lay bare hands on the scroll, which is exactly why it has endured for centuries. It is no ordinary paper but a holy thing, checked and re-checked so that not one error remains. So you must work together; you must learn to love one another — no matter the spot or the wrinkle.

這正是第二聖殿被毀時的教訓。當聖殿的事奉 (Avodah) 面臨終結，賢哲們必須徹底地轉向心的事奉 (Avodah SheBalev)。這個觀念經過數千年的精煉，至今仍在進行中，要等彌賽亞來臨才會完全。因為聖殿真正的問題是什麼？是彼此之間無端的仇恨——撒都該人內鬥、法利賽人內鬥，各派系決意不肯一同工作。事奉之所以失敗，從來不是因為祭物不夠，而是因為愛不夠。 This is the lesson of the Second Temple's destruction. When the Temple's Avodah faced its end, the sages had to turn radically toward Avodah SheBalev — the service of the heart and mind. That idea has been refined over millennia, remains in process today, and will be perfected only when Mashiach comes. For what was the true problem in the Temple? Baseless hatred of one another — Sadducees feuding among themselves, Pharisees among themselves, the factions resolved not to work together. Service fails not for lack of sacrifice, but for lack of love.

六、事奉的事奉 ── 喜樂中的勞苦VI. The Service of Service — Labor Carried in Joy

教師最後回到那個奇特的詞組：事奉的事奉 (Avodat Avodah)——「服務的服務」。這指的是利未人那份支持另一份服務的沉重工作。Targum Yonatan 解釋說，這是利未人在會幕四圍的守衛之職；拉什 (Rashi) 則說，這指利未人在會幕中的音樂——他們奏樂、歌唱，伴隨祭司 (Kohanim) 的獻祭。大衛在《詩篇》100:5 說：「你們當樂意事奉耶和華。」獻祭從來不是默然進行的——它伴隨著豎琴與打擊樂，伴隨著讚美的歌聲。 The teacher returns at last to that curious phrase: Avodat Avodah — “the service of service.” It names the heavy labor of the Levites that supported another service. Targum Yonatan reads it as the Levites' guard duty around the Mishkan; Rashi reads it as the Levites' music — they played and sang to accompany the sacrifice of the Kohanim. David says in Psalm 100, “Serve the LORD with gladness.” The offering was never made in silence — it was carried on harp and percussion, on the voice of praise.

拉夫·納坦松 (Rav Nathanson) 在此給出一個深刻的洞見：祭司所行的 Avodah，象徵實際遵行誡命——那份往往沉重、耗時、講究分毫不差的勞苦；而利未人的音樂，象徵那必須伴隨我們遵行的喜樂與熱忱。重點正在這裡：無論誡命多麼艱難、多麼磨人，我們都要帶著真實的喜樂去遵行。而這，恰恰是最難的部分。 Rav Nathanson offers a deep insight here: the Avodah performed by the Kohanim symbolizes the actual performance of the mitzvot — the often burdensome, time-consuming, exacting effort of meticulous observance; while the Levites' music symbolizes the joy and enthusiasm that must accompany that observance. And that is the point precisely: however hard, however grinding the commandment, we are to do it with genuine joy. Which is the hardest part of all.

納坦松說：人往往容易在「較容易認同」的誡命上找到意義——譬如贖罪日 (Yom Kippur) 的禁食，雖是極大的身體挑戰，但其宗教意義一目了然；反倒是節期 (Yom Tov) 的歡宴，享受美食固然容易，卻難以把那份歡愉灌注以應有的屬靈意義。於是，那「喜樂」的元素——利未人的音樂——本身就構成一份 Avodah：一份妥拉向你我每一個人提出的、艱難的挑戰。在最枯燥的本分裡仍然喜樂，這本身就是事奉。 Nathanson notes that people more easily find meaning in the “easier-to-identify-with” mitzvot — the fast of Yom Kippur, though a great physical challenge, carries an obvious religious significance; whereas the feasting of a Yom Tov is easy to enjoy yet hard to infuse with its proper spiritual meaning. So the element of joy itself — the Levites' music — constitutes an Avodah of its own: a difficult challenge the Torah sets before every one of us. To remain joyful in the dullest of duties is, in itself, service.

七、結語 ── 你的生命就是敬拜VII. Closing — Your Whole Life Is Worship

於是這整場哈夫達拉的教導，在這裡匯成一句話：工作、敬拜、服務，原是三股纏在一起的線。我們生命的主要目的，就是在敬拜中事奉哈舍姆、在服務中事奉眾人、在我們的本職呼召中事奉神。Avodah 意味著——每一天、每一個時辰、每一個當下，你我都在為祂而活。 And so the whole of this Havdalah teaching gathers into a single sentence: work, worship, and service are three strands woven into one. The chief end of our lives is to serve Hashem in worship, to serve others in service, and to serve God in the calling of our work. Avodah means that every day, every hour, every moment, you and I are living for Him.

無論我們是宣教士還是水管工，無論我們是寫一本書還是修剪草坪、把割下的草收攏成堆——我們所做的一切，都是為了哈舍姆的榮耀。這改變了一切。當你知道、也明白你是為誰而做、靠誰的力量而做時，你生命中就沒有一項任務太艱難、太卑微。每一份 Avodah 都是敬拜；你的服務與工作就是敬拜；你的生命，就是敬拜。 Whether we are missionaries or plumbers, whether we write a book or mow the lawn and rake the cut grass into piles — whatever we do, we do for the glory of Hashem. This changes everything. When you know and understand for whom you are doing it, and in whose strength, no task in your life is too hard or too menial. Every bit of Avodah is worship; your service and your work are worship; your life is worship.

教師以民數記裡那段意外的祝福作結——巴蘭 (Bil'am) 本要咒詛以色列，神卻使他口中只能流出祝福：「雅各啊，你的帳棚何等華美！以色列啊，你的居所何等佳美！」（民數記 24:5）。願這也成為我們此刻的禱告：願我們每日的勞苦——無論大小——都像那些華美的帳棚一樣，在神眼中成為佳美的敬拜。阿們，阿們，阿們。 The teacher closes with that unexpected blessing from Numbers — Bil'am set out to curse Israel, yet God let only blessing pour from his mouth: “How lovely are your tents, O Jacob, your dwelling places, O Israel!” (Numbers 24:5). May this be our prayer now: that our daily labor — great and small alike — would become, like those lovely tents, a beautiful worship in the eyes of God. Amen, amen, amen.

「你生命中沒有一項任務太艱難、太卑微——只要你知道你是為誰而做，又是靠誰的力量去做。每一份事奉 (Avodah) 都是敬拜。你的服務與工作就是敬拜。你的生命，就是敬拜。Ma tovu ohalecha Yaakov——雅各啊，你的帳棚何等華美。阿們。」 “No task in your life is too hard or too menial — when you know for whom you are doing it, and in whose strength. Every bit of Avodah is worship. Your service and your work is worship. And your life is worship. Ma tovu ohalecha Yaakov — how lovely are your tents, O Jacob. Amen.”
