## 月亮與太陽之戰：沙皮拉拉比的普珥節末世預言
## War of Moon and Sun: Rabbi Shapira's Purim End-Times Prophecy

### One-Sentence Thesis
Rabbi Shapira claims that current global events, particularly the October 7th attacks and the ongoing conflict involving Iran, are a divinely revealed "code" within Isaiah 24, signifying the "beginning of contraction" that culminates in a "war between the moon and the sun" (Islam and Christianity) at Purim, leading to the collapse of evil forces and the establishment of a "holy alliance" in preparation for the Messiah's imminent arrival.

### Argument Map
1.  Rabbi Shapira received a supernatural revelation from God while experiencing a severe illness, instructing him to revisit Isaiah 24 and his book *New Hamas*.
2.  God revealed that a previously "missed" prophetic connection exists between October 7th, current events with Iran, and the coming of the Messiah, encoded in Isaiah 24.
3.  Isaiah 24 describes the earth's collapse and disintegration, using the Hebrew word *Hitpo'rer* (崩解、瓦解).
4.  The word *Hitpo'rer* contains the root *Pur*, which is the origin of the word Purim (普珥節).
5.  This connection reveals that the "ultimate great collapse" and the "final battle" will begin on the "land where Purim took place" and will be activated on the "day of Purim."
6.  Isaiah 24:20 describes the earth shaking "like a drunkard" (*No'a Tano'a*, *Hitno'deda*), signifying profound global turmoil and societal breakdown, which has been activated again on Purim.
7.  The first phase of this prophetic unfolding (Isaiah 24:21) involves God punishing "the host of heaven on high, and the kings of the earth on the earth," which is "exactly what is happening now" with evil forces being punished on Earth due to a heavenly war.
8.  This punishment involves gathering them in a "dungeon" (*Bor*, 坑), like captives, who will be remembered, released, and delivered "after many days" (Isaiah 24:22).
9.  This gathering in the *Bor* is a "direct portrayal of October 7th," which he describes in his book *New Hamas* as the "beginning of the contraction."
10. This "contraction" will reach its "final stage at Purim," which is when the war began.
11. The next phase (Isaiah 24:23) states, "Then the moon will be disgraced, and the sun put to shame," because *Adonai Tzeva'ot* (Lord of Hosts) will reign on Mount Zion.
12. Drawing on *Rashi* and *Abarbanel*, the "moon" represents Islam/Muslim nations (Ishmael), and the "sun" represents Christian nations.
13. Therefore, the current war is a "war between the moon and the sun," i.e., between Islam and Christianity, which will lead to both groups being "disgraced" and "confused."
14. The Hebrew word *V'hafra ha'levanah* (月亮將蒙羞 - the moon will be disgraced) is linked to the root *Hafar* (挖掘 - to dig).
15. This implies that *Levanah* (the moon, representing Islam) is "digging tunnels" (*Hafar*), connecting to the "pits and dungeons" (*Bor*) mentioned earlier.
16. Those who oppose Jacob/Israel (identified as many Muslims and Christians) are "digging their own graves" and will enter an "empty deep pit" (*Bor*) devoid of heavenly glory, filled only with "snakes and scorpions."
17. The current "unholy alliance" (e.g., Lebanon, Iran) is being broken, and these nations will fall.
18. This collapse of *Klipa* (殼 - shell/evil forces) and *Sitra Achra* (邪惡勢力 - forces of evil), specifically the "collapse of Edom," will lead to the immediate establishment of a "holy alliance."
19. He issues an urgent call for individuals to "turn to the God of Israel, the God of Jacob" and join this "holy alliance," preparing for the month of Nisan, as the Messiah's coming is imminent.

### Key Hebrew Terms
*   **Hitpo'rer** (התפורר): "To disintegrate, to fall apart, to collapse."
    *   *Why it matters*: It's the central word describing the world's current state of breakdown, and Rabbi Shapira finds the root *Pur* (普珥節) within it, linking the collapse to Purim.
*   **Pur** (פור): "Pur" (as in Purim).
    *   *Why it matters*: Rabbi Shapira identifies this as a "code" within *Hitpo'rer*, connecting the global collapse and final battle to the Festival of Purim and the land where it took place.
*   **No'a Tano'a** (נוע תנוע): "To reel to and fro, to sway."
    *   *Why it matters*: Describes the earth's instability, likened to a drunkard, signifying the profound global shaking and lack of stability.
*   **Hitno'deda** (התנודדה): "To sway constantly, to swing."
    *   *Why it matters*: Reinforces the image of continuous, violent shaking and instability of the earth and nations.
*   **Bor** (בור): "Pit, dungeon, cistern."
    *   *Why it matters*: Used in Isaiah 24:22 for where the kings/forces are gathered. Rabbi Shapira connects this to the "tunnels" and the October 7th events, and later to the "empty deep pit" for those who oppose Israel.
*   **V'hafra ha'levanah** (וחפרה הלבנה): "And the moon will be disgraced/put to shame."
    *   *Why it matters*: This phrase from Isaiah 24:23 is central to his interpretation of the "war between the moon and the sun." He derives *Hafar* (to dig) from *Hafra*, linking the moon (Islam) to tunnel digging.
*   **Hafar** (חפר): "To dig."
    *   *Why it matters*: Rabbi Shapira connects this root, derived from *Hafra* in *V'hafra ha'levanah*, to the digging of tunnels by *Levanah* (Islam), thereby linking the moon's disgrace to the actions of those who dig tunnels (e.g., Hamas).
*   **Levanah** (לבנה): "Moon."
    *   *Why it matters*: Interpreted as representing Islam/Muslim nations, a key player in the prophesied "war between the moon and the sun."
*   **Adonai Tzeva'ot** (אדוני צבאות): "Lord of Hosts."
    *   *Why it matters*: Emphasizes God's sovereignty and power as the one who will reign on Mount Zion after the global turmoil, bringing about the ultimate victory.
*   **Klipa** (קליפה): "Shell, husk."
    *   *Why it matters*: In Kabbalistic thought, *Klipa* refers to forces of evil or impurity that conceal divine light. Rabbi Shapira uses it to describe the "evil forces" that will completely collapse.
*   **Sitra Achra** (סטרא אחרא): "The Other Side."
    *   *Why it matters*: An Aramaic term from Kabbalah referring to the realm of evil, the demonic, or forces opposed to holiness. Rabbi Shapira uses it to denote the "collapse of the forces of evil."
*   **Resh Peh Het** (ר פ ח): These are Hebrew letters (ר פ ח).
    *   *Why it matters*: Rabbi Shapira states October 7th is related to these letters, which represent the "redeemed sparks" or the "remnant." This is a form of esoteric interpretation, likely Kabbalistic, though he doesn't explicitly state the numerical value or full word.

### A Word on Discernment
Rabbi Shapira's teaching employs a method of biblical interpretation known as *drash* (דרש), which is homiletical, interpretive, and often allegorical, rather than *peshat* (פשט), the plain or literal meaning of the text. While *drash* is a legitimate and rich tradition within Jewish exegesis, particularly in Midrash and Kabbalah, it's crucial for the article author and readers to understand this distinction for intellectual honesty.

Several key claims fall into the *drash* category:
*   **The "Pur" code in "Hitpo'rer"**: While *Pur* is a root that can be found in *Hitpo'rer*, connecting it directly and prophetically to the Festival of Purim as the precise timing or catalyst for the "final battle" is a homiletical interpretation, not a literal reading of Isaiah 24. The plain meaning of *Hitpo'rer* is simply "to fall apart."
*   **"Hafar" from "Hafra"**: Deriving the meaning "to dig" (*Hafar*) from *V'hafra ha'levanah* (the moon will be disgraced) to specifically link *Levanah* (the moon/Islam) to the digging of tunnels is a highly interpretive and creative linguistic connection. The verb *Hafar* (to dig) and *Hafra* (from *ḥāpar*, to be ashamed/disgraced) are distinct Hebrew roots, though they share some letters. This is a classic example of *drash* where a word's sound or partial root is used to draw a deeper, often allegorical, meaning.
*   **Moon = Islam, Sun = Christianity**: While *Rashi* and *Abarbanel* are respected commentators, their interpretations of the moon and sun in Isaiah 24:23 as specific religious groups (Ishmael/Muslims and Christians) are allegorical and midrashic, not the *peshat* of the text. The plain sense of the verse speaks of celestial bodies being shamed before God's glory. Rabbi Shapira adopts and extends this *drash* to current geopolitical conflicts.
*   **October 7th as Isaiah 24:22**: Connecting the gathering of captives in the "dungeon" (*Bor*) in Isaiah 24:22 directly and exclusively to the October 7th attacks is a specific application of prophecy, which, while compelling to some, is an interpretive leap rather than a literal fulfillment universally accepted by all biblical scholars.
*   **"Resh Peh Het" and "redeemed sparks"**: This is an esoteric interpretation, likely Kabbalistic, linking specific Hebrew letters to a concept without providing a clear linguistic or contextual basis from the *peshat* of Isaiah 24.

The article should clarify that these interpretations, while deeply meaningful within Rabbi Shapira's Messianic Jewish framework, represent a particular hermeneutical approach that goes beyond the literal meaning of the text. This allows readers to appreciate the depth of his teaching while exercising intellectual honesty and critical discernment regarding the nature of the prophetic claims.
