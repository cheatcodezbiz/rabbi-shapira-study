Here is a structured analysis of Rabbi Dr. Itzhak Shapira's teaching, designed for an article author writing a bilingual study piece for Chinese Christians:

## 1. Title

**末世的預兆：從以色列的空戰到彌賽亞的來臨**
**Signs of the End Times: From Israel's Air Combat to the Coming of the Messiah**

## 2. One-sentence thesis

Rabbi Shapira argues that a recent military engagement involving an Israeli F-35 "Adir" jet shooting down an Iranian jet, coupled with the Hebrew meaning of "Adir" and its biblical connections, serves as a divinely ordained sign pointing to the imminent arrival of the Messiah and the unfolding of end-time prophecies.

## 3. Argument map

1.  **The Prophetic Urgency:** Rabbi Shapira begins by stating the profound significance of recent events, emphasizing that they are "astonishing, amazing, epic, and prophetic," indicating we are in the "days of the imminent coming of the Messiah."
2.  **The F-35 "Adir" Incident:** He highlights a specific news event on August 10, 2024 (or shortly thereafter, as the teaching refers to a recent event), where an Israeli F-35 fighter jet, named "Adir," shot down an Iranian Yak-130 over Tehran. This is noted as a historic first for the F-35.
3.  **The Significance of "Adir":** The rabbi connects the name of the aircraft, "Adir," to its Hebrew meaning: "Mighty One" or "Powerful One." This word is also found in biblical prophecy.
4.  **Biblical Prophecy - Isaiah 10:34:** He links the event to Isaiah 10:34, which speaks of the "forest of Lebanon" being "felled by the Mighty One" (Hebrew: *Adir*). This verse is interpreted as a prophecy of the downfall of spiritual enemies.
5.  **Biblical Prophecy - Isaiah 11:1:** The rabbi then connects Isaiah 10:34 to Isaiah 11:1, which speaks of a "shoot" from the "stump of Jesse" and a "Branch" that will bear fruit, symbolizing the Messiah.
6.  **The Dual Meaning of "Adir" and "Adir'ut":** He expands on the meaning of *Adir*, introducing *Adir'ut* (holiness of holinesses), representing the inner light of God that shatters evil forces (*Klipot*) and ushers in the Messiah's light.
7.  **Spiritual Warfare and the Fall of Nations:** The rabbi explains that the current conflicts (e.g., with Lebanon/Hezbollah, Iran) represent the "Klipot" (evil spiritual forces) and the "outer shell" of evil. The fall of these external forces precedes the ultimate defeat of Satan.
8.  **The Principle of Succession:** He introduces a key principle: "When one force falls, another force rises." This applies to nations and the spiritual powers behind them.
9.  **The Revelation of "Modia" and Mordechai:** The rabbi then shifts to a revelation connected to the weekly Torah portion (Parshat Bo), specifically Exodus 30:23 concerning the sacred anointing oil. He identifies the Hebrew word *Modia* within the spice ingredients.
10. **"Modia" as Mordechai:** He explains that *Modia* in Aramaic is equivalent to Mordechai. This connection is made through the interpretation of *Modia* as the "primary spice" (myrrh), symbolizing the Messiah (*Mashiach*).
11. **Mordechai as a Symbol of the Messiah:** Mordechai, derived from *Modia*, represents the Messiah's level, embodying beautiful fragrance, prayer, and divine judgment. He is contrasted with Amalek, representing laxity in prayer.
12. **The Imminent Arrival:** The rabbi concludes that the F-35 "Adir" striking Lebanon/Iran is a sign that Mordechai (symbolizing the Messiah) is about to appear, signaling the final confrontation and the coming of the Messiah.

## 4. All scriptural references

*   **Isaiah 10:34:** "The branches of the forest of Lebanon shall be felled by a mighty one, and by the hand of a powerful one." (Hebrew: וְגָרַע שְׂדֵי הַלְּבָנוֹן בְּאֵימָה וְהַיְתֶרֶת בְּאַדִּירִים יִכָּרֵתוּ - *və-gāraʿ śəḏê halləḇānwōn bə-’êymāh wə-hayyəṯereṯ bə-’aḏirîm yiḵārēṯū*)
    *   **Highlights:** "Mighty one" (*Adir*).
    *   **Meaning:** The rabbi interprets this as a prophecy of divine judgment against enemies, specifically linking "Adir" to the F-35 aircraft.
*   **Isaiah 11:1:** "A shoot will come up from the stump of Jesse; from his roots a Branch will bear fruit." (Hebrew: וְיָצָא חֹטֶר מִגֵּזַע יִשַׁי וְנֵצֶר מִשָּׁרָשָׁיו יִפְרֶה - *wə-yāṣā’ ḥōṭer mi-gēzaʿ yišay wə-neṣer miš-šōrāšāw yip̄rēh*)
    *   **Highlights:** "Shoot" (*ḥōṭer*), "stump" (*gēzaʿ*), "roots" (*šōrāšāw*), "Branch" (*neṣer*).
    *   **Meaning:** This verse is presented as the prophetic continuation of Isaiah 10:34, directly pointing to the Messiah (the Branch from Jesse's lineage).
*   **Exodus 30:23:** "Take the finest spices: 500 shekels of myrrh, half that amount, 250 shekels, of fragrant cinnamon..." (Hebrew: לַקַּח לְךָ סַמִּים טוֹבִים מָר דְּרוֹר חֲמֵשׁ מֵאוֹת וְקַנְּמוֹן בֶּשֶׂם חֲמִשִּׁים וּמָאתַיִם וְקָנֶה הַבֹּשֶׂם מָאתַיִם וַחֲמִשָּׁה - *laqqaḥ ləḵā sammîm ṭwôḇîm mār ḏərōr ḥamēš mē’ōṯ wə-qannəmôn beśem ḥamîššîm ûmā’ṯayim wə-qāneh habbōśem mā’ṯayim wa-ḥamîššāh*)
    *   **Highlights:** The rabbi focuses on the word for myrrh (*mār ḏərōr*), and the derived term *Modia*.
    *   **Meaning:** This verse is used to introduce the concept of *Modia* as a significant spice, linked to the Messiah.
*   **Leviticus 25:10:** "Consecrate the fiftieth year and proclaim liberty throughout the land to all its inhabitants. It shall be a jubilee for you..." (Hebrew: וְקִדַּשְׁתֶּם אֶת שְׁנַת הַחֲמִשִּׁים שָׁנָה וּקְרָאתֶם דְּרוֹר בָּאָרֶץ לְכָל יֹשְׁבֶיהָ יוֹבֵל הִיא תִהְיֶה לָכֶם - *wə-qiddašṯem ’eṯ šənaṯ haḥamîššîm šānāh ûqərāṯem ḏərōr bā’āreṣ ləḵol yōšəḇêhā yōḇēl hî’ ṯihəyeh lāḵem*)
    *   **Highlights:** "Liberty" (*ḏərōr*).
    *   **Meaning:** The rabbi connects *Deror* (liberty) to *Modia*, suggesting a deeper symbolic link related to redemption.

## 5. Key Hebrew terms

*   **שָׁלוֹם (Shalom):** Peace.
    *   **Gloss:** A greeting and a state of well-being, wholeness, and completeness.
    *   **Why it matters:** Used as a standard greeting, establishing a tone of blessing and peace at the beginning of the teaching.
*   **אָדִיר (Adir):** Mighty One, Powerful One, Great.
    *   **Gloss:** A term signifying strength, power, and majesty.
    *   **Why it matters:** This is the central term. It is the name of the F-35 aircraft and is directly linked to Isaiah 10:34, signifying divine power and judgment. Its use in a military context is seen as a prophetic sign.
*   **אֲדִירוּת (Adir'ut):** Holiness of holinesses, divine radiance, inner light.
    *   **Gloss:** Derived from *Adir*, it refers to a profound spiritual quality, the inner light within holiness that overcomes evil.
    *   **Why it matters:** It expands the meaning of *Adir* beyond mere physical power to spiritual power, connecting it to the Messiah's redemptive work.
*   **קְלִיפּוֹת (Klipot):** Husks, shells; evil spiritual forces.
    *   **Gloss:** In Kabbalistic thought, these are the negative spiritual forces or shells that conceal divine light and represent evil.
    *   **Why it matters:** The rabbi uses this term to describe the spiritual enemies being confronted, which will be shattered by the power of *Adir'ut*.
*   **מוֹדִיעַ (Modia):** A specific spice mentioned in Exodus 30:23 (myrrh).
    *   **Gloss:** A Hebrew term for a specific spice used in the holy anointing oil.
    *   **Why it matters:** The rabbi finds a hidden name within this spice, linking it to Mordechai and, by extension, the Messiah.
*   **מוֹר דְּרוֹר (Mor Deror):** Pure myrrh.
    *   **Gloss:** The specific spice mentioned in Exodus 30:23, translated as "pure myrrh."
    *   **Why it matters:** The rabbi connects the word *Deror* (liberty) within this phrase to the concept of redemption and the Messiah.
*   **מָּרְדְּכַי (Mordechai):** Mordecai (Biblical figure).
    *   **Gloss:** The Jewish hero from the Book of Esther who saved his people.
    *   **Why it matters:** The rabbi equates *Modia* with Mordechai, presenting Mordechai as a prefiguration or symbol of the Messiah.
*   **מָּשִׁיחַ (Mashiach):** Messiah.
    *   **Gloss:** The Anointed One, the promised redeemer.
    *   **Why it matters:** The entire teaching culminates in the anticipation of the Messiah's imminent arrival, with the events and names discussed serving as signs.
*   **דְּרוֹר (Deror):** Liberty, freedom.
    *   **Gloss:** The Hebrew word for freedom, particularly in the context of release or proclamation.
    *   **Why it matters:** Linked to Leviticus 25:10 (the Jubilee year) and the spice *Mor Deror*, it signifies the redemptive aspect of the Messiah's coming.

## 6. Key gematria / numerical claims

The rabbi does not explicitly mention gematria (numerical value of Hebrew letters) in this teaching. However, the date "August 10, 2024" is presented as a significant point of revelation. The number "35" in "F-35" is also implicitly highlighted in relation to the F-35's historical first.

## 8. What this means for Christians who love Israel

For Christians who love Israel, Rabbi Shapira's message offers a profound theological lens through which to interpret current geopolitical events. He presents the conflict and military actions not merely as political or strategic maneuvers, but as divinely orchestrated signs pointing towards the fulfillment of biblical prophecy. The specific incident involving the F-35 "Adir" jet is interpreted as a direct manifestation of God's power and judgment, echoing ancient prophetic declarations. This perspective encourages believers to see the hand of God at work in the affairs of nations, particularly in relation to Israel, and to understand these events as precursors to the ultimate redemption.

This teaching calls for a heightened spiritual awareness and a deeper engagement with biblical prophecy. By connecting the name "Adir" (Mighty One) to both the military aircraft and the prophetic declarations in Isaiah, Rabbi Shapira invites believers to recognize God's sovereignty over earthly powers and His active involvement in history. For Christians who hold the Hebrew Scriptures in high regard and believe in the ongoing relevance of God's covenants with Israel, this message provides a framework for understanding the present as a pivotal moment in salvation history. It suggests that the current conflicts are not random but are part of a larger divine plan leading to the culmination of God's purposes.

Ultimately, Rabbi Shapira's message is an exhortation to watchfulness and readiness. The identification of "Adir" and the subsequent connection to Mordechai (as a symbol of the Messiah) and the concept of *Adir'ut* (divine radiance) highlight the transition from judgment against evil to the manifestation of God's redemptive power. For Christians who love Israel, this means embracing a prophetic worldview that sees current events as unfolding signs of the Messiah's imminent return, urging them to live with faith, discernment, and anticipation for the full realization of God's kingdom.

## 9. A word on discernment

Rabbi Shapira's teaching is rich with interpretation that blends current events with biblical prophecy and Jewish mystical thought. It is important for the reader to discern the nature of his claims.

*   **Prophetic Interpretation:** The core of the teaching is the interpretation of a recent military event as a direct sign of end-time prophecy. While the rabbi presents this as a revelation from God, this is a form of *drash* (homiletical interpretation) that connects contemporary events to prophetic texts. The specific timing of the revelation on August 10, 2024, and its subsequent confirmation by the F-35 incident, is presented as a direct divine confirmation.
*   **Linguistic and Symbolic Connections:** The rabbi draws significant meaning from the Hebrew word "Adir" and its connection to the F-35 aircraft's name. He also links the spice *Modia* to Mordechai and the Messiah. These are symbolic and exegetical connections, rooted in Jewish tradition and mystical interpretation, rather than literal historical or scientific claims.
*   **Spiritual Warfare:** The discussion of *Klipot* and spiritual authorities falls within the realm of spiritual discernment and understanding of unseen spiritual realities, a common theme in many theological traditions, including Messianic Judaism.
*   **The Role of Current Events:** The rabbi's approach is to find prophetic significance in current events. While the events themselves are factual (e.g., the F-35 incident), their interpretation as specific prophetic signs is a matter of theological interpretation and revelation, not objective historical fact.

Readers should approach these interpretations with a spirit of prayerful discernment, comparing them with Scripture and seeking wisdom from the Holy Spirit, while appreciating the rabbi's passion and conviction.
