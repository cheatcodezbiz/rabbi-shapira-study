## 1. Title

**末日預言的迴響：從以東的戰爭到彌賽亞的來臨**
**Echoes of End-Times Prophecy: From the War of Edom to the Coming of the Messiah**

## 2. One-sentence thesis

Rabbi Shapira argues that current geopolitical events, particularly the conflict involving Iran and its regional implications, are direct fulfillments of ancient Jewish prophecies, signaling the imminent arrival of the Messiah and a global spiritual awakening.

## 3. Argument map

1.  **Introduction and Premise:** The rabbi begins by acknowledging the numerous inquiries he has received following his previous teachings on prophetic matters, indicating a strong interest in understanding current events through a prophetic lens. He states his intention to guide listeners on what to pay attention to now, as the path to the Messiah's coming is becoming clear.
2.  **The Prophecy of Edom and Iran:** He references a "shocking prophecy" from his prior work, "The Wheel Lines of Edom," which he claims is now being fulfilled in our generation. This prophecy concerns a final war between Iran (Persia) and Edom.
3.  **Identifying Edom:** The rabbi addresses the common question of whether America is Edom. He suggests that while America currently supports Israel, the coming war will cause significant internal division and anti-Semitic sentiment within the US. He posits that Donald Trump is a pawn in God's larger plan leading to the Messiah's arrival, and that America will become more "pro-Jewish."
4.  **Confirmation and Timing:** He asserts that this prophecy was confirmed on the Sabbath before Purim, linking it to the beginning of the end times and the final stages of Gog and Magog.
5.  **Rabbinic Teaching on End Times:** He cites a specific teaching from "our Rabbi's teachings," Chapter 36, which describes the events leading up to the Messiah's appearance:
    *   Kings of nations provoke each other.
    *   The King of Persia (Iran) provokes an Arab king (Saudi Arabia).
    *   Arab kings seek advice from America (Edom).
    *   The "cruel King of Persia" will attempt to destroy the world.
6.  **Current Events as Fulfillment:** The rabbi then connects these prophetic elements to recent geopolitical events:
    *   The conflict between Israel and its adversaries, with Iran playing a key role.
    *   The involvement of Saudi Arabia in advising the US.
    *   The US (Edom) advising Israel.
    *   The perceived intention of Iran to "destroy the entire world" and instigate World War III.
7.  **The Role of the US and Saudi Arabia:** He highlights a "shocking fact": Saudi Arabia, through Prince Salman, advised Donald Trump to strike Iran, which then influenced Israel's actions. This aligns with the prophecy of Arab kings advising Edom.
8.  **The Escalation to World War III:** The rabbi emphasizes that Iran's actions, including attacks on other Arab nations and Cyprus, are creating chaos intended to lead to World War III. He describes the nations and Israel experiencing fear and confusion.
9.  **Divine Intervention and Redemption:** He offers comfort, stating that God's actions are for Israel's benefit and that this is the "time of your redemption."
10. **Spiritual Dynamics of Decline and Rise:** He introduces a principle of redemption: as one power declines, another rises. He sees the power behind Hamas and Iran rising.
11. **The "Time of Jacob's Trouble":** He declares that we are in the "final phase" of "Jacob's trouble," which includes the war of Gog and Magog.
12. **Deuteronomy 4:30 and "Tzarah":** He connects this to Deuteronomy 4:30, focusing on the Hebrew word "tzarah" (צָרָה), meaning distress or suffering, and its root "tzar" (צר). He interprets this as a sign that in the latter days, people will turn to fear God.
13. **Mass Conversion and Spiritual Revival:** He predicts a significant sign of the end times will be widespread conversion and revival, with people turning to the God of Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob. He sees this as a spiritual battle between Satan's armies and God's army.

## 5. Key Hebrew terms

*   **Shalom (沙龍):** Transliteration of the Hebrew word for "peace" or "greeting." Used here as a standard greeting.
    *   **Why it matters:** Establishes a connection to Jewish tradition and a desire for peace, even amidst discussions of conflict.
*   **Rabbi (拉比):** Transliteration of the Hebrew word for "teacher" or "master." Used to refer to respected Jewish sages and authorities.
    *   **Why it matters:** Grounds the teaching within Jewish tradition and highlights the rabbi's authority as an interpreter of scripture and tradition.
*   **Edom (以東):** The biblical name for the descendants of Esau, Jacob's brother. In Jewish tradition, Edom is often symbolically associated with nations that have historically opposed Israel, particularly Rome and later Western powers.
    *   **Why it matters:** Crucial for the rabbi's interpretation of current geopolitical events, linking them to ancient prophetic narratives. His identification of America with Edom is a significant interpretive move.
*   **Tzarah (צָרָה):** Hebrew word meaning "distress," "suffering," "tribulation," or "anguish."
    *   **Why it matters:** The rabbi specifically highlights this word from Deuteronomy 4:30 to describe the "time of Jacob's trouble" and connects it to the current global situation. It signifies a period of intense hardship preceding redemption.
*   **Acharit Hayamim (אַחֲרִית הַיָּמִים):** Hebrew phrase meaning "the end of days" or "latter days."
    *   **Why it matters:** Directly relates to the eschatological focus of the teaching, indicating that the events being discussed are signs of the end times.
*   **Gog and Magog (哥哥和馬格):** Refers to the biblical figures and nations prophesied to wage a final war against Israel in the end times (Ezekiel 38-39).
    *   **Why it matters:** Identifies the current geopolitical conflicts as part of a larger, divinely ordained eschatological battle.

Finally, the rabbi's call for a "mass conversion and revival" turning to the God of Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob is a powerful message for Christians. It suggests that the end times will be characterized by a profound spiritual awakening, drawing people from all nations to the God of Israel. This aligns with the Great Commission and the Christian hope for a redeemed humanity. For those who love Israel, this message offers encouragement that God's plan encompasses not only the physical and spiritual restoration of His people but also a global turning towards Him, a testament to His faithfulness and ultimate sovereignty.

## 9. A word on discernment

Rabbi Shapira's teaching is rich with prophetic interpretation, drawing heavily on rabbinic tradition and applying it to current events. It is important for readers to approach such interpretations with discernment.

*   **Identification of "Edom":** The rabbi's identification of modern nations, particularly the United States, with biblical entities like "Edom" is a form of **midrash** (interpretive commentary) and **drash** (homiletical exposition). While rooted in traditional Jewish thought, the specific application to contemporary nations is an interpretive leap that requires careful consideration. It is not a literal, universally agreed-upon identification but rather an allegorical or symbolic application.
*   **Predictive Prophecy:** While the rabbi claims prophecies are being fulfilled, the specific timing and exact nature of future events remain subject to interpretation. His pronouncements about "World War III" and the "coming of the Messiah" are based on his understanding of prophetic patterns and signs, which are often open to multiple readings.
*   **Spiritual Warfare Language:** The rabbi uses strong language about "demons roaring" and "spiritual rulers." This is characteristic of spiritual warfare theology, which interprets earthly conflicts as manifestations of heavenly battles. While this can be spiritually insightful, it is important to distinguish between literal geopolitical analysis and spiritual interpretation.

Readers should engage with this material prayerfully, comparing its claims with their understanding of Scripture and seeking wisdom from the Holy Spirit, rather than accepting all interpretations as literal, historical fact. The value lies in the rabbi's insights into the spiritual significance of current events and the enduring relevance of biblical prophecy.
