## 逾越節的紅海預言：霍爾木茲、亞瑪力與彌賽亞時代的開啟
### The Passover Red Sea Prophecy: Hormuz, Amalek, and the Dawn of the Messianic Era

---

### 1. One-Sentence Thesis
Rabbi Shapira claims that the seventh day of Passover (Pesach) is a divinely appointed, optimal time for the collapse of the Iranian regime and the opening of the Strait of Hormuz, an event prophetically linked to the ancient Exodus, the defeat of Pharaoh and Amalek, and the imminent arrival of the Messianic era through the ingathering of a Gentile "Yitro remnant."

---

### 2. Argument Map

Rabbi Shapira's argument unfolds as a series of interconnected claims, drawing parallels between ancient Jewish texts and contemporary geopolitical events:

1.  **Current Geopolitical Context**: Donald Trump has set a deadline, and a significant announcement is expected.
2.  **Timing Coincidence**: This announcement coincides precisely with the seventh day of Passover (*Pesach*) in Israel.
3.  **Significance of Pesach Day 7**:
    *   It marks the transition from the intermediate days to a holy day.
    *   It is the day Jews traditionally recite scriptures about the Exodus from *Mitzrayim* (Egypt), the splitting of the Red Sea, and the opening of the "narrow place" (*Mitzrayim*).
    *   It is the day the "Song of the Sea" is read, commemorating Pharaoh's defeat and Israel's crossing.
    *   It is the day *Hallel* (Psalms 113-118), which speaks of the Red Sea, is recited.
    *   Talmudic tradition (*Pesachim* 117) teaches that this day signifies the opening of the strait and Pharaoh's drowning, representing complete redemption.
4.  **Linguistic Prophecy**: The Hebrew word *Mitzrayim* (Egypt) itself contains *Mitzah* (narrow place/strait), creating a linguistic link to the Strait of Hormuz.
5.  **Prophetic Application**: Just as the Red Sea (a "narrow place") opened and Pharaoh was defeated on Pesach Day 7, so too will the Strait of Hormuz open and the Iranian regime (modern-day Pharaoh/Amalek) begin to collapse on this day.
6.  **The Amalek Connection**: There is a deep, often misunderstood, connection between the splitting of the Red Sea and the war with Amalek, as explained by Rabbi Chaim Samuel Reggio and the *Mekhilta*.
    *   *Yitro* (Jethro) heard of both the Red Sea splitting and the war with Amalek, which prompted his journey.
    *   The war with Amalek (Iran) is now opening, directly related to the opening of the Strait of Hormuz.
7.  **The Sforno Commentary**: God's intervention against Amalek was not through overt miracles (due to Israel's sin/laxity in Torah at Rephidim) but through Moses' prayer and Joshua's battle, signifying a different mode of divine action. This contrasts with the overt miracles at the Red Sea.
    *   God revealed Himself as *YHVH Nissi* (God is my banner), the supernatural *Havaya* (One who brings all into existence), implying no favoritism, which *Yitro* understood.
8.  **The Yitro Foreshadowing**: The ultimate outcome of this end-time war (Iran/Amalek) will be the "arrival of *Yitro*."
    *   According to *Shemot Rabbah*, *Yitro* represents the "father of converts" (proselytes) who join Israel, uniting the "light of Moses" with the "light of Messiah."
    *   *Yitro* also foreshadows the "Spirit of Messiah" and the "remnant from the nations" (Gentiles) who will join the house of Israel.
9.  **Messianic Ingathering**: This war will lead to a complete separation of "sheep and goats" among the nations, with the *Yitro* remnant turning their hearts to the Jewish people and the God of Israel, leading to the coming of the Messiah's Spirit.
10. **Final Prophetic Declaration**: God is about to submerge all of Israel's enemies (Iran). If Israel believes and acts, and if Trump's actions align, the Strait of Hormuz will open, and humanity will cross into a new dimension of redemption, fulfilling Zechariah 8:23.

    *   **Hebrew Term**: **מִצְרַיִם** (*Mitzrayim*) – Egypt, literally "narrow places" or "straits." Rabbi Shapira highlights this to connect ancient Egypt with modern straits like Hormuz.
    *   **Hebrew Term**: **מֵצַר** (*Mitzah*) – Narrow place, strait. Rabbi Shapira states this term is "contained within *Mitzrayim*," emphasizing the linguistic connection to a "strait."

    *   **Hebrew Term**: **יְהוָה נִסִּי** (*YHVH Nissi*) – "The LORD is my Banner." Rabbi Shapira uses this to describe God's attribute revealed to Moses, signifying a supernatural, miraculous operation where "all things are equal" before Him.
*   **Exodus 18:1**: "**יִתְרוֹ** (*Yitro*) heard..." (referring to Jethro hearing about God's wonders).
    *   **Hebrew Term**: **יִתְרוֹ** (*Yitro*) – Jethro. Rabbi Shapira uses this figure as a key prophetic type for Gentile converts and the remnant who will join Israel in the Messianic era.
*   **Numbers 10:31**: "Moses said to Hobab [Yitro's son], 'Please do not leave us, you will be our eyes.'"
    *   **Context**: Rabbi Shapira alludes to this verse to explain *Yitro*'s role as "eyes" or "opening of the eyes" for Israel, leading to the "light of Messiah."
*   **Psalms 113-118**: The **הַלֵּל** (*Hallel*) Psalms, recited on Pesach Day 7.
    *   **Hebrew Term**: **הַלֵּל** (*Hallel*) – "Praise." These Psalms are said to speak "precisely of the formation and opening of the Red Sea."
*   **Genesis 33:20**: Jacob named an altar "**אֵל אֱלֹהֵי יִשְׂרָאֵל** (*El Elohei Yisrael*)," "God, the God of Israel."
    *   **Hebrew Term**: **אֵל אֱלֹהֵי יִשְׂרָאֵל** (*El Elohei Yisrael*) – "God, the God of Israel." Rabbi Shapira contrasts this with *YHVH Nissi*, suggesting that God shows favor to Israel when they are worthy, but *YHVH Nissi* represents a broader, supernatural, non-preferential divine action.
*   **Zechariah 8:23**: "Thus says the LORD of hosts: In those days ten men from the nations of every tongue shall take hold of the skirt of a Jew, saying, 'Let us go with you, for we have heard that God is with you.'"
    *   **Rabbi Shapira's Interpretation**: "So I say to you nations, behold, what God is about to do: He will submerge all of Israel's enemies." This is a highly interpretive application, connecting the ingathering of nations to the defeat of Israel's enemies.

### 4. Key Hebrew Terms

1.  **פֶּסַח** (*Pesach*) – **Passover**. The central holiday around which the entire prophecy revolves. Rabbi Shapira emphasizes the significance of its seventh day, linking ancient Exodus events to contemporary geopolitical predictions.
2.  **מִצְרַיִם** (*Mitzrayim*) – **Egypt**. Literally means "narrow places" or "straits." Rabbi Shapira uses this etymological connection to draw a direct parallel between the Red Sea crossing (Exodus from *Mitzrayim*) and the opening of the Strait of Hormuz.
3.  **מֵצַר** (*Mitzah*) – **Narrow place, strait**. Rabbi Shapira highlights that this term is "contained within *Mitzrayim*," reinforcing the linguistic and conceptual link between ancient Egypt and modern-day straits.
4.  **הַלֵּל** (*Hallel*) – **Praise**. Refers to Psalms 113-118, recited on special holidays, including the seventh day of Pesach. These Psalms are said to specifically recount the Red Sea event, making their recitation on this day prophetically significant for the "opening of the strait."
5.  **יִתְרוֹ** (*Yitro*) – **Jethro**. Moses' father-in-law. Rabbi Shapira presents *Yitro* as a crucial prophetic archetype, representing Gentile converts and the "remnant from the nations" who will join Israel and usher in the Messianic era after the defeat of Amalek/Iran.
6.  **אֵל אֱלֹהֵי יִשְׂרָאֵל** (*El Elohei Yisrael*) – **God, the God of Israel**. The name Jacob gave to an altar. Rabbi Shapira uses this to describe God's attribute of showing favor to Israel when they are worthy, contrasting it with *YHVH Nissi*.
7.  **יְהוָה נִסִּי** (*YHVH Nissi*) – **The LORD is my Banner**. The name Moses gave to an altar after the battle with Amalek. Rabbi Shapira interprets this as God's attribute as *Havaya* (the One who brings all into existence), operating supernaturally and miraculously, where "all things are equal" before Him, implying a universal aspect of God's power.
8.  **הֲוָיָה** (*Havaya*) – **Existence, Being**. Rabbi Shapira uses this term to describe God as "the One who brings all into existence," linking it to the supernatural and miraculous operations of *YHVH Nissi*.
9.  **הַשֵּׁם** (*Hashem*) – **The Name**. A common Jewish circumlocution for God's ineffable name (YHVH). Used by Rabbi Shapira in his closing blessing.

### 8. A Word on Discernment

It is crucial for an intellectually honest study to distinguish between *peshat* (the plain, literal meaning of a text) and *drash* (homiletical, interpretive, or allegorical exposition). Rabbi Shapira's teaching, while rich in Jewish tradition and deeply spiritual, relies heavily on *drash* to connect ancient texts to modern events.

Specifically, the following claims are examples of *drash* rather than *peshat*:
*   **Linguistic Connection of *Mitzrayim* to Hormuz**: While *Mitzrayim* can etymologically mean "narrow places," directly equating it with the Strait of Hormuz as a literal prophetic fulfillment in the context of the Red Sea splitting is a homiletical interpretation, not a literal reading of the Exodus narrative.
*   **Direct Prophetic Mapping of Pesach Day 7 Events**: Applying the specific events of the seventh day of Passover (Pharaoh's defeat, Red Sea splitting) as a direct, real-time prophetic template for the fall of the Iranian regime and the opening of the Strait of Hormuz is a highly interpretive *drash*. While the historical event is real, its direct, immediate, and specific application to current geopolitics is a homiletical extrapolation.
*   **Amalek as Iran**: Identifying modern-day Iran as the direct, literal fulfillment of "Amalek" is a common *drash* in some Jewish and Messianic Jewish circles, but it is not a *peshat* interpretation of the biblical text, which refers to a specific ancient people.
*   **Yitro as Gentile Remnant/Messiah's Spirit**: While the concept of Gentiles joining Israel is biblical (e.g., Isaiah 2:2-4, Zechariah 8:20-23), interpreting *Yitro* as a specific prophetic type for the "Spirit of Messiah" and the "remnant from the nations" who will join Israel in the end times is a homiletical expansion found in *Midrash* (like *Shemot Rabbah*), not a *peshat* reading of the Exodus narrative concerning Jethro.
*   **Interpretation of Zechariah 8:23**: Rabbi Shapira rephrases Zechariah 8:23 to state that God "will submerge all of Israel's enemies." The *peshat* of Zechariah 8:23 describes ten men from the nations taking hold of a Jew's garment, seeking God. While the verse speaks of Gentile ingathering, the Rabbi's specific interpretation of it as God submerging enemies is a significant *drash* that goes beyond the plain meaning.

Understanding these distinctions allows for an appreciation of the Rabbi's spiritual insights and the rich interpretive tradition of Judaism, while maintaining intellectual honesty regarding the literal meaning of the scriptures. It encourages listeners to engage with the message critically, discerning the homiletical applications from the plain biblical text.
