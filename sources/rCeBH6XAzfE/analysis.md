Here is a structured analysis of Rabbi Dr. Itzhak Shapira's teaching, designed for an article author writing a bilingual study piece for Chinese Christians:

## 1. Title

**末世的號角：拉比的預言性洞見** / **The Trumpet of the End Times: A Rabbi's Prophetic Insights**

## 2. One-sentence thesis

Rabbi Shapira argues that current geopolitical events, particularly the conflict involving Iran, are divinely orchestrated and will culminate in a significant prophetic fulfillment around the upcoming Passover (Pesach), mirroring the Exodus from Egypt and leading to the rebuilding of the Third Temple.

## 3. Argument map

1.  **Current Prophetic Urgency:** The "spirit of prophecy" is actively speaking through current events, as evidenced by previous prophetic pronouncements and interpretations (e.g., "New Hamas in 2023," "Fall of Edom in 2024," prophetic connections to the F-35).
2.  **The Central Question:** The primary concern is the timing of the end of current conflicts and the fall of nations like Iran.
3.  **Rabbinic Wisdom as Foundation:** The answer to this question is not new but is rooted in ancient Jewish Sages' teachings, which Rabbi Shapira aims to elucidate.
4.  **Biblical Precedent for Redemption:** The prophet Micah (7:15) foretells that future redemptions will be as miraculous as the Exodus from Egypt.
5.  **Current Conflict's Limited Duration:** Despite Iran's statements about prolonged conflict, Rabbi Shapira asserts, based on prophetic understanding, that the current war will not last another six months.
6.  **Cyclical Nature of Prophecy:** Prophecy operates in cycles, with events in the month of Nisan (the month of redemption) being particularly significant for both past and future redemptions.
7.  **The Secret of Nisan:** A crucial revelation is that Nisan is the month of redemption, when Israel's soul emerges from *Klipot* (impure shells) and enters holiness. The Hebrew word "Nisan" itself means "miracle."
8.  **Rabbinic Interpretation of Passover and Amalek:**
    *   *Ra'ay HaMelech* and *Marit HaAyin* suggest that on the eve of Passover (Pesach), Amalek will be annihilated.
    *   This annihilation is linked to the soul of Israel being saved from *Klipot*.
9.  **Imminent Collapse and Timeline:** Based on rabbinic interpretation, the collapse of the current regime (implied to be Iran and its allies) is expected on the eve of Passover. This aligns with predictions of a four-to-five-week conflict duration.
10. **The "Three and Three" Principle:** *Marit HaAyin* posits a principle where the merit of the first three stages leads to the latter three:
    *   **First Three:** Passover (soul's freedom), cutting off Amalek's descendants, eradication of Amalek.
    *   **Latter Three:** Building of the Temple, atonement and forgiveness for Israel, gathering of people, and the coming of the Messiah.
11. **Amalek's Imminent Eradication:** The speaker strongly believes Amalek's descendants will be cut off around this upcoming Passover.
12. **Nisan as the Month of Final Redemption:** The principle of redemption repeating in Nisan is reinforced, linking it to the final redemption.
13. **The Sequence of Events:** The annihilation of Amalek is a precursor to the rebuilding of the Third Temple, which will then lead to the coming of the Messiah.
14. **The Role of Adar:** The month of Adar is significant as the period when Israel's soul begins to awaken, leading to current successes. The war's start coincided with Adar, reinforcing this connection.
15. **The Significance of the Sabbath Before Passover:** This Sabbath is specifically linked to remembering Amalek and contains the "miraculous power of Passover."
16. **The War's Timing and Awakening:** The war's commencement at the start of Adar aligns with the rabbinic teaching of a great awakening in that month.
17. **The "Thirty Days" Before Passover:** This period is dedicated to studying Passover laws and is when the Holy One brings out Israel's souls from *Klipot*.
18. **Conclusion:** The fall of Iran and its regime will directly precede the rebuilding of the Third Temple, a fulfillment echoed by numerous prophets.

## 5. Key Hebrew terms

*   **Adir (阿迪爾):**
    *   **Transliteration:** *Adir*
    *   **Gloss:** Mighty, strong, great.
    *   **Why it matters:** Rabbi Shapira mentions a previous prophetic connection he made between this word and the F-35 fighter jet, indicating his method of finding prophetic significance in contemporary events through word association and numerology.
*   **Klipot (克利波特):**
    *   **Transliteration:** *Klipot* (singular: *Klipah*)
    *   **Gloss:** Shells, husks, impure or negative spiritual forces that conceal holiness.
    *   **Why it matters:** This is a central concept in Kabbalistic thought. The Rabbi uses it to describe the state of spiritual impurity from which Israel is being redeemed, particularly during Nisan and Passover. The emergence from *Klipot* signifies spiritual liberation and purification.
*   **Sitra Achra (西特拉阿赫拉):**
    *   **Transliteration:** *Sitra Achra*
    *   **Gloss:** The "other side," referring to the realm of evil or impurity, the opposite of holiness.
    *   **Why it matters:** This term, also from Kabbalistic literature, complements *Klipot*. Leaving the *Sitra Achra* signifies a decisive move towards holiness and divine favor, a core theme of the Passover redemption.
*   **Nis (尼散):**
    *   **Transliteration:** *Nis*
    *   **Gloss:** Miracle.
    *   **Why it matters:** The Rabbi explicitly states that the Hebrew month *Nisan* derives its name from this word, emphasizing that the month itself is inherently miraculous, aligning with the theme of redemption and divine intervention.
*   **Amalek (亞瑪力):**
    *   **Transliteration:** *Amalek*
    *   **Gloss:** An ancient enemy of Israel, symbolizing pure, unprovoked hatred and opposition to God's people and His plan.
    *   **Why it matters:** The Rabbi identifies the current enemies (implicitly Iran and its allies) as the modern-day descendants of Amalek. The prophesied annihilation of Amalek is a key indicator of the approaching Messianic era and the rebuilding of the Temple.
*   **Pesach (猶悅節):**
    *   **Transliteration:** *Pesach*
    *   **Gloss:** Passover.
    *   **Why it matters:** This is the focal point of the Rabbi's temporal prediction. He links the current events directly to the themes of Passover: redemption, liberation from oppression, and the emergence from spiritual impurity.
*   **Adar (亞達月):**
    *   **Transliteration:** *Adar*
    *   **Gloss:** The month preceding Nisan.
    *   **Why it matters:** The Rabbi connects the start of the current war to the beginning of Adar, interpreting this as a sign of the "awakening of Israel's soul," which he believes is a precursor to the redemptive events of Nisan.
*   **Marit HaAyin (外觀論):**
    *   **Transliteration:** *Marit HaAyin*
    *   **Gloss:** Literally "appearance of the eye," often referring to a commentary or a specific concept within Jewish thought related to outward perception or a particular interpretation. (In this context, it refers to a specific commentary or work.)
    *   **Why it matters:** This is cited as a primary source for the rabbinic interpretation linking Passover with the annihilation of Amalek and the subsequent rebuilding of the Temple.
*   **Ra'ay HaMelech (拉阿伊哈梅列赫):**
    *   **Transliteration:** *Ra'ay HaMelech*
    *   **Gloss:** Literally "the shepherd of the king" or "the vision of the king," referring to another commentary or work.
    *   **Why it matters:** This is cited alongside *Marit HaAyin* to support the idea of Amalek's annihilation on the eve of Passover.

## 9. A word on discernment

Rabbi Shapira's teaching is rich with interpretations drawn from Jewish mystical and rabbinic traditions, particularly Kabbalah (*Klipot*, *Sitra Achra*). While he grounds his argument in biblical prophecy (Micah) and historical patterns (Exodus, Nisan), much of his specific timing and interpretation relies on rabbinic commentaries (*Marit HaAyin*, *Ra'ay HaMelech*, *Chatam Sofer*) and the concept of cyclical prophecy.

The claim that the current war will end within a specific timeframe (not six months, potentially by Passover eve) and that this will directly lead to the rebuilding of the Third Temple is a form of *drash* (homiletical interpretation) and prophetic extrapolation based on rabbinic tradition and current events. It is not a literal, universally agreed-upon exegesis of scripture in the same way a historical account might be.

Therefore, while the underlying themes of redemption, divine intervention, and the importance of Israel are biblically sound, the precise timing and the specific identification of current actors with ancient enemies (Amalek) should be approached with discernment. Readers should distinguish between the foundational prophetic texts and the interpretive layers added by rabbinic tradition and contemporary application. The message is presented as an interpretation of "what the rabbis have said" rather than a direct, unambiguous biblical command or prophecy.
