## 8. What this means for Christians who love Israel

He presents a framework where current events are not random but are unfolding according to ancient prophetic timelines. The focus on the Strait of Hormuz, identified as a place of both strategic importance and spiritual warfare, suggests that believers should pay close attention to global events, particularly those involving Iran and the broader Middle East, as potential harbingers of the Messianic era. This perspective encourages a deeper engagement with biblical prophecy, urging believers to see the hand of God at work in history and to understand their role within His unfolding plan.

Furthermore, the identification of Hormuz as "Be-tzara" or "Batra," the stronghold of Satan, adds a crucial spiritual dimension. This implies that the conflicts in this region are not merely political or economic but are part of a cosmic battle between good and evil. For Christians who love Israel, this understanding calls for prayer, discernment, and a steadfast faith in God's ultimate sovereignty. It encourages them to stand with Israel, not just politically, but spiritually, recognizing that the destiny of Israel is inextricably linked to the return of the Messiah and the establishment of God's kingdom.

Ultimately, Rabbi Shapira's teaching is an exhortation to be watchful and prepared. By connecting ancient prophecies to contemporary events, he challenges believers to move beyond passive observation and to actively seek understanding, to study the Word, and to live with an awareness of the spiritual realities at play. For those who cherish Israel and anticipate the Messiah's return, this message serves as a powerful reminder that the prophecies are not distant pronouncements but are actively shaping the world around them, calling for a response of faith, prayer, and diligent study.

## 9. A word on discernment

Rabbi Shapira's teaching relies heavily on **drash** (homiletical interpretation) and the integration of ancient rabbinic commentaries with current events. While he grounds his message in biblical texts (Isaiah 34:6) and rabbinic tradition, the specific interpretations of place names (Bosra, Edom, Hormuz), the identification of "Zor" as the Antichrist, and the direct equation of the Strait of Hormuz with "Be-tzara/Batra" and Satan's stronghold are products of interpretive methods that go beyond literal exegesis.

The connection to Esther 8:1, for instance, is stated as a "decoding" without elaboration, suggesting a symbolic or numerical link rather than a direct textual one. Similarly, the interpretation of "Ula-i, Adonai, ve-teva, gadol, u-me-re-tzu-dim" as "sacrifice" and "slaughter" is a specific rabbinic understanding that may differ from other scholarly translations or interpretations of the Hebrew.

Therefore, while the message is presented as prophetic and urgent, readers should approach it with discernment, recognizing that the strength of the argument lies in the synthesis of ancient Jewish thought, biblical prophecy, and contemporary geopolitical analysis, rather than solely in a literal, historical, or universally agreed-upon exegesis. The emphasis on his books and further study indicates a desire for deeper engagement with these interpretive frameworks.
