## 呼召與爭戰：在彌賽亞的根基中合一 / The Call and the War: Unifying in Messiah's Foundations

### One-Sentence Thesis

Rabbi Shapira claims that a spiritual "war of faith" is currently dividing Christianity over support for Israel, demanding that true believers humbly recognize their Jewish roots and actively stand with the Jewish people as "Messiah's brothers" to fulfill their divine calling and prepare for the coming Jewish Kingdom.

### Argument Map

1.  **Contextual Setup:** We are in a "war of faith" (*Milchemet Emunah*) in the Middle East, paralleled by a spiritual war entering the Church, reflecting the "separation of sheep and goats" (Matthew 25:31-46).
2.  **The Crisis within Christianity:** A significant internal conflict exists among Christians regarding support for Israel and the Jewish people, with a rise of anti-Semitic rhetoric, blood libels, and conspiracy theories from self-proclaimed Christian figures.
3.  **Misinterpretation of "The Least of These":** Many Christians misinterpret Yeshua's words in Matthew 25:40 ("the least of these my brothers") to refer generically to the poor, neglecting that "my brothers" specifically refers to the Jewish people and Israel.
4.  **The True Meaning of *Vayikra* (וַיִּקְרָא):** The Torah portion *Vayikra* signifies a "holy, direct call" from God (not an accidental encounter like *Vayikar*), which is now extended to Gentiles who love the God of Israel, urging them to stand up.
5.  **The Current Spiritual Battle:** This is the time of Gog and Magog, and Christians are called to be "ground forces" in this spiritual and physical war, actively supporting Israel despite opposition.
6.  **The Humility of the Miniature *Aleph* (א):** The small *Aleph* in the Torah scroll's *Vayikra* symbolizes Moses' humility in receiving God's intimate call; Christians are exhorted to adopt this humility, rather than boasting over Israel or viewing the Jewish people as "small" for not accepting Yeshua.
7.  **Rebuke of Anti-Semitism and False Narratives:** Anti-Semitic comments, "genocide" accusations against Israel, and claims that Ashkenazi Jews are not "real Jews" are condemned as false, ignorant, and a form of "boasting against the natural branches" (Romans 11:18).
8.  **The Importance of Experiencing Israel:** Many critics of Israel have never visited the land; personal experience in Israel profoundly changes one's perspective, Bible reading, and faith, making it a crucial "pilgrimage."
9.  **Messiah's Future Kingdom:** Upon His return, Messiah will establish a Jewish Kingdom with a Jewish King on a Jewish throne in Jerusalem, a reality many Christians will find confusing if they do not understand Israel's central role.
10. **The War for the True Church:** Satan is attempting to dismantle Christianity from within by fostering anti-Israel sentiment, making this a fight for the integrity of the true Church.
11. **The Precious Call (*Yakar*):** The word *Yakar* (precious) is linked to *Vayikra*, emphasizing that listening to God's voice (the small *Aleph*) is precious, and the most precious call to action for Christians is to return to the Messianic faith of Acts.
12. **Call to Action: Return to Jewish Roots and Education:** Christians must return to the "Judeo-Christian faith" of Acts, understanding Yeshua's practice of Judaism and the biblical truth of Gentiles being grafted into Israel (not vice-versa), by studying the roots of their faith, particularly through joining a *Yeshiva* to learn Hebrew and Jewish heritage, thereby equipping themselves as humble, prepared believers.

### Key Hebrew Terms

*   ***Vayikra*** (וַיִּקְרָא) – "He Called." The name of the third book of the Torah (Leviticus) and its first Torah portion. Rabbi Shapira emphasizes its meaning as a direct, holy call from God, contrasting it with *Vayikar*. It matters because it frames the entire teaching as a divine summons to action for Christians regarding Israel.
*   ***Vayikar*** (וַיִּקָּר) – "He happened upon," "He chanced upon." Used in the Bible (e.g., Numbers 23:3-4) to describe an accidental or less direct encounter. Rabbi Shapira uses this to highlight that the call in *Vayikra* is *not* accidental but deliberate and intentional.
*   ***HaZaL*** (חז"ל) – An acronym for *Ḥakhameinu Zikhronam Livrakha* (חכמינו זכרונם לברכה), meaning "Our Sages, may their memory be for a blessing." It refers to the Jewish Sages of the Mishnaic and Talmudic periods. Rabbi Shapira uses it to invoke traditional Jewish interpretation of the Torah.
*   ***Aleph*** (א) – The first letter of the Hebrew alphabet. In the Torah scroll, the *Aleph* in *Vayikra* is written smaller than usual. Rabbi Shapira interprets this as symbolizing Moses' humility and applies it as a call for Christians to be humble rather than boastful towards Israel.
*   ***Milchemet Emunah*** (מלחמת אמונה) – "War of Faith." While not explicitly defined, the context implies a spiritual battle for truth and belief, particularly concerning the identity of the Church and its relationship with Israel.
*   ***Yeshua*** (ישוע) – The Hebrew name for Jesus. Used consistently by Rabbi Shapira and Pastor Matt to emphasize Jesus' Jewish identity and roots.
*   ***Yakar*** (יָקָר) – "Precious," "dear," "valuable." Pastor Matt connects this word to *Vayikra* (which shares the root *קרא* - *kara*, to call, and *יקר* - *yakar* can also relate to preciousness or honor in other forms), suggesting that listening to God's call (the small *Aleph*) is precious.
*   ***Ashkenazi*** (אשכנזי) – Jews of Central and Eastern European descent. Mentioned in the context of refuting the anti-Semitic conspiracy theory that Ashkenazi Jews are not "real Jews."
*   ***Sephardi*** (ספרדי) – Jews of Iberian Peninsula (Spain and Portugal) and North African/Middle Eastern descent. Mentioned in contrast to Ashkenazi, to highlight the absurdity of distinguishing "real" Jews based on geographic origin.
*   ***Chabad*** (חב"ד) – An acronym for *Chochma, Bina, Da'at* (Wisdom, Understanding, Knowledge), a prominent Hasidic movement within Orthodox Judaism. Rabbi Shapira mentions his good relationship with Chabad members as an example of his engagement with the broader Jewish community.
*   ***Yeshiva*** (ישיבה) – A Jewish institution for the study of traditional religious texts, primarily the Talmud and Torah. Pastor Matt's closing call to action is for Christians to join their "Bible Yeshiva" to learn Jewish roots.

### What This Means for Christians Who Love Israel

For Chinese Christians who love Israel, this teaching presents a profound call to re-evaluate their faith, identity, and actions. Firstly, it demands an **active and unwavering stand** with Israel and the Jewish people. This goes beyond passive support, urging believers to vocally defend against rising anti-Semitism, even when it emanates from within Christian circles. The teaching frames this as a spiritual "war of faith" and a "separation of sheep and goats," where supporting Israel is equated with supporting Yeshua Himself, emphasizing that "the least of these my brothers" refers primarily to the Jewish people. This challenges Christians to discern true faith from false narratives and to resist the "easy path" of conforming to anti-Israel sentiment.

Secondly, it calls for **profound humility and theological re-evaluation**. Symbolized by the "small Aleph" in *Vayikra*, Christians are exhorted to shed triumphalism and self-centeredness, recognizing that Gentiles are grafted into Israel's heritage, not the other way around. This means critically examining existing theological blind spots and understanding that Yeshua's identity, His practiced religion (Judaism), and the nature of the coming Kingdom are fundamentally Jewish. It challenges the notion that the Church has replaced Israel or that Israel is "small" because it has not yet collectively accepted Yeshua, instead advocating for a recognition of Israel's enduring chosenness and central role in God's plan.

Finally, the teaching provides a clear path for **education and engagement**. It encourages Christians to actively seek knowledge of their Jewish roots and Messianic faith, specifically through joining a "Bible Yeshiva" to learn Hebrew and Jewish heritage. This practical step is presented as essential for a deeper, more authentic relationship with the God of Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob, with the land of Israel, and for preparing for the Messianic King and His Jewish Kingdom. It's a call to equip oneself, to move beyond superficial understanding, and to unite Jew and Gentile into "one new humanity" as described in Scripture, thereby strengthening the true Church against internal spiritual attacks.

### A Word on Discernment

When engaging with Rabbi Shapira's teaching, it is important for Chinese Christians to exercise discernment, distinguishing between *peshat* (the plain, literal meaning of the text) and *drash* (homiletical, interpretive, or allegorical teaching).

1.  **The "Small Aleph" in *Vayikra***: Rabbi Shapira's interpretation of the miniature *Aleph* in the Torah scroll's *Vayikra* as symbolizing Moses' humility and applying it as a call for Christian humility is a classic example of *drash*. While the small *Aleph* is a recognized scribal feature with various traditional Jewish interpretations, its specific theological application here to Christian humility is an interpretive layer, not a direct textual claim from the *peshat* of the verse itself. It serves as a powerful homiletical point.
2.  **"The Least of These My Brothers" (Matthew 25:40)**: The direct equation of "the least of these my brothers" exclusively with the Jewish people is a strong Messianic Jewish interpretation. While it highlights a crucial aspect of Yeshua's identity and the historical context of His ministry, it differs from the broader Christian understanding which often includes all marginalized and suffering people. This is a specific theological reading, not a universally accepted literal reading, and should be understood as such.
3.  **"The Christian faith that will endure will be a Judeo-Christian faith"**: This statement is a theological prediction and a prescriptive vision for the future of Christianity, rather than a descriptive statement of current universal Christian belief or a direct biblical prophecy. It reflects a particular theological school of thought within Messianic Judaism and Christian Zionism, advocating for a return to Jewish roots as essential for the Church's authenticity and endurance.
4.  **"Messiah practiced Judaism" and the necessity of understanding Judaism**: While historically accurate that Yeshua practiced Judaism, the assertion that Christians *must* "understand some Judaism" to understand the foundations of their faith is a strong theological position. It advocates for a particular approach to Christian discipleship and identity, emphasizing the Jewish context of the New Testament and the continuity of God's covenants. This is a call to deeper study and engagement, rather than a literal command.
5.  **"The time of Gog and Magog"**: Identifying the current global situation as "the time of Gog and Magog" is a specific eschatological interpretation. While common in some Messianic Jewish and Christian Zionist circles, it is not a universally agreed-upon literal fulfillment of prophecy among all Christian traditions.

These interpretive elements are valuable for understanding the speaker's perspective and the theological framework of Messianic Judaism, but an intellectually honest study piece should acknowledge their nature as *drash* or specific theological interpretations rather than universally accepted literal readings.
