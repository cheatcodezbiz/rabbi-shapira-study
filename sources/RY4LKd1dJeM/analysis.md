### 1. Title

**被吞噬的草芥行動：俄巴底亞書1:18與彌賽亞時代的降臨**
**Operation Eaten Straw: Obadiah 1:18 and the Dawn of the Messianic Era**

### 2. One-sentence thesis

Rabbi Shapira claims that Hezbollah's "Operation Eaten Straw," named after a Quranic verse, is a direct, word-for-word fulfillment of Obadiah 1:18, signaling the imminent mutual destruction of the nations (Esau/Amalek) and the ultimate triumph of Jacob and Joseph through the coming of Yeshua the Messiah.

### 3. Argument map

1.  Hezbollah launched a new operation, "Operation Eaten Straw" (Hebrew: *Mebsai Hakash Hanehao*; Arabic: *Fajala hum ka asfin makulin*), in response to recent attacks on Israel.
2.  The operation's name is derived from an eschatological Surah (chapter) in the Quran, which describes an "army of the elephant" being made "like chewed-up dry straw."
3.  The Arabic phrase *Fajala hum ka asfin makulin* ("they will be devoured like straw") is presented as a word-for-word match to the Hebrew phrase *Vaasao tem kashnehao* ("they will be devoured like straw") found in Obadiah 1:18.
4.  Hezbollah's naming of this operation thus signifies that Israel's enemies are themselves declaring the fulfillment of Obadiah 1:18.
5.  Obadiah 1:18 prophesies: "The house of Jacob shall be a fire, and the house of Joseph a flame; the house of Esau shall be stubble, and they shall set them ablaze and devour them."
6.  In this prophecy, "Esau" represents the nations hostile to Israel, specifically identified with Amalek (Hezbollah/Iran), while "Jacob" represents the Jewish people and "Joseph" refers to those who belong to the Messiah (including believing Gentiles).
7.  According to Radak's commentary on Obadiah 1:18, the descendants of Esau will only meet their ultimate demise through the hand of Joseph or his descendants.
8.  Rashi's commentary on Obadiah 1:18 interprets the phrase "Thus says the Lord" at the end of the verse as pointing to the coming of the ruler, the Messiah, who will destroy these enemies.
9.  Midrashic tradition, connecting to 1 Samuel 30:17 (where only 400 escaped David's destruction), notes that the Hebrew word for "straw" (*kash*) has a numerical value (gematria) of 400, symbolizing the remaining demonic forces that will ultimately face the Messiah.
10. Further Midrashic interpretation identifies the "flame from the house of Joseph" that consumes Esau's stubble (Obadiah 1:18) with Joshua, who is from the tribe of Joseph and whose name is also Yeshua (Jesus), and who will defeat the Amalekites.
11. Aggadic tradition (Rabbi Pinchas in the name of Rabbi Shmuel bar Nachman) affirms that Esau will fall only by the hand of Rachel's descendants (Joseph), supported by references to Jeremiah 49:20, Genesis 30:25, and Numbers 24:19.
12. The ultimate outcome is a great chaos where the nations (Esau/Amalek, exemplified by Persia/America) will mutually destroy each other (referencing Yoma Chapter 10), leaving only Jacob and Joseph standing firm.
13. Therefore, the current geopolitical events, specifically Hezbollah's operation, are a clear sign of the imminent Messianic era and the final conflict leading to Yeshua's return and triumph.

### 4. All scriptural references

*   **Obadiah 1:18**: "雅各家必成為大火，約瑟家必成為火焰，以掃家必成為碎秸。" (The house of Jacob shall be a fire, and the house of Joseph a flame, and the house of Esau stubble.)
    *   Hebrew terms highlighted: *Vaasao tem kashnehao* (וְהָיָה בֵית-יַעֲקֹב אֵשׁ וּבֵית יוֹסֵף לֶהָבָה וּבֵית עֵשָׂו לְקַשׁ וְדָלְקוּ בָהֶם וַאֲכָלוּם) – "They will be devoured like straw." The Rabbi specifically focuses on *kash* (straw) and the concept of being consumed (*va'akhalum*).
*   **1 Samuel 30:17**: "當時大衛從黎明一直攻擊到第二天晚上，沒有人逃脫。除了那400個騎著駱駝逃走的少年人以外，在那場戰鬥中沒有任何一個人能夠成功逃脫。" (David attacked them from dawn until the evening of the next day, and not a man of them escaped, except four hundred young men who rode on camels and fled.)
*   **Jeremiah 49:20**: "群羊中最少的必將他們拖走。" (The least of the flock shall drag them away.)
*   **Genesis 30:25**: "拉結生了約瑟。" (Rachel bore Joseph.)
*   **Numbers 24:19**: "必有一位從雅各而出，毀滅城中的餘民。" (A ruler will come from Jacob and destroy the remnant in the city.)
*   **Genesis 37:2**: "他是一個少年，與辟拉的兒子們在一起。" (He was a youth, with the sons of Bilhah.)
*   **Talmudic Tractate**:
    *   **Yoma Chapter 10** (約馬第10章): Referenced for the distinction between Amalek and Esau, and their prophecy of mutual destruction.
*   **Quran Surah**: (Unspecified chapter, but described as "the story symbolizing the elephant" and an "eschatological chapter"). The specific Arabic phrase chanted and referenced is *Fajala hum ka asfin makulin* (فجعلهم كعصف مأكول) – "making them like chewed-up dry straw."

### 5. Key Hebrew terms

*   **Mebsai Hakash Hanehao** (מבצע הקש הנאכל):
    *   **Gloss**: "Operation Eaten Straw."
    *   **Why it matters**: This is the Hebrew translation of Hezbollah's operation name, forming the central linguistic and prophetic link to Obadiah 1:18.
*   **Kash** (קש):
    *   **Gloss**: "Straw," "stubble."
    *   **Why it matters**: It is the key term in Obadiah 1:18 describing Esau's fate and is central to the operation's name. It also forms the basis for a gematria claim.
*   **Maahilet** (מאכלת):
    *   **Gloss**: "Eating," "consumption," "knife" (for eating/slaughter).
    *   **Why it matters**: Rabbi Shapira connects the Arabic *Makulin* ("eaten") to this Hebrew root, emphasizing the shared meaning of complete consumption and destruction.
*   **Yeshua** (ישוע):
    *   **Gloss**: "Jesus," "Salvation."
    *   **Why it matters**: Identified as the ultimate Messiah figure, the "Joseph" who will defeat Amalek/Esau and bring the final end to the conflict.
*   **Shukay** (שוקי):
    *   **Gloss**: A less common rabbinic epithet for the Messiah, possibly related to "my leg/thigh" (*shok*), symbolizing power or rule.
    *   **Why it matters**: Rabbi Shapira states "Messiah Shukay will personally handle all of this," attributing the final destruction of enemies to this specific Messianic figure.
*   **Amalek** (עמלק):
    *   **Gloss**: A historical, archetypal enemy of Israel, representing existential opposition to God's people.
    *   **Why it matters**: Rabbi Shapira identifies Hezbollah/Iran with Amalek, framing the current conflict as a continuation of this ancient, spiritual struggle.

### 6. Key gematria / numerical claims

*   **400**:
    *   **Claim**: The Hebrew word for "straw" (קש - *kash*) has a numerical value (gematria) of 400.
    *   **Connection**: This number is linked to the 400 young men who escaped David's destruction in 1 Samuel 30:17. These 400 are interpreted as representing the "remaining demonic forces" that will ultimately be confronted and defeated by the Messiah.

### 8. What this means for Christians who love Israel

This teaching presents a powerful and urgent call for Christians who love Israel to recognize current geopolitical events as direct fulfillments of biblical prophecy, particularly Obadiah 1:18. It reinforces the belief that God's word is active and unfolding in real-time, providing a prophetic lens through which to view global conflicts. The identification of Hezbollah/Iran with "Amalek" and "Esau" elevates the conflict beyond mere politics, framing it as a spiritual, eschatological battle with ancient roots.

The message emphasizes that ultimate victory belongs to "Jacob and Joseph," interpreted as the Jewish people and those who belong to the Messiah (Yeshua), including believing Gentiles. This understanding provides a framework for Christians to see their role in the end times not as passive observers but as active participants called to "join Jacob, join Joseph, join Yeshua, join Israel." It encourages active prayer, sharing of this prophetic message, and deeper study of the Scriptures and Jewish traditions, fostering a sense of shared destiny and purpose with the Jewish people in the unfolding Messianic age.

Crucially, the teaching posits that the destruction of the nations (Esau/Amalek) will be a mutual, self-inflicted chaos, leading to their ultimate demise, while Jacob and Joseph will stand firm. This offers a hopeful perspective amidst global turmoil, directing believers to place their trust in the coming Messiah, Yeshua, as the one who will bring the final resolution and establish His kingdom. It is a profound call to align with God's chosen people and His Messiah in anticipation of His glorious return.

### 9. A word on discernment

Rabbi Shapira's teaching is deeply rooted in *drash* (דרש), a homiletical and interpretive method common in rabbinic tradition that seeks deeper, often non-literal, meanings in scripture by connecting disparate texts and concepts. While this approach offers profound spiritual insights and theological connections, it is important for Chinese Christians to understand that not all claims are presented as *peshat* (פשט), the plain or literal meaning of the text.

Specific examples of *drash* in this teaching include:

*   **"Word-for-word" fulfillment**: While the Arabic phrase *Fajala hum ka asfin makulin* shares a strong semantic and phonetic resemblance to the Hebrew in Obadiah 1:18, claiming it is a "word-for-word" match across two different languages is a homiletical connection highlighting a shared prophetic theme, rather than a precise linguistic equivalence.
*   **Identification of modern entities with biblical archetypes**: Identifying Hezbollah/Iran as "Amalek" and the "house of Esau" is a classic rabbinic interpretive method to apply ancient prophecies to contemporary foes. While it draws on historical and theological parallels, it is an interpretive application rather than a literal, explicit biblical statement about these specific modern nations.
*   **Gematria and numerical symbolism**: The connection between the gematria of "straw" (קש - *kash*, numerical value 400) and the 400 men who escaped in 1 Samuel 30:17 is a traditional *drash* technique. Gematria is a form of Jewish mysticism and numerology, offering symbolic insight rather than a literal interpretive method for *peshat* or direct prophetic prediction.
*   **Midrashic interpretations of "Joseph" and "Joshua/Yeshua"**: While there are strong theological connections and typologies between Joseph, Joshua, and Yeshua in Messianic Jewish thought, the Midrashic statements linking Joshua's tribal lineage to Joseph and then directly equating "Joshua's name is also Yeshua" are interpretive expansions, not direct biblical statements. They highlight a Messianic typology rather than a literal identity.
*   **"Messiah Shukay"**: This specific epithet for the Messiah is not widely recognized in mainstream Jewish or Messianic Jewish theology and appears to be a unique or less common rabbinic interpretation.

For Chinese Christians, understanding these distinctions allows for a deeper appreciation of the richness and depth of Jewish thought and its interpretive traditions, while maintaining a critical and biblically grounded approach to prophecy. It encourages engagement with the spiritual truths conveyed without necessarily adopting every interpretive detail as a literal prophetic prediction.
