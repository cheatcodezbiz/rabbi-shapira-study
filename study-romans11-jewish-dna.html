《罗马书11章的犹太基因》
研习指南 The Jewish DNA of Romans 11
A Christian's Study Guide

八堂课程，从基督教与犹太教的双重视角，伴你重新认识那位「拉比扫罗」(Rabbi Shaul)——你以为你认识的「使徒保罗」。 Eight lessons. A companion for Christians who love the Jewish people, walking through Rabbi Shapira's reclamation of "Rabbi Shaul" — the apostle Paul you thought you already knew.

基于沙皮拉拉比博士《罗马书11章的犹太基因 · 第一卷》(2025) Based on The Jewish DNA of Romans 11, Vol. Alef by Rabbi Dr. Itzhak Shapira (2025)
8 堂课程8 Lessons · 约 50 分钟通读~50 min read-through · 自助 / 小组皆宜Self-paced or small group · 📖 购买原书Get the Book ·

本指南不是要替你「读完」沙皮拉拉比的《罗马书11章的犹太基因》，而是要陪你认真地读它。这是一本让你重新认识使徒保罗——也就是「拉比扫罗」——的书。沙皮拉拉比一开始就直接挑战了基督教与犹太教对这个人物的双重误读：教会把他变成了「反妥拉」的基督教创始人；正统犹太教则把他视为「反犹」的叛徒。这本书要还原一个被误解了两千年的犹太拉比——他的法利赛根基、他的末世论世界观、他对外邦人的爱、以及他写《罗马书》第11章时心中的核心秘密：外邦人的丰满。如果你是一位爱犹太人的基督徒，这份指南是为你而设的。 This guide is not a shortcut around Rabbi Shapira's The Jewish DNA of Romans 11 — it is a companion for reading it carefully. This is a book about meeting the apostle Paul again as "Rabbi Shaul." Rabbi Shapira opens by dismantling two opposite misreadings of this man: the Church has made him the founder of an "anti-Torah" religion; Orthodox Judaism has labeled him an "antisemitic" traitor. The book seeks to restore a Jewish rabbi who has been misread for two thousand years — his Pharisaic roots, his end-times worldview, his fierce love for the nations, and the secret at the heart of Romans 11: the fullness of the Gentiles. If you are a Christian who loves the Jewish people, this guide is for you.

本指南共 八堂课，每一堂围绕拉比书中的一个核心教导。每一堂都包含五个部分： The guide is structured as eight lessons, each centered on a core teaching from the book. Every lesson follows the same five-part rhythm:

① 拉比说什么—— 用拉比自己的语境概述他的论点；
② 关键经文—— 拉比引用的圣经依据；
③ 希伯来语之眼—— 一个关键的希伯来词，连接两个传统；
④ 基督徒视角—— 福音派 / 教会传统如何接收这一教导；
⑤ 诚实的提问—— 可与拉比一起继续追问的问题，不是反驳，而是更深的对话。 ① What the Rabbi Says — his argument in his own frame;
② Key Scripture — the biblical text he is reading;
③ Through Hebrew Eyes — one Hebrew word that bridges the two traditions;
④ A Christian Lens — how Evangelical / Church tradition receives the teaching;
⑤ Honest Questions — questions to keep asking the rabbi — not to refute, but to go deeper.
א  ·  01

从马德里的教堂讲台开始——「我们不需要犹太教」 It Begins in a Madrid Pulpit — "We Don't Need Judaism"

① 拉比说什么 ① What the Rabbi Says

沙皮拉拉比讲述了发生在西班牙马德里一座建于宗教裁判所时期的教堂里的事件：一位备受尊敬的牧师在台上向欢呼的会众宣告——「基督教的耶稣、犹太教的耶书亚、《古兰经》中的尔撒，都是同一位。我们基督徒只在耶稣的名下得救——我们不需要犹太教。」拉比当时站在那里，仿佛回到五百年前——他成了被迫改宗的「Anusim」中的一员，眼睁睁看着那座冰冷的教堂用保罗的话定罪犹太人。这一刻，他做出了一个决定：他必须把保罗从基督教的手中夺回来，归还给犹太人。这不是仇恨教会，而是纠正一个历史性的错误。本书因此而生。 Rabbi Shapira opens with an event that happened in a Madrid church built during the Inquisition: a respected pastor declared from the pulpit to a cheering congregation — "The Jesus of Christianity, the Yeshua of Judaism, and the Isa of the Quran are the same person. We Christians are saved in the name of Jesus — we don't need Judaism." Standing there, the rabbi felt himself thrown back five hundred years — one of the forced converts (the Anusim) watching a cold cathedral use Paul's own words against the Jewish people. In that moment he made a decision: Paul had to be reclaimed from Christianity and returned to the Jewish people. Not as hatred toward the Church but as the correction of a historic error. This book was born from that moment.

② 关键经文 ② Key Scripture

「你不可向枝子夸口；若是夸口，当知道不是你托着根，乃是根托着你。」 "Do not boast over the branches. If you do, consider this: you do not support the root, but the root supports you." 《罗马书》11:18 · Romans 11:18

③ 希伯来语之眼 ③ Through Hebrew Eyes

הַשְׁקָפָה · Hashkafah——「世界观」、「立场」。这是本书的关键术语。拉比指出：你不能把保罗从他自身的犹太 hashkafah 中抽走，再用基督教两千年的滤镜审问他——那样得出的结论必然是错的。要听懂保罗，必须先承认他是一世纪法利赛拉比——他自己说：「我是 Pa'rush ben Pa'rush，法利赛人的法利赛人」（《使徒行传》23:6）。这个原则一旦被建立，整本《罗马书》就开始呼吸希伯来语的呼吸。 הַשְׁקָפָה · Hashkafah — "worldview," "standpoint." This is the book's key term. The rabbi insists: you cannot extract Paul from his Jewish hashkafah, then interrogate him through two thousand years of Christian filters and expect to land on the truth. To hear Paul you must concede he is a first-century Pharisaic rabbi. He said it himself: "I am a Pa'rush ben Pa'rush — a Pharisee, son of Pharisees" (Acts 23:6). Once that principle is in place, the entire epistle to the Romans starts breathing Hebrew air.

④ 基督徒视角 ④ A Christian Lens

很多基督徒习惯把「保罗」与「彼得、约翰」并列，仿佛他们都是在新宗教的开端站立。但《罗马书》11:18 是保罗对外邦基督徒说的——明确地说：「不是你托着根」。换句话说，保罗自己预先警告了过去两千年里教会的姿态。马德里那位牧师的话，不仅是对犹太人的羞辱——更是对保罗本人话语的逆转。读这本书的第一个挑战，就是承认：历史上有大量「以保罗之名」说出的话，正是保罗本人最严厉警告过的事。 Many Christians line up "Paul" beside "Peter, John" as if they all stand together at the launch of a new religion. But Romans 11:18 is Paul speaking to Gentile Christians, and he is direct: "You do not support the root." Paul, in other words, anticipated and pre-warned against exactly the posture the Church has worn for twenty centuries. The Madrid pastor's words are not only an insult to Jews — they are the reversal of Paul's own words. The first challenge of this book is to admit: much that has been said throughout history "in Paul's name" is precisely what Paul himself most sternly warned against.

⑤ 诚实的提问 ⑤ Honest Questions

  • 如果有一位犹太拉比说：「我要把保罗从教会手中夺回来。」我的第一反应是什么？是好奇，还是自卫？这个反应本身在告诉我什么？If a Jewish rabbi tells me "I'm reclaiming Paul from the Church" — what is my first reaction? Curiosity or defensiveness? What is that reaction telling me about myself?
  • 在我所听过的「保罗」教导中，有多少其实是保罗自己警告过的——「枝子向根夸口」？我能举出具体例子吗？In all the "Paul" sermons I've heard, how much was actually Paul's own warning against — "branches boasting over the root"? Can I name a concrete example?
  • 我读《罗马书》时，是把它读为「基督徒写给基督徒」的书，还是「一位犹太拉比写给罗马教会的余民」的书？这两种读法会带出怎样不同的内容？When I read Romans, am I reading it as "a Christian writing to Christians" or as "a Jewish rabbi writing to the remnant of the Roman congregation"? What changes between those two readings?
ב  ·  02

第四次革命——外邦人的觉醒 The Fourth Revolution — When the Nations Wake Up

① 拉比说什么 ① What the Rabbi Says

沙皮拉拉比引用当代极端正统派思想家——拉比以撒·金斯伯格——一段震撼人心的米德拉什：犹太信仰史上经历过三次革命：(1) 犹大王子把口传妥拉写下来（约 1,800 年前）；(2) 迈蒙尼德时代允许妥拉学者受奖学金（约 1,000 年前）；(3) 一百多年前开放女性的妥拉教育。如今，第四次也是最后一次革命正在发生——「把妥拉的光带给万国，唤醒外邦人内心的「圣洁火花」」。这位拉比用的语言几乎与拉比扫罗在《罗马书》11 章中说的「外邦人的丰满」一字不差——只是相隔两千年。 Rabbi Shapira cites a stunning teaching from the Chabad thinker Rabbi Yitzchak Ginsburgh: Jewish history has seen three revolutions — (1) Judah the Prince writing down the Oral Torah (~1,800 years ago); (2) the post-Maimonidean permission for Torah scholars to receive support (~1,000 years ago); (3) the opening of Torah education to women (~150 years ago). Now, a fourth and final revolution is breaking — "to bring the light of Torah to the nations, to awaken the holy sparks within Gentiles." The wording is almost word-for-word what Rabbi Shaul called "the fullness of the Gentiles" in Romans 11 — only two thousand years later.

② 关键经文 ② Key Scripture

「雅各余剩的人必在多国民中，如从耶和华那里降下的露水，又如甘霖降在草上；他们不依赖任何人，也不寄希望于世人。」 "The remnant of Jacob shall be in the midst of many peoples, like dew from the LORD, like showers on the grass — which delay not for man, nor wait for the children of mankind." 《弥迦书》5:7 · Micah 5:7

③ 希伯来语之眼 ③ Through Hebrew Eyes

הִתְעוֹרְרוּת · Hit'orerut——「觉醒」。这个词不是知识上的明白——它是从沉睡中苏醒。拉比说：今天在万国中正在发生的，是预言性的、超自然的觉醒——一群外邦人开始认领自己「雅各余民」的身份。他们不是因被人传讲而苏醒，而是「如露水从天而降」——拉什注释这节经文说：「露水非因人而来。」也就是说，圣灵正在直接越过许多教会的讲台，把人唤醒。 הִתְעוֹרְרוּת · Hit'orerut — "awakening." Not intellectual recognition — it is waking from sleep. The rabbi says: what is happening in the nations right now is a prophetic, supernatural awakening — a body of Gentiles beginning to own their identity as "the remnant of Jacob." They are not waking because someone preached to them; they are waking "like dew from heaven." Rashi notes the dew "does not come because of man." The Spirit is bypassing many pulpits and waking the sleepers directly.

④ 基督徒视角 ④ A Christian Lens

「外邦人的丰满」——plērōma tōn ethnōn，《罗马书》11:25 的关键词——基督徒读了两千年，却很少从希伯来视角读懂。它不是「满了一定数量的外邦人就轮到犹太人」这种计数式读法。拉比让我们看见：它是列国中的余民被唤醒、认领自己在以色列家中的身份，从而触发犹太人整体的得救。这一刻，金斯伯格拉比和拉比扫罗几乎站在同一个讲台上——哪怕他们彼此并不知道。 "The fullness of the Gentiles" — plērōma tōn ethnōn, the key phrase of Romans 11:25 — has been read by Christians for two thousand years, almost never from a Hebrew viewpoint. It is not a numerical reading: "once the Gentile quota is full, it switches back to Israel." The rabbi makes us see it differently: it is the remnant within the nations being awakened to claim their identity within the household of Israel — and that awakening triggering Israel's collective salvation. At this moment, Rabbi Ginsburgh and Rabbi Shaul stand on almost the same platform — without ever knowing each other.

⑤ 诚实的提问 ⑤ Honest Questions

  • 如果今天一位极端正统派的犹太拉比和拉比扫罗对「外邦人的丰满」给出了几乎一致的描述——这说明了什么？我以前是如何理解这个词的？If a contemporary ultra-Orthodox rabbi and the apostle Paul give almost the identical description of "the fullness of the Gentiles" — what does that tell me? How did I understand the phrase before?
  • 我自己的觉醒，是被某个人「说服」的，还是「如露水从天而降」的？两者有何分别？哪一种比较接近圣经的描述？My own awakening — was it because someone convinced me, or did it come "like dew from heaven"? What's the difference, and which is closer to Scripture's picture?
  • 如果我自己就是「雅各余民」中的一员——这意味着什么责任？我能为犹太民族今天的处境做些什么？（提示：拉比写本书前的背景，是 2023 年 10 月 7 日。）If I myself am part of "the remnant of Jacob" — what responsibility comes with that? What can I do, today, for the Jewish people? (Hint: the rabbi wrote this book in the aftermath of October 7, 2023.)
ג  ·  03

奖与罚——犹太信仰的引擎 Reward and Punishment — The Engine Beneath Faith

① 拉比说什么 ① What the Rabbi Says

这一章会让许多基督徒坐不住。沙皮拉拉比指出：奖与罚（Sachar Ve'Onesh）是法利赛犹太信仰的核心支柱之一——也是耶稣本人和拉比扫罗共同的世界观。许多教会教导「一次得救，永远得救」、「恩典之外不再有审判」——这不是保罗本人的立场。保罗在《罗马书》2:6 直接写道：「祂必照各人的行为报应各人」——这句话的希伯来背景是《诗篇》62:13 和《先父的伦理》。但拉比的关键澄清在这里：保罗讲的不是律法主义。他讲的是——每个人按各自的「起点」与「终点」之间的提升，受神的衡量。 This chapter unsettles many Christian readers. Rabbi Shapira insists: Reward and Punishment (Sachar Ve'Onesh) is one of the foundational pillars of Pharisaic Judaism — and is the shared worldview of Yeshua Himself and Rabbi Shaul. Many churches teach "once saved, always saved" or "under grace, no more judgment" — that is not Paul's position. Paul writes plainly in Romans 2:6: "He will repay each one according to his deeds" — quoting Psalm 62 and echoing Pirkei Avot. But here is the rabbi's crucial qualification: Paul is not preaching legalism. He is preaching that each person is measured by the climb between his "starting point" and his "endpoint".

② 关键经文 ② Key Scripture

「祢是大有怜悯，也是大有公义；因为祢按各人所行的报应他。」 "Steadfast love belongs to You, O Lord. For You will render to a man according to his work." 《诗篇》62:12 · Psalm 62:12（拉比扫罗在《罗马书》2:6 引用）

③ 希伯来语之眼 ③ Through Hebrew Eyes

שָׂכָר וָעֹנֶשׁ · Sachar Ve'Onesh——「奖赏与惩罚」。这不是「律法主义」的代名词；恰恰相反，它的根基是上帝是可信的——祂不会让义人徒然劳苦，也不会让恶人逍遥法外。奖赏（sachar）这个词在希伯来语里也意为「工价」——祂会付清祂所欠的。这与「白白称义」并不冲突——它在更深的层次上回应：「那么，被白白称义之后，我所行的还重要吗？」拉比的答案是：极其重要——因为奖赏的尺度是提升的幅度。 שָׂכָר וָעֹנֶשׁ · Sachar Ve'Onesh — "reward and punishment." Not a synonym for legalism; precisely the opposite. Its foundation is that God is trustworthy — He will not let the righteous toil in vain, and He will not let the wicked walk free. Sachar in Hebrew also means "wages" — God pays out what He owes. This does not contradict "freely justified" — it answers a deeper question: "once I have been freely justified, does what I do still matter?" The rabbi's answer is profoundly yes — because the measure of reward is the distance you climbed.

④ 基督徒视角 ④ A Christian Lens

耶稣自己说：「人子要在祂父的荣耀里同祂的众使者降临，那时要照各人的行为报应各人」（《马太福音》16:27）。同一句话。保罗在《哥林多前书》3 章详细讲过——每个人在火中受验，房屋若烧毁本人虽然得救，仍是「损失」。基督徒常把「因信称义」与「奖赏与惩罚」分成两本书——但保罗自己把它们写在同一封信里。读完这一章后，你将不能再轻松地说「恩典之外不再算账」——因为是谁在算账？是同一位救你的弥赛亚。 Yeshua said it Himself: "The Son of Man is going to come… and then He will repay each person according to what he has done" (Matt 16:27). Identical sentence. Paul spelled it out in 1 Corinthians 3 — each one's work is tested by fire; if the building burns, the person is still saved, but he loses. Christians often shelve "justification by faith" and "reward and judgment" in two separate books. Paul writes them in the same letter. After this chapter you can no longer comfortably say "grace cancels all accounting" — because the One doing the accounting is the same Messiah who saved you.

⑤ 诚实的提问 ⑤ Honest Questions

  • 我所相信的福音，是否给「奖赏与惩罚」留有位置？还是我把它整章删除了——为了维持一种「不必紧张」的恩典？Does the gospel I believe leave room for "reward and punishment"? Or have I removed the whole chapter to keep a "no-pressure" version of grace?
  • 如果衡量我的尺度不是到达多高而是提升多少——我对自己原本的起点有多诚实？神看见的「我」，比我看自己更深。If I am measured not by how high I reach but by how far I climb — how honest am I about my actual starting point? God sees "me" further down than I see myself.
  • 我对「每一个善行都被记录」的反应，是恐惧还是感恩？这两种反应之间，哪一个更像耶稣对人说话的语气？When I hear "every act is being recorded," do I respond with fear or with gratitude? Which of those two is closer to the tone in which Yeshua spoke to people?
ד  ·  04

梯子的层级——你不必跑到犹太人的位置 The Ladder — You Don't Have to Climb to the Jewish Floor

① 拉比说什么 ① What the Rabbi Says

这是本书最具解放性的一章。沙皮拉拉比说：在 Sachar Ve'Onesh 的属灵账本里，神不在「终点」数你的诫命，而在「起点」与「终点」之间数你的提升。引用《先父的伦理》：「从第一级升到第五级的恶人，比从第五级升到第七级的义人，得到更大的奖赏。」用一句拉比自己的话：「不是你跑到哪里，而是你从哪里跑出来。」这意味着——外邦人不必爬到犹太人的位置，才能得到神的丰满奖赏。每一个人都有自己的梯子。这也是为什么保罗不要求外邦皈依者受割礼。他不是反对妥拉——他是不肯把全副妥拉的轭压到一群刚刚离开偶像崇拜的人身上，以免他们摔下来跌入深渊。 This is the book's most liberating chapter. Rabbi Shapira teaches: in the spiritual ledger of Sachar Ve'Onesh, God does not count commandments at the finish line; He counts the climb between starting point and finish line. From Pirkei Avot: "The wicked man who rises from the first rung to the fifth receives greater reward than the righteous man who rises from the fifth to the seventh." In the rabbi's own words: "It is not where you arrive — it is where you ran from." Therefore — Gentiles do not have to climb up to the Jewish floor to receive the fullness of God's reward. Each person has his own ladder. This is why Paul does not require Gentile converts to be circumcised. He is not against Torah — he refuses to load the full yoke onto people who have just stepped off the altars of idolatry, lest they slip and fall further than they were.

② 关键经文 ② Key Scripture

「宁可有人对你说：『请你上来』，强如在贵族面前叫你退下。」 "It is better that someone say to you, 'Come up here,' than that you be put lower in the presence of the noble." 《箴言》25:7 · Proverbs 25:7

③ 希伯来语之眼 ③ Through Hebrew Eyes

אֱמוּנָה · Emunah——「信」。这是希伯来语里动态的信。它的同根词是 oman（艺术家）和 aman（阿们）——一个不断实践、修炼自己手艺的人。耶利米书 5:1 说神在耶路撒冷找「寻求 emunah 的人」——「寻求」(mevakesh) 这个词本身就含着「不肯停下」的意思。所以保罗说「因信称义」时，他从未意指那种「站着不动的接受」式的信。他指的是——一种艺术家式的、动态的、不停在实践中精修的信。这恰恰是「梯子的提升」所需要的引擎。 אֱמוּנָה · Emunah — "faith." In Hebrew it is active. Its cognates are oman (artisan) and aman (amen) — someone who practices and refines his craft. Jeremiah 5:1 says God searches Jerusalem for "those who seek emunah" — and the verb mevakesh ("seeks") carries the sense of "refusing to stop." So when Paul says "justified by faith," he never means a "stand-still receiving" faith. He means a faith like an artisan's — dynamic, refined in the doing. This is exactly the engine for "climbing the ladder."

④ 基督徒视角 ④ A Christian Lens

福音派常把「因信称义」简化为「同意一组命题」——但希伯来语的 emunah 不是「同意」，是「跟着走」、「反复在做」。这恰好是雅各书 2 章——「信心若没有行为就是死的」——的根。雅各和保罗从来不是对立的；他们用希伯来语说的是同一回事——只不过保罗强调引擎，雅各强调结果。沙皮拉拉比把这个引擎称为「pnimiyut」——内化。任何没有内化的诫命遵守，都会让人成为伪君子；任何没有 pnimiyut 的妥拉，导致死亡（保罗的原话）。 Evangelicals often reduce "justified by faith" to "agreeing to a set of propositions." But Hebrew emunah is not "agreeing"; it is "walking along," "doing it again and again." This is precisely the root of James 2 — "faith without works is dead." James and Paul have never been at war; speaking Hebrew, they say the same thing — Paul stresses the engine, James the fruit. Rabbi Shapira names this engine pnimiyut — internalization. Any commandment-keeping without internalization turns a person into a hypocrite; any Torah without pnimiyut, in Paul's exact words, "brings death."

香气测试——两位弥赛亚的同一张面孔 The Aroma Test — Two Messiahs, One Face

① 拉比说什么 ① What the Rabbi Says

在《玛拉基书》3:1，「主」（Adon）忽然进入祂的圣殿——立约的使者。伊本·以斯拉的注释令基督徒震惊：「这位也许就是约瑟之子弥赛亚！」而《以赛亚书》11:3 描写弥赛亚审判的方式更让人不安——「他必以敬畏耶和华来感知真理：行审判不凭眼见，断是非也不凭耳闻」——希伯来语 v'hericho，「藉嗅觉感知」。审判不是用眼睛或耳朵——是用鼻子。保罗在《哥林多后书》2:16 描述同一图像：「在这等人身上是死的香气，在那等人身上是活的香气。」沙皮拉拉比指出：约瑟之子弥赛亚（受苦的）和大卫之子弥赛亚（得胜的）不是两个人——是同一位的两次显现。祂的两张面孔，闻你身上的同一种香气。 In Malachi 3:1, "the Lord" (Adon) suddenly enters His temple — the messenger of the covenant. Citing the Chomat Anakh, Rabbi Shapira notes: every klipah emits a smell. When Messiah arrives He breathes over every person — and the one who carries His own fragrance has passed the "aroma test." The "Spirit of Christ," the "fragrance of Christ," the "indwelling of Christ" — in Hebrew tradition these are facets of one reality.

Jeremiah cries: "Men call them 'discarded silver,' for the LORD has rejected them." I pawned you, with the sanctuary (Mishkan) as collateral (Mishkun) — until the sun goes down [i.e., Messiah comes]." Rabbi Bahya's reading is stunning: Mishkan and Mishkun share the same root — "dwelling" and "pledge." This is exactly Paul's cry in Romans 11:1-2 — "Has God rejected His people? By no means!"

מִשְׁכָּן / מַשְׁכּוֹן · Mishkan / Mishkun——「圣所」和「抵押」共用同一个希伯来词根שׁ-כ-ן「居住」。这不是文字游戏——它是神学。神暂时把祂自己的居所当作抵押物典当出去——祂多么舍不得回到这地。这与约伯记 14:13 中那位等待赎回的灵魂、与《启示录》12 章那位被逼到旷野的妇人——是同一幅图画。以色列的「掳」从来不是「休」。 מִשְׁכָּן / מַשְׁכּוֹן · Mishkan / Mishkun — "sanctuary" and "pledge" share the same Hebrew root sh-k-n, "to dwell." This is not a pun — it is theology. God pledged His own dwelling place as collateral — so painful was His longing to return. This is the same picture as the soul awaiting redemption in Job 14:13, and the woman driven into the wilderness in Revelation 12 — one image. Israel's exile has never been a divorce.

If God never divorced His Bride, who is qualified to replace her? Paul wrote eleven chapters in advance to answer: "By no means." The rabbi shows that Israel's two states — haktanot (lowered) and hagdalot (raised) — match the Messiah's own two appearances. When the Church persecuted Israel believing it had become "the new Israel" — it was persecuting its own kin.

外邦人的丰满——Goy 与 Nochri 之别 The Fullness of the Gentiles — Goy vs. Nochri

① 拉比说什么 ① What the Rabbi Says

本书的命脉在这一章。沙皮拉拉比指出希伯来圣经里有两个完全不同的词，常常被一并译为「外邦人」：Goy（国族成员、与亚伯拉罕之约可有联系的人）和 Nochri（外国人、与以色列圣约毫无关系的人）。以色列自己也被称为 Goy Kadosh——「圣洁的国」。所以「Goy」并非贬义。拉比扫罗以惊人的预言眼光看见：在末后的日子，会有一群从 Nochri 升级为 Goy 的外邦人——他们不是肉身的犹太人，但他们有「犹太基因」（hashkafah, kavanah, ahavat Yisrael, mesirut nefesh）。这就是plērōma tōn ethnōn——外邦人的丰满——的真正含义。它不是数字。它是身份的转化。 The book's artery runs through this chapter. Rabbi Shapira shows that the Hebrew Bible uses two utterly different words, both often flattened to "Gentile": Goy (a national member, potentially in covenantal relation to Abraham) and Nochri (a foreigner, with no covenantal relation at all). Israel herself is called Goy Kadosh — "a holy nation". Goy is not pejorative. Rabbi Shaul, with stunning prophetic vision, foresaw that in the latter days a body of Gentiles would be lifted from Nochri to Goy — not Jewish in the flesh, but carrying the "Jewish DNA" (hashkafah, kavanah, ahavat Yisrael, mesirut nefesh). Plērōma tōn ethnōn — the fullness of the Gentiles — is not a number. It is a transformation of identity.

גּוֹי / נׇכְרִי · Goy / Nochri。19 世纪德国正统派伟大拉比雅各·茨维·梅克伦堡提出一个革命性的修订：晨祷里那句「赞美你，没有让我成为 Goy」应当改为「赞美你，没有让我成为 Nochri」——因为今天许多外邦人是 Goy，他们承认创造之主、敬畏祂的妥拉、不再拜偶像。Sanhedrin 59a 更进一步：「一个学习妥拉的外邦人，被视为如大祭司」。这与彼得前书 2:9——「君尊的祭司」——是同一句话，跨越两千年的回响。 גּוֹי / נׇכְרִי · Goy / Nochri. The 19th-century German Orthodox rabbi Yaakov Tzvi Mecklenberg proposed a revolutionary reading of the morning blessing: instead of "Blessed are You, who has not made me a Goy," he insisted it should read "who has not made me a Nochri" — because today many Gentiles are Goyim, who confess the Creator, honor His Torah, and have walked away from idols. Sanhedrin 59a goes further: "A Gentile who studies Torah is considered like the High Priest." This is the identical sentence as 1 Peter 2:9 — "a royal priesthood" — echoing across two thousand years.

④ 基督徒视角 ④ A Christian Lens

基督徒读《罗马书》11:25 时，常把「外邦人的丰满」当作「该轮到犹太人了，所以我们的时间用完了」——一种被动的计时器。但希伯来视角彻底翻转：它是责任，不是计时器。我们这群从 Nochri 升级为 Goy 的外邦人，使命是抓住一个犹太人的衣襟——不是为了让他改宗，而是与他同去耶路撒冷。这条命令完全消解了基督徒和犹太人「谁让谁皈依」的拉锯战。撒迦利亚没有说「那犹太人改宗加入我们」——他说的是「我们抓住他的衣襟，跟他一起走」。 When Christians read Romans 11:25, they often hear "the fullness of the Gentiles" as "now it's the Jews' turn — so our time is up" — a passive countdown clock. The Hebrew lens reverses everything: it is responsibility, not a countdown. We Gentiles who have been raised from Nochri to Goy — our task is to take hold of a Jew's hem — not to convert him, but to walk to Jerusalem with him. This command dissolves the long Christian-Jewish argument about "who converts whom." Zechariah does not say "that Jew joins us" — he says "we grab his hem and go with him."

如果读完这八堂课，你心中开始承认——你以为认识的「保罗」其实只是历史投影出的影子，而真正的「拉比扫罗」是一位为犹太民族焦虑到日夜伤痛的犹太弟兄——那么，本书已经做完了它要做的事。沙皮拉拉比把保罗还给了犹太人，但同时也把保罗还给了基督徒——只是这一次，是真正的他，不是教会两千年来制造出的那个滤镜版本。剩下的事，是你的：带着这个新发现的「拉比扫罗」，回到你自己的圣经，回到你自己的会堂，回到你身边的犹太朋友身边。 If after these eight lessons you begin to concede — that the "Paul" you thought you knew was a shadow projected by history, and the real "Rabbi Shaul" was a Jewish brother sleepless with grief over his own people — then this book has done what it set out to do. Rabbi Shapira has given Paul back to the Jews, and at the same time he has given Paul back to the Christians — only this time, the real one, not the filtered version manufactured by two thousand years of Church tradition. What remains is yours: carry this newly-discovered "Rabbi Shaul" back to your Bible, back to your assembly, back to the Jewish friend at your side.
