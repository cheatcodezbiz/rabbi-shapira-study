弥赛亚的诞生
与伊朗的陷落 The Birthing of Messiah
& The Fall of Iran

沙皮拉拉比博士从希伯来妥拉的视角，解读伊朗动荡的末世意义 A prophetic reading of Iran's uprising through the lens of the Hebrew Torah

沙皮拉拉比博士 · Ahavat Ammi 事工 Rabbi Dr. Itzhak Shapira · Ahavat Ammi Ministries
2026 · 5 · 10 · 约 12 分钟阅读~12 min read · ▶ 观看原始视频 (8:06)Watch Original (8:06) ·

在登上飞往以色列航班的数小时前，沙皮拉博士拉比录制了这支沉重的视频——他将前往应许之地，展开一段代祷与守望的时节。他打开《出埃及记》的第一章，将其与从伊朗德黑兰传出的最新头条相互对照。他在那里所看见的令人震动：今天我们所见证的伊朗街头暴动，竟以暗码的形式，被写入了一段三千五百年前的希伯来经文之中——而它们所昭示的，是犹太拉比所称的「弥赛亚的诞生」(הולדת המשיח)。 In a sober video recorded just hours before he boarded a plane for Israel — for what he describes as a season of intercession and prayer — Rabbi Dr. Itzhak Shapira opens the first chapter of the Book of Exodus and reads it against the headlines pouring out of Iran. What he finds there is unsettling: the very rebellions we are watching unfold in Tehran are, in his reading, written in coded form into a 3,500-year-old Hebrew text. They signal nothing less than what the rabbis call the birthing of Mashiach — the Messiah being born to the world.

这是一个份量极重的声明，沙皮拉拉比的论证也甚为谨慎。他并非引用个人异象或私人预言，而是按照犹太智者近两千年来所运用的米大示（Midrash）与塔木德（Talmud）解经传统，逐节研读本周的妥拉段落——《Shemot 段落》，即《出埃及记》的开篇。本文尝试一步步地走过他的论证，在合适之处加以省思，并提出这个问题：对于爱以色列的基督徒而言，这意味着什么？ This is a substantial claim, and Rabbi Shapira makes it carefully. He is not appealing to a personal vision or a private prophecy. He is reading the Torah portion of the week — Parashat Shemot, the opening of Exodus — through the same midrashic and Talmudic methods Jewish sages have used for nearly two thousand years. What follows is an attempt to walk through his argument step by step, to test it where testing is appropriate, and to ask: what does this mean for Christians who love Israel?

一、一段古老的塔木德预言I. An Ancient Talmudic Forecast

论证始于今日的新闻，却根植于塔木德——《Yoma 篇 10a》。在那里，智者们论及末日之前将要兴起并衰落的四大帝国： The argument begins not with the news, but with a line from the Talmud — Yoma 10a. There, the sages discuss the four kingdoms that will rise and fall before the end:

「波斯（Persia）注定要落入罗马（Rome）的手中。」——《塔木德 · Yoma 10a》 "Persia is destined to fall into the hands of Rome." — Talmud, Yoma 10a

在古代拉比的地理观中，「波斯」所指即今日的伊朗；而「罗马」——或以更具神学性的名称「以东」(Edom)——则是指各时代的西方强权。对于第一个千年的拉比而言，罗马指的是罗马帝国以及后来的基督教世界；而对于今日的沙皮拉拉比，「罗马／以东」可合理地包括美国以及更广泛的西方联盟。换言之，塔木德预言：末日的最后一个征兆之一，将是波斯政权的崩溃，并落入西方的势力范围。 In ancient rabbinic geography, Persia is the territory we today call Iran, and Rome — or in its more theological name, Edom — is the great Western power of any given age. For the rabbis of the first millennium, Rome meant the Roman Empire and later Christendom. For Rabbi Shapira, applied to our day, "Rome / Edom" can plausibly include the United States and the broader Western alliance. The Talmud, in other words, predicted that one of the final signs of the end of days would be the collapse of the Persian regime into the orbit of the West.

无论你接受塔木德为权威经典，或仅仅视之为一份冷静的古代见证，这个预言都令人深思。今日伊朗政权的崩塌，按此解读，并非单纯的地缘政治事件——而是一个记号。 Whether one accepts the Talmud as authoritative or simply as a sober ancient witness, the prediction is striking. The collapse of Iran's current regime is, on this reading, not merely geopolitics. It is a marker.

二、本周妥拉段落中的密码II. The Code in This Week's Torah Portion

沙皮拉拉比将其解读锚定于《出埃及记》第一章——希伯来名为「Shemot」(שְׁמוֹת)，意为「众名」。经文以一波压迫的浪潮揭开序幕：法老开始惧怕以色列人的繁衍，于是他召见两位希伯来收生婆： Rabbi Shapira anchors his reading in the very first chapter of Exodus — Shemot (שְׁמוֹת), meaning "Names." It opens with a rising tide of oppression: Pharaoh has begun to fear the multiplication of Israel, and so he turns to two Hebrew midwives:

「埃及王对希伯来的收生婆——一名施弗拉，一名普阿——说话。」——出埃及记 1:15 "And the king of Egypt spoke to the Hebrew midwives, of whom the name of one was Shifra, and the name of the second was Pua." — Exodus 1:15

两位妇人。两个名字。两个看似次要的人物，却撑起了一场庞大的圣经戏剧。然而拉比们始终将这两位收生婆视为神学份量极重的关键：因为正是她们的决定，使摩西得以存活。没有施弗拉与普阿，便没有摩西；没有摩西，便没有出埃及；没有出埃及，便没有以色列。 Two women. Two names. Two seemingly minor figures in a vast biblical drama. And yet the rabbis have always read these two midwives as enormous theological weight-bearers, because their decision was the decision that allowed Moses to live. Without Shifra and Pua, no Moses; without Moses, no Exodus; without Exodus, no Israel.

有一个经典解读认为施弗拉与普阿即是约基别（摩西的母亲）与米利暗（他的姊姊）；但还有一个并行的米大示传统——也是沙皮拉拉比所引用的——将施弗拉与普阿读为「列国中义人的余民」：是敬畏神、拒绝为腐败政权服务的外邦收生婆。无论采用哪一种解读，经文的教导都是一致的：对不公权柄的圣洁反抗，正是拯救者诞生的土壤。 There is a classical reading in which Shifra and Pua are Yocheved (Moses's mother) and Miriam (his sister). But there is a parallel midrashic tradition — and this is the one Rabbi Shapira draws on — that reads Shifra and Pua as standing for the righteous remnant of the nations: Gentile midwives who feared God and refused to serve a corrupt regime. Whichever reading one prefers, the lesson of the text is the same: a moment of holy disobedience against unjust authority becomes the soil out of which the deliverer is born.

三、普阿名字中的数值密码（Gematria）III. The Gematria of Pua

在此，沙皮拉拉比运用了 「Gematria」——犹太人解读希伯来字母数值的传统技艺。他指出：「普阿」(פּוּעָה) 这个名字的数值总和为 161——而这正是希伯来短语「Beit David」（בית דוד），即「大卫家」的数值。 Here Rabbi Shapira draws on gematria, the Jewish discipline of reading the numerical value of Hebrew letters. The name Pua (פּוּעָה), he explains, sums to 161 — the same numerical value as the phrase "Beit David" (בית דוד), the House of David.

其含义令人震动。普阿的名字本身就编码了大卫王朝——根据犹太与基督教的共同传统，弥赛亚正是出自这一王室血脉。她对法老的反抗——她圣洁地拒绝杀害希伯来男婴——历史上保存了那条血脉，最终诞生了摩西，并在预言中指向「大卫之子」。 The implication is electric. Pua's name itself encodes the Davidic dynasty — the royal line out of which, according to both Jewish and Christian tradition, the Messiah comes. Her rebellion against Pharaoh — her holy refusal to murder the Hebrew baby boys — is what historically allowed the survival of the line that would lead, eventually, to Moses, and prophetically, to the Son of David.

对于基督徒读者，这带来特殊的冲击。「Mashiach Ben David」——「大卫之子弥赛亚」——是新约反复用以称呼拿撒勒人耶书亚（Yeshua）的称号。 马太福音以这句话开卷：「亚伯拉罕的后裔，大卫的子孙，耶稣基督的家谱。」沙皮拉拉比所指出的，正是：《出埃及记》第一章中那两位收生婆之一的名字本身，就编码了基督徒所声明耶稣身为弥赛亚的这个称号。 For Christian readers, this hits with particular force. Mashiach Ben David — Messiah, son of David — is the title the New Testament uses repeatedly for Yeshua of Nazareth. Matthew opens his gospel with: "The book of the genealogy of Jesus Christ, the son of David, the son of Abraham." The very name of one of the two midwives in Exodus 1, on Rabbi Shapira's reading, encodes the title that Christians confess about Jesus.

四、隐藏在「敬畏」中的「伊朗」IV. The Hidden Word "Iran"

沙皮拉拉比走得更远。他转向接下来的一节经文——其中圣经说，那两位收生婆「敬畏神」，拒绝了法老的命令。希伯来文中表达「敬畏」的字（在《出埃及记》1:17 的形式中）是「vatira'ena」(וַתִּירֶאןָ)。他指出：在这个字里面，藏着「伊朗」(איראן) 的辅音。 Rabbi Shapira goes further. He turns to the next verse, where Scripture says that the midwives feared God and refused Pharaoh's order. The Hebrew word for "feared" — in the form of Exodus 1:17 — is vatira'ena (וַתִּירֶאןָ). And inside this word, he claims, lie the encoded consonants of "Iran" (איראן).

这是一种字面解读吗？不是——也不应如此呈现。这是 「Drash」——犹太解经中的「讲道层次」，它寻找的是隐藏的模式与共鸣，而非经文的字面意思（Peshat）。这是一种类似于基督教「预表式解读」(typological reading) 的方法。重点并不是说，妥拉真的拼出了「伊朗」这个字；重点是：对于一个从字词「形式」中寻找意义的传统而言，这个描述收生婆神圣敬畏的字，其辅音与我们今日所见、那个正在反抗其腐败政权的国家之名，发生共鸣——这一点本身就极具意涵。 Is this a literal reading? No — and it should not be presented as one. This is drash, the homiletical layer of Jewish interpretation, which looks for hidden patterns and resonances rather than plain meaning (peshat). It is the rabbinic equivalent of a typological reading. The point is not that the Torah literally spells "Iran"; the point is that for a tradition that finds meaning in the form of words, it is striking that the word describing the midwives' holy fear should resonate, in its consonants, with the modern name of the very nation now in revolt against its own corrupt regime.

沙皮拉拉比所指出的模式是这样的：那段描述「对暴政的圣洁反抗」（一场催生救赎者的反抗）的希伯来经文，同时编码了今日我们所看见、正在革命中的那个国家的名字。而其中那位最关键的收生婆——普阿——的名字，则编码了「大卫家」。所以，按他的解读，公式是： The pattern Rabbi Shapira finds is this: the same Hebrew passage that describes a holy rebellion against tyranny — a rebellion that births the deliverer — also encodes the name of the nation we are watching rebel today. And the name of the midwife whose rebellion mattered most, Pua, encodes the House of David. So the equation, in his reading, is:

伊朗反抗其政权 → 普阿反抗法老 → 大卫之子弥赛亚的诞生 Iran rebelling against its regime → Pua rebelling against Pharaoh → the birthing of the Davidic Messiah

五、对于爱以色列的基督徒，这意味着什么？V. What This Means for Christians Who Love Israel

即使我们撇开那些较具推测性的数值对应，沙皮拉拉比所指出的神学模式，也值得每一位相信亚伯拉罕、以撒、雅各的神的基督徒认真省察。 Even setting aside the more speculative gematria, the theological pattern Rabbi Shapira is pointing to deserves careful attention from Christians who believe in the God of Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob.

首先，它将当代事件置于圣经末世论的框架之中。伊朗——以其领导人自己的话来说——将自己定位为现代以色列国最主要的敌人。其飞弹瞄准耶路撒冷；其代理人在多条边界上环绕以色列；近半个世纪以来，「以色列必亡」的口号从其讲坛上不绝于耳。这个政权的崩溃并非历史的旁支故事——根据古代拉比与每日新闻的双重见证，这是一场连希伯来经文本身都已预想的事件。 First, it situates current events within the framework of biblical eschatology. Iran has positioned itself — by its own leaders' words — as the principal modern enemy of the State of Israel. Its missiles are aimed at Jerusalem; its proxies surround Israel on multiple borders. For nearly half a century, "Death to Israel" has been chanted from its podiums. The collapse of that regime is not a side story. It is, on the testimony of both ancient rabbis and the daily news, an event the Hebrew Scriptures themselves contemplated.

其次，它将基督教的末世论带回它的希伯来根源。长久以来，基督教圈子里的末世教导，与孕育它的犹太经文脱节。然而，新约本身就充满了直接源于拉比传统的弥赛亚范畴：「大卫之子」、「弥赛亚的产难」（马太福音 24:8 的希腊文 odinōn，正是希伯来文 chevlei Mashiach——「弥赛亚之痛」这个拉比概念的翻译）、「余民」、「被掳者的归回」。沙皮拉拉比并非在引入陌生的范畴——他在恢复早期教会本就熟悉的范畴。 Second, it returns Christian eschatology to its Hebrew roots. For too long, end-times teaching in Christian circles has been disconnected from the Jewish texts that gave it birth. Yet the New Testament itself is saturated with messianic categories drawn directly from rabbinic tradition: the Son of David, the birth pangs of Messiah (Matthew 24:8 — odinōn, the Greek for birth pangs, is the New Testament rendering of the rabbinic concept of chevlei Mashiach, "the birth pangs of the Messiah"), the remnant, the gathering of the exiles. Rabbi Shapira is not introducing foreign categories. He is restoring categories the early church already knew.

第三，它重新定位了外邦基督徒的角色。沙皮拉拉比所引用的那一个米大示传统，将施弗拉与普阿读为「列国中的人」——她们是敬畏以色列之神的外邦妇女，拒绝为腐败的权柄效力，而她们的忠诚成为了拯救者诞生的关键工具。今日为以色列祷告的基督徒、拒绝当代道德妥协的基督徒、为生命与犹太民族挺身而出的基督徒——他们不是这个故事的旁观者。他们站在施弗拉与普阿的谱系之中。 Third, it reframes the role of Gentile Christians. Shifra and Pua, in the midrashic reading Rabbi Shapira is using, are of the nations. They are Gentile women who feared the God of Israel and refused to serve a corrupt power, and their faithfulness was instrumental in the birthing of the deliverer. Christian believers who pray for Israel, who refuse the moral compromises of their own age, who stand for life and for the Jewish people, are not bystanders in this story. They stand in the line of Shifra and Pua.

六、关于分辨VI. A Word on Discernment

如果本文呈现沙皮拉拉比的解读时，仿佛其中每一个细节都无可置疑，那将既不公平于他、也无益于读者。事实并非如此。Gematria 与 Drash 是解经的艺术，而非数学的证明。明智的读者——犹太人或基督徒——对其中某些字音的对应，会给出不同的权重评估。塔木德关于波斯落入罗马之手的那句话是真实且古老的；而在 vatira'ena 一字中发现「伊朗」，则是一种讲道式的修辞。 It would be unfair to Rabbi Shapira and unhelpful to readers if this article presented his reading as if every detail were beyond question. It is not. Gematria and drash are interpretive arts, not mathematical proofs. Reasonable readers — Jewish and Christian — will weigh some of the verbal coincidences differently. The Talmudic line about Persia falling to Rome is real and ancient; the discovery of "Iran" inside vatira'ena is a homiletical flourish.

但这个更大的模式——即希伯来圣经的神将祂的旨意编织进人类历史的织物之中、妥拉跨越千年仍在发声、义人对不公权柄的反抗正是救赎被诞生的时刻——这个模式是圣经的，而非推测。它在《出埃及记》中。它在福音书中。它是十字架的模式。 But the larger pattern — that the God of the Hebrew Scriptures has woven His purposes into the fabric of human history, that the Torah speaks across millennia, that the rebellion of the righteous against unjust power is the very moment in which deliverance is born — this pattern is biblical, not speculative. It is in Exodus. It is in the Gospels. It is the pattern of the cross.

七、诞生仍在继续VII. The Birthing Continues

沙皮拉拉比以一段任何基督徒读者都能受用的劝勉作为结束——无论他们接受他解经方法到何种程度： Rabbi Shapira closes with a charge that any Christian reader can take to heart, regardless of how much of his interpretive method they accept:

「大卫之子弥赛亚，正在此刻被诞生于这个世界。预备你自己。Am Yisrael Chai——以色列的子民万岁！」 "Mashiach Ben David is being born to the world right now. Get yourself ready. Am Yisrael Chai."

对于犹太听众而言，这是千年来的渴望。对于基督徒听众而言，这个语言则格外铿锵有力：「Mashiach Ben David」——大卫之子弥赛亚——正是拿撒勒的耶书亚，是基督徒早已声明为弥赛亚的那一位。犹太人对「诞生」——对弥赛亚时代破晓——的盼望，在启示录的呼喊中找到了基督教的回响：「主耶稣啊，我愿你来！」 For a Jewish audience, this is the longing of millennia. For a Christian audience, the language is striking: Mashiach Ben David is Yeshua of Nazareth, the One Christians already confess as Messiah. The Jewish hope of a birthing — the messianic age dawning — finds its Christian echo in the cry of Revelation: "Even so, come, Lord Jesus."

无论德黑兰的暴动本身是否就是那一刻，这个呼召是基督徒一向所熟知的：为耶路撒冷的平安祈祷，警醒时兆，预备那位即将到来的王。 Whether or not the riots in Tehran are themselves the moment, the call is one Christians have always known: pray for the peace of Jerusalem, watch the times, and be ready for the King who is coming.
