聆聽羊角號的聲音 Listen to the Sound of the Shofar / Trumpet

在「吹角節」(Yom Teruah) 的全球直播裡，金牧師 (Pastor John Kim) 從妥拉一路講到啟示錄——羊角號的聲音是神給這世代的記號：它呼喚萬民從沉睡中醒來、悔改、預備迎見那要在末次號筒中降臨的君王。 In the global Feast of Trumpets broadcast, Pastor John Kim traces the shofar from the Torah all the way to Revelation — the sound of the ram's horn is God's sign to this generation: a summons to wake from sleep, to return, and to be ready for the King who comes at the last trumpet.

金牧師 (Pastor John Kim) · Ahavat Ammi 事工 Pastor John Kim · Ahavat Ammi Ministries
2026 · 5 · 25 · 約 12 分鐘閱讀~12 min read · ▶ 觀看 · 聆聽羊角號的聲音Watch · Listen to the Sound of the Shofar ·

「我相信，此刻在我們四圍所發生的一切，都是神給我們的記號，是羊角號的聲音。」在「吹角節」(Yom Teruah) 的全球直播裡，從美國維吉尼亞州來的金牧師 (Pastor John Kim) 與沙皮拉拉比同站一處——一位猶太彌賽亞信徒拉比，一位韓裔牧師——一同聆聽那從妥拉一路響到啟示錄的號角。羊角號 שׁוֹפָר shofar（「公羊的角」）不是宗教儀式裡的裝飾，而是神在這末後的世代向萬民發出的呼喚：醒來、悔改、預備迎見那要在末次號筒中降臨的君王。 “I believe everything going on around us is truly God's sign to us — the sound of the trumpet.” In the global Feast of Trumpets (Yom Teruah) broadcast, Pastor John Kim, from Virginia, stands alongside Rabbi Shapira — a Messianic Jewish rabbi and a Korean pastor together — listening to one horn that sounds from the Torah all the way to Revelation. The shofar שׁוֹפָר shofar (“ram's horn”) is no liturgical ornament; it is God's summons to the nations in these last days: wake up, return, and be ready to meet the King who comes at the last trumpet.

一、號角從妥拉響到啟示錄I. The Horn That Sounds from Torah to Revelation

金牧師一開口就把羊角號放回它在整本聖經裡的位置：「正如你們都知道的，吹角節對神有極深的意義。我們看見羊角號從妥拉裡出來，一路出現在新約 (Brit Hadashah)，直到啟示錄。」這聲音不屬於某一個時代——它是一條貫穿整本聖經的線，把摩西在西奈山下所聽見的號角，與約翰在拔摩海島所聽見的號角，連成同一個呼喚。 Pastor Kim begins by placing the shofar back where it belongs in the whole of Scripture: “As you all know, the Feast of Trumpets has a very significant meaning to God. We see the trumpet, the shofar, coming out from the Torah, into the Brit Hadashah (the New Covenant), all the way to Revelation.” This sound belongs to no single age — it is a single thread running through the whole Bible, joining the horn Moses heard at Sinai to the horn John heard on Patmos as one and the same summons.

在希伯來傳統中，吹角節有它自己的名字：יוֹם תְּרוּעָה Yom Teruah，「吹號之日」(day of the blast)，也就是後來所稱的「猶太新年」(Rosh Hashanah)。它不是一個喧鬧的節慶，而是一個警醒之日——號角一響，全會眾就要停下來，側耳聆聽。金牧師說，他相信「此刻正是神揭示這號角意義的時候——從妥拉，也從新約。」 In the Hebrew tradition the Feast of Trumpets has its own name: יוֹם תְּרוּעָה Yom Teruah, “the day of the blast,” later called Rosh Hashanah, the Jewish New Year. It is not a loud party but a day of alertness — when the horn sounds, the whole assembly is to stop and listen. Pastor Kim says he believes “it is the time for God to reveal the meaning of the trumpet — from the Torah, and from the Brit Hadashah.”

二、שׁוֹפָר 羊角號的三種聲音II. The Three Voices of the Shofar

羊角號並不是隨意亂吹的。在傳統的吹角節敬拜中，羊角號有它固定的「聲音語言」，每一種聲音都帶著屬靈的意義： The shofar is not blown at random. In the traditional Feast of Trumpets service the horn has a fixed “language” of sounds, and each sound carries spiritual meaning:

תְּקִיעָה tekiah——一聲悠長、穩定、不間斷的長音，宣告「王來了，站起來！」
תְּרוּעָה teruah——「破碎的號聲」(the broken blast)，一連串急促、破裂、像哭泣或警報般的短音，催逼人心碎、催逼人悔改。
תְּקִיעָה גְּדוֹלָה tekiah gedolah——「大號角」，一聲拉到極長的長音，預表那要召聚萬民的「末次號筒」。——吹角節的羊角號傳統 תְּקִיעָה tekiah — one long, steady, unbroken note, announcing “the King is here; stand up.”
תְּרוּעָה teruah — “the broken blast,” a rapid series of broken, sobbing, alarm-like notes that break the heart and press it toward repentance.
תְּקִיעָה גְּדוֹלָה tekiah gedolah — “the great blast,” a single note drawn out as long as the breath will go, a picture of the “last trumpet” that gathers the nations.— the shofar tradition of Yom Teruah

這三種聲音正好對應金牧師整段信息的脈絡：tekiah 宣告君王已經作王；teruah——那「破碎的號聲」——呼喚這破碎的世代回轉悔改；而 tekiah gedolah 則把我們的眼目舉向那「末次號筒」，舉向主在空中與我們相遇的那一日。羊角號的聲音，本身就是一篇從悔改到迎見君王的講章。 These three voices map onto the very shape of Pastor Kim's message: tekiah announces that the King already reigns; teruah — “the broken blast” — calls a broken generation back to repentance; and tekiah gedolah lifts our eyes to the “last trumpet,” to the day we meet the Lord in the air. The sound of the shofar is itself a sermon, moving from repentance to the meeting of the King.

三、תְּרוּעָה 破碎的號聲 ── 悔改的呼召III. Teruah, the Broken Blast — a Call to Repent

羊角號最核心的聲音，是那「破碎的號聲」תְּרוּעָה teruah。「Teruah」這個字在希伯來文裡，同時帶著「吶喊」「警報」與「破碎的哭聲」的意思——它不是悅耳的旋律，而是叫人扎心的聲音。猶太傳統教導：在吹角節聽見 teruah，就是聽見一個呼召——תְּשׁוּבָה teshuvah，「回轉」(repentance / return)，回轉歸向神。 The shofar's central voice is the broken blast, תְּרוּעָה teruah. In Hebrew the word carries the senses of “shout,” “alarm,” and “broken weeping” all at once — it is not a pleasant melody but a sound meant to pierce the heart. Jewish tradition teaches that to hear the teruah at Yom Teruah is to hear a call to תְּשׁוּבָה teshuvah, “repentance / return,” a turning back to God.

這正是金牧師信息的脈搏。他並不把吹角節當成一場安全距離外的「預言觀察」，而是當成一個悔改的祭壇。在這場直播的末了，他帶領一個極不尋常的舉動：他代表自己的國家，跪下來，向「萬王之王」(King of kings) 認罪——為一種「把那位真正的君王換成別的君王」的「取代」而悔改。 This is the pulse of Pastor Kim's message. He does not treat the Feast of Trumpets as “prophecy-watching” from a safe distance, but as an altar of repentance. At the close of the broadcast he leads an unusual act: as a representative of his own nation he kneels and confesses to the King of kings — repenting of a kind of “replacement,” of swapping the true King for another king.

「諸天哪，側耳聽我們的聲音。我們實在建立了王國，卻擁立了錯誤的王。我們承認自己在我們的王、我們的父面前有罪……今天我們站在你面前，在 Rosh Hashanah——彌賽亞耶書亞與祂百姓的加冕之日。」——金牧師的認罪禱告（節錄） “Heavens, give ear to our voice. We truly built a kingdom, but we enthroned the wrong king. We confess our guilt before our King, our Father… Today we stand before You on Rosh Hashanah — the coronation of Messiah Yeshua with His people.”— from Pastor Kim's confession prayer

這就是 teruah 的果子：聽見那破碎的號聲，就不能再站在觀眾席上——你必須下到悔改的地方，把生命的王位重新交還給那位真正配得的君王。 This is the fruit of the teruah: to hear the broken blast is to be unable to remain a spectator — you must come down to the place of repentance and hand the throne of your life back to the King who alone is worthy of it.

四、末次號筒 ── 我們要在空中迎見主IV. The Last Trumpet — Meeting the Lord in the Air

金牧師的眼目沒有停在現今的危機，而是舉向那「末次號筒」。他說：「在許許多多號角吹響之後，一直到啟示錄的號角吹響之後，我們相信，在末次號筒的時候，我們要在空中與主相遇。」這正是保羅在哥林多前書 15:52 與帖撒羅尼迦前書 4:16 所描寫的盼望——「號筒末次吹響的時候……死人要復活成為不朽壞的，我們也要改變。」 Pastor Kim's eyes do not stop at the present crisis; they lift to the “last trumpet.” He says: “After many trumpets have been blown, all the way to the trumpet of Revelation, we believe that at the last trumpet we will meet the Lord in the air.” This is the hope Paul describes in 1 Corinthians 15:52 and 1 Thessalonians 4:16 — “at the last trumpet… the dead will be raised imperishable, and we shall be changed.”

他把這份盼望說成一種活著的姿態：「願我們持續向那屬靈的號角聲敞開耳朵」——直到天使吹響末次號筒的那一日，「啟示錄的話就要活過來，我們要永遠與主同在。這就是我們今天所緊緊抓住的盼望。」羊角號因此不只是回顧過去的記號，更是向前張望的記號：每一次號聲，都在替那最後一次號聲彩排。 He frames this hope as a posture of living: “as we continue opening our ears to the spiritual sound of the trumpet” — until the day the angels sound the last trumpet, “the words of Revelation will come alive, and we will be with the Lord forever. That is the hope we hold on to today.” The shofar is therefore not only a sign that looks back; it is a sign that looks ahead: every blast is a rehearsal for the final one.

五、號角召聚萬民 ── 在彌賽亞裡成為一V. The Horn Gathers the Nations — One in the Messiah

羊角號在聖經裡一向是召聚的器具——召聚以色列上聖山、召聚百姓赴節期、最終召聚萬民歸向神。金牧師見證說，就在這場直播的當天，他與來自非洲、歐洲、南美洲、以及世界各地的人一同敬拜，深受激勵。號角的聲音跨過了語言與時區的界線。 In Scripture the shofar is always an instrument of gathering — gathering Israel to the holy mountain, gathering the people to the feasts, and finally gathering the nations to God. Pastor Kim testifies that on the very day of this broadcast he worshipped together with people from Africa, Europe, South America, and all over the world, and was deeply stirred. The sound of the horn crossed the boundaries of language and time zone.

「現今我們因著語言、時區、地點而有界限；但將來在神的國度裡，再沒有語言的界限。我們要自由地彼此交通，自由地一同敬拜主。」——金牧師 “Today we may have boundaries because of language; but in the future, in God's kingdom, there will be no more boundaries of language. We will freely communicate, we will freely worship the Lord together.”— Pastor Kim

金牧師把這份合一說得很具體：當以色列百姓歸回那地、聽見福音、並信靠彌賽亞耶書亞時，他們就與列國（外邦人）聯合，在基督裡成為「一個新人」(one new man，弗 2:15)。號角不是要把人分開的聲音，而是要把分散的兒女召聚回同一個家的聲音——「我們在彌賽亞裡是一個身體。」 Pastor Kim makes that unity concrete: when the people of Israel return to the land, hear the gospel, and trust in Messiah Yeshua, they are united with the nations (the Gentiles) into “one new man” in the Messiah (Eph 2:15). The horn is not a sound that divides; it is the sound that gathers scattered children back into one house — “we are one Body in Messiah.”

六、號角是這世代的記號VI. The Horn Is a Sign for This Generation

金牧師反覆回到一個信念：今日世上所發生的一切，都是「神給我們的記號，是羊角號的聲音」。他並不是要人陷入末世的恐慌，而是要人從「沉睡」中醒過來。羊角號在歷史中始終是一個「警醒的鬧鐘」——它的功用不是製造焦慮，而是叫沉睡的人坐起來，重新對齊神的時間表。 Pastor Kim returns again and again to one conviction: everything happening in the world today is “God's sign to us, the sound of the trumpet.” He is not trying to push people into end-times panic, but to wake them from “sleep.” Throughout history the shofar has been an alarm clock for the soul — its purpose is not to manufacture anxiety but to make the sleeping sit up and re-align with God's timetable.

他特別指出神在這個時代的「記號」是三重的：以色列百姓歸回那地；他們聽見福音；他們信靠耶書亞之後，與萬民聯合成為一個新人。他相信外邦人的數目正在添滿，而猶太人歸回的時候已經臨近（羅 11:25）——這一切都隨著號角聲一同響起。當你聽見四圍的「號聲」時，正確的回應不是恐懼，而是 teshuvah：回轉、警醒、舉目仰望。 He names God's “sign” for this age as threefold: the people of Israel returning to the land; their hearing of the gospel; and, once they trust in Yeshua, their uniting with the nations into one new man. He believes the fullness of the Gentiles is being filled up and the hour of Israel's return is drawing near (Rom 11:25) — and all of it sounds together with the horn. When you hear the “blasts” around you, the right response is not fear but teshuvah: turn, stay alert, and lift up your eyes.

七、對華人信徒的意義VII. What This Means for Chinese Believers

第一，吹角節不是猶太人「專屬」的節慶，而是全教會的警鐘。金牧師作為一位外邦牧師站在這場吹角節敬拜中，正是給華人信徒的榜樣：我們不是旁觀者，而是被號角召聚的萬民之一。聽見 teruah，就要回應 teshuvah。 First, the Feast of Trumpets is not a Jewish-only festival but an alarm bell for the whole Church. That Pastor Kim, a Gentile pastor, stands inside this Yom Teruah service is itself a model for Chinese believers: we are not spectators but among the nations the horn gathers. To hear the teruah is to answer with teshuvah.

第二，號角呼召我們從「被動觀看末世」轉向「主動悔改禱告」。許多華人教會的末世論偏向「看戲」——觀察新聞、對照預言。但金牧師的吹角節提醒我們：號角的目的是叫人下到祭壇，不是坐在觀眾席。當你看見世界的動盪，正確的姿態是跪下禱告、為列國與以色列代求。 Second, the horn calls us to move from “passively watching the end times” to “active repentance and prayer.” Much Chinese-church eschatology leans toward spectating — scanning the news, matching it to prophecy. But Pastor Kim's Feast of Trumpets reminds us the horn's purpose is to bring people to the altar, not to the bleachers. When you see the world shaking, the right posture is to kneel, pray, and intercede for the nations and for Israel.

第三，「末次號筒」的盼望，給我們在患難中站立的力量。金牧師在直播中說，他的國家（韓國）經歷過許多苦難、許多被列強欺壓的痛，因此特別能體會以色列的傷痛。華人教會同樣走過苦難——而號角的聲音正是對這樣的教會說的：王要回來，死人要復活，我們要在空中迎見主。這盼望叫我們在最黑暗的時刻，仍能挺身昂首。 Third, the hope of the “last trumpet” gives us strength to stand in tribulation. Pastor Kim says his nation (Korea) has known much suffering and domination by greater powers, and so understands Israel's pain. The Chinese church has walked through suffering too — and the horn speaks precisely to such a church: the King is returning, the dead will rise, and we will meet the Lord in the air. This hope lets us lift our heads even in the darkest hour.

八、關於分辨VIII. A Word on Discernment

金牧師的信息中有一部分是針對韓國這個民族的特定呼召——例如把韓國與以色列並列為「雙胞胎」、視韓國為以賽亞書 60 章裡那「從東方來幫助以色列」的鷹。這些是他作為一位韓裔牧師、在他自己處境裡領受的個人應用與默想，帶有講道者的熱情，未必是每一處都能逐字套用的釋經結論。 Part of Pastor Kim's message addresses a specific calling for the Korean nation — for example, pairing Korea with Israel as “twins,” and reading Korea as the eagle “from the east” who helps Israel in Isaiah 60. These are personal applications and meditations he receives as a Korean pastor in his own context, carrying a preacher's passion, and are not exegetical conclusions to be applied word-for-word everywhere.

但他信息的核心——羊角號是貫穿妥拉到啟示錄的記號、是悔改 (teshuvah) 的呼召、指向末次號筒與主的再來、並召聚萬民在彌賽亞裡合一——都是聖經的明文。利未記 23:24、民數記 29:1、哥林多前書 15:52、帖撒羅尼迦前書 4:16、啟示錄 8–11 章，都把這號角的神學立得穩穩的。聽號角的人，總要分辨：哪些是講道者的熱忱，哪些是聖經白紙黑字的命令。 But the core of his message — that the shofar is a sign running from Torah to Revelation, a call to teshuvah, a pointer to the last trumpet and the Lord's return, and a gathering of the nations into one in the Messiah — is all plain Scripture. Leviticus 23:24, Numbers 29:1, 1 Corinthians 15:52, 1 Thessalonians 4:16, and Revelation 8–11 set this trumpet theology on solid ground. Those who hear the horn must always discern: what is the preacher's zeal, and what is the plain command of Scripture.

九、結語 ── 聽見號角，就要醒來IX. Closing — Hear the Horn, and Wake Up

「願羊角號的聲音吹遍全世界。敞開你的耳朵聽那破碎的號聲——它呼喚你回轉 (תְּשׁוּבָה teshuvah)。預備你自己，因為在末次號筒吹響的時候，王要回來，我們要在空中迎見祂。從妥拉到啟示錄，這是同一個聲音。聽見了，就要醒來。」 “Let the sound of the shofar be blown across all the world. Open your ears to the broken blast — it is calling you to return (תְּשׁוּבָה teshuvah). Prepare yourself, for at the last trumpet the King is returning and we will meet Him in the air. From the Torah to Revelation, it is one and the same sound. If you have heard it — wake up.”
