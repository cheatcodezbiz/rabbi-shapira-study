中国的余民与末世嫁接的艺术 China's Remnant and the End-Time Art of Grafting

沙皮拉拉比博士从中国大陆现场开讲，宣告一件令他震惊的事：Shiur Torah 第一次从中国发向全世界，而《弥迦书》七章 15 节所应许的「如同出埃及那样的奇事」(niflaot) 正在末世的中国被开启。 Speaking live from mainland China, Rabbi Dr. Shapira opens with what he calls an astonishing reality: a Shiur Torah is being broadcast for the first time from China to the entire world, and the niflaot (wonders) of Micah 7:15 — the promise of an Exodus-style intervention — are being uncovered in the China of the last days.

拉比沙皮拉博士这一次没有从耶路撒冷开讲，而是从中国内地开讲。他没有露面的弟兄姊妹围着他，他们用流利的希伯来文回应他、提问他、与他同唱。「我看到了，意识到了，在中国里面有一群真正的余民正在聚集在一起。」这不是套话。在拉比看来，这是末世预言图景的一块——他要把这块拼图与《弥迦书》七章 15 节连起来：「我要把奇事 (niflaot, נפלאות) 显给他们看，正如出埃及之日。」如果先知所说为真，那么末世的「出埃及」并不只发生在以色列——它正在中国被开启。本文是这场讲解的纵深展开：余民的形成、Parasha 与生命的对应、Micah 7:15 的预言份量，以及为何「嫁接的艺术」(art of grafting) 才是末世中国基督徒的真正身份。 This time Rabbi Dr. Shapira opens not from Jerusalem but from mainland China. The brothers and sisters around him — faces hidden for safety — respond to him in fluent Hebrew, ask him questions, sing with him. “I've seen and realized that a group of true remnants is gathering together in China.” This is not a rhetorical flourish. In the rabbi's reading, it is one piece of the end-time prophetic picture, and he sets out to connect it directly to Micah 7:15: “As in the days when you came forth out of Egypt, I will show you wonders (niflaot, נפלאות).” If the prophet means what he says, then a last-days Exodus is not unfolding only in Israel — it is being unsealed in China. What follows is the long arc of that teaching: the formation of the remnant, the weekly Parasha as a living mirror, the prophetic weight of Micah 7:15, and why the art of grafting turns out to be the real identity of the Chinese believer in the last days.

一、史无前例 ── 第一次从中国向万国发出的 Shiur TorahI. Unprecedented — A Shiur Torah from China to the Nations

拉比开场就抓住一个历史性的瞬间：这是第一次，一个传统的Shiur Torah（שיעור תורה，「妥拉讲解」）不是从耶路撒冷、不是从纽约、不是从特拉维夫——而是从中国内地发向整个世界。这不是普通的网络直播。在拉比看来，这是末世神工的一个公开记号。 The rabbi opens by seizing a historic moment: for the first time, a traditional Shiur Torah (שיעור תורה, “Torah lesson”) is being broadcast not from Jerusalem, not from New York, not from Tel Aviv — but from mainland China to the entire world. This is not a routine livestream. In the rabbi's reading, it is a public sign of God's last-days work.

「我非常兴奋，因为今天的这个关于妥拉的讲解是在中国发生的，发放到全世界。」——沙皮拉拉比博士 “It's amazing to me that the Shiur Torah today is happening in China and being broadcast to the entire world.”— Rabbi Dr. Itzhak Shapira

为了保护这群信徒，镜头里看不见他们的脸——但他们的声音听得见。「他们嗓子很大。」拉比笑着说。在他的眼里，这种「没有脸却有声音」的图景本身就是末世的余民的图景：在世界看来他们是隐形的，但他们的呼求与赞美却响到天上去。余民 (Remnant, 漁民/she'erit, שארית)从来都是这样诞生的：在一个不友善的时代，藏在一个不显眼的地方，却被神记念。 To protect this circle of believers, the camera does not show their faces — but their voices are unmistakable. “They are loud,” the rabbi grins. To him this image — faces hidden but voices ringing — is itself a portrait of the end-time remnant: invisible to the world, but their cry and their praise reach heaven. The Remnant (She'erit, שארית) is always born this way: in an unfriendly age, tucked into an unremarkable place, yet remembered by God.

拉比还提到一件让他震惊的事：「我居然能够在中国，跟人用希伯来文畅通地交流。」三千年前的语言，从一群中国信徒的口里说出来——这本身就是 Micah 7:15 所说的「奇事」(niflaot)的一个微小却真实的证据。 He notes something else that astonished him: “I found people I can converse with in Hebrew, here in China.” A language three millennia old, spoken fluently by a circle of Chinese believers — this itself is a small but real instance of the very niflaot (“wonders”) that Micah 7:15 describes.

二、每週的妥拉份額 (Parasha) ── 永不失準的鏡子II. The Weekly Torah Portion (Parasha) — A Mirror That Never Misses

在拉比沙皮拉的传统里，每一周的Parasha（פרשה，「妥拉份额」）都不是死字句，而是一面活的镜子，对应着会众那一周正在经历的真实生命。拉比甚至以一种近乎敬畏的语气说：「很奇妙的就是，每周的妥拉份额，正巧的就是讲述着我们正在经历的事情。」 In Rabbi Shapira's tradition, the weekly Parasha (פרשה, “Torah portion”) is not a dead text but a living mirror that matches what the community is actually walking through that week. He says it with something close to reverence: “It's amazing how it's never failed and how the Torah is always speaking about what's happening.”

「每一周我们都有一个所谓的妥拉份额。然后对应妥拉份额，我们来学习自己生命里面正在发生的一些事情。很奇妙的就是，每周的妥拉份额，正巧的就是讲述着我们正在经历的事情。」——沙皮拉拉比博士 “Every Shabbat we read the Parasha. And we see what's happening in our life. It's amazing how it has never failed — the Torah is always speaking about what is happening.”— Rabbi Dr. Itzhak Shapira

本周的份额是Terumah（תרומה，「举献物」、「奉献」），讲的是会幕的建造、金、银、铜、紫、蓝、朱红丝线、皂荚木——一切都按神所示的样式。把这份妥拉份额读在中国，意味着什么？拉比的判断是：神正在中国的内地立一个属灵的会幕——不是石头的，不是金子的，而是一群愿意被神居住的心。 This week's portion is Terumah (תרומה, “offering” / “heave-offering”) — the building of the Tabernacle: gold, silver, bronze, purple, blue, scarlet, acacia wood, all according to the pattern God showed. To read this Parasha here, the rabbi argues, is no coincidence: God is laying a spiritual Tabernacle inside mainland China — not of stone, not of metal, but of a community of hearts willing to be indwelt.

三、《弥迦书》七章 15 节 ── 「我要把奇事显给他们看，正如出埃及之日」III. Micah 7:15 — “I Will Show Them Wonders, as in the Days of the Exodus”

拉比走进他论证的核心：《弥迦书》第七章十五节。希伯来文：Ki'mei tzeitcha me'eretz Mitzrayim ar'enu nifla'ot（כִּימֵי צֵאתְךָ מֵאֶרֶץ מִצְרָיִם אַרְאֶנּוּ נִפְלָאוֹת）。「正如你出埃及之日，我要将奇事显给他看。」 Now the rabbi moves to the core verse: Micah 7:15. In Hebrew: Ki'mei tzeitcha me'eretz Mitzrayim ar'enu nifla'ot (כִּימֵי צֵאתְךָ מֵאֶרֶץ מִצְרָיִם אַרְאֶנּוּ נִפְלָאוֹת). “As in the days when you came forth out of the land of Egypt, I will show him wonders.”

「神说，我要把启示，启示就是神迹 (niflaot)，显示给他们看，好像出埃及地的时候一样。彌迦正在跟我們說一件很重要的事情。」——沙皮拉拉比博士 “God says, I will show them revelation — and revelation means miracles (niflaot) — just as in the days when they came out of the land of Egypt. Micah is telling us something very important.”— Rabbi Dr. Itzhak Shapira

希伯来文niflaot（נפלאות）不是日常的「好事」、「祝福」——它特指出埃及式的裂海级的奇事：法老的全心刚硬，神的十灾，过红海，云柱火柱，西奈山的雷与号声。Yalkut Shimoni §499 等末世midrash（米德拉什）传统也把niflaot读为「末世前夕、列国前的最后一场神迹大彰显」。拉比的解读极为大胆：这一波niflaot已经开始，它的舞台之一就是末世的中国。 The Hebrew niflaot (נפלאות) does not mean ordinary blessings — it points specifically to Exodus-magnitude wonders: Pharaoh's hardened heart, the ten plagues, the parting of the Red Sea, the pillars of cloud and fire, the thunder of Sinai. Late-Second-Temple and rabbinic midrash (notably Yalkut Shimoni §499) read niflaot as “the final great display of signs before the nations on the eve of the Messianic age.” Rabbi Shapira's reading is bold: this wave of niflaot has already begun, and one of its stages is last-days China.

请注意这个对应：出埃及不是从「以色列」开始——它是从埃及开始，从一个外邦帝国、一个奴役之地开始。先知故意用「出埃及」作类比，意思是：末世的奇事并不会只在以色列的境内发生——它会发生在原本看似与神最远的地方。一个无神论政权下的中国，一个共产党管辖下的内地——按弥迦的语法，正是niflaot最合适的舞台。 Catch the analogy: the Exodus did not begin in Israel; it began in Egypt — inside a foreign empire, a land of bondage. The prophet deliberately uses the Exodus as the template, which means: the last-days wonders will not occur only within Israel's borders — they will erupt in the places that look furthest from God. China, under an atheist government, in the heart of the interior — by Micah's grammar, this is exactly the right stage for niflaot.

四、你是约瑟的余民吗？(Are you the Joseph Remnant?)IV. Are You the Joseph Remnant?

拉比在这场讲解里预告了第二天的深入课程，题目让人难忘：「Are you the Joseph remnant?」——「你是不是约瑟的余民？」这个问题不是修辞——它是一把钥匙。约瑟是「失散于列国」的那一支：他被卖到埃及、改换名字、穿外邦的衣裳、与外邦女子结婚、生下「玛拿西」和「以法莲」。他在外邦中，却没有被外邦吞掉。他反而成了把全家从饥荒中救出来的那一位。 Within this same teaching the rabbi previews the next day's deep-dive course, with a title that lingers: “Are you the Joseph remnant?” The question is not rhetorical — it is a key. Joseph is the brother who was scattered into the nations: sold into Egypt, renamed, dressed in foreign clothes, married to a foreign woman, fathering Manasseh and Ephraim. He was in the nations — but the nations did not swallow him. Instead he became the one who, from inside Egypt, saved his whole family from famine.

弥赛亚犹太传统里有一个深刻的概念：Mashiach ben Yosef（משיח בן יוסף，「约瑟之子的弥赛亚」）——那位先于Mashiach ben David（大卫之子的弥赛亚）出现、为以色列与列国之间打开通路的受苦弥赛亚之表征。「约瑟余民」是这一血脉的延伸：散在万国、外表像外邦、内心却被神纪念、并在末世被神用来救自己的家的一群人。 Messianic-Jewish tradition carries a deep concept: Mashiach ben Yosef (משיח בן יוסף, “Messiah son of Joseph”) — the suffering-servant figure who precedes Mashiach ben David (Messiah son of David), opening the path between Israel and the nations. The “Joseph remnant” is the human extension of that lineage: a people scattered among the nations, outwardly looking foreign, inwardly remembered by God, and in the last days used by Him to rescue the family.

拉比的暗示再清楚不过：在他眼中，中国的余民正是这条「约瑟脉络」上的一支。看起来彻底是「外邦人」、看起来在另一个法老的辖区、看起来与以色列毫无关系——但这正是约瑟的处境，也正是niflaot开始展开的地方。 The rabbi's implication is unmistakable: in his eyes, the Chinese remnant stands on this same Joseph line. They look entirely Gentile, they live under another Pharaoh's jurisdiction, they appear unrelated to Israel — but this is precisely Joseph's situation, and precisely the stage on which niflaot begin to unfold.

五、嫁接的艺术 ── 不是替换，而是连接V. The Art of Grafting — Not Replacement, but Connection

整场讲解最让中国基督徒应当停下来思想的，是拉比一直在背景里指向的那个意象：嫁接 (grafting)。使徒保罗在《罗马书》十一章用「橄榄树」描写以色列与外邦：外邦信徒不是另起一棵新树，乃是「逆性嫁接到那棵好橄榄树上」(罗马书 11:17, 24)。 The image quietly running through the entire teaching — and what every Chinese Christian should slow down to consider — is grafting. In Romans 11, Paul describes Israel and the nations with the olive tree: the Gentile believer does not start a new tree, but is “grafted, contrary to nature, into the good olive tree” (Romans 11:17, 24).

「不要向旧枝子夸口；若是夸口，当知道不是你托着根，乃是根托着你。…他们因不信，所以被折下来；你因信，所以立得住。」——罗马书 11:18, 20 “Do not boast against the branches… you do not support the root, the root supports you. They were broken off because of unbelief, and you stand by faith.”— Romans 11:18, 20

嫁接是一门艺术，不是一道宣言。园丁不能强行把一根野枝按到树干上就完事——他要削、要对齐、要捆扎、要等待树皮愈合、要看树液是否真的流通。这正是拉比这一周做的事：他不是来「告诉中国人他们其实就是犹太人」，他也不是来「让中国基督徒模仿犹太礼仪」——他是在做嫁接的园丁：让一棵真正的中国树枝对齐那棵被神栽种了三千年的橄榄树，让两边的树液真正流通。 Grafting is an art, not a declaration. The gardener cannot just press a wild shoot against the trunk and walk away — he has to cut, align, bind, wait for the bark to heal, and watch whether sap actually flows. That is exactly what the rabbi is doing this week: he is not here to “tell Chinese believers they are secretly Jewish,” nor to “get Chinese Christians to imitate Jewish ritual.” He is doing the gardener's work — aligning a real Chinese branch with the olive tree God has been cultivating for three thousand years, so that the sap actually flows.

中国漁民 (yumin, 渔民——拉比反复使用的词，字面是「捕鱼的人」，呼应主对彼得的呼召「我要叫你们得人如得鱼」(马太福音 4:19)) 在这棵橄榄树上的位置，不在叶子，而在枝子。叶子可以飘走，枝子要承担果子。嫁接成功的标志，是结同样的果子。 The Chinese yumin (渔民, the “fishers” — a word the rabbi keeps returning to, echoing Yeshua's call to Peter, “I will make you fishers of men”, Matthew 4:19) take their place on this olive tree not as decorative leaves but as branches. Leaves can blow away; branches carry fruit. The sign that a graft has succeeded is that it bears the same fruit.

六、为什么是中国？── 出埃及式末世预言的地理学VI. Why China? — The Geography of an Exodus-Style Prophecy

拉比并没有冷冰冰地论证为什么是中国。但他的语气里、他选的经文里、他强调的「正在聚集」、「正在形成」、「正在用希伯来文交流」——他在说：神的工作不是「未来式」，而是「现在进行式」。 The rabbi does not coldly argue why it is China. But his tone, his text choices, his emphasis — “now gathering,” “now forming,” “now conversing in Hebrew” — all say one thing: God's work here is not a future tense, it is present.

拉比传统中，万国之间神一直保留有「chasidei umot ha-olam」（חסידי אומות העולם，「列国中的义人」）。先知以赛亚预言「我要差遣那些逃脱的，到列国去，到他施 (Tarshish)、普勒 (Pul)、路德 (Lud)、土巴 (Tubal)、雅完 (Yavan) 那遥远的海岛去」(以赛亚书 66:19)——希伯来传统中这一连串地名指向地极的远方。中国不在这份古名单里，但它在「地极」这个范畴里，且地理位置最为遥远。 Rabbinic tradition has always recognized God's chasidei umot ha-olam (חסידי אומות העולם, “righteous of the nations”) scattered throughout the Gentile world. Isaiah prophesies, “I will send those who escape to the nations — to Tarshish, Pul, Lud, Tubal, Yavan, and the distant isles” (Isaiah 66:19) — a list the Hebrew tradition reads as pointing to the farthest ends of the earth. China is not in the ancient name-list, but it sits squarely inside the category “ends of the earth” — and is geographically the most distant of all.

在拉什 (Rashi)、拉达克 (Radak)、以及《Yalkut Shimoni》§499 的末世传统中，niflaot 临到的迹象之一，就是列国中的隐藏者突然开始认识神的名。从这个角度看，过去三十年中国家庭教会的爆炸性增长、家庭式的彼此牧养、地下的妥拉学习——并不是一个偶然的宗教社会学现象。它有可能是先知所预见的「奇事」正在地极慢慢启幕。 In Rashi, Radak, and the eschatological strand of Yalkut Shimoni §499, one of the marks that the niflaot are dawning is that the hidden ones in the nations suddenly begin to know the Name of God. Viewed through this lens, the explosive growth of the Chinese house-church movement, household-scale discipleship, and underground Torah study over the past thirty years is not just a sociological curiosity. It may be the prophet's “wonders” slowly opening their curtain at the ends of the earth.

七、对神在末世的工作要敏感VII. Sensitivity to What God Is Doing in the Last Days

拉比反覆嘱咐一句话：「我们必须要敏感，神在末世正在做的工。」这句话听起来温和，其实极为锋利。在末世，敏感的反面是麻木——把神迹当作偶然，把先知当作过时，把弟兄当作陌生人，把以色列当作政治议题，把中国家庭教会当作社会现象。一个麻木的世代，会在niflaot真正展开时认不出。 The rabbi repeats one charge: “We must be sensitive to what God is doing in the last days.” It sounds gentle. It is in fact razor-sharp. In the last days, the opposite of sensitivity is numbness — treating miracles as coincidence, treating prophets as obsolete, treating brothers as strangers, treating Israel as a political issue, treating China's house churches as a sociological curiosity. A numb generation will fail to recognize the niflaot when they actually arrive.

「我们必须要敏感，神在末世正在做的工。…有一个圣洁圣文经节，关于末世是有一个重要重要的意义。」——沙皮拉拉比博士 “We all need to be sensitive to what God is doing in the last days. There is one holy scripture verse that holds important, important significance regarding the last days.”— Rabbi Dr. Itzhak Shapira

敏感是有代价的。它意味着你愿意把一些「巧合」重新看作「记号」；愿意把一些「人的故事」重新看作「神的故事」；愿意为以色列、为中国家庭教会、为那看不见脸却嗓门很大的弟兄姊妹付上你的祷告时间、你的话语成本、甚至你的安全感。 Sensitivity has a price. It means being willing to re-read “coincidences” as signs; willing to re-read “human stories” as “God's story”; willing to pay — in prayer time, in conversational courage, sometimes in personal safety — for Israel, for China's house churches, for the brothers and sisters whose faces are hidden but whose voices are loud.

八、对中国基督徒，这意味着什么？VIII. What This Means for Chinese Christians

第一，你不是「来晚了的旁听者」。在弥迦的语法里，你的位置在niflaot开启的第一线。那位在「无神论的国土」里把心交给耶书亚 (Yeshua) 的中国家庭教会信徒，按拉比的解读，就是「以色列出埃及」类比里的另一支队伍——同一位神，同一种niflaot，只是从另一片土地上出来。 First, you are not a latecomer in the back row. In Micah's grammar, your seat is in the front row of the niflaot. The Chinese house-church believer who gave her heart to Yeshua under an officially atheist government is, on the rabbi's reading, another column in the same Exodus — same God, same wonders, leaving a different Egypt.

第二，你的「约瑟身份」必须被严肃看待。约瑟在外邦中保持洁净（不与波提乏的妻子犯罪），约瑟在外邦中保持身份（他对哥哥们说「我是约瑟，我们的父亲还在吗？」，创世记 45:3），约瑟在外邦中预备粮仓——为饥荒、为列国、为家。如果你是「约瑟余民」中的一员，那么洁净、身份、预备这三件事，就是你在末世的功课。 Second, your “Joseph identity” must be taken seriously. Joseph stayed clean in the foreign land (refusing Potiphar's wife). Joseph kept his identity in the foreign land (“I am Joseph — is my father still alive?”, Genesis 45:3). Joseph stored grain in the foreign land — for famine, for the nations, for the family. If you are part of the “Joseph remnant,” then purity, identity, and provision are your three end-times disciplines.

第三，与以色列站在一起，并不是「外交立场」——它是橄榄树枝对橄榄树根的承认。「不是你托着根，乃是根托着你。」(罗马书 11:18) 当你为以色列祷告、当你为耶路撒冷求平安 (诗篇 122:6, Sha'alu shelom Yerushalayim, שאלו שלום ירושלים)、当你拒绝把反犹的暗流吸到自己的嘴里——你就是在演练「嫁接的艺术」。 Third, standing with Israel is not a “diplomatic stance” — it is the branch recognizing the root. “You do not support the root; the root supports you” (Romans 11:18). When you pray for Israel, when you ask for the peace of Jerusalem (Psalm 122:6, Sha'alu shelom Yerushalayim, שאלו שלום ירושלים), when you refuse to let antisemitic undercurrents into your own mouth — you are practicing the art of grafting.

第四，你的隐藏不是缺点。约瑟在埃及隐藏了很多年。但有一天，神把他从监狱里召出来，让他在法老面前讲解梦。那一天到的时候，他的预备就显出来。中国家庭教会今天的隐藏——那些「不露脸的弟兄姊妹」——并不是神的失败；它是约瑟在波提乏家里、在监牢里的那一段。niflaot会在合适的时间到。 Fourth, your hiddenness is not a flaw. Joseph stayed hidden in Egypt for years. Then one day God called him out of the dungeon to interpret a dream before Pharaoh — and his long preparation paid off in a single morning. The hiddenness of China's house churches today — “the brothers and sisters whose faces are not shown” — is not God's failure; it is Joseph's stretch inside Potiphar's house and inside the prison. The niflaot arrive in their proper hour.

九、关于分辨 ── 这是 Drash，不是 P'shatIX. A Word on Discernment — This is Drash, not P'shat

如果这篇文章把拉比的每一句话都当作字面层次的预言应许，那将既不公平于他，也无益于读者。事实并非如此。把《弥迦书》七章 15 节的niflaot具体应用在「2026 年的中国家庭教会」——这是drash（דרש，讲道与启发层次的解经），不是字面的p'shat（פשט）。这是一个极具属灵份量的应用，但它仍是应用，不是字面的「这一节就是讲中国」。 It would be unfair to Rabbi Shapira and unhelpful to readers to present every line of his teaching as a literal-level prophetic prediction. It is not. Applying Micah 7:15's niflaot specifically to “China's house churches in 2026” is drash (דרש, the homiletical/applicational layer of interpretation) — not p'shat (פשט, the plain reading). It is a spiritually weighty application, but it remains an application, not the literal sense “this verse is about China.”

同样，「约瑟余民」是弥赛亚犹太传统中一个有力但建构性的概念——不是一节单一经文的字面教义。聪明的读者要分辨：哪些是经文清楚说的（神要在末世显出 Exodus 式的奇事；外邦信徒是嫁接进橄榄树的；神有「列国中的义人」），哪些是拉比的应用（这正在中国发生；中国家庭教会正是约瑟脉络中的一支）。前者是经文，后者是受圣灵感动的应用——值得严肃考量，但不应等同于经文本身。 Likewise, the “Joseph remnant” is a powerful but constructive concept within Messianic-Jewish tradition — not the literal doctrine of any one verse. Wise readers should distinguish: what the text plainly says (that God will display Exodus-style wonders in the last days, that Gentile believers are grafted into the olive tree, that God has His “righteous of the nations”) from the rabbi's application (that this is happening in China now, that the Chinese remnant stands on the Joseph line). The first is Scripture; the second is Spirit-led application — to be weighed seriously, but never collapsed into Scripture itself.

但是——即使我们对每一个具体应用都保留谨慎——那个更大的图景仍然站立：神信实地在万国中工作；他对以色列的应许并未废去；他的奇事 (niflaot) 在末世必再次被显出；外邦人因信被嫁接进同一棵橄榄树。这个图景是经文本身的图景，不是推测。它在弥迦书里。它在罗马书里。它在我主耶书亚的事工里。 And yet — even if we hold every specific application with appropriate caution — the larger picture still stands: God is faithful in His work among the nations; His promises to Israel are not annulled; His niflaot will be displayed again in the last days; Gentile believers are grafted by faith into the one olive tree. This larger picture is biblical, not speculative. It is in Micah. It is in Romans. It is in the ministry of our Lord Yeshua.

十、呼召 ── 嗓子很大的余民X. The Call — A Remnant with a Loud Voice

拉比这场讲解的开始充满笑声：「他们嗓子很大。」这不仅仅是一个温暖的玩笑。在末世的预言图景中，嗓子大不大、要不要为以色列开口、敢不敢在网络平台上承认耶书亚 (Yeshua) 是弥赛亚——这一切，决定了你是「叶子」还是「枝子」。叶子飘走，枝子结果子。 The rabbi opens this teaching with a laugh: “They are loud.” It is more than a warm joke. In the end-times prophetic picture, whether your voice is loud, whether you will open your mouth for Israel, whether you dare to confess Yeshua as Messiah on the platforms watching you — these decide whether you are a leaf or a branch. Leaves drift away. Branches carry fruit.

中国的余民——隐藏的脸、流利的希伯来文、第一次从中国发到全世界的Shiur Torah——是一个先知性的预兆。它在告诉每一位读到这篇文章的华人基督徒：你已经站在 niflaot 的边缘上。问题不是「神是否要做事」——他正在做。问题是「你愿不愿意被算入那群嗓子很大的余民里」。 China's remnant — hidden faces, fluent Hebrew, the first-ever Shiur Torah from China to the nations — is a prophetic sign. It tells every Chinese Christian reading this article: you are already standing at the edge of niflaot. The question is not “Will God act?” — He is already acting. The question is: “Will you be counted among the loud remnant?”

「神说，我要把启示，启示就是神迹 (niflaot)，显示给他们看，好像出埃及地的时候一样。彌迦正在跟我們說一件很重要的事情。中国的弟兄姊妹们，请你们要敏感神在末世正在做的工——你们就是那群嗓子很大的余民。」——沙皮拉拉比博士 “God says: I will show them revelation — and revelation means miracles — as in the days when they came out of Egypt. Micah is telling us something very important. Brothers and sisters in China: be sensitive to what God is doing in the last days. You are the loud remnant.”— Rabbi Dr. Itzhak Shapira
