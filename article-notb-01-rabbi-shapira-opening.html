新娘的約定：5786 新娘之夜開幕 The Appointment of the Bride: Opening the Night of the Bride 5786

從北美到南美，從歐洲到非洲，從澳洲到亞洲——全世界都在同一個「約定」(mo'ed) 下相聚。沙皮拉拉比博士為這場史詩級的全球五旬節 (Shavuot) 直播揭幕：今年的主題是「為婚禮而戰」(Milchemet HaChatunah)——神作為一位戰士回來，為祂的婚禮、為祂的chuppah (婚棚)、為祂唯一的新婦而戰。 From North America to South America, from Europe to Africa, from Australia to Asia — the whole world is gathered under one mo'ed, one divine appointment. Rabbi Dr. Itzhak Shapira opens this epic worldwide Shavuot broadcast and names this year's theme: Milchemet HaChatunah — “The War for the Wedding.” God is returning as a warrior, fighting for His wedding, for His chuppah, for His one and only bride.

沙皮拉拉比博士 · Ahavat Ammi 事工 Rabbi Dr. Itzhak Shapira · Ahavat Ammi Ministries
2026 · 5 · 23 · 约 8 分钟阅读~8 min read · ▶ 觀看 · 開場Watch · Opening Segment ·

「שָׁלוֹם Shalom，大家晚安。」沙皮拉拉比 (Rabbi Shapira) 用希伯來語的「平安」開啟了一場橫跨六大洲的史詩級直播——5786 年「新娘之夜」(Night of the Bride / Leil HaKallah ליל הכלה)。「無論您身處何方，」他說，「從非洲到亞洲，從北美到南美，我們今天都正朝著一個約定 (mo'ed) 前進。」這個「mo'ed」(מועד) 不是普通的會議——它在希伯來聖經中專指神親自設立的相會時刻。今晚的這個 mo'ed，就是神給以色列、給萬國、給祂自己唯一的新婦的一個約定。 “שָׁלוֹם Shalom, and good evening.” With the Hebrew greeting of peace, Rabbi Shapira opens an epic worldwide livestream that spans six continents — the 5786 Night of the Bride (Leil HaKallah, ליל הכלה). “Wherever you are,” he says, “from Africa to Asia, from North America to South America, we are all heading today toward an appointment (mo'ed).” That word mo'ed (מועד) is not an ordinary meeting — in the Hebrew Bible it refers to a time of meeting that God Himself has set. Tonight's mo'ed is the appointment God has made with Israel, with the nations, and with His one and only bride.

一、一個全球的約定 (Mo'ed)I. One Global Appointment (Mo'ed)

拉比沙皮拉一開口便鋪開地理：「如果您在北美或南美，我們即將進入新娘之夜；如果您在歐洲，您正在迎接新娘之日；如果您在澳洲，您已經深入您自己的世界。」這不是修辭裝飾——這是對「同時性」的宣告。從加州到首爾、從聖保羅到開普敦、從台北到墨爾本——在不同的時區、不同的語言、不同的文化背景中，神的子民正同時抬起頭來，朝同一個約定前進。 Rabbi Shapira opens by mapping the globe: “If you are in North America or South America, we are entering the Night of the Bride; if you are in Europe, you are waking up to the Day of the Bride; if you are in Australia, you are already deep inside your world.” This is not rhetorical decoration — it is a declaration of simultaneity. From California to Seoul, from São Paulo to Cape Town, from Taipei to Melbourne — across different time zones, languages, and cultures, the people of God lift their heads at the same moment and turn toward the same appointment.

這場直播正在 Avat Ami International Network 上同步播出。它不是一個地方教會的特別聚會，也不是某個宗派的內部會議——它是一個全球的、跨洲的、跨語言的聚集。沙皮拉拉比指出：這正是「mo'ed」一字本來的份量。在利未記 23 章，神所定的「聖會」(miqra'ei kodesh)——逾越節、五旬節 (Shavuot)、住棚節——本來就是呼召全民、跨越邊界、同時聚集的神聖時刻。 The broadcast is going out simultaneously on Ahavat Ammi International Network. It is not a local-church special meeting, not an internal denominational event — it is a global, multi-continental, multilingual gathering. Rabbi Shapira lets the word mo'ed carry its full biblical weight: in Leviticus 23, the “holy convocations” (miqra'ei kodesh) that God Himself sets — Passover, Shavuot, Sukkot — are precisely those sacred moments meant to summon the whole people across all borders to gather as one.

二、新娘之夜 (Leil HaKallah)II. Night of the Bride (Leil HaKallah)

為什麼叫「新娘之夜」？拉比沙皮拉解釋：「這個約定，朋友們，是上帝與我們所有人之間一個非常特別的約定。今晚擺在我們面前的，是上帝賜下《律法》(Torah) 給以色列的一天。」 Why call it the Night of the Bride? Rabbi Shapira explains: “This appointment, friends, is a very special appointment that the God of Israel has with all of us. This evening that is before us is the day that God gave Israel the Torah.”

這把我們直接帶回西奈山下。出埃及記 19 章告訴我們：神在第三個月，藉著火、雲、號聲，與以色列立約。在猶太傳統中，西奈山下的這場相遇從來都是用婚禮的語言來理解的——神是新郎 (Chatan חתן)，以色列是新娘 (Kallah כלה)，妥拉 (Torah תורה) 就是婚約 (ketubah כתובה)，西奈山 就是chuppah (婚棚 חופה)。猶太人在每年的五旬節 (Shavuot שבועות) 都會徹夜誦讀妥拉——叫做「Tikkun Leil Shavuot」——就是在重演當年新娘等待新郎赴婚禮的那一夜。 This takes us straight back to the foot of Sinai. Exodus 19 tells us that in the third month, through fire and cloud and trumpet blast, God cut covenant with Israel. In Jewish tradition, that meeting at Sinai has always been understood in the language of a wedding: God is the Bridegroom (Chatan, חתן); Israel is the Bride (Kallah, כלה); the Torah (תורה) is the ketubah (כתובה, the marriage contract); and Sinai itself is the chuppah (חופה, the bridal canopy). Each year at Shavuot (שבועות), Jews stay up the entire night studying Torah — the Tikkun Leil Shavuot — re-enacting that very night when the bride awaited her bridegroom for the wedding.

所以「新娘之夜」並非詩意的隱喻，而是歷史的、聖約的、婚姻的實在。沙皮拉拉比說：今晚的直播，就是與全世界一同重演 (reenactment) 出埃及記、五旬節、與使徒行傳第二章——因為使徒行傳第二章的五旬節，正是同一場婚禮的另一波客人入席：聖靈降臨，外邦人被接入新娘的家。 So the “Night of the Bride” is not a poetic metaphor — it is a historical, covenantal, marital reality. Tonight's broadcast, Rabbi Shapira says, is a worldwide reenactment of Exodus, of Shavuot, and of Acts chapter 2 — because Acts 2's Pentecost is another wave of guests being seated at the same wedding: the Spirit is poured out and the nations are grafted into the bride's house.

三、為婚禮而戰 (Milchemet HaChatunah)III. The War for the Wedding (Milchemet HaChatunah)

「今年的主題是基於先知以賽亞書。神作為一位戰士來臨；祂作為一位戰士回歸。今年的主題，就是『為婚禮而戰』。」拉比沙皮拉鄭重宣告本年度的標題——希伯來文叫作 Milchemet HaChatunah (מלחמת החתונה)，意思就是「為婚禮而戰」、「為 chuppah 而戰」。 “This year's theme is based on the prophet Isaiah. God is coming as a warrior; He is returning as a warrior. This year's theme is the War for the Wedding.” Rabbi Shapira lays down the year's title: Milchemet HaChatunah (מלחמת החתונה) — “The War for the Wedding,” “The War for the chuppah.”

「耶和華必像勇士出去，必像戰士激動熱心，要喊叫，大聲吶喊，要用大力攻擊仇敵。」——以賽亞書 42:13 “The LORD shall go forth like a mighty man; He shall stir up His zeal like a man of war. He shall cry out, yes, shout aloud; He shall prevail against His enemies.”— Isaiah 42:13

以賽亞所描寫的這位「耶和華 ish milchamah」(אִישׁ מִלְחָמָה，「戰士耶和華」) 並非離婚禮很遠——恰恰相反，祂正是為婚禮而戰。為了讓新娘平安到達 chuppah 之下，新郎必須先擊敗一切阻擋婚禮的勢力：宗教仇恨、屬靈的偽冒、攻擊以色列的鄰邦、想要奪走新娘的取代神學、以及那個自古以來就想破壞神與祂百姓婚禮的「HaSatan」(הַשָׂטָן，「敵對者」)。 The Isaianic figure of “YHWH ish milchamah” (אִישׁ מִלְחָמָה, “the LORD a man of war”) is not far from the wedding — He is fighting precisely for the wedding. For the bride to arrive safely under the chuppah, the bridegroom must first defeat everything that stands against the wedding: religious hatred, spiritual counterfeits, hostile neighbors against Israel, replacement theologies that would steal the bride, and the ancient HaSatan (הַשָׂטָן, “the adversary”) who has tried since the beginning to wreck God's wedding with His people.

所以——「為婚禮而戰」不是修辭的恫嚇，而是聖經的記敘。新娘之夜不是一場單純的慶典，而是仍在進行的戰役中的一個更新誓言之夜。整個 12 小時的直播，從拉比沙皮拉開場、Chazan Golden 重演出埃及記和使徒行傳、到世界各地的拉比與牧者的教導，全都圍繞這個主題：新郎為了祂的新娘而戰；新娘必須學會與祂並肩站立。 So the “War for the Wedding” is not rhetorical thunder — it is biblical reporting. The Night of the Bride is not merely a celebration but a night of renewed vows in the middle of an ongoing battle. The full twelve-hour broadcast — from Rabbi Shapira's opening, to Chazan Golden's reenactment of Exodus and Acts, to teachings by rabbis and pastors around the world — all circle this one theme: the Bridegroom fights for His bride; the bride must learn to stand with Him.

四、Yeshua 就是 Torah，Torah 就是 MashiachIV. Yeshua Is Torah, and Torah Is Mashiach

在這段開場中，拉比沙皮拉作出了一個非常大膽、極具神學份量的宣告： In this opening segment, Rabbi Shapira makes a bold, theologically weighty claim:

「耶書亞就是妥拉；妥拉就是彌賽亞。它們是同一回事。」 “Yeshua is the Torah, and Torah is Mashiach. They are one and the same.”

這並不是隨口說的口號，而是直接呼應約翰福音 1:1 與 1:14：「太初有道 (希臘文 Logos λόγος，希伯來文背景中即「妥拉」)，道與神同在，道就是神…道成了肉身，住在我們中間。」當約翰寫下「Logos」這個字時，他並不是在引用希臘哲學，而是在引用第二聖殿時期猶太教中對Memra (מימרא，「神的話」) 與Torah 的位格化理解。拉比沙皮拉所說的「Yeshua = Torah = Mashiach」，正是這個猶太思想結構的延續：新郎、婚約、新娘——三者在彌賽亞的位格中合一。 This is not a slogan tossed off — it is a direct echo of John 1:1 and 1:14: “In the beginning was the Word (Greek Logos, λόγος; in its Hebrew background, the Torah). The Word was with God, and the Word was God… and the Word became flesh and dwelt among us.” When John wrote Logos, he was not quoting Greek philosophy but Second-Temple Jewish thought about Memra (מימרא, “the word of God”) and the personified Torah. “Yeshua = Torah = Mashiach” extends precisely that Jewish structure: Bridegroom, ketubah, and Bride are united in the person of the Messiah.

所以當你在五旬節 (Shavuot) 慶祝妥拉的賜下時，你其實也在慶祝彌賽亞的賜下；當你接受耶書亞時，你並不是與妥拉斷絕，而是與妥拉的位格化相遇。這個觀念，對華人基督徒尤為重要——因為許多華人教會的教導，把「律法」和「恩典」對立起來，彷彿耶穌來「廢除」了律法。但耶書亞自己說：「莫想我來要廢掉律法和先知；我來不是要廢掉，乃是要成全。」(太 5:17)。 So when you celebrate the giving of the Torah at Shavuot, you are also celebrating the giving of the Messiah. When you receive Yeshua, you are not severed from Torah — you are meeting Torah in its personified form. This matters especially for Chinese Christians, because much Chinese-church teaching pits “law” against “grace,” as if Jesus came to “abolish” the law. But Yeshua Himself says: “Do not think that I came to abolish the Law or the Prophets; I did not come to abolish, but to fulfill” (Matthew 5:17).

五、行動：拿、看、給V. Action: Take, Watch, Give

拉比沙皮拉強調：這場聚集不是讓你旁觀，而是讓你參與。他直接列出三件具體的事——「拿」、「看」、「給」。 Rabbi Shapira insists this gathering is not for spectators but for participants. He lays out three concrete actions — take, watch, give.

第一，「拿」——把今晚的教材放在自己手裡。「現在是您最好的機會，請將它拿到 Kindle 上，讓它進入您的手中。」這呼應猶太傳統中對學習 (limud לימוד) 的重視：你必須主動把書打開、放在桌上、進入內容。 First — take. Get tonight's material into your own hands. “This is your best chance — get it on Kindle, get it in your hands.” This echoes the Jewish ethos of limud (לימוד, “learning”): you must actively open the book, lay it on the table, and enter the text.

第二，「看」——完整地參與這 12 小時。「您將會聽到來自世界各地的教導…我們將會有來自世界各地的教導。」這不是要求你坐在屏幕前 12 小時——而是要求你意識到，在這 12 小時中，全球的弟兄姊妹正在輪流獻上他們的教導、禱告、與敬拜。你的「在場」(presence) 本身就是一種獻祭。 Second — watch. Engage the full twelve hours. “You will hear teachings from all over the world.” This is not a demand that you sit glued to a screen for twelve hours — it is a call to recognise that across those twelve hours, brothers and sisters around the globe are taking turns offering their teachings, prayers, and worship. Your simple presence is itself a kind of offering.

第三，「給」——奉獻支持。事工這個節 (Chag) 的內部目標，是為亞洲三場妥拉復興籌集 18,500 美元，並為三卷妥拉卷軸 (Sifrei Torah ספרי תורה) 預備。請注意「亞洲三場復興」這個細節——這直接呼召華人、韓國、日本、東南亞的信徒：亞洲是這位新郎-戰士行軍的下一個方向之一。 Third — give. Support the work. The ministry's internal goal for this Chag (festival) is to raise $18,500 toward three Torah revivals in Asia and toward three new Torah scrolls (Sifrei Torah, ספרי תורה). Notice that detail — three revivals in Asia — it is a direct call to Chinese, Korean, Japanese, and Southeast-Asian believers: Asia is one of the next directions in which this Bridegroom-Warrior is marching.

六、致中文與韓文觀眾 ── Baruch HaBaVI. To the Chinese and Korean Audience — Baruch HaBa

在開場的結尾，拉比沙皮拉特別轉過身來，向亞洲的觀眾致意：「所以，今天從中國或韓國觀看我們的朋友，無論您身處何處，我們以『Baruch haba, B'Shem Adonai』的詞語歡迎您。」 Near the end of the opening, Rabbi Shapira turns specifically to his Asian audience: “To those of you watching today from China or from Korea, wherever you are — we welcome you with the words ‘Baruch haba, B'Shem Adonai.’”

בָּרוּךְ הַבָּא בְּשֵׁם יְהוָה
「Baruch haba b'Shem Adonai」
「奉耶和華的名來的，是應當稱頌的。」——詩篇 118:26 בָּרוּךְ הַבָּא בְּשֵׁם יְהוָה
“Baruch haba b'Shem Adonai”
“Blessed is he who comes in the name of the LORD.”— Psalm 118:26

這句祝福並非隨意挑選——這是猶太人千百年來在逾越節 Seder 桌上唱、在歡迎彌賽亞時唱的句子。耶書亞自己也引用過這句話 (太 23:39)：「你們將不再見我，直到你們說：『奉主名來的是應當稱頌的。』」當沙皮拉拉比把這句祝福用在從中國或韓國觀看的人身上時，他實際上是在說：你們不只是「另一群觀眾」——你們是新郎在亞洲所等待的、那群開口呼喊「Baruch haba」的人。 That blessing is not chosen at random — it is the line Jews have sung for centuries at the Passover Seder table and as the cry of welcome to the Messiah. Yeshua Himself quotes it (Matthew 23:39): “You shall not see me again until you say, ‘Blessed is He who comes in the name of the Lord.’” When Rabbi Shapira directs this blessing to those watching from China or Korea, he is in effect saying: you are not merely “another audience” — you are the very ones in Asia whom the Bridegroom is waiting for, to lift up the cry “Baruch haba!”

七、對華人信徒的意義VII. What This Means for Chinese Believers

第一，這個開場顛覆了我們對「全球教會」的想像。它不是「西方信徒透過短宣去『拯救』東方的人」——它是新郎-戰士耶書亞在亞洲、非洲、歐洲、美洲同時開戰、同時赴婚禮。華人信徒並不是「邊陲」——我們正站在「三場亞洲復興」的呼召前線。 First, this opening overturns our picture of the “global church.” It is not “Western believers going on short-term missions to ‘save’ the East.” It is Yeshua, the Bridegroom-Warrior, simultaneously waging battle and processing toward the wedding across Asia, Africa, Europe, and the Americas. Chinese believers are not the “periphery” — we are standing on the front line of a call to three Asian revivals.

第二，「五旬節 = 妥拉的賜下 = 彌賽亞的賜下」這個猶太式神學結構，給華人教會帶來一個禮物：我們不必再在「律法」和「恩典」之間焦慮地左右搖擺。妥拉本身就是新郎的ketubah；耶書亞就是新郎本人。讀妥拉就是讀祂的情書；行妥拉就是與祂同行。 Second, the Jewish theological structure “Shavuot = giving of Torah = giving of Messiah” offers Chinese churches a gift: we no longer need to sway anxiously between “law” and “grace.” The Torah is the Bridegroom's ketubah; Yeshua is the Bridegroom Himself. To read Torah is to read His love letter; to walk in Torah is to walk with Him.

第三，「Milchemet HaChatunah」(為婚禮而戰) 提醒華人信徒：屬靈生活並不是「舒服地等待主再來」。新郎正在打仗——為了讓祂的新娘平安到達 chuppah，祂在打仗。我們不能站在旁邊。我們要拿起妥拉、拿起禱告、拿起對以色列的祝福、拿起對亞洲復興的奉獻——與祂一起作戰。 Third, Milchemet HaChatunah — the War for the Wedding — reminds Chinese believers that the spiritual life is not “comfortably waiting for the Lord to return.” The Bridegroom is at war — for His bride to arrive safely at the chuppah, He is fighting. We cannot stand to the side. We must pick up the Torah, pick up prayer, pick up blessing for Israel, pick up giving for Asian revival — and fight alongside Him.

八、結語 ── 婚禮在召喚VIII. Closing — The Wedding Is Calling

「שָׁלוֹם Shalom。從中國、從韓國、從你所站立的任何一塊土地——我們以『Baruch haba, B'Shem Adonai』歡迎你。新娘之夜已經開始。為婚禮的戰役正在進行。耶書亞就是妥拉，妥拉就是彌賽亞。請拿起、請觀看、請奉獻。新郎正在為你開戰；新娘的家正向你敞開。Am Yisrael Chai。」 “Shalom. From China, from Korea, from whatever ground you are standing on — we welcome you with Baruch haba, B'Shem Adonai. The Night of the Bride has begun. The War for the Wedding is on. Yeshua is Torah, and Torah is Mashiach. Take. Watch. Give. The Bridegroom is fighting for you; the house of the Bride is opening to you. Am Yisrael Chai.”
