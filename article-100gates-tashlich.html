五分鐘認識「塔什利赫」 Tashlich in 5 Minutes

在新年的池水邊，我們把口袋裡的麵包、石頭、塵屑投進水裡——這是 תַּשְׁלִיךְ（塔什利赫，「投擲、拋去」）的古老習俗。但這個動作真正的意思是什麼？它如何指向那位說「去吧，從此不要再犯罪」的彌賽亞？ By the new-year pond we dig the bread, the stones, the lint from our pockets and cast them into the water — the ancient custom of תַּשְׁלִיךְ (Tashlich, “casting off”). But what does the act really mean? And how does it point to the Messiah who said, “Go and sin no more”?

Jaxon Edwards · Ahavat Ammi 事工 Jaxon Edwards · Ahavat Ammi Ministries
2026 · 5 · 25 · 约 5 分钟阅读~5 min read · ▶ 觀看 · 五分鐘認識「塔什利赫」Watch · Tashlich in 5 Minutes ·

「大家新年快樂。我們現在站在池水邊，正在進行所謂的『塔什利赫』儀式。」Jaxon Edwards 弟兄這樣開場。תַּשְׁלִיךְ（Tashlich，意為「投擲、拋去」）是猶太人在新年（Rosh Hashanah）期間的古老習俗：人們來到流動的水邊，象徵性地把過去一年的罪投進水裡。動作只需要五分鐘，背後卻是一整套關於悔改、赦免與重生的神學。 “Shana Tov, everyone. We're here by the pond, and we are in what's called the Tashlich service.” So Jaxon Edwards begins. תַּשְׁלִיךְ (Tashlich, “casting off”) is an ancient Jewish custom kept during the New Year (Rosh Hashanah): people come to flowing water and symbolically cast the sins of the past year into it. The act takes five minutes; behind it lies a whole theology of repentance, forgiveness, and rebirth.

一、塔什利赫是什麼？I. What Is Tashlich?

塔什利赫在猶太傳統中有許許多多的做法。其核心觀念是：你正在釋放過去一年的罪，把它們投進水裡。通常人們會帶麵包，讓魚或鴨子吃掉；也有人用石頭的傳統；還有人把口袋裡的塵屑（lint）摳出來，丟進水中。 Tashlich has many, many traditions in Judaism. The core idea is this: you are releasing the sins of the previous year, throwing them into the water. Usually people bring bread that the fish or the ducks can eat; some keep the tradition of rocks; others dig down into their pockets, pull out the lint, and toss it in.

「許多人會把他們的 tzitziot（流蘇繸子）在河面上抖一抖，或者抖一抖褲腳的反摺——所有這一類的動作。」Jaxon 說。這個習俗背後的希伯來名詞 תַּשְׁלִיךְ，正是「拋去、投擲」這個動詞——你親手把罪「拋」出去。如果你有機會，也可以和我們一同參與。 “A lot of people will take their tzitziot and shake them over the river, or shake out their pants cuffs — all those things,” Jaxon says. The Hebrew noun behind the custom, תַּשְׁלִיךְ, is the verb “to cast, to throw” — you cast the sin away with your own hand. And if you get the chance, you can participate with us.

二、彌迦書 ── 「投入深海」II. Micah — “Cast Into the Depths of the Sea”

塔什利赫的整個畫面，來自彌迦書 7 章。Jaxon 帶領大家翻到祈禱書（Machzor）第 156 頁，誦讀彌迦書 7:18 以下： The whole picture of Tashlich comes from Micah chapter 7. Jaxon turns the congregation to page 156 in the Machzor and reads from Micah 7:18 onward:

「有何神像你，赦免罪孽，饒恕祂產業之餘民的過犯？祂不永遠懷怒，喜愛施恩。祂必再憐憫我們，將我們的罪孽踏在腳下，把我們一切的罪投入深海。」——彌迦書 7:18-19（וְתַשְׁלִיךְ） “Who is a God like You, who pardons iniquity and forgives transgression for the remnant of His heritage? He does not maintain His wrath forever, for He desires to do kindness. He will again show us compassion; He will suppress our iniquities, and You will cast all their sins into the depths of the sea.”— Micah 7:18-19 (וְתַשְׁלִיךְ)

請注意這節經文的動詞——「投入」（וְתַשְׁלִיךְ，ve-tashlich）——正是這整個儀式得名的字根。我們手中投進水裡的麵包、石頭、塵屑，是一幅活生生的圖畫，宣告神對祂百姓所應許的事：祂要親自把我們的罪投入海的最深處。赦免不是因為我們配得，而是因為「祂喜愛施恩」。 Notice the verb in this verse — “You will cast” (וְתַשְׁלִיךְ, ve-tashlich) — the very root that gives the whole ritual its name. The bread, the stones, the lint we throw into the water are a living picture of what God promises to His people: He Himself will cast our sins into the deepest part of the sea. Forgiveness comes not because we deserve it, but because “He desires to do kindness.”

三、詩篇 130 ── 從深處呼求III. Psalm 130 — A Cry From the Depths

接著誦讀的是詩篇 130 篇——「上行之詩」。它的第一句，正與彌迦書的「深海」遙相呼應：Mima'amakim k'raticha Adonai——「我從深處向你求告，主啊。」我們把罪投進「深處」；而我們的呼求，也正是從生命的「深處」發出的。 Next comes Psalm 130 — a Song of Ascents. Its opening line answers Micah's “depths of the sea”: Mima'amakim k'raticha Adonai — “Out of the depths I cry to You, O LORD.” We cast our sins into the depths; and our cry, too, rises from the depths of life.

「主啊，你若究察罪孽，誰能站立得住呢？但在你有赦免之恩，要叫人敬畏你。我等候耶和華，我的心等候；我也仰望祂的話。」——詩篇 130:3-5 “If You, O LORD, kept a record of iniquities, O Lord, who could stand? But with You there is forgiveness, that You may be feared. I wait for the LORD, my soul waits, and in His word I hope.”— Psalm 130:3-5

這就是塔什利赫的神學核心：沒有人能靠自己的功德站立在神面前。若神逐筆記下我們的罪，沒有一個人站得住。我們唯一的盼望，是神「不究察」、是「在祂有赦免」。這正是 תְּשׁוּבָה（teshuvah，「回轉、悔改」）的根基——悔改不是用善行去抵消罪，而是回轉，把自己交給那位喜愛赦免的神。 This is the theological heart of Tashlich: no one can stand before God on the strength of his own merit. If God kept a ledger of our sins, not one of us could stand. Our only hope is that God does not “keep a record,” that “with Him there is forgiveness.” This is the foundation of תְּשׁוּבָה (teshuvah, “return, repentance”) — repentance is not offsetting sin with good deeds, but turning back and entrusting yourself to the God who loves to forgive.

四、投出去 ── 成為「重造的新人」IV. The Casting — Becoming a “Remade Creature”

讀完經文，就是動手的時刻。「我們現在要把它拿出來——無論是你的麵包、石頭，還是你口袋裡的塵屑——投進這片水裡。」Jaxon 說。「當我們這樣做的時候，我們正在釋放這一切的罪，並且以一個被重造的新人、一個復活的生命，進入新的一年。」 After the readings comes the moment of action. “We're now taking it out — whether it be your bread or rocks or the lint from your pockets — and casting it into the body of water,” Jaxon says. “As we do this, we are releasing all of these sins, and we are coming into the new year as a remade creature, as a resurrected being.”

就在這裡，Jaxon 把這個猶太古老的習俗，與耶書亞（Yeshua）的話直接相連：「正如耶書亞所說：『去吧，從此不要再犯罪。』」塔什利赫所象徵的——把舊年的罪投入水中、成為新造的人——正指向那位能真正使人從罪的捆綁中得釋放的彌賽亞。儀式是影子；釋放的實底，在祂裡面。 Right here, Jaxon ties the ancient Jewish custom directly to the words of Yeshua: “As Yeshua says, go and sin no more.” What Tashlich pictures — casting the old year's sins into the water and becoming a new creation — points to the Messiah who can truly free a person from bondage to sin. The ritual is the shadow; the substance of the release is found in Him.

五、結語 ── 願今年是釋放之年V. Closing — May This Be a Year of Release

Jaxon 從自己口袋裡摳出塵屑，投進水中，作為這篇短教導的收束。塔什利赫之所以動人，不在於塵屑或石頭本身，而在於它把一個抽象的真理變成可觸可見的動作：罪不是永遠洗不掉的污點，而是一種能被釋放的捆綁。 Jaxon digs the lint from his own pockets and casts it into the water, closing this short teaching. What makes Tashlich moving is not the lint or the stones themselves, but that it turns an abstract truth into a tangible, visible act: sin is not a permanent stain, but a bondage that can be released.

「願我們眾人都有一個甜美的新年、一個充滿 mitzvot（誡命善行）、沒有罪的生命。當我們釋放我們的罪時，我們也在釋放我們對罪的捆綁。願這一年，對我們所有人都是釋放之年。Shana Tova。」 “May we all have a sweet new year, a life of mitzvot and a life of no sin. As we release our sin, we are releasing our bondage to sin. And may this year be a release for all of us. Shana Tova.”
